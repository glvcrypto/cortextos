```
The single fastest way for a local business to climb Google's local results is also the one most owners treat as an afterthought. Reviews.

I look at Google Business Profiles across Northern Ontario every week. The pattern is almost always the same. A business does good work, has happy customers, and sits on twelve reviews while the competitor down the road has ninety. The work isn't the problem. The system for asking is.

Here is the four-step system I set up for every client. All of it is free.

Step 1: Ask in person, at the peak moment. The best time to ask for a review is the moment the job is done and the customer is visibly happy, not a week later by email. For an owner-operated business this is your advantage. You are the one finishing the job, so you are the one who can ask.

Step 2: Hand them the direct link. Google gives every profile a short review link. Telling someone to look you up on Google and leave a review adds friction at every step, and friction is where reviews die. Save the link in your phone and text it on the spot.

Step 3: Reply to every review, good and bad. Google's local ranking treats owner replies as a signal of a real, active business. A reply to a critical review is not for the upset customer. It is for the next hundred people who read it. This is the step almost everyone skips.

Step 4: Never buy reviews. Google's filter is good at catching fake review patterns, and the penalty can be a full profile suspension. A slow, real review profile beats a fast, fake one every time.

None of this needs a budget. It needs a habit.

What is your business sitting at for Google reviews right now?

```
