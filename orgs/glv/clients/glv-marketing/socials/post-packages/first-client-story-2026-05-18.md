My first local SEO client ran an auto repair shop. He'd been in business for nine years and had one question: why wasn't he showing up on Google.

He thought the answer would be technical. A broken link, a setting he'd missed, something a developer had done wrong.

What I found was a Google Business Profile that had been auto-generated from data Google collected from other sources. The address was slightly wrong. The hours weren't set. There were no photos. The primary category was set to the broad version instead of the specific service type. One review, from 2019.

I told him the website wasn't the priority. Not yet. The map pack is where most local service business searches convert on mobile, and his profile had never been claimed, verified, or touched. We claimed and verified the listing, corrected the address, set the right primary category, uploaded 12 photos, set hours, and started asking customers for reviews in person.

Within 90 days he was in the local pack for his primary service and city. Calls picked up noticeably.

Here's the rule I built for every engagement that followed: document the state of things on day one before changing anything. He couldn't tell me how many calls he was getting before we started, so I couldn't prove how much it grew.

That rule has saved me from making things worse more times than I can count.
