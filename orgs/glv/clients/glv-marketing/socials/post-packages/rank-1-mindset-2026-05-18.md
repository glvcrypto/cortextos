**Caption:**

```
"I want to be number one on Google."

It's the first thing most local business owners say when they hire a marketing agency. I understand why. It sounds like the goal. It sounds like winning.

For my first year of client work I tried to deliver exactly that. Then I started paying attention to what actually produced calls and form submissions, and I changed the way I set client expectations entirely.

Here's what I learned:

Rank #1 means different things depending on the search. The local 3-pack, the organic position one, position one for a broad category, position one for a specific service plus city phrase. A business can rank first in one of those and not appear at all in the others.

Broad keyword traffic doesn't equal qualified leads. I had a client ranking page one for a category-level keyword in a competitive city. Traffic was up. Calls were not. People finding them via that keyword were researchers, students, businesses in the wrong service area. None of them converted.

What I track now: map pack visibility for the top two or three high-intent phrases. GBP Insights: calls per month, direction requests, website clicks. GSC clicks on service and city pages. Contact form submissions from organic.

Rank is a means, not the outcome. I don't promise "page one rankings" in my first call. I ask the client: when someone in your city searches for what you do, what do you want to happen?

What do you track to measure local SEO success?
```
