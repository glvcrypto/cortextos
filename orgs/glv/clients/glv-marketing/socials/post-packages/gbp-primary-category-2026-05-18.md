There's one field in Google Business Profile that carries more weight on local pack rankings than anything else.

Most businesses set it at signup in 15 seconds and never revisit it. It's the primary category.

Google uses primary category to decide which searches your profile is eligible to appear for. If your primary category is too broad, you're competing against every business in that broad category, not just the ones doing your specific service. "Contractor" puts you beside roofers, painters, and framers. "Plumbing Contractor" puts you beside your actual competitors for the searches that matter.

The fix starts with competitor research. Open a private browser. Search your primary service and city. Click into the top three businesses in the local pack. Check the category displayed on their profile. That's your research baseline.

Then check Google's full category list. GBP has several hundred categories, and new ones get added regularly. The specific category you need may exist and be more precise than you realised.

The change itself takes 30 seconds in GBP Manager. Edit profile, business information, category. Change the primary. Leave accurate secondaries in place.

Give it 30 days before drawing conclusions. Category changes take time to register in local search. Check your map pack visibility before and after using a private browser or a rank grid tool.

Primary category is the first thing I change on almost every new local client audit. Free, takes under a minute, and the biggest single needle-mover in the profile.
