**Caption:**

```
The 6-point pre-audit I run before making any change to a client's Google Business Profile.

Most people jump straight into optimising. I learned to stop and look first.

I've inherited profiles from bad agencies, found suspended listings clients didn't know about, and made the mistake of editing something before I had a baseline screenshot. These 6 checks now take 10 minutes at the start of every new engagement.

Check 1: Who is the Primary Owner? Every GBP profile has one Primary Owner with full control, including the ability to transfer or delete the listing. If a previous agency is Primary Owner and the relationship ended badly, your client has a serious problem.

Check 2: Is there a duplicate listing? Open a private browser and search the business name. If two profiles come up, the duplicate suppresses the primary.

Check 3: Is the profile verified, flagged, or suspended? GBP doesn't always email businesses when there's a problem. Check the status in GBP Manager before assuming the profile is active.

Check 4: Screenshot the current state before changing anything. Primary category, hours, description, photo count. Baseline documentation takes 5 minutes and saves weeks of confusion later.

Check 5: Are there pending user-suggested edits? Google lets anyone suggest edits. Check the queue before adding your own.

Check 6: Does the NAP match the website exactly? Business name, address, and local phone number need to match GBP, the website footer, the LocalBusiness schema, and the top citation directories.

10 minutes. Every new client. Every time.
```
