## Exec Summary

- **Budget:** $500 CAD/mo — enough for 15–25 qualified leads/month in a Tier 2 Canadian market
- **Platform:** Meta (Facebook + Instagram) — right call; Google Search is viable as add-on at $1K+/mo but premature at $500

## 1. Buyer Personas

### SSM Market Context
- Population: 72,051 city proper; ~150–300K within 100km radius
- Median home price: $345K (March 2026); well below Ontario average of $780K+
- 34% single-person households; 24.9% of population 65+; median age 45.4
- Algoma Steel pending 1,000+ layoffs in 2026 — economic headwind for first-time buyers, tailwind for downsizers looking to simplify

### Persona 1: The Downsizer (Primary — Highest Quality Leads)
- **Age:** 55–75
- **Situation:** Kids grown, family home too big, fixed income or approaching retirement
- **Pain:** High property taxes, maintenance burden, heating costs of oversized home
- **Hook:** "Less house, more life" — lifestyle upgrade framing, not sacrifice
- **Motivation to act:** Algoma layoffs + rising costs create urgency; high senior % in SSM = large addressable segment
- **Lead magnet:** Pricing guide + video walkthrough of finished unit
- **Budget fit:** These buyers often have equity from selling family home — higher purchase probability

### Persona 2: The First-Time Buyer (Primary — Highest Volume)
- **Age:** 25–40
- **Situation:** Priced out of traditional housing; renting; wants to own something
- **Pain:** Can't afford $345K+ even in SSM; mortgage qualifying harder post-rate hikes
- **Hook:** "Own a home under $150K — while your neighbors pay $350K+" 
- **Motivation to act:** Affordability crisis is real and acute; tiny home = only path to ownership for many
- **Lead magnet:** Cost calculator or buyer's guide
- **Budget fit:** Lower purchase price = easier financing approval, shorter sales cycle

### Persona 3: The Land Owner / Off-Grid Buyer (Secondary — High Value, Low Volume)
- **Age:** 30–60
- **Situation:** Has rural land in Northern Ontario; wants a structure without traditional construction timeline/cost
- **Pain:** Financing off-grid builds is difficult; most banks won't touch it
- **Hook:** "You've got the land. We've got the home." (already in our copy bank — Ad 6 from earlier campaign plan)
- **Lead magnet:** Financing guide — builder financing as major differentiator
- **Note:** Small segment in SSM but high purchase intent and high avg. transaction value

### Persona 4: The Remote Worker / Relocator (Emerging — Lower Priority)
- **Age:** 28–50
- **Situation:** Relocated or considering relocation from GTA/Ottawa to lower cost area; wants move-in ready option
- **Pain:** New-construction wait times (12–24 months); can't rent in rural areas
- **Hook:** Local market opportunity framing; SSM as affordable base
- **Lead magnet:** "Why Sault Ste. Marie" local market report
- **Budget fit:** Lower volume but higher income, higher purchase commitment

## 4. Lead Capture: Meta Lead Form vs. Landing Page

**Recommendation: Meta native Lead Form**

| Factor | Lead Form | Landing Page |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| CPL | $25–35 CAD (estimated) | $35–55 CAD |
| Form friction | Low (pre-filled) | Higher (new page load) |
| Lead volume | Higher | Lower |
| Lead quality | Medium | Higher |
| Setup complexity | Low | High (requires dev + pixel) |
| **Winner at $500/mo** | ✅ | ❌ |

**Lead form fields (keep to 4 max):**
1. First name
2. Email
3. Phone (optional — label it clearly)
4. One qualifier: *"Do you have land already, or still exploring your options?"*

**Thank-you message:** "Thanks — Joey will be in touch within 1 business day."

**Important:** Joey needs a follow-up system. Lead form leads go cold fast. Ideal: email within 1 hour, call within 24 hours. At 15–25 leads/month, this is manageable manually.

## 5. Ad Angles to Test in Stage 3

Five creative angles ranked by predicted performance for this market:

### Angle 1: Price Comparison / Affordability (Highest predicted volume)
- **Hook:** "Own a home under $150K — while your neighbors pay $350K+"
- **Format:** Carousel (traditional home vs. tiny home, cost side-by-side)
- **Audience fit:** First-time buyers (Persona 2)
- **Why it works:** Immediate, quantified, locally relevant; SSM median $345K makes the contrast stark

### Angle 2: Lifestyle / Downsizer (Highest predicted lead quality)
- **Hook:** "The kids are grown. The house is too big. There's another way."
- **Format:** 30–60 sec video (founder-style UGC or polished walkthrough of finished unit)
- **Audience fit:** Downsizers (Persona 1)
- **Why it works:** Emotional and aspirational; frames tiny home as life upgrade, not sacrifice; zero fear/scare

### Angle 3: "Got Land?" / Off-Grid
- **Hook:** "Got land in Northern Ontario? We've got your home."
- **Format:** Static image or short video; land parcel + completed tiny home
- **Audience fit:** Land owners / off-grid buyers (Persona 3)
- **Why it works:** Directly solves the land problem; builder financing angle is untapped by competitors

### Angle 4: Build Process / Trust
- **Hook:** "Watch a Titan tiny home get built — start to finish"
- **Format:** 60–90 sec Reel (time-lapse or narrated build process)
- **Audience fit:** All segments (builds trust); essential for high-consideration purchase
- **Why it works:** De-risks the purchase; Joey + Kathryn on camera = brand credibility; UGC outperforms polished

### Angle 5: Local Opportunity / SSM-Specific (if geo stays SSM-radius)
- **Hook:** "Sault Ste. Marie is changing. Are you ready to own?"
- **Format:** Carousel (local market data + Titan home) 
- **Audience fit:** Relocators / remote workers; locally-aware buyers
- **Why it works:** Local specificity converts better than generic; SSM housing data creates urgency
