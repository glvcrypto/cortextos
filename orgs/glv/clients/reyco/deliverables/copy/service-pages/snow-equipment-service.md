## Common Snowblower Problems

A few things we see week to week:

- **Won't start in the cold.** Almost always fuel-related — old gas from last spring, gummed carburetor, or a weak battery on electric-start models.
- **Starts but bogs out under snow load.** Carb adjustment, fuel filter, or the throttle linkage.
- **Auger spins slowly or not at all.** Sheared shear pins, worn belt, or auger gearbox issue.
- **Drive doesn't engage.** Drive belt slipping, friction wheel worn, control cable out of adjustment.
- **Smoke from the muffler.** Oil level too high, head gasket, or rings on a higher-hour unit.
- **Pull cord won't recoil.** Recoil spring or pulley assembly.
- **Headlight or chute control not working.** Wiring or switch issue.

If you're not sure what's going on, describe it when you call. Damian can usually tell from the symptoms whether it's an hour on the bench or a full diagnostic.

## Snowblower Service — Common Questions

**How long does snowblower service take?**
Most repairs take 3 to 5 business days. Simple jobs like a tune-up or shear pin swap can be same day if the bench isn't backed up. In peak season (December through February) the bench books out one to three weeks — book early.

**How much does snowblower service cost?**
A typical annual tune-up runs 1 to 2 hours of bench time plus parts. Carburetor work is 1 to 3 hours plus parts. Major engine repairs get a written quote after the diagnostic. Either way, we call you with a clear number before any work starts.

**Can I service my snowblower myself?**
You can do basic maintenance — change the oil, swap the plug, check the belt, run fuel stabiliser at end of season. The risks of DIY are missing the things you can't see: a worn auger gearbox bearing, a glazed friction wheel, a fuel pickup screen full of varnish. Our annual service includes a full inspection so we catch those before they leave you stranded after a snowfall.

**My snowblower won't start in the cold — what's the most common cause?**
Almost always fuel. Modern ethanol-blend gas goes off in three to six months, and snowblowers tend to sit with a half-tank of gas all summer. Drain the tank, run fresh fuel through, and try again. If it still won't fire, bring it in — a quick carburetor clean often gets you back to clearing the driveway without a full rebuild.

**Do you service brands beyond Cub Cadet and Toro?**
Yes. We're authorised dealers for Cub Cadet and Toro, but our techs work on most major snowblower brands. If you're not sure whether we cover yours, call ahead with the make and model and we'll tell you straight.

**Do you do mid-winter emergency repairs?**
We try to fit time-sensitive jobs in around scheduled service, especially for customers who already booked annual service with us in the fall. If your snowblower goes down after a big storm, call as soon as you notice — the earlier you're in the queue, the sooner we can have you running again.
