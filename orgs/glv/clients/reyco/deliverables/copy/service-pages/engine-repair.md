## How Engine Repair Works at Reyco

Drop the equipment off, or call ahead and we'll book a slot on the right bench (marine, small engine or ATV — depends on what you've got). Here's the usual flow:

1. **Intake.** We tag the unit, write down what's wrong (or what you've noticed), and get your contact info.
2. **Diagnostic.** Lee or Damian inspects the engine — compression, fuel, spark, and the parts most likely to be at fault for what you described. For bigger engine work we pull the heads or run a leakdown test.
3. **Estimate first, then approval.** We call you with a clear price before any work starts. No surprise bills.
4. **Repair.** Most jobs use OEM parts pulled straight from our [parts room](/service/order-parts/). Same-day pickup if it's small, 3 to 5 business days if it's bigger or we're waiting on a part. Major engine work (rebuild, head gasket, full powerhead replacement) can take 1 to 3 weeks depending on parts lead time.
5. **Test before pickup.** We run the engine before you take it home. For marine work, that's a full water-ready test. For small engine work, full start and run test. We'd rather catch a problem on our bench than send you out with it.

## Repair vs Replace — How We Help You Decide

This is the call most owners get wrong, in either direction. Some pour money into a 15-year-old riding mower that's about to need a transmission too. Others scrap a perfectly good outboard that needed a $400 fuel system rebuild.

Here's how we think about it:

- **What's the equipment worth used?** A repair that costs more than 60% of the equipment's used value is usually a bad call.
- **What else is about to fail?** A blown head gasket on an engine with 1500 hours and worn bearings is just the first bill. We tell you what we see during diagnostic.
- **Parts availability.** If your engine is from a brand that no longer makes the parts (or charges $300 for a $40 part), repair stops making sense.
- **What's the new equivalent cost?** Sometimes a new mower is $1200 and the rebuild is $900. The repair wins. Sometimes a new outboard is $8,000 and the rebuild is $3,000. The repair wins bigger.

We give you both numbers — repair cost and roughly what new costs. You decide. We'd rather sell you a new engine or unit than waste your money on a repair that won't last another season.

## Engine Repair — Common Questions

**How much does small engine repair cost?**
A typical annual service or tune-up runs 1 to 3 hours of bench time plus parts. Carburetor work is 1 to 3 hours plus parts. Major work (head gasket, top-end rebuild) gets a written quote after the diagnostic. We always call you with a clear number before any work starts.

**How long does engine repair take?**
Small engine repairs typically take 3 to 5 business days. Marine engine work is similar, longer if we're waiting on parts. Major rebuilds or powerhead replacements can run 1 to 3 weeks depending on parts lead time and bench backlog.

**How do I decide whether to repair or replace?**
We give you both numbers — repair cost and roughly what new costs — and you decide. The rough rule we use: a repair that costs more than 60% of the equipment's used value is usually a bad call. We'll also flag what else looks worn during diagnostic so you're not paying $400 today only to face $800 next year.

**Do you work on car or truck engines?**
No. Our bench is set up for marine, small engine and ATV work — outboard, snowblower, mower, chainsaw, generator, ATV. Different parts catalogue, different specialty entirely. If you need automotive engine work, we'll point you at a shop in town that does it.

**Can you rebuild an old outboard or just sell me a new one?**
Both, depending on what makes sense. If parts are still available and the powerhead isn't catastrophically damaged, a rebuild is often the right call — and a lot cheaper than a new outboard. If the engine is from a brand that's discontinued the parts, or the rebuild quote is half the cost of new, we'll tell you that too.

**What's the difference between a tune-up and engine repair?**
A [tune-up](/service/tune-ups/) is preventive — annual service to keep the engine running well. Engine repair is reactive — fixing something that's already broken. We do both, and an honest tune-up often catches the small things before they become repair bills.
