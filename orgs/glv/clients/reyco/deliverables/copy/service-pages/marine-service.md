## How marine service works at Reyco

Drop the boat off (trailer or hull), or call ahead and we'll book a slot on Lee's bench. Here's the usual flow:

1. **Intake.** We tag the boat, write down what's wrong (or what you've noticed), and get your contact info.
2. **Diagnostic.** Lee inspects the engine, fuel system, electrical and the parts most likely to be at fault for what you described.
3. **Estimate first, then approval.** We call you with a clear price before any work starts. No surprise bills.
4. **Repair.** Most jobs use OEM Mercury and Princecraft parts pulled straight from our parts room. Same-day pickup if it's small, 3 to 5 business days if it's bigger or we're waiting on a part.
5. **Water test where it matters.** For engine and drive train work, we run the boat on muffs (or in-water where required) before you take it home. If it's not running right, we'd rather catch that on our bench than 20 km out on Lake Superior.

## Signs Your Boat Needs Service

A few signs your boat is overdue:

- Engine won't start or starts hard, especially first run of the season
- Rough idle, hesitation under throttle, or loses power up on plane
- Vibration that wasn't there last season
- Smoke from the exhaust (especially blue or white)
- Charging or electrical drop-outs (gauges flicker, accessories cut out)
- Steering stiffness or play
- Trolling motor losing thrust or running hot

If you're not sure what's going on, describe it when you call. Lee can usually tell from the symptoms whether it's an hour on the bench or a full diagnostic.

## Marine Service — Common Questions

**How long does marine service take?**
Most repairs take 3 to 5 business days once we've diagnosed the issue. Annual service or a small fix can be same day if the bench isn't backed up. In peak spring season (mid-April through May) the bench books out a week or two — book early.

**How much does outboard or boat repair cost?**
A typical annual outboard service runs 1 to 3 hours of bench time plus parts. Fuel system or carburetor work is 1 to 3 hours plus parts. Major engine or drive train repairs get a written quote after the diagnostic. Either way, we call you with a clear number before any work starts.

**How often should I service my boat engine?**
The Mercury service interval calls for a full service once per season or every 100 hours, whichever comes first. For a typical Northern Ontario boater that's once a year, usually at spring commissioning. If your boat sits a lot or runs hard, lean toward the hour count over the calendar.

**Why won't my outboard start after sitting all winter?**
Almost always old fuel. Modern ethanol-blend gas goes off in three to six months and gums up the carburetor or fuel injectors. If you didn't winterize properly, the carb may need a clean. Bring it in with the make and model and we'll diagnose from there — often a fuel system flush gets you back on the water without a full rebuild.

**Do you service brands beyond Mercury?**
Yes. We're Mercury's authorised marine service dealer in Sault Ste. Marie, but our techs work on most major outboard, sterndrive and trolling motor brands. If you're not sure whether we cover yours, call ahead with the make, model and engine details and we'll tell you straight.
