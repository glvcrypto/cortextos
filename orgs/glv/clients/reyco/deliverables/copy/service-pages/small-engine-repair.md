## Small Engine Repair Services

Small engines run nearly everything outside the house, and they all break in roughly the same ways. Our shop handles:

- [Lawn mowers](/service/lawn-equipment/) (push, self-propelled, riding, zero-turn)
- [Snowblowers](/service/snow-equipment/) (single, two and three stage)
- Chainsaws and trimmers
- Leaf blowers and pressure washers
- Generators and water pumps
- Most other 2-stroke and 4-stroke gas engines under 30 hp

If you bought it locally, there's a good chance we already service the brand. If you didn't, bring it in anyway. We'll have a look and tell you straight whether we can help, or who can.

## How service actually works

Drop the equipment off, or call ahead and we'll book a slot with one of our small engine mechanics. Damian heads up our bench, with two other techs backing him up during the busy seasons. Here's the usual flow:

1. **Drop off and intake.** We tag the unit, write down what's wrong (or what you've noticed), and get your contact info.
2. **Diagnostic.** Damian or one of the other techs inspects the engine — compression, fuel, spark, and the parts most likely to be at fault for what you described.
3. **Estimate first, then approval.** We call you with a clear price before any work starts. No surprise bills.
4. **Repair.** Most jobs use OEM parts pulled straight from our parts room. Same-day pick up if it's small, 3 to 5 business days if it's bigger or we're waiting on a part.
5. **Pick up.** We test it before you take it home. If it's not running right, we'd rather catch that on our bench than in your driveway.

## Signs Your Small Engine Needs Repair

A few signs your small engine is overdue for service:

- It's been sitting since last season and won't start
- It runs but loses power under load
- Smoke from the muffler (especially blue or black)
- Pull cord is loose, frayed, or won't recoil
- Blade or bar isn't cutting clean
- You've poured fuel stabiliser in and it still struggles

If there's an old can of last year's gas in the tank, that's the most common one we see in the spring. Modern ethanol-blend fuel goes off in three to six months. Empty the tank, run fresh fuel through, and if it still won't fire, we can take it from there.
