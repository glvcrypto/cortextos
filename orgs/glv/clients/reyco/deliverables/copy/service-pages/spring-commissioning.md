## What Spring Commissioning Means

Spring commissioning is the work that gets a winterized boat back to water-ready. It's the mirror image of fall [winterization](/service/winterization/): everything that was drained, fogged, removed or stabilized over winter has to be checked, refilled, replaced or reinstalled before the boat goes back in.

A proper commissioning catches the small things that build up over five months in storage — a corroded battery terminal, a cracked fuel line, a prop that picked up dock damage at haul-out — before they turn into a ruined first weekend of the season.

If you didn't winterize the boat properly last fall, this is also where we find out.

## What's in Reyco's Spring Commissioning Service

Our standard spring commissioning is a full water-ready check, not a quick once-over. We work the same Mercury procedure in reverse, plus the practical extras a Northern Ontario season demands:

- **Battery installed** (or replaced if it didn't survive winter), terminals cleaned and tested
- **Fuel system check** — fresh fuel, line and primer bulb inspected, water separator cleared, carb/injector check if the boat sat with old fuel
- **Lower unit gear oil** drained and refilled (a milky drain tells us a seal needs attention before launch, not after)
- **Engine fluids** topped or changed per Mercury service interval
- **Cooling system flush** and water pump test
- **Spark plugs** inspected and replaced if needed
- **Prop inspection** — dings, dents, shaft seal, hub condition
- **Steering and controls** check — cables, hydraulic lines, throttle response
- **Electrical** — bilge pump, navigation lights, gauges, charging system
- **Hull and trailer check** — hull damage, transom integrity, trailer bearings, tires, lights, brakes
- **Final water-ready test** on muffs (or in-water where required) so we know it runs before you do

If we find something that wasn't on the original quote — a bad fuel line, a dead battery, a worn impeller — we call you with a real number before any extra work happens. Same workflow as our [marine service](/service/marine/) bench.

## Spring Commissioning — Common Questions

**When should I book spring commissioning in Sault Ste. Marie?**
Late February to mid-March is ideal. By April the bench is filling fast and you risk missing the early-season weekends. If you winterized with us last fall, your spring slot is already pre-noted in our system — call in March to confirm.

**Do I need spring commissioning if I winterized properly?**
Yes. Winterization protects the boat from freeze damage; commissioning gets it back to water-ready. They're different jobs. Even a perfectly winterized boat needs the battery reinstalled, fluids checked, fuel refreshed, and a full water-ready test before you launch. Skipping commissioning is how a perfect winterization still ends with a tow truck on opening weekend.

**Can I commission my boat myself?**
You can do parts of it — installing the battery, putting fresh fuel in, running it on muffs in the driveway. The risk is missing the things you can't see from the cockpit: a weeping seal, a corroded electrical connector, a worn impeller, hub damage on the prop. Our commissioning includes a full inspection, so we catch those before launch.

**What if you find something wrong during commissioning?**
We call you with a real quote before any extra work happens. The standard commissioning package is a flat price for the work listed above. Anything else — a bad fuel line, a dead battery, prop repair — gets quoted per item, and you approve before we touch it.

**Do you commission boats you didn't winterize?**
Yes. We commission most makes and engine brands, whether you winterized with us, with another shop, or DIY. Mercury and Princecraft are our authorised lines, but call us with the make and model and we'll quote it. Boats that weren't winterized properly may need extra fuel system or carburetor work — we'll flag that at intake.
