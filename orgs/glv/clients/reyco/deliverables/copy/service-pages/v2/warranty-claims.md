## 3. Multi-Section Body

### Section A — How Warranty Claims Work (Process)

You bring it. We handle it. Here's the flow:

1. **Bring it in.** Drop the equipment at our parts and service counter on White Oak Drive. Bring your proof of purchase (receipt or warranty card) and the make, model and serial number. If you bought it from us, we already have the file.
2. **Diagnostic.** Our techs inspect the equipment and confirm whether the failure is covered under the manufacturer's warranty terms. We'll tell you upfront if it's a warranty job, an out-of-warranty repair, or a mix of both.
3. **We file the claim.** As an authorised dealer, we submit the claim directly to the manufacturer (Mercury, Cub Cadet, Toro, Echo, Hisun, Princecraft, Minn Kota, Bercomac, Humminbird). No shipping equipment back to head office, no waiting on pre-approval calls from you.
4. **Repair completed.** Once the manufacturer authorises the claim, we do the work using OEM parts pulled from our [parts room](/service/order-parts/). You pick up the equipment when it's ready. If anything beyond the warranty scope comes up, we quote it separately before we touch it.

The short version: you bring it, we handle it.

### Section B — How Long Warranty Claims Take (Duration)

Diagnostic and claim filing usually happen within **a few days of drop-off**. Manufacturer authorisation timing then depends on the brand and the part:

- **Most claims:** approved within **one to two weeks** of submission.
- **Complex claims** (powerheads, major component failures, multi-part assemblies): can run **two to four weeks**, sometimes longer if the manufacturer requests the failed part back for inspection.
- **Repair time after authorisation:** same as our standard service turnaround — same-day to 3-5 business days for most jobs, 1-3 weeks for major engine work.

We'll quote a realistic timeline at intake and update you when the manufacturer responds. If your equipment is needed back fast for the season, mention that on drop-off — we flag time-sensitive jobs at the parts counter.

### Section D — Why Authorised Service Matters (Trust + Per-Brand Notes)

This is the most important thing on the page, so it gets its own section: **most manufacturer warranties require service to be done by an authorised dealer.** Skip the annual service, or have it done at a non-authorised shop, and your claim can be denied.

Per-brand notes on the warranties we see most often (always refer to your owner's manual — the manufacturer's published warranty is the legal document):

- **Mercury Outboard.** Standard pleasure-use warranty on new outboards is three years (extended programmes apply for some models and registration windows). Annual service done by an authorised Mercury dealer is required to keep the warranty in force. Mercury looks at dealer service history when reviewing major-component claims like powerheads or lower units.
- **Cub Cadet.** Residential warranties typically run two to three years on the unit, with longer coverage on engines and certain components. Service per the owner's manual schedule is required.
- **Toro.** Residential lawn equipment carries a multi-year warranty (varies by model). Snowblowers and zero-turns have separate engine/component terms. Authorised dealer service preserves coverage.
- **Echo.** Five-year consumer warranty on most handheld equipment (chainsaws, trimmers, blowers). Commercial use is shorter.
- **Hisun.** ATV and side-by-side warranties vary by model — typically one to two years on the unit, with separate terms on the engine and frame.
- **Princecraft.** Hull warranties run multiple years (lifetime on certain components, varies by model).
- **Minn Kota.** Multi-year warranty on most trolling motors. Battery and component sub-warranties have separate terms.

For exact warranty terms on your specific unit, bring your owner's manual or warranty card in. We'll read it with you and tell you straight what's covered and what isn't.

## 5. FAQ — Common Questions

**Is regular service required to keep my warranty?**
Yes. Most manufacturer warranties (Mercury, Cub Cadet, Toro, Echo and others) require annual service per the owner's manual schedule, done by an authorised dealer. Skipping the service or going to a non-authorised shop can give the manufacturer grounds to deny a future claim. We're authorised for every brand we sell, so service done here keeps the warranty in force. (See our [tune-ups page](/service/tune-ups/) for the seasonal schedule.)

**What voids my warranty?**
Common warranty-voiding actions across most brands: aftermarket parts on a covered component, modifications to the engine or fuel system, missed service intervals, service done by a non-authorised shop, neglect or damage from running with the wrong fluids. Owner's manual lists the specifics — bring it in and we'll walk through it.

**Do I need my original receipt or warranty card?**
Yes — proof of purchase (with the date) is what starts the warranty clock. Most manufacturers also want the warranty card registered (some auto-register at point of sale, some don't). If you bought from us, we have the receipt. If not, dig out the original or contact the original dealer for a copy.

**What if I bought the equipment somewhere else?**
We'll still service it under warranty. Manufacturer warranties travel with the equipment, not the dealer — Mercury, Cub Cadet, Toro and others honour authorised-dealer service regardless of where the unit was originally sold. Bring your proof of purchase and we'll file the claim.

**Who pays for shipping if a part has to go back to the manufacturer?**
For warranty claims, the manufacturer typically covers part replacement and authorised labour. Shipping logistics for the failed part (if it has to be returned for inspection) are handled between us and the manufacturer — you don't pay shipping on a covered claim. We'll flag any out-of-warranty cost separately before we start.

**How long does a warranty claim take?**
Diagnostic and claim filing usually happen within a few days of drop-off. Manufacturer authorisation timing varies — most claims are approved within one to two weeks; complex claims (powerheads, major component failures) can take longer. We'll quote a realistic timeline at intake and update you when the manufacturer responds.
