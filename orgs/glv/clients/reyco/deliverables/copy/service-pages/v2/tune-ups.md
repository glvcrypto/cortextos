## 3. Multi-Section Body

### Section A — Why Tune-Ups Matter (Process + Value)

A tune-up isn't a bench check — it's preventive work that pays off three ways:

- **Extends the life of the equipment.** Clean fuel, fresh oil, healthy ignition — the basics that keep the engine running for another 200 hours instead of 50.
- **Preserves your warranty.** Most manufacturer warranties (Cub Cadet, Toro, Mercury) require documented annual service done by an authorised dealer. Skip it and your warranty claim gets harder.
- **Keeps you out of the peak-season queue.** If your snowblower fails after the first dump in December, every shop in town is booked two to three weeks out. Annual service in September means you're not in that queue.

The math is simple. A tune-up runs 1 to 2 hours of bench time plus parts. A breakdown during the wrong week of the season costs you the whole season.

## 5. FAQ — Common Questions

**How much does a lawn mower tune-up cost?**
Typically 1 to 2 hours of bench time plus parts (oil, filter, plug, sharpened blade). Real number quoted at intake. Carburetor work or anything beyond the basic tune-up gets a separate written quote before we touch it.

**What does a snowblower tune-up include?**
Engine oil change, spark plug, carburetor and fuel system check, auger and drive belt inspection, friction wheel check, shear pin check, scraper bar adjustment, and a full start test. The fuel system check is the most important — that's where most snowblower no-starts come from.

**How often should I tune up my outboard?**
The Mercury service interval calls for a full service once per season or every 100 hours, whichever comes first. For a typical Northern Ontario boater that's once a year, usually at spring commissioning. If your boat sits a lot or runs hard, lean toward the hour count over the calendar.

**Will an authorised tune-up affect my warranty?**
Yes — in your favour. We're authorised for Cub Cadet, Toro, Mercury and Princecraft, so service done here is warranty-compliant. Skipping annual service or going to a non-authorised shop is what causes warranty claim trouble.

**Can I do a tune-up myself?**
You can do parts of it — change the oil, swap the plug, run fresh fuel through. The risk of DIY is missing the things you can't see: a fuel filter going to varnish, a worn impeller, a glazed friction wheel, a blade out of balance. Our tune-ups include a full inspection so we catch those before they leave you stranded.

**When's the best time to book a tune-up?**
Before the season starts. Lawn mowers: March to mid-April. Snowblowers: September to mid-October. Outboards: March to mid-April. By peak season the bench is two to three weeks out.
