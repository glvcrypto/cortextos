### Section A — How Service Works (Process)

Drop the unit off, or call ahead and we'll book a slot on Damian's bench. Here's the usual flow:

1. **Intake.** We tag the snowblower, write down what's wrong (or what you've noticed), and get your contact info.
2. **Diagnostic.** Damian inspects the engine, fuel system, drive belt, auger and the parts most likely to be at fault for what you described.

5. **Start test before pickup.** We start the unit and run it briefly before you take it home. If it's not starting clean, we'd rather catch that on our bench than at 6 AM after a 30 cm overnight dump.

### Section C — What We Fix (Equipment & Common Issues)

The snowblower bench handles:

- **Annual scheduled service** (recommended pre-season, September to October)
- **[Snowblower tune-ups](/service/tune-ups/)** — fuel system, plugs, belts, lube
- **Repair** — won't start, loses power, auger issues, drive belt, shear pins, control cables
- **OEM Cub Cadet and Toro parts** — most common items in stock, others ordered in
- **Emergency mid-season starts** when we have bench capacity (priority for customers who already booked annual service with us)
- **Storage prep** at end of season (fuel stabiliser, oil change, blade and bearing check)
- **Single, two and three-stage** machines all serviced

A few things we see week to week:

- **Won't start in the cold.** Almost always fuel-related — old gas from last spring, gummed carburetor, or a weak battery on electric-start models.
- **Starts but bogs out under snow load.** Carb adjustment, fuel filter, or the throttle linkage.
- **Auger spins slowly or not at all.** Sheared shear pins, worn belt, or auger gearbox issue.
- **Drive doesn't engage.** Drive belt slipping, friction wheel worn, control cable out of adjustment.
- **Smoke from the muffler.** Oil level too high, head gasket, or rings on a higher-hour unit.
- **Pull cord won't recoil.** Recoil spring or pulley assembly.

If you're not sure what's going on, describe it when you call. Damian can usually tell from the symptoms whether it's an hour on the bench or a full diagnostic.

## 5. FAQ — Common Questions

**How long does snowblower service take?**
Most repairs take 3 to 5 business days. Simple jobs like a tune-up or shear pin swap can be same day if the bench isn't backed up. In peak season (December through February) the bench books out one to three weeks — book early.

**How much does snowblower service cost?**
A typical annual tune-up runs 1 to 2 hours of bench time plus parts. Carburetor work is 1 to 3 hours plus parts. Major engine repairs get a written quote after the diagnostic.

**Can I service my snowblower myself?**
You can do basic maintenance — change the oil, swap the plug, check the belt, run fuel stabiliser at end of season. The risks of DIY are missing the things you can't see: a worn auger gearbox bearing, a glazed friction wheel, a fuel pickup screen full of varnish. Our annual service includes a full inspection.

**My snowblower won't start in the cold — what's the most common cause?**
Almost always fuel. Modern ethanol-blend gas goes off in three to six months, and snowblowers tend to sit with a half-tank of gas all summer. Drain the tank, run fresh fuel through. If it still won't fire, a quick carburetor clean often gets you back to clearing the driveway.

**Do you service brands beyond Cub Cadet and Toro?**
Yes. We're authorised dealers for Cub Cadet and Toro, but our techs work on most major snowblower brands. Call ahead with the make and model and we'll tell you straight.

**Do you do mid-winter emergency repairs?**
We try to fit time-sensitive jobs in around scheduled service, especially for customers who already booked annual service with us in the fall. Call as soon as you notice the issue.
