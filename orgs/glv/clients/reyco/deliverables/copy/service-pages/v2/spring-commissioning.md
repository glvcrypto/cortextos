## 2. Intro Paragraph

Spring commissioning is the work that gets a winterized boat back to water-ready. It's the mirror image of fall [winterization](/service/winterization/): everything that was drained, fogged, removed or stabilized over winter has to be checked, refilled, replaced or reinstalled before the boat goes back in. Our shop on White Oak Drive has been the marine shop here for more than 60 years, and the parts room sits a few steps from the marine bench — gear oil, plugs, batteries, water pump impellers and seals are all in stock.

Lee runs the bench. He's commissioned enough Northern Ontario boats to know which ones hide trouble where the manual doesn't mention it.

## 3. Multi-Section Body

### Section A — How Spring Commissioning Works (Process)

Drop the boat off, or if it's already in winter storage at our facility, mention that when you call — we can pull it onto the bench directly.

1. **Intake.** We tag the boat, confirm the make/model/engine, and pull last fall's winterization notes if you booked it with us.
2. **Battery and electrical.** Battery installed (or replaced if it didn't survive winter), terminals cleaned and tested. Bilge pump, navigation lights, gauges, charging system all checked.
3. **Fuel system check.** Fresh fuel, line and primer bulb inspected, water separator cleared, carb/injector check if the boat sat with old fuel.
4. **Engine and lower unit.** Lower unit gear oil drained and refilled (a milky drain tells us a seal needs attention before launch, not after). Engine fluids topped or changed per Mercury service interval. Cooling system flush + water pump test.
5. **Prop, steering, hull check.** Prop inspected for dings/dents/hub condition. Steering and controls checked. Hull and trailer go through a damage + bearings + lights + brakes inspection.
6. **Final water-ready test.** Run on muffs (or in-water where required) so we know it runs before you do. If it's not running right, we'd rather catch that on our bench than 20 km out on Lake Superior.

If we find something that wasn't on the original quote — bad fuel line, dead battery, worn impeller — we call you with a real number before any extra work happens. Same workflow as our [marine service](/service/marine/) bench.

### Section B — When to Book (Duration + Seasonal Calendar)

Boating season in Sault Ste. Marie usually opens between mid-April and mid-May depending on ice-out. Book your commissioning ahead of that:

- **Late March to mid-April:** still workable, but the bench fills fast. Two weeks ahead minimum.

A typical commissioning is **a single bench session** once the boat is in — quoted as a flat package. Turnaround is usually within the week, faster if the bench is open.

## 5. FAQ — Common Questions

**When should I book spring commissioning in Sault Ste. Marie?**
Late February to mid-March is ideal. By April the bench is filling fast and you risk missing the early-season weekends. If you winterized with us last fall, your spring slot is already pre-noted in our system — call in March to confirm.

**Do I need spring commissioning if I winterized properly?**
Yes. Winterization protects the boat from freeze damage; commissioning gets it back to water-ready. They're different jobs. Even a perfectly winterized boat needs the battery reinstalled, fluids checked, fuel refreshed, and a full water-ready test before you launch. Skipping commissioning is how a perfect winterization still ends with a tow truck on opening weekend.

**Can I commission my boat myself?**
You can do parts of it — installing the battery, putting fresh fuel in, running it on muffs in the driveway. The risk is missing the things you can't see from the cockpit: a weeping seal, a corroded electrical connector, a worn impeller, hub damage on the prop. Our commissioning includes a full inspection, so we catch those before launch.

**What if you find something wrong during commissioning?**
We call you with a real quote before any extra work happens. The standard commissioning package is a flat price for the work listed above. Anything else — a bad fuel line, a dead battery, prop repair — gets quoted per item, and you approve before we touch it.

**Do you commission boats you didn't winterize?**
Yes. We commission most makes and engine brands, whether you winterized with us, with another shop, or DIY. Mercury and Princecraft are our authorised lines, but call us with the make and model and we'll quote it. Boats that weren't winterized properly may need extra fuel system or carburetor work — we'll flag that at intake.
