## 2. Intro Paragraph

A Sault Ste. Marie winter regularly drops below −20 °C. Water that sits in an engine block, a lower unit or a cooling passage expands when it freezes, and metal cracks. Fuel that sits in a tank for five months goes off and gums up the carburetor or injectors. Our shop on White Oak Drive has been the marine shop here for more than 60 years, and the parts room sits a few steps from the marine bench — the fogging oil, fuel stabiliser, gear lube and seals we need are all in stock.

Lee runs the bench. He's winterized enough Northern Ontario boats to know which models hide water in places the manual doesn't mention.

## 3. Multi-Section Body

### Section A — How Winterization Works (Process)

Drop the boat off (trailer or hull), or call ahead and we'll book a winterization slot on Lee's bench. Here's the usual flow:

1. **Intake.** We tag the boat, note the make/model/engine, and confirm whether you want shrink-wrap and battery storage included.
2. **Fuel system prep.** Fuel stabiliser added and run through the engine so what's left in the tank doesn't gum up over winter.
3. **Engine fogging.** Mercury procedure for 2-stroke and 4-stroke outboards — coats the internal engine surfaces so they don't corrode while the boat sits.
4. **Lower unit drain and refill.** Fresh gear oil, plus a check for water intrusion. A milky drain tells us a seal is bad before next season.
5. **Cooling system flush + battery storage.** Salt, sand or river debris cleared from the cooling passages; battery removed and trickle-charged so it's ready in spring.
6. **Inspection report.** We flag anything we noticed that's worth addressing before [spring commissioning](/service/spring-commissioning/) — so you're not finding out the day before launch.

## 5. FAQ — Common Questions

**When should I winterize my boat in Sault Ste. Marie?**
Mid-August to mid-September is ideal. By late September the bench is filling fast and you're racing the first hard frost. If you wait until October, book at least two weeks ahead. After the first freeze, damage may already be done before we touch the boat.

**Can I winterize my boat myself?**
Some boaters do, and if you have the manual, the parts and the time, it can be done in a driveway. The risk is missing a step — water trapped in a single cooling passage will crack a powerhead, and a fuel line that wasn't fogged properly will gum up by spring. If you DIY and miss something, the bill in spring is bigger than the winterization service. Our package follows the Mercury procedure exactly and includes an inspection report.

**What's the difference between winterization and just storing the boat?**
Storage is what you do with the boat after winterization is done. Winterization is the engine, fuel system and cooling work that keeps the boat from freeze damage. Both matter — but winterization is the one that protects the expensive parts.

**Do you winterize boats you didn't sell?**
Yes. We winterize most makes and engine brands. Mercury and Princecraft are our authorised lines, but if you have a different outboard, sterndrive or hull, call us with the make and model and we'll quote it.

**Can I store my boat at Reyco over winter?**
Call us about storage availability. Indoor storage is limited and books up fast — outdoor with shrink-wrap is the usual option, and we can quote both with your winterization package.
