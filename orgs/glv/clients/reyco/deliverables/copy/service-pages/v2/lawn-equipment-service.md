### Section A — How Service Works (Process)

Drop the mower off, or call ahead and we'll book a slot on Damian's bench. Here's the usual flow:

1. **Intake.** We tag the mower, write down what's wrong (or what you've noticed), and get your contact info.
2. **Diagnostic.** Damian inspects the engine, deck, blades, drive belt, transmission and the parts most likely to be at fault for what you described.
3. **Estimate first, then approval.** We call you with a clear price before any work starts. No surprise bills.
4. **Repair.** Most jobs use OEM Cub Cadet or Toro parts pulled straight from our parts room. Same-day pickup if it's small, 3 to 5 business days if it's bigger or we're waiting on a part.
5. **Test before pickup.** We start the mower and check the cut before you take it home. If something's off, we'd rather catch that on our bench than the first time you drop the deck on a Saturday morning.

### Section C — What We Fix (Equipment & Common Issues)

If it has a small engine and cuts grass, our shop services it:

- **Push mowers** (gas, electric-start, battery)
- **Self-propelled walk-behinds**
- **Riding lawn tractors** (Cub Cadet and most major brands)
- **Zero-turn mowers** (Cub Cadet, Toro, others)
- **Commercial walk-behinds** for landscapers
- **Trimmers and string mowers** (covered on our [small engine repair](/service/small-engine/) page)

A few signs your lawn equipment is overdue:

- **Won't start** after sitting all winter (almost always old fuel — see the FAQ below)
- **Hard pull or weak start** on a recoil-start mower
- **Engine bogs out** under a heavy cut or wet grass
- **Cuts unevenly or leaves strips** — usually blade balance, deck level, or belt tension
- **Smoke from the muffler** (especially blue or white)
- **Vibration that wasn't there last season** — blade balance or bearing issue
- **Drive doesn't engage** on a self-propelled or riding mower
- **Belt squealing** or transmission slipping on a riding/zero-turn

If you're not sure what's going on, describe it when you call. Damian can usually tell from the symptoms whether it's an hour on the bench or a full diagnostic.

## 5. FAQ — Common Questions

**How long does lawn mower service take?**
Most repairs take 3 to 5 business days. Simple jobs like a blade sharpen or oil change can be same day if the bench isn't backed up. In peak spring (April through May) the bench books out one to two weeks.

**How much does a lawn mower tune-up cost?**
A typical annual tune-up runs 1 to 2 hours of bench time plus parts (oil, filter, plug, blade sharpen). Carburetor work is 1 to 3 hours plus parts. Major engine or transmission repairs get a written quote after the diagnostic.

**Will service at Reyco affect my Cub Cadet or Toro warranty?**
No — the opposite. We're an authorised Cub Cadet and Toro dealer, so service done here is warranty-compliant. Service done at a non-authorised shop can void your warranty.

**My mower won't start after sitting all winter — what's the most common cause?**
Old fuel. Modern ethanol-blend gas goes off in three to six months and gums up the carburetor. Drain the tank, run fresh fuel through. If it still won't fire, a quick carburetor clean often gets you running.

**Do you service riding mowers and zero-turns?**
Yes. Push, self-propelled, riding, zero-turn — all on Damian's bench. For the larger machines, call ahead so we can plan bench space and trailer drop.

**Do you service brands beyond Cub Cadet and Toro?**
Yes. We're authorised dealers for Cub Cadet and Toro, but our techs work on most major lawn equipment brands. Call ahead with the make and model and we'll tell you straight.
