## Methodology Note — Critical for Interpretation

**What Semrush can measure:** National/regional Canadian queries with sufficient monthly search volume to appear in their index.

**What Semrush cannot measure:** Hyper-local queries for small cities. Sault Ste. Marie has ~75,000 population — below Semrush's city-level sampling threshold. All "X sault ste marie" queries that returned ERROR 50 (Not Found) are NOT zero-traffic queries. They are queries too low-volume for Semrush's national panel to register. These queries still exist and are searched.

**What this means for strategy:**
- KD=0 on all SSM-specific terms = **zero local competition**. Any page Reyco builds is the only relevant local result.
- Volume for SSM local terms: use the national "near me" variant as a proxy. SSM captures a proportional share of that national volume. For "snowblower repair near me" (1,600/mo CA), SSM at ~0.25% of Canadian population would represent ~4/mo — but local intent means SSM searches concentrate around local providers, and Reyco is the only one with a page.
- **The strategic conclusion is unchanged**: build local pages, own local results.

**Column key:**
- `Vol (CA)`: Monthly search volume, Canada database, Semrush
- `KD`: Keyword Difficulty 0–100 (Semrush). <20 = easy, 20–40 = moderate, 40+ = hard
- `CPC`: Cost Per Click (CAD) — signals commercial intent and advertiser competition
- `Source`: VERIFIED = Semrush live data | LOCAL-EST = city-level, Semrush blind spot, estimated
