Capacity means two things on a snow blower: clearing width (how much snow the machine cuts per pass) and handling capability (how much snow volume and density the machine can move without bogging). For Sault Ste. Marie buyers, both matter. SSM's average of 290 centimetres of annual snowfall produces conditions that stress undersized equipment, and the specific type of snow that falls in November and March (wet, heavy, and dense) is particularly demanding on machines that were not selected for capacity.

---

## Clearing Width: Matching the Machine to the Driveway

Clearing width is the most straightforward capacity metric. A two-stage machine with a 24-inch clearing width cuts a 24-inch swath with each pass. A 30-inch machine covers more ground per pass.

**24-inch clearing width:** The entry point for two-stage machines. Adequate for a single-car driveway up to approximately 50 feet in length. On a typical SSM single-car driveway, a 24-inch clearing width means two or three passes to clear the full width. In a moderate snowfall, this is manageable. After a heavy overnight storm that deposits 30-40 centimetres, the clearing time increases proportionally.

**28-inch clearing width:** The step-up that most SSM buyers should consider. Clears a typical single-car driveway in two passes rather than three. For a two-car driveway or any driveway over 12 feet wide, the 28-inch machine is meaningfully faster than the 24-inch without being significantly harder to manoeuvre.

**30-inch clearing width:** The standard for mid-range residential two-stage machines. Handles a double driveway in two to three passes. The Cub Cadet 3X series and comparable mid-range Toro units in this width range are the most common machines in the SSM market for buyers with driveways at the typical residential scale.

**Larger than 30 inches:** For commercial applications, large double driveways, or properties with extensive paved areas to clear. At 34 inches and larger, the machine is heavier and less manoeuvrable, but the coverage per pass significantly reduces total clearing time on large surfaces.

For most SSM residential buyers with a standard double driveway, a 28 to 30-inch two-stage machine is the practical sweet spot.

## Engine Capacity and Snow Type

Clearing width determines how much snow the machine cuts per pass. Engine displacement and power determine what the machine can do with that snow. This distinction is particularly important in SSM where snow type varies significantly through the season.

**SSM snow type, November and March:** The snowfalls at the beginning and end of the Northern Ontario season frequently arrive at temperatures just at or below zero, producing wet, dense, heavy snow. This is the snow that collapses snow fort roofs and breaks tree limbs. It is also the snow that stalls underpowered two-stage machines. A wet snow event of 20 centimetres puts more load on an auger and impeller than 30 centimetres of dry January powder.

**Engine displacement.** Two-stage machines in the residential market run engines from approximately 200cc to 420cc. The smaller engines handle light-to-moderate snowfall competently. For SSM's wet snow conditions, an engine in the 300cc and above range provides the torque needed to keep the auger feeding without slowing down or stalling under load.

**The three-stage advantage for heavy wet snow.** The Cub Cadet 3X series adds an accelerator between the augers and the impeller that breaks up incoming snow before throwing it. This design handles wet, heavy, partially compacted snow, including the plow windrow at the end of the driveway, more effectively than a two-stage machine running the same engine size. For SSM buyers who have experienced two-stage machines bogging in wet March snow, the 3X design addresses that specific limitation.

## The End-of-Driveway Plow Windrow

Every SSM property with municipal street access experiences the plow windrow: the ridge of compacted snow that the city plow deposits at the end of the driveway each time it passes. Plow windrows are the most demanding clearing task for a residential snow blower because the snow in a windrow is compacted, often partially frozen, and piled higher than the natural snowfall on the driveway.

A 24-inch two-stage machine with a smaller engine will slow down or require multiple passes at the windrow. A 30-inch three-stage machine with a larger engine drives through a typical plow windrow without the same hesitation.

If you clear your driveway after the plow has come through rather than before (which is the more common pattern), the plow windrow is the real test of your machine's capacity. Choose accordingly.

## Single-Stage vs Two-Stage Capacity Comparison

Single-stage machines are appropriate for light-snowfall markets and paved driveways without significant pitch variation. They are not adequate for SSM's snowfall volumes, plow windrows, or the wet snow events that characterise the beginning and end of the season. A single-stage machine in SSM is the correct tool for a small deck or walkway. It is not adequate for a full driveway.

The capacity question for SSM buyers is not whether to buy a two-stage. It is which two-stage, or whether the three-stage design is worth the additional investment given how often heavy wet snow conditions occur.
