## What Boat Warranties Actually Cover

Marine warranties split into two distinct categories, and confusing them is the most common source of frustration.

**Engine warranty** covers defects in materials and workmanship on the engine itself. On a new Mercury outboard, this is a manufacturer warranty issued by Mercury Marine and serviced through authorised Mercury dealers. The warranty covers manufacturing defects, not wear, not damage from misuse, and not problems caused by incorrect installation or non-standard modifications.

**Hull warranty** covers structural defects in the boat hull and deck. This is issued by the boat manufacturer, Princecraft, for example, offers a limited structural warranty on their aluminium and fibreglass hulls. Hull warranty and engine warranty are separate and handled separately.

**What neither covers:** normal wear items (impellers, belts, anodes, propellers), damage from collision or grounding, deterioration from improper storage, and problems caused by maintenance gaps. If the annual service was skipped, the gear lube was never changed, or the impeller ran dry, warranty claims on the downstream damage will be denied.

## The Authorised Dealer Requirement

For Mercury engine warranty service in Ontario, the work must be performed at an authorised Mercury dealer. This is not a technicality, it is a condition of the warranty.

An authorised dealer has factory-trained technicians, access to the Mercury diagnostic software, and a direct service relationship with Mercury Marine for warranty claim reimbursement. When you bring warranty work to an authorised dealer, the dealer files the claim with Mercury on your behalf, receives the parts and labour reimbursement from Mercury, and you pay nothing for covered repairs.

Bringing warranty work to an independent shop that is not Mercury authorised voids the warranty for that repair. The independent shop may do good work, but Mercury has no warranty relationship with them and will not reimburse the claim.

## What Triggers a Valid Warranty Claim

A valid warranty claim requires three things: the defect must be covered under the warranty terms, the boat must be within the warranty period, and the service history must demonstrate proper maintenance.

**Coverage:** The defect must be a manufacturing defect, not wear or damage. A water pump failure caused by a cracked impeller housing is a warranty item. An impeller failure because it was never replaced on schedule is a maintenance item.

**Warranty period:** Mercury FourStroke outboards carry a limited warranty from the date of purchase. The specific term depends on the model and year, your dealer documentation will have the exact period. Claims must be initiated while the engine is within the warranty period.

**Service history:** Mercury requires warranty service to be performed at authorised dealers on the standard service intervals. The break-in service (first 20 hours), the annual oil change, and impeller replacement must all be on file. Gaps in the service record give Mercury grounds to deny claims that might otherwise be valid. The service record does not need to be from the selling dealer, any authorised Mercury dealer's service records count.

## How the Claim Process Works

The process at an authorised dealer is straightforward when the above conditions are met.

1. You bring the boat in and describe the problem.
2. The technician diagnoses the failure and determines whether it is warranty-eligible.
3. If the failure is covered, the dealer contacts Mercury to pre-approve the claim before any parts are ordered.
4. Mercury approves or denies based on the described failure and service history.
5. If approved, the repair is completed using Mercury-supplied warranty parts. Your cost is zero for covered work.
6. The dealer files the warranty claim and receives reimbursement from Mercury directly.

The only time this process gets complicated is when the service history is incomplete, the modification history raises questions, or the failure is borderline between a defect and wear. In those cases, Mercury may request additional documentation or conduct a technical review before approving.

## What Voids Warranty Coverage

Several situations will void Mercury warranty coverage, either partially or entirely.

**Non-standard modifications:** Aftermarket parts installed in the fuel system, ignition system, or electrical system that are not Mercury-approved can void coverage on the affected systems.

**Improper installation:** An engine that was mounted incorrectly, wrong transom height, wrong bracket, incorrect rigging, creates a grounds-for-denial situation when problems arise that can be attributed to the installation.

**Overloading:** Operating the boat beyond the hull's rated capacity, or exceeding the maximum HP rating for the transom, voids engine and hull warranties for related damage.

**Neglected maintenance:** As noted above, missing scheduled service intervals is the most common reason claims are denied on otherwise covered defects.

**Unauthorised repairs:** Warranty work performed at non-authorised shops cannot be submitted for reimbursement and, depending on the repair, may affect the validity of subsequent claims.
