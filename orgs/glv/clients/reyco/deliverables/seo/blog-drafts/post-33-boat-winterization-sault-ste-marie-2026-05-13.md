In Sault Ste. Marie, the boating season ends in October. The temperatures that follow, sustained below-zero cold from November through March, will damage an unprotected outboard and deteriorate untreated fuel in ways that are fully preventable with a proper winterization service. The cost of skipping winterization is not theoretical. It is a cracked block or a seized lower unit discovered at the start of the following season.

---

## Why Sault Ste. Marie Winterization Is Not Optional

Northern Ontario winters are long and cold. Sault Ste. Marie averages first frost in early October and sustained below-zero temperatures from November onward. Residual water in an outboard cooling system that was not flushed and protected will freeze, expand, and crack the cooling passages in the engine block or the lower unit housing. This is not wear. It is structural damage. A cracked block on a newer Mercury outboard is a major repair.

Beyond freeze damage, a Northern Ontario boat that goes into storage without fuel management will have a fuel system that is varnished and blocked seven months later. A battery left discharged in an unheated space will be dead or significantly degraded by spring. Rubber and canvas components that were not cleaned and protected before storage will show accelerated deterioration.

Winterization is the service that prevents the entire category of these problems.

## What Boat Winterization Covers

### Engine Winterization

**Cooling system flush and anti-freeze.** The raw-water cooling circuit in an outboard circulates lake water through the engine to cool it during operation. All of that water must be removed from the system before storage. For outboards, the standard procedure is to flush the cooling system with fresh water using muffs connected to the lower unit, then run engine anti-freeze through the system to displace residual water in passages that the flush alone does not clear. A flushed-but-not-anti-frozen outboard stored in a Sault Ste. Marie garage at -15 degrees Celsius will still have water in the internal passages that can freeze.

**Fogging.** Cylinder walls and piston rings are coated with fogging oil, a corrosion-inhibiting spray applied through the air intake or spark plug holes. This protects the internal engine surfaces during the months the engine is not running.

**Oil change.** Four-stroke outboards receive a fresh oil change at winterization. Used oil contains combustion byproducts and trace acids that accelerate corrosion of internal engine surfaces when they sit in contact for a seven-month interval.

**Gear lube change and inspection.** The lower unit gear lube is drained and inspected. Milky or grey lube indicates a water seal leak that should be repaired before storage, not after. Water-contaminated gear lube freezes and expands, causing damage to bearings and gears in the lower unit.

**Fuel system.** The carburettor or fuel injection system is treated per the manufacturer's specification. For carburetted engines, this typically means either running the fuel system dry or adding stabiliser and circulating it through. For fuel-injected engines, the fuel system management steps vary by model. The fuel tank is treated with stabiliser to prevent ethanol phase separation over the storage interval.

### Electrical Systems

**Battery.** The battery is removed from the boat or placed on a maintenance charger. A battery left uncharged in cold storage through a Northern Ontario winter will lose charge capacity. Most marine battery manufacturers recommend connecting to a trickle charger for extended storage.

**Connections.** Battery terminals and electrical connections are cleaned and protected against corrosion before storage. Corrosion at connections during storage is one of the most common causes of electrical faults found at spring commissioning.

### Hull and Exterior

**Freshwater flush.** The hull, trailer bunks, and any fishing gear that contacted open water are rinsed with fresh water before storage. This removes sediment, organic material, and residual lake water from surfaces that would otherwise sit damp for months.

**Canvas.** Boat canvas is cleaned, dried, and stored or fitted properly to prevent water pooling. Canvas that traps moisture during storage develops mould. Canvas left fitted on the boat in a way that allows ice to form in pooled areas will have stress damage to seams and snaps by spring.

**Trailer.** Wheel bearings are inspected and packed if needed. Tyre pressure is checked and set to the storage pressure. Lights are tested. A trailer that has had a season of launch ramp use has had its bearings in and out of the water repeatedly; inspection at winterization is the time to catch deteriorating bearings before they become a tow problem mid-season.

## When to Book Winterization in Sault Ste. Marie

The window for winterization in SSM runs from September through mid-October. Boats stored before the first frost are protected before any freeze risk. Boats that come in after freeze events have already been exposed to the risk.

September is the better booking month. The service shop is not yet at peak demand, turnaround is faster, and there is no urgency driven by an approaching freeze date. A boat winterized in September and stored through April is a boat with a full seven months of protection applied correctly.

October winterization is still on time. November is cutting it close depending on the year.
