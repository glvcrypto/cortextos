## What Affects the Cost of Small Engine Repair

**Type of equipment**

A push mower with a clogged carburettor is a different job than a riding mower with a failed transmission. Equipment complexity is the first thing a technician considers. Handheld power tools (chainsaws, string trimmers, leaf blowers) tend to be quicker to diagnose and repair than larger-displacement engines on riding mowers or zero-turn units.

**What is actually wrong**

Diagnosis drives the quote. A simple tune-up (spark plug, air filter, fresh fuel) takes an hour or less. A carburettor rebuild or fuel system overhaul takes longer and involves more parts. Ignition problems, compression failures, and internal engine damage cost more because of both labour time and parts.

**Parts availability**

For authorised brands, parts come in faster. Reyco Marine stocks parts on-site for Echo, Cub Cadet, and Toro, which cuts turnaround. For equipment from other manufacturers, lead times can stretch the job out by a few days depending on the supplier.

**Age and condition**

Older equipment sometimes surfaces secondary issues once a technician opens it up. A carburettor job can reveal a worn gasket or a cracked fuel line that was not visible from the outside. Technicians at Reyco provide a written quote before any work begins, so you know what you are authorising before a wrench is picked up.

## Common Jobs and What Drives Their Cost

**Tune-ups**

A basic tune-up covers spark plug inspection or replacement, air filter check, fuel line inspection, and a running test. It is the lowest-cost service and the most effective way to prevent more expensive repairs down the road. For most two-stroke and four-stroke equipment, it is a straightforward job.

**Carburettor cleaning and rebuild**

Old fuel breaks down and leaves varnish deposits in carburettor passages. Equipment that sat over the winter without stabilised fuel is a common candidate. A clean takes less time than a full rebuild. If the float, needle, or jets are worn or corroded, the cost goes up. A properly rebuilt carburettor extends equipment life significantly.

**Belt and drive system work**

Self-propelled mowers, riding mowers, and snowblowers rely on belts and drive cables. Worn belts are a predictable wear item. Replacing them is a moderate job. If a belt failure caused secondary damage to a pulley or drive gear, the repair gets more involved.

**Auger and impeller repair (snowblowers)**

Shear pins are the first line of defence when a snowblower hits a solid object. They are designed to break so the auger shaft does not. Replacing shear pins is a low-cost fix. If the auger took damage before the pin gave way, the repair cost is higher. Reyco stocks common shear pins for Cub Cadet and Toro at the counter. No appointment needed for those.

**Generator and pressure washer service**

Generators and pressure washers that sit unused for long stretches often come in with stale fuel and varnished carburettors. Service costs depend on the engine size and what the fuel damage did to internal components.
