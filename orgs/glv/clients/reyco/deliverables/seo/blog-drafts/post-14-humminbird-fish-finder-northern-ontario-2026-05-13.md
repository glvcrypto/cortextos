## What Humminbird Does Now

Humminbird has been building fish finders since the 1970s, but the current product generation is fundamentally different from what most experienced anglers have on their boats.

The technology that changed everything for Northern Ontario fishing is side imaging and down imaging sonar. Traditional 2D sonar shows you what is directly below the transducer as a cone-shaped return. Side imaging extends that view laterally, producing a wide-area picture of bottom structure and fish on both sides of the boat as you move. On a Shield lake where structure is irregular, submerged rocks are unpredictable, and fish relate to specific transition points, side imaging is the difference between guessing where the structure is and seeing it.

Current Humminbird units include MEGA Imaging, which uses higher-frequency sonar for sharper returns in water less than 100 feet deep. On Northern Ontario Shield lakes, most productive fishing water is in this depth range, which means MEGA Imaging delivers its sharpest results exactly where you are fishing.

GPS integration and waypoint marking are standard on all current Humminbird units above the entry level. The ability to mark a rock pile, a deep transition, or a productive point and return to it reliably across seasons changes how you build knowledge of a lake over time.

## Humminbird for Northern Ontario Conditions

The practical considerations for Northern Ontario fish finder selection:

**Transducer mounting.** Aluminium fishing boats typically mount transducers on the transom with a bracket. The transducer needs to be positioned below the hull with clean water flow and away from turbulence from the lower unit. Correct mounting is as important as the unit itself; a well-mounted entry-level unit outperforms an incorrectly mounted higher-end unit.

**Screen size and brightness.** Northern Ontario fishing often involves full-sun conditions on the water. A screen that is readable in direct sunlight matters. Humminbird's current lineup specifies nit ratings (brightness) that address outdoor readability; this is worth checking against the conditions you typically fish in.

**Networking.** Current Humminbird units support Ethernet networking to share sonar and GPS data between multiple units on the same boat. If your console and bow mount will both have units, networked units allow you to see the same sonar data at both positions without running separate transducers.

## Minn Kota Trolling Motors

Minn Kota is the dominant brand in electric trolling motors in Canada. The product range covers hand-control entry units through fully GPS-integrated bow-mount motors with autopilot functions.

For Northern Ontario fishing, the features that matter most in a trolling motor are:

**Thrust.** The general rule is 2 pounds of thrust per 100 pounds of boat weight (loaded). A 1,600-pound aluminium fishing boat loaded with two anglers and gear needs approximately 50 to 60 pounds of thrust at minimum. Wind and current conditions on Northern Ontario lakes mean many buyers go higher.

**Shaft length.** The trolling motor shaft needs to put the motor at the correct depth with the bow in fishing position. Standard shaft lengths for Northern Ontario aluminium fishing boats in the 16 to 18 foot range are typically 52 to 60 inches. Getting this wrong means either the prop cavitates at the surface in a chop or the unit is too long for the deployment bracket.

**Anchor Lock and GPS functions.** Minn Kota's Spot-Lock GPS anchor function is, for Northern Ontario fishing, one of the most practically valuable features in the current lineup. Shield lake rock structure means you are frequently positioning on specific points, drop-offs, and current seams that are hard to hold manually. Spot-Lock holds your GPS position without an anchor, leaving you on the structure and both hands free to fish.

## Integration Between Humminbird and Minn Kota

Both Humminbird and Minn Kota are owned by Johnson Outdoors, which means they are designed to integrate. Current Minn Kota bow-mount motors with i-Pilot Link can connect to Humminbird units via Bluetooth and MEGA Live cable.

The practical result of that integration on a Northern Ontario fishing boat: you can mark a waypoint on your Humminbird and navigate the trolling motor to it automatically, or follow a depth contour while the trolling motor tracks the GPS path. For walleye and pike fishing along specific structure lines on Shield lakes, this is a significant advantage over manual boat control.
