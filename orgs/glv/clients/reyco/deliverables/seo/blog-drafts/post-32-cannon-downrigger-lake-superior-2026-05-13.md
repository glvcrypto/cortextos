Trolling at depth for Lake Superior chinook salmon, lake trout, and coho requires more than a rod in a holder and a lure in the water. The fish hold at specific temperature breaks and depth ranges that vary with season and conditions. Getting a bait to precisely 40 feet while the boat moves at trolling speed is a task for a downrigger, not for lead weights or planer boards. For Northern Ontario anglers who fish Superior seriously, a downrigger is not optional equipment.

---

## What a Downrigger Does

A downrigger suspends a weight, called a cannon ball, at a precise depth below the boat using a cable on a reel. The fishing line is clipped to the cable or to a release clip near the weight. The rod is placed in a rod holder with a bend in the tip. The bait or lure runs at the depth of the weight without any of the resistance, drag, or limited depth capability that lead-core line or diving weights introduce.

When a fish strikes, the line releases from the clip, and the angler fights the fish on light tackle with nothing pulling against them below the surface. The result is a clean presentation at the target depth and a direct fight once the fish is on.

For Lake Superior chinook and lake trout, where fish suspend in the thermocline at 40 to 100 feet in July and August, a downrigger is the difference between fishing at the right depth and fishing well above or below where the fish actually are.

## Manual vs Electric Downriggers

**Manual downriggers** use a hand crank to deploy and retrieve the weight. On a calm inland lake at moderate depths, manual downriggers work. On Lake Superior, where you may be setting weights to 60 or 80 feet on multiple rods simultaneously and then retrieving them rapidly when a fish is on another rod, the physical demand of manual cranking at depth under time pressure is significant. Manual is a starting point; for regular Superior fishing, electric is the standard.

**Electric downriggers** deploy and retrieve using a powered motor at the touch of a switch. The weight drops to the programmed depth and stops. When you need to clear a rod, retrieve a weight, or reset depth, it takes seconds rather than minutes. On a two-person boat running four rods at depth on Lake Superior, electric downriggers are not a luxury. They are the practical requirement for managing the spread efficiently.

Cannon's electric units also include a line counter that tracks depth precisely. On Superior, where you are looking for a two-degree temperature break at a specific depth, the difference between "about 60 feet" and "exactly 62 feet" can matter.

## Mounting on an Aluminum Fishing Boat

Downriggers mount to the transom or to the gunwale via a base plate and swivel arm. On a Princecraft Yukon or equivalent aluminum fishing boat in the 17 to 19-foot range, most Cannon mounting bases are compatible with the transom and gunwale geometry.

The key considerations for installation:

**Mounting point clearance.** The downrigger arm needs to swing without hitting the motor, the kicker motor mount, or other equipment at the stern. Walk through the full deployment position before you drill.

**Cable clearance.** The cable runs vertically from the downrigger spool to the weight below. Confirm the cable path is clear of the prop wash area and does not contact the hull or the lower unit during depth changes.

**Weight selection.** Cannon ball weights are available in a range of sizes. For Lake Superior, eight to twelve pounds is the standard range for recreational trolling. Heavier weights hold depth better at higher trolling speeds and in current. The downrigger model you choose must be rated for the weight you intend to use.

## Pairing with Humminbird Electronics

For anglers running Humminbird chartplotters and fish finders on the same boat as Cannon downriggers, the Johnson Outdoors integration is worth understanding.

Compatible Humminbird units can display downrigger weight depth, cable angle, and count information directly on the chartplotter screen. When a fish shows on the sonar at 55 feet and your downrigger weight is at 52 feet, you can set the depth precisely without looking away from the screen. For anglers trolling multiple rods at different depths, the ability to manage all depth information from one display simplifies the operation.

The specific Humminbird models that support Cannon integration vary by product generation. Confirm compatibility with the Humminbird unit you are running before purchasing, as not all older units support the integration.

## Where to Start

For Northern Ontario anglers who are new to downrigger fishing, the entry point is typically one or two electric units on the stern, a basic Cannon Optimum or equivalent, with eight-pound weights and a learning season at moderate depths on inland lakes before committing to open Superior runs. The technique of setting baits, managing spread, reading the sonar, and responding to strikes at depth is learnable with a few trips and one rod.

For experienced trollers upgrading from manual units or adding capacity to an existing spread, the Digi-Troll series provides the precision and speed of deployment that serious Lake Superior trolling demands.
