There is a difference between a boat that passed a visual inspection in the driveway and a boat that is actually ready for the water. Every spring, Reyco Marine's service team works through a structured commissioning process that catches things most owners miss during a walk-around.

This is not a list of things you should check yourself before calling us. It is an explanation of what a professional spring commissioning includes and why each step matters for a safe, reliable launch.

## Why Professional Commissioning Matters

A winter storage period does things to a boat that are not visible from the outside. Fuel degrades. Water passages that were not fully drained can harbour corrosion or ice damage. Electrical connections oxidise. Impeller rubber deteriorates even without running hours.

A thorough commissioning catches these issues before they strand you on the water or cause engine damage that costs significantly more than the service itself.

Reyco is a Mercury and Princecraft authorised service centre. The technicians are factory-trained, use factory diagnostic tools, and work on marine equipment all season. Commissioning is not a side job here.

## What the Commissioning Covers

**Battery and electrical system**

The season starts with the battery. Technicians install the battery (or inspect your existing one), clean the terminals, and test the charging circuit under load. Gauges, lights, and the bilge pump are all verified. A bilge pump that does not work is a safety issue, not a nuisance.

**Fuel system**

Fresh fuel goes in. The fuel lines and primer bulb are inspected for cracks, brittleness, and swelling. Degraded primer bulbs are a common find after a season in storage. The fuel system is checked for leaks under pressure before the engine runs.

**Lower unit**

Gear oil is drained and refilled. The colour and consistency of the old oil tells a lot: milky gear oil means water intrusion, which indicates a seal failure that needs to be addressed before the engine takes any load. This is one of the checks that owners frequently skip on a self-service.

**Engine fluids**

Engine oil is checked or changed based on Mercury's service interval schedule. Coolant is checked and topped. The system is checked against the service requirements for your specific engine configuration.

**Cooling system**

The water pump is tested and the cooling passages are flushed. A failed impeller in the first weeks of the season can cause an overheat in minutes. Reyco replaces impellers on a scheduled basis rather than waiting for failure.

**Spark plugs**

Plugs are inspected for fouling, gap, and wear. Replacement is done when warranted based on condition and hours, not just age.

**Propeller and shaft seal**

The prop is inspected for dings, bent blades, and signs of fishing line wrap around the shaft. A bent blade vibrates at speed and puts stress on the lower unit bearing. The shaft seal condition is checked at the same time.

**Steering and controls**

Steering cables and throttle controls are checked for stiffness, fraying, and correct tension. Stiff steering is an early sign of cable wear. Ignored, it becomes a safety issue at speed.

**Hull and trailer**

The hull is visually inspected for cracks, blistering, or impact damage that developed over winter. The trailer is checked for bearing condition, lighting, and bunks or rollers that may have shifted.

**Final water-ready test**

Before the boat leaves the shop, it runs on muffs. Engine temperature, RPM range, charging voltage, and system function are all confirmed. You pick up a boat that has been tested, not just inspected.

## What Happens If You Skip It

A skipped commissioning is not free. The cost shifts from a scheduled service to an unscheduled repair, often at a less convenient time. A failed impeller mid-lake, a fuel line that lets go at speed, or a bilge pump that does not work when water comes in are all foreseeable outcomes of deferred service.

The commissioning exists because marine engines are subjected to hard use, UV, humidity, and corrosive water. A season of that without a proper start-of-season check shortens equipment life and raises your repair probability.

There is also the warranty question. If you have a Mercury engine under factory warranty and the engine fails due to a missed service interval, warranty coverage can be affected. Keeping your commissioning on schedule with an authorised centre protects that coverage and keeps a paper trail of properly completed services.

For boats bought at other dealers, this matters just as much. Reyco services boats regardless of where they were purchased, and the commissioning documentation is yours.
