## Why Pontoon Boats Make Sense for Ontario Lakes

Pontoon boats work well in Ontario because of the variety of water most Ontario boaters are working with. A family that launches on a calm 500-acre shield lake in the morning and crosses to a larger system in the afternoon needs a boat that handles both.

Pontoons offer stable platforms that are forgiving for passengers who are not experienced boaters. The large deck is useful when the passenger count varies, two people one trip, eight the next. And because Ontario families often use their boat for mixed purposes (fishing in the morning, swimming in the afternoon), the open deck layout of a pontoon accommodates that flexibility better than a dedicated fishing boat.

The trade-off against a traditional fishing boat is clear: pontoons are slower through the water at similar horsepower, they are less nimble in tight situations, and they are not ideal for rough open water. For Lake Superior runs or serious river fishing, a pontoon is not the right choice. For inland shield lake use with family passengers, it is a practical and popular option.

## The Princecraft Pontoon Lineup

Princecraft builds their pontoon range under the Vectra series. The lineup covers entry-level recreational pontoons through larger, better-equipped models suited to frequent family use.

**Vectra 19:** The entry-level Princecraft pontoon at 19 feet. Suited for calm inland lake use with smaller families or couples. The 19-foot hull works well at lower horsepower, a 60 to 90hp Mercury FourStroke on a Vectra 19 handles typical inland lake use competently. This is the most affordable entry point into the Princecraft pontoon lineup.

**Vectra 21 and 22:** The mid-range Princecraft pontoons at 21 and 22 feet. More seating, more deck space, and the ability to carry more passengers comfortably. The larger hull benefits from 90 to 115hp to maintain good planing speed with a full load. The 21/22 range is the most common choice for Ontario families who want the pontoon to be the primary family boat for the season.

**Vectra 23 and above:** The larger Princecraft pontoon platforms. More HP required to move the additional weight, better suited for frequent use and larger families. At this size, 115 to 150hp is appropriate depending on use. Buyers considering the larger Vectra platforms are typically looking at a boat that functions as a full-season family vessel rather than a recreational supplement.

Princecraft also offers tri-toon configurations (three tubes rather than two) on some models. Tri-toons ride higher in the water, handle rough conditions better than standard pontoons, and support higher horsepower. For buyers who want a pontoon but expect to encounter choppier water, the tri-toon upgrade is worth considering.

## Ontario-Specific Buying Considerations

**Shield lake bottom and launching.** Northern Ontario shield lakes have rocky bottoms and often rudimentary launches. A pontoon going in at a rough boat launch needs a trailer setup that handles the launch correctly. The aluminium tubes on a pontoon are not fragile, but contact with rock during launching is avoidable with good technique and the right trailer configuration. If you regularly launch on unimproved ramps, ask the dealer about trailer setup.

**Storage and trailering.** Pontoons are wide. A 21-foot pontoon with a 96-inch beam does not fit in a standard residential garage. Before buying, measure your storage. If garage storage is out, covered outdoor storage in the winter protects the upholstery and electrical components better than uncovered storage. Ask your dealer about storage options in SSM if needed.

**Season length.** Northern Ontario's boating season runs approximately May through October. A pontoon that is well-maintained and properly winterised lasts many seasons. The winterisation process on a pontoon includes engine fogging, water system draining (if equipped), upholstery protection, and battery management. Budget for end-of-season service as part of the annual ownership cost.

**HP selection for the hull.** Pontoon hulls have a maximum HP rating on the capacity plate, the same as any hull. Princecraft pontoons in the 19 to 22-foot range have HP ratings that vary by model and configuration. Buying the pontoon and engine together at the dealer ensures the engine is matched to the hull within the rated range.

## Buying Through a Dealer vs. Private Sale

Private pontoon sales in Ontario are common. A used Princecraft pontoon at three to five years old is often well-priced. But there are things to verify before buying used that a dealer sale handles automatically.

**Engine history.** On a dealer sale, the engine service history is part of the paperwork. On a private sale, you are asking for it and taking the seller's word on what they share. An engine without a documented service history is an unknown quantity.

**Hull condition.** Pontoon tubes dent and, in some cases, develop pinholes. A visual inspection of the tubes at waterline and below, and a check for waterlogging in the tubes (a loaded-side list is one indicator), is part of any used pontoon evaluation.

**Electronics and upholstery.** Used pontoon upholstery is often the most visually degraded component. Faded or cracked upholstery is a cosmetic issue but affects the boat's perceived condition significantly. Electronics (fish finders, stereo systems, navigation lights) should all be tested before purchase.

A new pontoon purchase at a dealer comes with manufacturer warranty on the hull and a Mercury engine warranty on the outboard. The service relationship is established from day one.
