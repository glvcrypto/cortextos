Princecraft and Lund are two of the most compared aluminum fishing boat brands in Northern Ontario, and the comparison comes up constantly when buyers are narrowing down their choice. Both are purpose-built aluminum fishing boats with decades of development behind them. The question is which one fits Northern Ontario conditions better, and whether local availability plays into your buying decision.

## Who Makes Each Brand

**Princecraft** is a Canadian brand manufactured in Princeville, Quebec, and sold through the Brunswick Corporation distribution network. Princecraft has been building aluminum fishing boats for Canadian buyers since the 1960s, with a product line shaped by the specific demands of Canadian freshwater fishing, Canadian weather, and Shield lake terrain. Canadian assembly, with Canadian buyers and Canadian lake conditions as the design reference point.

**Lund** is a Minnesota-based aluminum boat manufacturer with roots going back to 1948, now part of the White River Marine Group under Bass Pro Shops and Cabela's. Lund has a strong following across the upper Midwest and Canada, built primarily around walleye fishing in the Great Lakes region and the lake systems of Minnesota, Wisconsin, and Ontario. A proven product with a deep heritage in multi-species aluminum fishing boats.

Both brands are considered premium-tier aluminum fishing boats. The comparison is not about quality differences at equivalent price points. It is about which design approach and which regional availability context makes more sense for your situation.

---

## Hull Design and Construction

Both Princecraft and Lund use welded aluminum construction, which is the standard for premium aluminum fishing boats in this market. Riveted aluminum boats, which were the industry norm into the 1980s, have been largely displaced at the premium tier by welded construction, which produces a stronger, watertight hull without rivet seams that can loosen over time.

**Princecraft** builds their hulls with a deep-V design across the multi-species and fishing-focused range. The Yukon series in particular uses a modified deep-V hull intended for the specific conditions of Shield lakes: rocky shorelines, variable depth profiles, and the kind of chop that inland lakes generate when a weather system moves through. The hull design prioritises rough water capability while remaining manageable by a single operator.

**Lund** is particularly well-regarded for their Pro-V hull, a deep-V design that has been refined over multiple decades specifically for walleye fishing on large bodies of water. The Pro-V is a long-standing reference point for open-water stability in demanding conditions. The Impact series targets bass fishing with a more moderate-V design suited to calmer water.

For Northern Ontario multi-species fishing, including walleye, pike, bass, and lake trout depending on where you are fishing, either hull design is a capable platform. The difference is in degree, not kind.

## Model Range for Northern Ontario Buyers

**Princecraft Yukon series:** The Yukon SE and Yukon EX in the 17 to 19-foot range are the models most directly targeted at Northern Ontario multi-species fishing. Deep-V hull, aerated livewells, trolling motor mount, rod storage, and available with a range of Mercury outboard configurations. The Yukon is built for the same use pattern most Northern Ontario anglers run: a combination of walleye jigging, pike casting, and lake trout trolling depending on the season.

**Princecraft Sport series:** Smaller footprint, 14 to 16 feet, for buyers who primarily fish smaller Shield lakes where launching and trailering on gravel ramps is part of the routine. Lower price point, simpler configuration, still welded construction.

**Lund Pro-V series:** 17 to 21 feet, built specifically for open-water walleye fishing. Very high build quality, premium price tier. Strong resale value. The Pro-V is a widely respected boat that earns its price, but it positions at the higher end of what Northern Ontario buyers typically spend on a fishing boat.

**Lund Impact:** Moderate-V hull, targeted at calmer lakes and bass applications. Less relevant for the rougher water conditions of Northern Ontario Shield lakes and the Lake Superior tributaries.

## Northern Ontario Conditions: What Favours Each Brand

Northern Ontario Shield lakes are demanding. Rocky shorelines, variable weather, and the kind of wind-generated chop that builds quickly on exposed lake sections make hull design genuinely relevant rather than theoretical.

Both Princecraft and Lund produce boats that handle these conditions competently in their respective segments. The practical difference for a Northern Ontario buyer is not hull performance alone. It is access to the full ownership picture: purchasing, warranty service, parts, and repairs.
