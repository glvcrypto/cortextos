# Reyco Marine, Sault Ste. Marie's Marine and Small Engine Dealer, Launches New Website

*For immediate release, Sault Ste. Marie, ON*

Reyco Marine, a full-service marine and small engine dealership on White Oak Drive in Sault Ste. Marie, has launched a new website at reycomarine.com. Owner Casey Davieaux built the business to serve local customers across marine repair, small engine service, lawn, snow, and ATV/UTV equipment, with authorised brands and in-house technicians under one roof in the Sault.

Reyco Marine has been part of Sault Ste. Marie's small engine and outdoor equipment landscape for decades. Over that time, the shop added dealer agreements, service capabilities, and staff as local demand grew. What started as a repair and parts operation became a dealership covering marine, lawn, snow, ATV, and UTV equipment under a single address on White Oak Drive. For Casey Davieaux, the growth reflected a consistent idea: customers in the Sault should not have to drive south to find authorised service and OEM parts for the equipment they use every season.

## A Full Range of Services for Every Season

Reyco Marine handles service and repair across marine engines, small engines, lawn care equipment, snow blowers, and ATVs and UTVs. The shop is an authorised dealer for Mercury outboard motors, Princecraft boats, Toro lawn and snow equipment, Echo handheld power tools, EZGO vehicles, Cub Cadet outdoor power equipment, Hisun UTVs, and accessories from Minn Kota, Cannon, and Humminbird. R&J rounds out the brand lineup.

The shop's workload shifts with the seasons in a way that keeps the bays running year-round. April and May bring the first push of marine repair as Sault Ste. Marie boat owners prepare for the water: winterization reversal, engine diagnostics, fuel system service, and water tests before launch. Summer keeps the shop busy with in-season repairs, parts orders, and warranty work for customers who want to stay on the water rather than wait in a queue. Fall brings winterization for marine equipment, storage preparation, and the first wave of snow blower tune-ups as temperatures drop. From November through March, the service calendar fills with snow blower repairs, engine overhauls, and small engine work that sat all summer and needs attention before the first snowfall arrives.
