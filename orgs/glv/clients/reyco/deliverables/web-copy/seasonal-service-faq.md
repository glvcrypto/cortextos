Lake Superior stays cold enough to cause cold water shock well into July — the surface temperature in most Northern Ontario bays doesn't reach 21°C until mid-summer. For boaters in the Algoma district, that means the season is shorter, the stakes of skipping service are higher, and the timing of spring and fall work matters more than most people realise.

We answer the most common seasonal service questions below.

---

### Spring Commissioning

**What is spring commissioning and do I actually need it?**

Spring commissioning is the process of preparing your boat and outboard for the season after winter storage. If your equipment was properly winterised, spring commissioning confirms it came through the cold in good shape — and catches the problems before you're on the water. Lee, Damian, and Cody work through a standard commissioning checklist: flush the cooling system, inspect the impeller, change gear oil, check belts and anodes, and verify fuel system integrity. Skipping it and finding a cracked impeller at the dock on the walleye opener is a worse problem than booking in April.

**What does spring commissioning include?**

A standard commissioning service includes cooling system flush, impeller inspection and replacement if needed, gear oil change, spark plug check, battery load test, fuel system inspection, and a full functional test before we return the unit. If we find something during commissioning — a cracked housing, a failed seal — we'll call you before we proceed. You get a written estimate for any work beyond the commissioning scope.

**When should I book spring commissioning?**

Book in April. Walleye season on most Algoma district inland lakes opens the third Saturday in May — to be ready, your equipment needs to be in our shop by the last week of April. If you bring it in during the first two weeks of May, Aaron will do his best to schedule you, but we cannot guarantee the timing during our peak window.

**How long does spring commissioning take?**

Standard commissioning — no parts replacement needed — takes 2 to 4 business days once your unit is in the shop. If we find a failed impeller or need to order a part, add 2 to 3 business days for the parts order. The earlier in the spring you book, the more buffer you have.

**Can I do spring commissioning myself?**

You can perform basic checks yourself — battery charge, visual hull inspection, fuel lines. The impeller is the part most people miss. Impeller failure does not give warning before it happens, and a seized impeller at running temperature damages the powerhead. If you are not comfortable pulling the lower unit and inspecting the impeller housing, bring it to us. It is a short job for Cody or Lee; it is an expensive repair if it fails on the water.

**Do you commission boats you didn't sell?**

Yes. We commission outboards and sterndrives regardless of where you purchased them — Mercury, Suzuki, Yamaha, Honda. Warranty work is the only exception, which requires dealer authorisation from the original seller. Call the service desk and we'll book you in.

### Fall Winterisation

**Why does winterisation matter in Northern Ontario?**

Sault Ste. Marie averages 290 centimetres of snowfall per year — more than Toronto, Ottawa, and Kingston combined. The freeze-up timeline for the Algoma district means water left in cooling passages and engine blocks is not a risk you can take lightly. A single freeze-thaw cycle can crack a powerhead. Winterisation is not optional maintenance in a Northern Ontario climate; it is the difference between a boat that works in spring and one that needs a major engine repair.

**What does winterisation include?**

Winterisation covers flushing and draining the cooling system, fogging the cylinders, stabilising the fuel system, changing gear oil, greasing fittings, and confirming the unit is properly stored for the season. Aaron reviews every winterisation checklist before we return the unit. If we find something during the process — a worn seal, an anode that needs replacement — we'll flag it before your equipment goes into storage so you don't pull it out in April with a surprise.

**When should I book fall winterisation?**

Book in September or early October. By late October our schedule fills quickly as the weather closes in and more owners rush to get units in before freeze-up. If you wait until November, we'll fit you in where we can, but the lead time will be longer and we cannot guarantee completion before the first hard freeze in the Sault.

**Do you winterise equipment you didn't sell?**

Yes. We winterise outboards, sterndrives, and boats regardless of where they were purchased or serviced previously. Call the service desk with the make, model, and year and we'll give you a realistic timeline before you make the drive.

**What if I want to store my boat at Reyco over winter?**

We service equipment brought to our facility. For storage arrangements, call Casey or Aaron directly — storage availability and terms change year to year and we do not publish details online.

### Impeller Service

**What is a water pump impeller and when should it be replaced?**

The impeller is the rubber pump inside your outboard's lower unit that circulates cooling water through the engine. Most manufacturers recommend replacing it every 2 to 3 seasons or 100 hours — whichever comes first. On Lake Superior and Algoma district inland lakes, where water temperatures stay cold and seasonal use is compressed into a shorter window, inspection every season is the right call. Lynn can confirm the correct impeller part number for your specific motor before you book.

**How do I know if my impeller has failed?**

The most common signs are overheating warnings, reduced water output from the tell-tale (the stream of water that exits the outboard while running), or an engine that runs hot at idle. Do not continue running a motor that is showing overheating indicators — the damage escalates quickly. Trailer it in and let Lee or Cody diagnose before you run it again.

**Is impeller replacement something I can do myself?**

Yes, if you are mechanically comfortable pulling the lower unit and working with small rubber parts. The risk is improper reassembly — a lower unit seal not seated correctly will leak gear oil. If you are not confident in the reassembly, bring it to us. An impeller replacement is a modest service job; a lower unit seal failure is not.

### Storage and Off-Season

**Can I drop off my boat for the winter without a winterisation appointment?**

Call ahead before you drop off. Walk-ins go into the queue behind pre-booked appointments, and in October the queue is long. A quick call to the service desk gives you a realistic intake date and lets Aaron schedule your work efficiently. Dropping off unannounced in late October does not guarantee we can complete winterisation before freeze-up.

**What should I do before I bring my boat in for winter service?**

Remove personal gear, safety equipment, and any electronics you want to store indoors separately. Leave the fuel tank at least a quarter full so we can add stabiliser. If you have a cover, bring it — we'll confirm it's properly fitted before storage. If you have questions about what to remove or leave, call the service desk and Damian or Lee will walk you through it.
