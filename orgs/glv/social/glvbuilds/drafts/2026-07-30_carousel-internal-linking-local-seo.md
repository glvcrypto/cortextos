## Slide 2 — Why Internal Links Matter for Local SEO

**Headline:** Internal links tell Google what your pages are about — and which ones matter.

**Body:**
Every link from one page to another is a signal.
It tells Google: "this page is related to that page — and this one is more important."
For local service businesses, the goal is to concentrate authority on the pages that should rank: service pages and city landing pages.
Most local sites have service pages floating with no internal links pointing to them.

*Visual: Unlinked page cluster vs. connected hub-and-spoke structure*

---

## Slide 3 — The Structure I Use

**Headline:** Homepage → Service pages → City pages → Blog posts.

**Body:**
The rule: every page that should rank should receive at least one internal link from a relevant, authoritative page.
Homepage links to all main service pages (navigation and body).
Each service page links to city-specific landing pages for that service.
Blog posts link back to the most relevant service page.
It's a hub-and-spoke model. The hub accumulates authority. The spokes rank for long-tail queries.

*Visual: Diagram — Homepage at centre, service pages radiating out, city pages and blog posts connecting to each service page*

## Slide 4 — The Anchor Text Rule

**Headline:** Use descriptive anchor text — not "click here."

**Body:**
"Lawn mower repair in Sault Ste. Marie" as anchor text tells Google what the destination page is about.
"Click here" or "learn more" tells Google nothing.
The anchor text should match the keyword cluster of the page you're linking to.
Keep it natural — don't force exact-match anchors into every sentence.
One descriptive anchor per link is all you need.

## Slide 5 — The Service Page Checklist

**Headline:** Every service page should link to:

**Body:**
→ Related service pages (e.g. Lawn Mower Repair links to Tune-Ups and Small Engine Repair)
→ City landing pages for that service (e.g. Lawn Mower Repair Sault Ste. Marie)
→ Any blog post that covers that service in detail
→ The contact page or booking form — the conversion endpoint

If a service page has zero outgoing links to relevant pages, it's an island. Fix that first.

*Visual: Service page checklist with 4 checkboxes*

---

## Slide 6 — Blog Post Rule

**Headline:** Every blog post earns its place by linking to a service page.

**Body:**
A blog post that doesn't link to the service page it supports is a missed opportunity.
The post ranks for an informational query → reader clicks a contextual link → lands on the service page.
That's the conversion path.
If a post has no link to a service page, add one in the body — not just in the footer or sidebar.

*Visual: Blog post → arrow → service page flow*

## Slide 7 — Start Here

**Headline:** Don't build the whole structure at once. Start with one page.

**Body:**
Pick the service page that should rank for your most valuable query.
Add internal links to it from: the homepage body, the most relevant blog post, and at least one related service page.
Then repeat for the next most important page.
Internal linking is an ongoing audit — not a one-time setup.
