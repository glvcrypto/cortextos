# First 90-Day Milestone Report — Carousel

## LinkedIn caption

**When a client asks "is it working yet?" at 90 days, I don't pull rank positions. I show them this report instead.**

The first 90 days of local SEO is almost never about rank improvements. It's about foundations. And a lot of clients don't understand that until you show them exactly what was built.

I use a milestone-based 90-day report — six sections, one page. It's different from the monthly rank-and-traffic report I send after that. The goal isn't to show movement. It's to show readiness.

Here's what's in it:

**Section 1: GBP — claimed, verified, and optimised.** Primary category confirmed. Services tab filled. Photos uploaded. Description rewritten. Access secured (client as Primary Owner). Q&A seeded.

**Section 2: Citations — submitted.** Which directories were claimed or corrected. NAP consistency confirmed across top 10 sources. Any duplicates flagged and resolved.

**Section 3: Pages — live.** Any new service or location pages built. Title tag formula applied. FAQPage schema added. Internal links connected to anchor pages.

**Section 4: Rank baselines set.** Starting positions recorded for the five most important service + city search terms. Not to show wins — to have a documented before. Everything after month three is measured against this baseline.

**Section 5: Traffic benchmarks.** GBP website clicks, calls, and direction requests in month one vs. month three. GA4 UTM baseline for GBP traffic source.

**Section 6: Next 90 goals.** The three highest-priority items for months four through six. Usually: review generation system, first local content pages, and one technical fix identified in the audit.

One page. Client signs off. Monthly reporting starts from here.
