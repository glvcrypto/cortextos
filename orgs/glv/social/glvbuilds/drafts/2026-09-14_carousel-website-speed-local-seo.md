# Website Speed for Local SEO — The One That Costs You Leads

## Slide 1 — Hook

Most local SEO work focuses on ranking.

Here's the signal that business owners understand immediately: if your service page loads slow on mobile, you're losing leads Google already sent you.

---

## Slide 2 — Why Speed Matters for Local Specifically

Local search is predominantly mobile.

More than 60% of local searches happen on a phone. If a searcher taps your GBP "Website" link and the page takes 5 seconds to load — they're gone before they read a word.

Google's Core Web Vitals measure load performance. Poor LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) is a page experience signal that affects rankings. But the more immediate cost is the conversion you lose before you ever get to rank for anything.

---

## Slide 3 — Check Your Score (Free, 2 Minutes)

Go to PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev).

Paste the URL of your highest-priority service page — not your homepage.

Switch to Mobile view.

Read the LCP score:
- Under 2.5s = Good
- 2.5–4.0s = Needs Improvement
- Over 4.0s = Poor

If you're in "Needs Improvement" or "Poor" on mobile, you're working harder than you need to.

---

## Slide 4 — Fix 1: Compress Your Images (Biggest Lever)

Images are the most common cause of slow LCP.

What to do:
1. Convert images to WebP format (smaller file size, same quality)
2. Serve images at the size they're actually displayed — not 3000px wide for a 400px container
3. Add `loading="lazy"` to images below the fold

These three changes alone can move LCP from "Poor" to "Good" on most small business service pages.

---

## Slide 5 — Fix 2: Upgrade Your Hosting

There's a ceiling on what image optimisation can do on shared hosting.

Shared hosting means your site shares server resources with hundreds of other websites. When traffic spikes — including your competitor's traffic — your response time degrades.

Switching to managed WordPress hosting (SiteGround, WP Engine, Kinsta) or a VPS removes that ceiling.

For many local service businesses, a hosting upgrade is the fastest page speed improvement available.

---

## Slide 6 — Fix 3: Remove Render-Blocking Scripts

Every plugin that loads JavaScript or CSS on every page adds time before the browser can render anything useful.

Check which plugins are active on your service pages. Ask your developer to audit for render-blocking resources using the PageSpeed Insights "Opportunities" section.

Common culprits: chat widgets, third-party form builders, sliders, social media embeds.

You don't need to remove features — you need them loading after the main content, not before.

---

## Slide 7 — Start Here

1. Run PageSpeed Insights on your main service page (mobile, not desktop)
2. Note the LCP score and the top 3 "Opportunities" listed
3. Fix images first — it's free and immediate
4. Evaluate hosting if images are already optimised
5. Bring in a developer for script audit if hosting is already solid

## LinkedIn Caption (300–600 words)

I've had conversations with local business owners who are spending money on GBP ads and outranking competitors in the local pack — and still losing leads because their service page loads in 7 seconds on mobile.

The ranking is doing its job. The page isn't.

Local search is a mobile-first experience. When someone taps "Website" on your GBP, they're on a phone, probably out running errands, probably with a specific question. If the page doesn't load in under 3 seconds, they hit back and call the next result.

Google's Core Web Vitals measure this. LCP — Largest Contentful Paint — is the time it takes for the main content of the page to become visible. Google's target is under 2.5 seconds on mobile. Most small business service pages I audit are in the 3–6 second range.

**The fastest way to check: PageSpeed Insights.**

Go to pagespeed.web.dev. Paste your most important service page URL. Switch to Mobile. Read the LCP number.

Under 2.5s: good. 2.5–4.0s: fix this quarter. Over 4.0s: fix this month.

**Fix 1 — Compress images (free, high impact)**

Images are the most common cause of slow LCP on local service pages. The fixes: convert to WebP format, serve images at display size not original upload size, and add lazy loading to below-the-fold images.

These changes are free (most image optimisation plugins handle them) and can move a page from "Poor" to "Good" in one deployment.

**Fix 2 — Upgrade hosting (medium cost, removes ceiling)**

If images are already optimised and LCP is still slow, the bottleneck is often shared hosting. On shared hosting, your site competes for server resources with hundreds of others.

Managed WordPress hosting from providers like SiteGround, WP Engine, or Kinsta costs more per month than shared hosting — but for a local service business generating leads online, the cost of a hosting upgrade is usually recovered in a week.

**Fix 3 — Remove render-blocking scripts (developer task)**

Every plugin that loads JavaScript or CSS on page load adds time before the browser can paint anything useful. Chat widgets, form builders, sliders, social embeds — all of these can be optimised to load after the main content.

PageSpeed Insights lists render-blocking resources under "Opportunities." A 30-minute developer audit of that list usually surfaces two or three easy wins.

Ranking is how you get in front of the searcher. Page speed is how you keep them.
