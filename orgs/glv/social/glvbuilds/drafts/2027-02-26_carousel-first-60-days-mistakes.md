## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
> "I've audited local SEO for a lot of newly launched businesses. The same three mistakes show up every time."

---

**Slide 2 — Mistake 1**
**Building citations before GBP is verified.**
Citations list your business on other directories.
They work because they point back to an anchor — your GBP profile.
If GBP isn't verified yet, citations point to nothing.
GBP verification comes first. Always. Even if it takes 2 weeks.

---

**Slide 3 — Mistake 2**
**Writing service pages before GSC is tracking.**
New service pages fly blind without GSC data.
You don't know which queries Google is associating with your pages.
You can't see indexation errors until they're already costing you.
Set up GBP → set up GSC → verify the site is crawling → then write the pages.

---

**Slide 4 — Mistake 3**
**Targeting competitive terms in month one.**
"[Service] in [Major City]" is a 6–12 month play.
Month one is not the time to chase the highest-volume keyword in your market.
The first win: your business name showing up cleanly in Maps and organic search.
Build the base before you build the reach.

---

**Slide 5 — What to do instead**
**The right sequence for the first 60 days:**
1. GBP verified and appearing for brand name search ✓
2. Homepage NAP matching GBP exactly ✓
3. Contact page with address, phone as `tel:` link, hours ✓
4. GSC verified, crawl data flowing, coverage errors checked ✓
5. First 5 reviews collected (guided ask, not incentivised) ✓

Everything else comes after this foundation is confirmed.

---

**Slide 6 — The timeline reframe**
**Month 2 looks like nothing is working. That's normal.**
Day 30 signal check: GBP appearing for brand name + GSC crawling + no coverage errors = on track.
If those three aren't there — stop and fix the foundation before adding anything.
The mistake at month 2: adding more content, more citations, more spend, without checking whether the base is solid.

---

## LinkedIn Caption

Every newly launched business I audit has made at least one of these three mistakes in the first 60 days. Usually all three.

**Mistake 1: Building citations before GBP is verified.**
Citations work because they point to your GBP profile as the authoritative anchor. Building them before that anchor is verified is working in the wrong order. Wait for the green verification checkmark. Then build outward.

**Mistake 2: Writing service pages before GSC is live.**
Service pages written without GSC tracking are flying blind. You can't see which queries Google is associating with your new pages. You can't catch indexation errors before they sit undetected for weeks. The sequence: GBP verified → GSC property added and verified → confirm the site is crawling → then write and publish pages.

**Mistake 3: Targeting competitive keywords in month one.**
"[Service] in [City]" for a major market is a 6–12 month play for a new business with no GBP authority, no reviews, and no indexed pages. Month one has one local SEO job: make sure your business name shows up correctly in Maps and organic search. That's the entire goal. Everything else builds on it.
