# LinkedIn: Year-end reflection — New Year's Eve

## LinkedIn Caption (full)

**It's New Year's Eve. I'll keep this short.**

Three things local SEO taught me this year — not theory, just what I actually saw working:

**1. The fix is almost always simpler than the diagnosis.**
The clients who saw the biggest gains weren't the ones who added the most tactics. They were the ones who corrected one foundational problem — wrong primary category, one broken redirect, a NAP mismatch in a key directory — and then stayed consistent for six months. The compounding is in the consistency, not the cleverness.

**2. The report matters less than the call.**
I can send a clean one-page report with six sections. If I don't spend 20 minutes walking through it, it doesn't land. The clients who renewed without hesitation were the ones I talked to monthly — not the ones who had the most impressive data.

**3. "Is this working?" is a question worth asking out loud.**
Not in a dashboard. Not in a spreadsheet. Out loud, to the client, in the first five minutes of the check-in call. The answer resets the priority list every time. It saved me months of the wrong work this year, more than once.

Happy New Year. See you in January.
