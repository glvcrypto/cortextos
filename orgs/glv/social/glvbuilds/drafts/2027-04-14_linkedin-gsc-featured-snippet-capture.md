## LinkedIn Post

GSC shows you when Google is already reading your page for a question it's answering elsewhere.

Most people miss it.

Featured snippets — the answer boxes at the top of search results — don't come from domain authority. They come from pages that answer a specific question clearly and early. GSC shows you which questions yours is already close on.

**Three-step workflow:**

Open GSC → Performance → Queries. Filter by question words — "how," "what," "why," "best," "vs," "cost." Sort by position. Look for pages ranked 1–8 with significant impressions.

Open each query in a private browser. Check the featured snippet source. Is it a competitor? If yes: what's their format? How long is the answer paragraph? What's the H2 wording?

If the page exists on your site but isn't holding the snippet: add a dedicated H2 using the exact question phrasing Google associates with that query. Write the answer in the paragraph immediately below — direct answer in the first sentence, 40–60 words total, then follow-up context.

**The format Google consistently pulls:**

- H2: the exact question (matches natural language, not keyword-stuffed)
- First sentence under H2: the direct answer in under 10 words
- Following 1–3 sentences: supporting context

Works for cost questions, process questions, comparison questions, and common pre-call qualifiers ("do I need a referral for physiotherapy?").

**Two things that don't work:**

Long intro paragraphs before the answer — Google can't extract a clean snippet from paragraph four. And structured data alone won't do it — the snippet is pulled from visible page text, not schema.

If you audit one page this week, pick the one with a question query sitting at position 3–8 and over 100 impressions. That's the fastest conversion.
