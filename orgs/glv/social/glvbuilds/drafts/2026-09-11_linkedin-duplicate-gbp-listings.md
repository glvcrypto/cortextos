# Duplicate GBP Listings — How to Find and Fix Them

## LinkedIn Post

The most common local SEO emergency call I get isn't a ranking drop.

It's: "There are two of us on Google Maps."

Duplicate GBP listings are more common than most business owners realise — and more damaging than they expect.

**Why duplicates happen:**

— An old listing from a previous address was never closed
— Google auto-generated a listing from third-party data
— A former employee or agency created a second profile
— A business moved but the original GBP was left active at the old address

**Why it matters:**

Google splits signals between duplicate profiles. Reviews may be scattered across both. Local pack rankings are diluted because neither profile has the full weight of the business's citations, links, and engagement history.

The worst case: an unclaimed duplicate is publicly editable. Anyone with a Google account can suggest changes — including wrong phone numbers, false hours, or a closure status.

**How to find duplicates:**

Search the business name from a private browsing window. Then search the business name plus the address. Then search the business phone number.

If more than one profile appears — or an unfamiliar listing shows up — you have a duplicate.

**What to do:**

Rule one: always claim the duplicate before requesting removal. An unclaimed listing can be edited by anyone.

If the duplicate is unclaimed: claim it, then report it as a duplicate via the Google Business Profile manager under "Advanced settings" → "Remove this listing."

If the duplicate is claimed by someone else: file a business redressal request through Google's support channel.

If the duplicate has reviews you want to preserve: contact Google Support before removing — in some cases review consolidation is possible.

**Priority order:**

1. Identify all duplicates
2. Claim any unclaimed ones
3. Remove or merge
4. Verify the surviving profile has correct NAP

Fix the duplicate before optimising anything else.
