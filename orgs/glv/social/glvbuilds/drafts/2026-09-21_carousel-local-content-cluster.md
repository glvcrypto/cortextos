## Slide 2 — The 5-page cluster

**The 5-page local content cluster:**

1. Main service page (city + service)
2. How-it-works page (process / what to expect)
3. FAQ page (long-tail question keywords)
4. Comparison page (us vs. the alternative)
5. Areas-served page (suburb + service variations)

---

## Slide 3 — Page 1: Main service page

**Page 1 — The anchor**

Format: `[City] + [Service]` — e.g. "Lawn Mower Repair in Sudbury"

This is the one page that needs to rank. Everything else links to it and passes authority. Without a strong anchor, the cluster doesn't function.

---

## Slide 4 — Page 2 + 3: Process + FAQ

**Pages 2 & 3 — Long-tail traffic**

Process page: "What to expect when you bring your [equipment] in for service" — answers the question a buyer Googles before they call.

FAQ page: Pull real questions from Google's "People Also Ask" box and your own GBP Q&A section. Each H2 is a long-tail keyword.

---

## Slide 5 — Page 4: Comparison

**Page 4 — The converter**

"[Our service] vs. [the alternative]"

Examples:
- "Repair vs. Replace: Small Engine Guide"
- "Mobile Marine Service vs. Shop Drop-Off"
- "DIY vs. Professional Tune-Up"

These pages attract high-intent visitors who are close to a decision.

---

## Slide 6 — Page 5: Areas served

**Page 5 — The coverage map**

One H2 per suburb or service area. Each section: 1–2 sentences mentioning the suburb + the service.

Google indexes each section as relevant for that suburb + service query. One page does the work of ten thin location pages.

## LinkedIn caption

Most local service businesses have one page per service, if that. No process page. No FAQ. No comparison. No suburbs.

A single-page approach leaves most of the ranking surface on the table.

Here's the five-page cluster structure I build for every service that matters to a client:

**Page 1 — The anchor**
`[City] + [Service]` — this is the target page. Strong H1, service-area specificity, E-E-A-T signals, clear CTA. Everything else in the cluster links back to this page.

**Page 2 — Process / How it works**
"What to expect when you bring your [equipment] in for service." Buyers Google this before they call. A process page answers the pre-decision question and captures the traffic that would otherwise go to a competitor who does have one.

**Page 3 — FAQ**
Pull from Google's People Also Ask box and from your GBP Q&A section. Each question becomes an H2. Each answer is 80–150 words. Long-tail keyword coverage without targeting thin one-liners.

**Page 4 — Comparison**
"[Service] vs. [alternative]" pages attract high-intent visitors. Someone Googling "repair vs. replace small engine" is close to a decision and actively shopping. A well-written comparison page converts better than most service pages.

**Page 5 — Areas served**
One H2 per suburb. Two sentences mentioning the suburb + service. One URL. Google surfaces it for suburb-specific searches. No thin location pages required.

Build the anchor first. Add one page per month. By month five, you have a cluster that's indexed, cross-linked, and covering the full keyword surface for that service.
