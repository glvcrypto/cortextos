## LinkedIn Caption

Most local service businesses have one "Services" page.

That's why they don't rank for anything useful.

A single page covering "plumbing, electrical, HVAC, and renovations" ranks for none of those things. Google can't tell what you're actually about. Neither can a customer searching for "kitchen drain repair Sudbury."

Here's what I build instead:

**One dedicated page per service + city combination.**

That means:
- `/drain-repair-sudbury/`
- `/hot-water-heater-replacement-sudbury/`
- `/emergency-plumber-sudbury/`

Each page covers one specific service, one location, and answers the questions that searcher actually has: how fast do you come, what does it cost, do you serve my neighbourhood.

Not because I like making more work for the developer. Because that's what ranks.

Four things every dedicated local service page needs:

1. **The service + city in the H1.** Not "Our Services." "Drain Repair in Sudbury."
2. **One conversion action.** Not three. One. Call, form, or booking link — pick the one that fits how your customers actually contact you.
3. **A FAQ block.** The questions people type into Google before they pick up the phone. Answer them on the page.
4. **Internal links to sibling pages.** If you do drain repair and hot water heaters, link between them. Google follows the thread.

The businesses I audit that rank well in local packs almost always have this pattern. The ones that don't, have one page that tries to do everything.

What's the service you're trying to rank for right now?
