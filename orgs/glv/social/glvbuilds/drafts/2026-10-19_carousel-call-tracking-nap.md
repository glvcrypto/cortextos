## Slide 2

**Why this is a real tension**

NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) is a local ranking signal.
Google expects your phone number to match across GBP, your website, and every directory listing.

Call tracking uses dynamic numbers that replace your real number — so the marketing team can see which campaign drove the call.

If the tracking number shows up in GBP or directories, your NAP is inconsistent. That's a problem.

---

## Slide 3

**The rule: one number for everything local**

Your primary phone number — the one in GBP, on your website footer, and in every directory — must be your real, permanent business number. Never replace this with a tracking number.

This is the number search engines use to verify your business exists. Changing it confuses citation signals.

---

## Slide 4

**How to use call tracking without breaking NAP**

The correct setup: dynamic number insertion (DNI).

Your website shows your real number in the footer and in structured data (NAP block).
A JavaScript snippet swaps the number visible to website visitors — but only in the main body, not in the footer/schema.

Google crawls the real number in your schema. Visitors (and your ad platform) see the tracking number. Both are happy.

---

## Slide 5

**What to tell your developer**

Three requirements:
1. Real number stays in: LocalBusiness schema, footer/contact page, GBP, all directories
2. Tracking number only appears via DNI in the page body (above the fold call CTA)
3. DNI script does not run on the footer or on pages Google uses for NAP verification

Most reputable call tracking platforms (CallRail, WhatConverts) have GBP integration that handles this correctly out of the box. Check the settings.

---

## Slide 6

**The audit check**

Once tracking is live, verify:
1. Search your business name on Google — click through to your site. Does the footer show your real number?
2. Check your GBP — is the primary number still the real one?
3. Run a citation audit (BrightLocal, Whitespark) — confirm no directories picked up the tracking number
4. Check your LocalBusiness schema with the Rich Results Test — real number should appear
