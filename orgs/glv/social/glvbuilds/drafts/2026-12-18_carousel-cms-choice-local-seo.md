## LinkedIn Caption (~440 words)

"Which website platform is best for local SEO?"

I get asked this at least twice a month. The honest answer: the gap between the major platforms is smaller than most people think — but the choice still matters for specific things.

Here's what I've seen actually matter across client sites:

**The four things that decide it**

Schema control, clean URL structure, crawlability, and hosting choice. Every major platform is indexable by Google. Not every platform gives you full control over all four — and that's where real differences emerge.

**WordPress**

The highest ceiling. Yoast or RankMath handles LocalBusiness schema, FAQ schema, and AggregateRating schema — all the types that matter for local service businesses. Custom URL slugs, full control over meta tags, and hosting choice (which is critical for page speed).

The trade-off: it requires technical comfort or a developer to set up correctly. Not the right call for a client who can barely log in.

**Squarespace**

Solid for most local service businesses. Auto-generates LocalBusiness schema. Clean URLs. Acceptable speed out of the box. It handles the things that matter for most service-business local SEO without requiring any technical setup.

Where it falls short: FAQ schema implementation is limited without custom code injection, and some schema types aren't possible natively. Not a dealbreaker for most clients — but something to know before recommending it.

**Wix**

Has improved significantly since 2022 — the native SEO panel covers the basics, and they've fixed a number of the crawlability issues that made it a harder recommendation a few years ago. Some URL structure quirks remain in certain template configurations.

I'd audit the technical setup before recommending it for a competitive market. For simpler deployments, it works.

**Shopify**

Retail-first. Strong on product schema. Fine for a local business with physical inventory who also sells online.

Not built for service-page SEO — the page hierarchy and URL structure work against ranking for service+city queries. I don't recommend it for service businesses.

**The rule I follow**

The best platform is the one your client will actually maintain. A well-maintained Squarespace site that gets updated, gets GBP posts added, and gets new service pages — outperforms a neglected WordPress installation that hasn't been touched in two years.

Don't switch a client off a clean, working platform unless there's a specific technical problem it's causing. Migrations cost link equity and take time to recover from.
