## LinkedIn Post

Your Google Business Profile and your website are two separate ranking systems.

Most local businesses only actively manage one of them.

Here's why both matter — and why improving one doesn't automatically help the other.

**The local pack (map results)**

When someone searches "plumber Sault Ste. Marie" or "marine repair near me," Google typically shows three business listings above the organic results. That's the local pack — sometimes called the map pack.

Ranking in the local pack is driven by your Google Business Profile, not your website. The main signals: how complete your GBP is, your review count and recency, your primary category, proximity to the searcher, and how active your profile is (photos, posts, Q&A).

Your website barely factors in here.

**Organic results**

Below the local pack are the traditional blue-link results. Ranking here is driven by your website — content quality, on-page SEO, backlinks, technical health, internal linking.

Your GBP barely factors in here.

**Why this matters in practice**

You can rank #1 in the local pack and not appear at all in organic results for the same search term — and vice versa.

Both positions appear on the same SERP. A searcher sees the map pack first. Some click a map result. Others scroll past and click an organic result. The searcher type and intent varies.

A local business that only manages its GBP captures one audience. One that also has optimised service pages captures both.

**The practical takeaway**

These aren't redundant channels. They're parallel systems targeting different parts of the same search result page.

This week: audit your GBP — categories, completeness, photos, review velocity. That's your local pack lever.

Next week: audit your top service pages — title tag, H1, on-page content, mobile speed. That's your organic lever.

Two separate audits. Two separate ranking systems. One SERP.
