## LinkedIn Caption (for sharing the reel)

Before I look at a new client's keyword rankings, I install Microsoft Clarity on their website.

It's free. It takes two minutes to set up via GTM. And it tells me something keyword tools can't: what real visitors do once they arrive.

Session recordings show me where visitors click, where they hesitate, and where they rage-click — that's when someone taps the same spot over and over because it looks interactive but isn't. Usually a heading styled like a button, or an image they expected to link somewhere.

Scroll heatmaps show me how far down the page visitors get before they leave. For most local service sites, the answer is: not far enough. The contact form is at the bottom. The phone number is in the footer. On mobile, most visitors leave before they find either.

What I fix first based on Clarity data:

• Phone number or click-to-call button above the fold on mobile
• CTA button with enough contrast and padding to look tappable
• Contact page simplified — one form, no more than 4–5 fields, no extra friction

Clarity won't tell you what to write on your service pages. But it'll show you whether the visitors you're already sending to your site are actually converting — or leaving before they get the chance.
