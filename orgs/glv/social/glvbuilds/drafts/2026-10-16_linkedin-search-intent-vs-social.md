## LinkedIn post

A new client asked me last week: "Should I be on Google or focus on social media?"

My answer: it depends on one thing — where your customer is in their decision.

**When someone types "plumber near me" into Google, they've already decided they need a plumber.**
They're not browsing. They're buying. That's search intent.

**When someone scrolls Instagram, they haven't decided anything yet.**
They might see your post and remember your name later. That's brand awareness.

For most local service businesses I work with — trades, health, legal, automotive — the buying window is short and urgent. Search wins every time for those clients. A customer with a burst pipe isn't going to discover you on TikTok.

**The exception:**
Visually-led businesses — restaurants, hair salons, fitness studios, interior designers — where the product itself is the thing people browse. There, Instagram and Pinterest do meaningful discovery work.

**The practical rule I give clients:**
Start with Google (GBP + one indexed service page per service). Once you're converting visitors into calls, add social to build recognition over time.

Doing both badly is worse than doing one well.

Where does most of your client work come from — Google or social? Curious what the mix looks like for people in this feed.
