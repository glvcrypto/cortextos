## LinkedIn Caption

My first local SEO client ran an auto repair shop. He'd been in business for nine years and had one question: "Why aren't I showing up on Google?"

He thought the answer would be technical. A broken link, a setting he'd missed, something a developer had done wrong.

Here's what I found, and what I told him.

**1. He existed on Google. Just barely.**
The business had a Google Business Profile. It had been auto-generated from data Google collected from other sources. His address was slightly wrong. His hours weren't set. There were no photos. His primary category was set to the broad version instead of the specific service type. He had one review from 2019.

**2. The root cause wasn't technical.**
He wasn't not showing up because of a website problem. He wasn't showing up because the profile had never been claimed, verified, or touched. There was no active signal telling Google the business was accurate, maintained, or worth surfacing to customers with urgent intent.

**3. The first fix wasn't what he expected.**
He assumed I'd start with the website. I told him I wasn't going to touch it until the GBP was right. The map pack is where most local service business searches convert, especially on mobile. The profile was the first priority. We claimed and verified the listing, corrected the address, set the right primary category, uploaded 12 photos, set hours, and started asking customers for reviews in person.

**4. I was honest about the timeline.**
He asked when he'd start showing up. I told him 6 to 12 weeks before we'd see consistent improvement. Some earlier, some later, depending on how fast reviews came in and how competitive his postal codes were. I didn't promise page one. I told him what we'd be doing and what signals we were building.

**5. The baseline lesson I learned from him.**
Within 90 days he was in the local pack for his primary service and city. He told me calls had picked up noticeably. I didn't have a specific number to point to because I hadn't tracked baseline call volume before we started. I logged that as a rule for every engagement that followed: document the state of things on day one before changing anything.

**6. What I tell every new client now.**
"Before I look at your website, I need 20 minutes with your Google Business Profile. That's where your customers are making the decision to call you or your competitor." I said a version of that to him first. I still say it on every intro call.

If you're starting with a local client and not sure where to begin, start where customers make the decision to act.
