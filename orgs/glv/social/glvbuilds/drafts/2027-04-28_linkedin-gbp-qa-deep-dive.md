# 2027-04-28 — LinkedIn Text Post + IG Static
**Topic:** GBP Q&A — seeding, monitoring, and why the content is indexed
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## LinkedIn Post

GBP has a Q&A section that most business owners don't look at until someone has already posted a misleading answer.

Here's how it actually works and what to do with it.

---

**How GBP Q&A works**

Anyone with a Google account can post a question on your GBP listing. Anyone — including you — can answer it.

The most helpful answer (voted up by users) appears first. If you answer a question as the business owner, your answer is labelled as "Response from the owner." It doesn't automatically appear first unless it gets upvoted.

Anyone can also upvote an existing question. Questions with more upvotes appear higher in the Q&A section.

---

**The indexing part most people miss**

Q&A content is indexed by Google. Questions and answers on your listing can appear in Knowledge Panel results and occasionally in featured snippets.

This means the language in your Q&A answers — including service names, city names, and specific details — is crawlable content. An answer that says "Yes, we offer WSIB-covered physiotherapy in [city] — no referral needed" does more work than an answer that says "Yes, we do."

---

**Seeding your own Q&A**

You can post questions from a personal Google account (not your business account) and answer them from your business profile.

Questions worth seeding:
- "Do you offer [specific service]?"
- "Is a referral required?"
- "Do you direct-bill [plan name]?"
- "Are you accepting new patients / new clients?"
- "What are your hours during [holiday period]?"
- "Is there parking available?"

Each answer is an opportunity to use natural service+city language in your own words — without it reading like a keyword-stuffed service description.

---

**Monitoring for inaccurate answers**

The risk of open Q&A is that someone answers incorrectly — about your hours, your pricing, your services. Google doesn't notify you when a new question or answer is posted.

Set a monthly reminder: GBP dashboard → Q&A. Check for new questions, vote up your own correct answers, and flag any inaccurate answers from the public through the "Report" function.

Five minutes a month. Most businesses skip it for years.

---

**The answer format that works**

Short, direct, and natural. Start with "Yes" or "No" where possible. Include the specific service name and city in longer answers. Don't write Q&A answers like marketing copy — they read worse and get voted down by users who found them unhelpful.
