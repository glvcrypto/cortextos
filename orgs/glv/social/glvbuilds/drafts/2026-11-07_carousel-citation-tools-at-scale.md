## LinkedIn Caption

Manual citation management works fine for your first 5 directories.

After that, it becomes a liability — one wrong phone number can sit uncorrected across 40 listings for months.

Here's how I approach citations once a client's listing profile grows.

**Slide 1 — Hook**
Correcting one NAP error manually at scale takes longer than the original citation buildout.

**Slide 2 — When manual stops working**
Manual citation management is fine up to ~10 directories.
Beyond that, scale problems emerge: you can't remember which directories you've claimed, login credentials get lost, and one address change cascades across everything you touched.
The trigger to use a tool: new phone number, address change, or 20+ directories needing correction.

**Slide 3 — The three tools I compare**
1. **BrightLocal Citation Builder** — manual submissions + managed service option. Transparent, one-time cost per citation or monthly plan. Good for: clients who want audit-level reports.
2. **Whitespark Citation Finder + Managed Citations** — strong Canadian database (important for Canadian clients). Find missing citations first, then submit. Good for: local-market-specific citation strategy.
3. **Yext** — automated syndication across a large network. Ongoing subscription; if you cancel, suppressions lift and old data can resurface. Good for: enterprise or multi-location clients with budget. High cost for solo-agency single-location clients.

**Slide 4 — The 5 fields that matter**
Every tool checks these — make sure your source-of-truth doc is correct before syncing:
1. Business name (exactly as on GBP — no "& Sons" vs "and Sons" discrepancies)
2. Address (unit number format matters — "Suite 2" vs "#2" creates duplicates)
3. Phone (local number, not tracking number)
4. Website (include or exclude trailing slash consistently)
5. Primary category (match GBP primary category where directory allows it)

**Slide 5 — The monthly spot-check (no tool required)**
Once citations are clean, I run a 5-directory spot-check on the first Monday of each month:
Pick 5 directories at random. Search the business name in private browser. Compare name, address, phone, website to your GBP. Flag any discrepancy. Takes under 10 minutes.

**Slide 6 — When to use paid vs. manual**
Use paid tool when: 10+ locations, 50+ directories, or address/phone change just happened.
Use manual when: new client under 20 directories, budget is tight, or you need citation transparency (you know exactly where they're listed).
Don't use Yext for single-location small businesses unless the client has enterprise budget — the suppression dependency is a long-term risk.

I learned this the hard way on a client whose address changed when they moved locations.

We'd built citations manually across 35 directories over 18 months. When the address changed, I had no clean list of where we'd submitted. Took three weeks to track down and correct everything.

Now every engagement starts with a citation audit log — a spreadsheet listing every directory, the URL of the claimed listing, login email used, and date verified. It's a 30-minute setup that saves hours on every future update.

Tools like BrightLocal make this easier with their audit dashboard, but the underlying principle is the same: know where you're listed before you submit anywhere new.
