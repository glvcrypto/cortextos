# The Service Page Formula I Use for Every Local SEO Client

## LinkedIn Post

Most local service pages don't rank because they don't tell Google — or the reader — anything specific.

They say "Professional [Service] in [City]" and stop there.

---

Here's the formula I use for every service page rewrite:

**Title tag (under 60 characters)**
[Primary service keyword] in [City] | [Business name]

Example: "Lawn Care in Burlington | Greenfield Landscaping"
Not: "Professional, Affordable Lawn Services — We Do It All"

One keyword intent. One city. One brand. That's it.

---

**H1 (the on-page headline)**
[Service] in [City] — [Differentiator or qualifier]

Example: "Lawn Care in Burlington — Residential and Commercial"

The H1 and the title tag should reinforce the same keyword intent — but not copy each other word-for-word. The title tag is for Google's SERP. The H1 is for the reader who lands on the page.

---

**First paragraph (under 100 words)**

Four things in the opening paragraph:
1. Name the service and city in the first sentence
2. State what the service actually includes (scope — not "quality results")
3. One trust signal (years serving the area, specific brands, certifications)
4. A soft CTA (link to booking, or "call for availability")

That's it. No keyword stuffing. No five-paragraph intro. No "welcome to our website."

---

Most service page rewrites I do come down to the same three fixes:
— Title tag changed from a slogan to a keyword + location
— H1 rewritten from vague to specific
— First 100 words rewritten to include actual scope and a trust signal

The rest of the page matters — but if those three elements are wrong, the page won't rank and won't convert.
