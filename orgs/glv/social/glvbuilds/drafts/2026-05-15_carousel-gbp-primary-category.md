## LinkedIn Caption

There's one field in Google Business Profile that carries more weight on local pack rankings than anything else.

Most businesses set it during signup in 15 seconds, don't revisit it for years, and then wonder why they're not showing up for their core service in their city.

It's the primary category.

Here's what I've found, and what to do about it:

**1. Primary category is the highest-weight single field in GBP.**
Google uses it to decide which searches your profile is eligible to appear for. If your primary category is too broad, you're competing against every business in that broad category, not just the ones doing your specific service. "Contractor" puts you beside roofers, painters, and landscapers. "Plumbing Contractor" puts you beside your actual competitors for the searches that matter.

**2. Most businesses picked it at signup and never looked back.**
I ask every new client what their GBP primary category is. Fewer than one in ten can tell me without looking it up. The ones that do know almost always picked something generic during the signup flow because Google's suggestions lean broad and the field doesn't feel important at the time.

**3. The fix starts with competitor research.**
Open a private browser. Search your primary service and city. Click into the top three businesses in the local pack. Check the category displayed on their profile. What are the top-ranked competitors using specifically? That's your research baseline.

**4. Then check Google's full category list.**
GBP has several hundred categories, and new ones get added regularly. The specific category you need may exist and be more precise than you realised. "HVAC Contractor" and "Air Conditioning Contractor" are two different categories with different implications depending on your service mix.

**5. The change takes 30 seconds.**
GBP Manager → Edit profile → Business information → Category. Change the primary category. Leave accurate secondary categories in place. You don't need to remove old secondaries when adding a better primary.

**6. Give it 30 days before drawing conclusions.**
Category changes take time to register in local search. I've seen movement in as little as two weeks. I've also seen it take six weeks. Set a calendar reminder and check your map pack visibility before and after using a private browser or a rank grid tool.

Primary category is the first thing I change on almost every new local client audit. It's free, takes under a minute, and it's the highest-leverage single field in the profile.
