## LinkedIn Caption

The GBP business description field gets 750 characters and most businesses use it like a legal disclaimer.

Here's the framework I use when I write or rewrite a client's description.

**Lead with service + location in sentence one.**

The first sentence should tell Google and the reader what you do and where you do it. "[Service] in [City] and the surrounding area" is enough for the signal. Everything after expands on it.

**No promotional language, phone numbers, or URLs.**

Google's guidelines explicitly prohibit promotional content (slogans, taglines with superlatives) and contact information in the description. Google can reject or suppress descriptions that violate these guidelines. I've seen descriptions silently stripped from profiles with no notification to the business owner.

**Three elements, in order:**

1. Primary services — what you actually do, written plainly
2. Service area — city, region, or "serving [City] and surrounding communities"
3. One trust signal — years in operation, a specific specialisation, a relevant accreditation

That's the 750 characters well spent. Everything else is noise.

**What to avoid:**

Keyword stuffing reads as spam to both Google and real readers. Copy-pasted homepage text is often too long, too broad, and ignores the character limit. Vague superlatives ("best," "top-rated," "leading") add no information.

Write it like it's the first three lines of your About page — because on mobile search, that's exactly how it appears when a searcher taps your profile.
