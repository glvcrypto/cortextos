## Slide 2

**Why these two images matter more than the rest**

Your logo and cover photo are the first visual impression a searcher gets — before they read your name, your reviews, or your hours.

The cover photo displays as the banner across the top of your GBP profile. It also appears in the knowledge panel on desktop. The logo appears in the circular thumbnail beside your business name in local pack results and on Maps.

Neither is chosen automatically from your photo uploads. You set them explicitly.

---

## Slide 3

**Cover photo specifications**

Google's minimum: 480 × 270px, 16:9 ratio.

What I recommend: 1080 × 608px minimum — so it renders sharply on all screen sizes.

Format: JPG or PNG, under 5MB.

What works: an on-location action shot that shows your team, your service environment, or your product. Avoid: stock imagery, heavy text overlays, or graphic-only images. Google may override your selection with a user-uploaded photo if yours scores poorly on engagement.

---

## Slide 4

**Logo specifications**

Google's minimum: 250 × 250px square, but Google displays it as a circle.

What I recommend: 500 × 500px minimum, with your logo centred with clear space on all sides so nothing is clipped when cropped to circle.

Format: JPG or PNG, under 5MB.

Check: search your business name in Maps and look at the circular thumbnail. If your wordmark is getting clipped or your icon is off-centre — update the logo file.

---

## Slide 5

**The Google override problem**

Google can select a "customer-chosen" cover photo for your listing — chosen by Google's algorithm based on user engagement — and display it instead of your uploaded cover photo.

You cannot fully prevent this. But you can reduce the likelihood: upload a high-quality, on-location cover photo that performs better on engagement than generic stock photos. Blurry, low-resolution, or highly promotional images are more likely to get overridden.

Check your listing in a private browser periodically. If the displayed cover photo isn't yours, you can flag photos via GBP Manager → Photos → Customer photos.

---

## Slide 6

**The audit (takes 5 minutes)**

1. Open GBP Manager → Photos → By owner
2. Confirm your cover photo and logo are set — not just uploaded to general photos
3. Open a private browser and search your business name on Google Maps — check how your logo thumbnail appears in the list view
4. On desktop, search your business name and inspect the knowledge panel — check the cover photo banner
5. On mobile, do the same — the cover photo displays slightly differently (taller crop)
