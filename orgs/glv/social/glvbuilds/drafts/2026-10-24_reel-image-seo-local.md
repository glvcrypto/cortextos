## LinkedIn caption

Most businesses upload images straight from their phone or camera app. The filename is something like "DSC04512.jpg" and the alt text field is left blank.

That's a missed SEO opportunity, four times over.

**The 4-step image prep routine I run before every upload:**

1. **Rename the file** — the filename is an on-page SEO signal. Before uploading, rename it to describe the service and location: "boiler-installation-edmonton.webp". Not every image needs a different name, but every service-area photo, before-and-after shot, or team-on-site image should have a descriptive name that includes the service type.

2. **Write alt text** — alt text is the text Google uses to understand what an image shows, and the fallback text displayed when an image fails to load. Write one descriptive sentence: "technician installing boiler in Edmonton basement." Avoid keyword stuffing. Avoid "image of" as a prefix. Avoid leaving it blank, which tells Google nothing and excludes your images from Google Image search.

3. **Compress to under 100KB** — Squoosh is free, browser-based, and takes 30 seconds. Select WebP format and drag the quality slider until you hit 100KB or below. Images uploaded at 1–3MB directly increase page load time, which affects Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) — one of Google's Core Web Vitals ranking factors.

4. **Use WebP format** — WebP files are typically 25–50% smaller than JPEG at equivalent visual quality. All major browsers support it. Most WordPress installations handle it natively now. Make it the default format for all new image uploads.

**Bonus for GBP uploads specifically:**
Geo-tag the image file with GPS coordinates matching your service area before uploading to GBP. Some GBP management platforms do this automatically. For manual uploads, a free tool like GeoImgr adds the coordinates directly to the image metadata.

None of these steps take more than two minutes per image once the habit is set. File naming and alt text are the two most common gaps I find when inheriting a client site that's been live for a few years.
