## Slide 2 — Step 1: Primary Category First

**Headline:** Step 1: Choose the right primary category.

**Body:**
This is the single most important decision on the entire profile.
It controls which searches trigger your listing.
"Marine Repair Shop" ranks for different searches than "Boat Repair Shop."
Research 3–4 options. Pick the one with the highest local search volume for your market.

## Slide 3 — Step 2: Storefront vs. Service Area Business

**Headline:** Step 2: Storefront or service area? Get this right.

**Body:**
If customers come to you → show your address.
If you go to customers → hide your address and set a service area.
Mixing these up creates ranking problems Google never explains clearly.
A plumber showing a residential address gets flagged. A dealership hiding its address loses map pack visibility.

*Visual: Two-column comparison — storefront vs. SAB*

## Slide 4 — Step 3: Services + Products with Keywords

**Headline:** Step 3: Fill every service field. Use real keywords.

**Body:**
Don't just list "Repair." Write "Small Engine Repair — lawn mowers, snow blowers, chainsaws."
Each service description is indexed.
Products work the same way — keyword-rich titles and descriptions.
Most businesses leave these blank or write one word.

*Visual: Services section filled in with example descriptions*

## Slide 5 — Step 4: 10 Photos at Launch Minimum

**Headline:** Step 4: Load 10 photos before you ask for a single review.

**Body:**
Google profiles with more photos get more views — consistently.
At a minimum at launch: exterior (2), interior (2), team (2), at-work/service (2), equipment or products (2).
The "At Work" category is the one most businesses skip (covered in a previous post).
Geo-tag your photos before uploading if the business is location-dependent.

*Visual: Photo category checklist*

## Slide 6 — Step 5: Seed 3 Q&As Yourself

**Headline:** Step 5: Don't wait for customers to ask questions. Ask them yourself.

**Body:**
The Q&A section is public and indexed.
Seed it with 3–5 questions customers actually ask — and answer them with natural keywords.
"Do you offer same-day service?" → "Yes, we offer same-day service for most small engine repairs in [city]."
Set a Google alert for new questions so nothing goes unanswered.

## Slide 7 — Step 6 + CTA

**Headline:** Step 6: Post your first GBP update within 48 hours.

**Body:**
An active profile outranks an inactive one with better reviews. Every time.
Post a service update, a photo, or a seasonal offer within two days of verification.
Then keep posting — weekly if possible, monthly at minimum.
