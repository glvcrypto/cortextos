## LinkedIn caption

Every statutory holiday, some businesses show up on Google as "hours might differ" or outright "closed" — not because they are closed, but because they never set special hours.

GBP special hours override your regular hours for specific dates. Google uses them to display real-time open/closed status in search results and Maps.

If you don't set them, Google flags the status as uncertain. Customers searching at 9am on a holiday Monday see an ambiguous listing. Some don't call. Some call a competitor who looks open.

The fix takes about five minutes in GBP Manager:

Business Information → Hours → Special hours → Add date → Set hours (or mark closed)

You can add the whole year in one session. For Canadian businesses, that's 9–13 dates depending on your province (federal statutory + provincial-specific).

A few things to get right:

- If you're actually open on a holiday, set the correct hours — don't leave it blank
- If you close early, set reduced hours rather than marking fully closed
- Check your listing after each update — GBP sometimes reverts changes if there's a verification issue

This is one of the fastest GBP wins I do for every new client in session one. It's not glamorous, but it prevents good leads from bouncing to a competitor who has their hours right.
