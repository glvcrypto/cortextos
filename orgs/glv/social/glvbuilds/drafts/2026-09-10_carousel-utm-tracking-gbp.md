# UTM Tracking for GBP — Know Exactly What Your Profile Is Sending

## Slide 1 — Hook

I track exactly how many website visits come from Google Business Profile for every client I manage.

One UTM setup in the GBP website field gives you the full picture.

---

## Slide 2 — What UTM Parameters Are

UTM parameters are tags you add to the end of a URL.

When someone clicks a tagged link, GA4 records where the click came from.

Without UTM tags on your GBP website link, GA4 lumps GBP clicks into "Direct" traffic — invisible and unmeasurable.

With UTM tags: you see exactly how many sessions, leads, and calls started from your Google Business Profile.

---

## Slide 3 — The Parameters I Use

Standard GBP UTM setup:

```
?utm_source=google&utm_medium=gmb&utm_campaign=gbp
```

Add this to the end of your website URL in GBP.

So if your website is `yourbusiness.com`, the GBP website field becomes:
`yourbusiness.com/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=gmb&utm_campaign=gbp`

---

## Slide 4 — Where to Add It

1. Log into your Google Business Profile
2. Click "Edit profile"
3. Find the "Website" field
4. Replace the plain URL with your UTM-tagged URL
5. Save

That's the full setup. Takes under two minutes.

---

## Slide 5 — How to Read It in GA4

In GA4:
— Go to Reports → Acquisition → Traffic acquisition
— Filter by Session medium: `gmb`
— Or filter by Session campaign: `gbp`

You'll see: sessions, engaged sessions, conversions, and revenue attributed directly to GBP clicks.

---

## Slide 6 — What You'll Learn

After 30–60 days of data:

— What percentage of website traffic comes from GBP
— Which pages GBP visitors land on (service pages, homepage, contact)
— Whether GBP visitors convert at a different rate than organic search visitors
— Which months show GBP traffic spikes (seasonal demand signal)

This data tells you whether your GBP investment is working — and where to focus next.
