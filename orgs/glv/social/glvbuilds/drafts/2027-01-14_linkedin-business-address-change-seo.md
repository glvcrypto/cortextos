# Business Address Change — The Local SEO Protocol Most Businesses Skip

## LinkedIn Caption (~450 words)

A client told me last week they're moving locations in 60 days.

My first response wasn't "congratulations." It was: "We need to start updating your SEO before the moving truck shows up."

Moving a business address is one of the most underestimated SEO events for a local business. It touches every layer of how Google knows where you are.

Here's what needs to happen, in order.

**Start 30 days before the move.**

Don't wait until moving day. Citations — the directories that list your name, address, and phone number — take weeks to crawl and update across the web. If your old address is still indexed in Google's eyes when the move happens, you're creating a NAP conflict that can take months to sort out.

Run a citation audit (BrightLocal or Whitespark work for Canadian businesses) before you do anything else. Log every directory that has the old address. Update them all in one batch.

**Update the website the day of the move — not before.**

Contact page, footer, and LocalBusiness schema address field all need to match the new address exactly. Wait until you're physically in the new location before making the change. If you update the website early and then something delays the move, you've created a different NAP conflict.

**GBP: update address the day of the move. Expect re-verification.**

Google will likely send a postcard or request a video verification after an address change on an established listing. This is normal. Don't delete the listing and create a new one — you will lose every review and every month of profile history.

Change the address in GBP Manager, go through verification, and flag the re-verification as a pending task so it doesn't get missed.

**GSC: nothing to change.**

Google Search Console tracks your domain, not your address. No property changes needed if the domain isn't changing.

**Check the GBP website field UTM tags** still point to the correct landing page after the move. This is easy to miss.

The one thing I tell every client before a move: the address in GBP, on your website, in your LocalBusiness schema, and across all your citations must all match exactly — down to suite number format and whether "Street" is abbreviated. A mismatch between any two of these creates a signal Google can't fully trust.

Thirty days of lead time prevents most of the problems.

If you've moved a business location and kept the local rankings intact, I'd like to know what you did first.

## Instagram Caption (~110 words)

Moving your business location is one of the most underestimated SEO events for a local business.

It touches everything: GBP, citations, website schema, LocalBusiness structured data.

The protocol, in order:

→ 30 days before: run a citation audit, update every directory in one batch
→ Day of move: update GBP address (expect re-verification — do not delete and recreate)
→ Day of move: update website contact page, footer, and LocalBusiness schema
→ GSC: nothing changes — it tracks domain, not address

The mistake: waiting until after the move to start. By then, the old address is already indexed everywhere.
