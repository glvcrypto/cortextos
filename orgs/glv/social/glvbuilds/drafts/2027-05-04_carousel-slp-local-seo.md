## Slide 2 — GBP Category

"Speech Pathologist" is the correct Google Business Profile primary category for most private S-LP clinics in Canada. "Speech Therapist" is common US terminology; Google's category library uses "Speech Pathologist" as the primary term in the Canadian index.

If your clinic also offers augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) assessment, "Speech Pathologist" still applies — AAC is within S-LP scope, not a separate category. If practitioners also hold RD (Registered Dietitian) or OT credentials and your clinic is truly multidisciplinary, add the appropriate secondary categories.

Setting the wrong primary category is the most common GBP error for S-LP clinics — and it suppresses appearance in local filters.

## Slide 3 — Per-Condition Pages

"Speech therapy for toddlers [city]." "Stuttering treatment [city]." "Apraxia of speech therapy [city]." "AAC assessment [city]." "Adult stroke speech therapy [city]."

Each of these is a separate search made by a caregiver or patient with a specific situation. One "services" page listing all conditions ranks for none of them specifically.

Per-condition pages — written in the language caregivers and patients actually use, with information on what the assessment looks like, how many sessions are typically required, and what to expect — convert at a higher rate than a general clinic overview.

---

## Slide 4 — S-LP Bio Pages

Each registered S-LP at your clinic is a separate ranking asset.

A named bio page — Registered Speech-Language Pathologist (S-LP or SLP), provincial registration number (CASLPO in Ontario, CSHBC in British Columbia, SAA in Saskatchewan and Nova Scotia), areas of specialisation (fluency, voice, acquired neurological, pediatric language, AAC), languages spoken, real headshot — creates one search opportunity per practitioner.

A "meet the team" grid listing six S-LPs gives you one page where there could be six. Caregivers searching for a bilingual S-LP or a practitioner specialising in adult aphasia will not find a grid entry in the SERPs. They will find a named bio page.

---

## Slide 5 — OHIP Billing Clarification

OHIP covers S-LP services delivered in hospital settings. It does not cover private clinic S-LP services. Most private clinic patients pay out-of-pocket or via extended health benefits.

Your booking page needs to address this directly: which extended health plans the clinic direct-bills, what the out-of-pocket session rate is (or "call for current rates" if you prefer not to publish), whether a referral is required (it is not, in most provinces for private S-LP), and whether school-based or community funding programs are accepted.

Leaving this unclear loses patients who would have booked had they known their coverage applied.

---

## Slide 6 — Wait Time and Intake

Two questions every patient or caregiver has before booking an S-LP: how long is the wait, and what does the first appointment involve?

Most S-LP clinic websites answer neither clearly.

A current wait time — even a range or "call for current availability" with a visible phone number — signals that the clinic is operational and actively taking new patients. A brief intake overview (initial assessment session, standardised tools used, report provided, recommendation timeline) removes uncertainty and builds trust before the call.
