# 2027-05-15 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Local SEO for independent pharmacies — compounding, pharmacist bios, prescription transfer page
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Independent pharmacies have three local SEO assets that no chain pharmacy can compete with. Most don't use any of them."

---

**Beat 1 — Compounding page:**
"'Compounding pharmacy [city].' 'Custom medication [city].' 'Pet compounding pharmacy [city].' Compounding — the preparation of custom medications, dosage forms, or formulations not commercially available — is something chain pharmacies cannot offer. It's a category where an independent pharmacy has zero chain competition in local search, if they have a page for it. A compounding page that explains what compounding is, what conditions it addresses, what a prescription transfer looks like, and whether the pharmacy compounds for veterinary use — covers the searches that chains cannot rank for and the questions an independent can answer without a national competitor showing up in the same result."

---

**Beat 2 — Pharmacist bio pages:**
"Each registered pharmacist at an independent pharmacy is a separate local SEO asset. A named bio page — Registered Pharmacist, provincial registration number (OCP in Ontario, CPSBC in British Columbia), languages spoken, areas of focus (diabetes management, travel health, compounding, medication reviews) — creates a searchable page that a 'staff' section never will. In markets with large newcomer populations, language is often the primary selection criterion for a pharmacist. A bilingual pharmacist with a named bio page in a second language is a distinct ranking opportunity. A name in a grid is not."

---

**Beat 3 — Prescription transfer page:**
"'Transfer prescription to [pharmacy name].' 'Switch my pharmacy [city].' These searches are made by people who have already made the decision to change pharmacies. They are looking for confirmation that the process is easy and for the specific steps to do it. A prescription transfer page — what's needed, how long it takes, whether the pharmacy contacts the old pharmacy on behalf of the patient, and a phone number or form to start — converts at a higher rate than any other page on the pharmacy website. It exists to reduce friction for someone who is already ready to switch. Most independent pharmacy sites don't have one."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Compounding page where chains can't compete. One bio per pharmacist. A prescription transfer page for the patient who's already decided to switch."

---
