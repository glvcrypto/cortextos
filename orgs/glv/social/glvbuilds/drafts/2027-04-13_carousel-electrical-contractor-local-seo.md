## Slide 3 — Gap 2: Provincial Licence on Every Service Page

**Licence numbers belong on your service pages — not buried in About.**

- Ontario: ESA contractor licence + ECRA/TECA number
- BC: BC Safety Authority electrical licence
- Alberta: APECB electrical contractor certificate
- Red Seal Journeyperson endorsement where applicable

Google quality raters check these signals for high-stakes trades queries.

One line per tech bio page. One line in the GBP description.

## LinkedIn Caption

I've audited local SEO for electrical contractors in three provinces.

Every account appeared when someone searched the company name. Almost none appeared for "licensed electrician [city]" or "EV charger installation [city]" — the searches that run when there's a real job to quote.

Here are the five gaps I find on nearly every account.

**1. GBP category precision**

"Electrician," "Electrical Contractor," and "Commercial Electrician" are distinct Google categories with different search filter behaviour. Check what the top 3 map pack competitors in your city actually use — open a private browser, search your category in Google Maps, and click the top three listings. Most contractors default to "Electrician" at signup and never change it. If you do commercial work, you're leaving a category filter unset.

**2. Provincial licence numbers on every service page**

Google quality raters check for these on high-stakes trades queries. Not buried in About — on the service page itself, on individual technician bio pages, in the GBP description.

- Ontario: ESA contractor licence + ECRA/TECA number
- BC: BC Safety Authority electrical licence
- Alberta: APECB electrical contractor certificate
- Red Seal Journeyperson endorsement where held

One line is enough. It confirms Google's E-E-A-T signals without any copywriting.

**3. EV charger installation page**

"EV charger installation [city]" is a growing search category with lower competition than general electrical services in most Canadian cities right now. The window is closing — most markets still have early-mover opportunity. One dedicated page: 300–400 words covering the scope, provincial permit process (required in most provinces for Level 2 installation), and panel upgrade note. Add a matching GBP service entry.

**4. Emergency electrical page**

"Emergency electrician [city]" and "breaker keeps tripping [city]" are mobile, urgency-driven searches. The buyer needs a phone number in the first 10 seconds. Phone above fold, hours visible without scrolling, GBP Q&A seed answering whether you offer emergency service.

**5. Service area setup for home-based operations**

Most residential electrical contractors don't run from a commercial address. If that's you: don't display your home address in GBP. Set service areas by city instead. Google uses your verified address for proximity ranking internally — customers never see it. Set service areas to where you actually take jobs, not cities you'd like to rank in. Google cross-references this against your actual reviews and service page content.
