**Topic:** Florist local SEO — seasonal event search intent and Google Merchant Center product listings

## Script

**[Hook — text on screen]**
Florists have one of the most predictable search intent spikes of any local business. Most aren't set up to capture them.

**[Cut — on screen]**
Search intent for florists clusters around events: Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, graduations, weddings, funerals. These don't spread evenly — they spike hard and drop fast.

**[Step 1 — on screen]**
Event-specific pages. "Mother's Day flowers [city]" is a separate page from "wedding flowers [city]." Build each page 6–8 weeks before the event. GBP Offer post with an end date goes up 5 days out. Event pages built after the spike hit are too late.

**[Step 2 — on screen]**
Google Merchant Center product listings. If you sell arrangements with consistent pricing, you're eligible for Product Listings in Google Shopping. What customers see: your arrangement, your price, "Available nearby." The minimum: product name, price, image, availability, and store code. Update daily — stale inventory data suspends the programme.

**[Step 3 — on screen]**
Review timing. The best ask is immediately after delivery — before the flowers wilt and before the event memory fades. Prompt: "mention the occasion if you're comfortable." Event-based review text ("sent for a wedding") becomes justification text in local pack results.

**[Payoff — on screen]**
Most florists are set up for "flower shop near me." The ones ranking at Mother's Day built the event pages and Merchant Center listings months before.

**[CTA — on screen]**
Mother's Day is about 8 weeks away. If that page isn't live yet, this week is the window.
