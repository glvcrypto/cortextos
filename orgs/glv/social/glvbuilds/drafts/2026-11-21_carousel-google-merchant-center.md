## Slide 2 — What Google Merchant Center is

**Merchant Center is the platform that powers Google Shopping results — and the free tier exists.**

Two tiers:
- **Free organic listings** — product appears in Shopping tab + sometimes in main search results, no ad spend required
- **Paid Shopping ads** — pay-per-click product ads appearing at the top of search and Shopping tab

Most retailers know about the paid ads. Almost none have claimed the free listings.

---

## Slide 3 — Free local inventory listings

**When someone searches for a product "near me," Merchant Center can show your in-store stock — for free.**

Google surfaces local inventory in Shopping results when a product is available nearby. This means a customer searching "[product] near me" or "[product] in [city]" can see that your store has it in stock before they visit.

No ad spend. No ongoing cost. Just a product feed Google can read.

---

## Slide 4 — Step 1 and 2: Account and GBP link

**Step 1: Create a Merchant Center account** at merchant.google.com. Use the same Google account as your GBP profile.

**Step 2: Link your GBP profile.** In Merchant Center → Business information → Linked accounts → add GBP. This is what connects your physical store location to your product listings.

Without the GBP link, your free listings won't appear in local results.

---

## Slide 5 — Step 3 and 4: Website verify and product feed

**Step 3: Verify and claim your website.** Merchant Center needs to confirm you own the domain. Easiest method: add a meta tag to your site header, or use Google Tag Manager.

**Step 4: Upload a product feed.** A feed is a file listing your products: item ID, title, description, price, availability, condition, image link.

Options:
- Manual entry (small catalogues)
- Spreadsheet upload
- WooCommerce, Shopify, and most e-commerce platforms have a Merchant Center feed plugin — often a 1-hour setup

---

## Slide 6 — What to include in each product listing

**Five fields Google needs to surface your products well:**

1. **Title** — specific: brand + model + size/colour if relevant (not just "blue shirt")
2. **Description** — what it is, who it's for, key specs
3. **Price** — must be accurate and match your website
4. **Availability** — in stock / out of stock / preorder
5. **Image** — clean product photo on white or plain background; no text overlays

Incomplete or mismatched listings get disapproved. Price on your site must match price in the feed.

---

## Slide 7 — Who this is for

**This is for retailers with physical in-store inventory. Not service businesses.**

Merchant Center is built for product-based businesses: hardware stores, marine dealerships, equipment retailers, apparel, sporting goods, home goods.

It is not designed for service businesses that sell labour or expertise rather than products you can ship or hand across a counter. If you run a service business and aren't sure whether this applies — it probably doesn't.
