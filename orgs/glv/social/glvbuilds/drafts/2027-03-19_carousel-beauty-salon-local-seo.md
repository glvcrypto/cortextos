## LinkedIn Caption

A hair salon, a chair rental, and a solo aesthetician can all register a GBP listing under "Beauty Salon." That doesn't mean they should have the same local SEO setup.

Beauty and personal care is one of the most fragmented local service categories on Google. People aren't searching "beauty salon near me" when they want a balayage specialist. They search for the service — or for the practitioner by name.

Here's how the strategy changes by business model:

**Chair rental vs. owner-operated**
If you own the salon: GBP under the salon name, all stylists linked, one profile with authority. If you rent a chair: your own GBP as a "Hair Stylist," "Nail Technician," or "Aesthetician" — whichever matches your primary service. Most chair renters I've audited are invisible in local search. They have no individual GBP and appear under the salon's umbrella when clients search their name directly.

**Category precision**
"Beauty Salon" is the default choice. But Google recognises Hair Salon, Nail Salon, Eyebrow Bar, Waxing Hair Removal Service, Tanning Salon, Skin Care Clinic, and Eyelash Bar as distinct categories. Each has different filter and search behaviour. Set your primary category to your highest-revenue service. Check what the top three competitors in your area are using.

**GBP attributes**
Beauty profiles have more applicable attributes than most business types: Women-owned, NB-friendly, By appointment only vs. Walk-ins welcome, Wi-Fi, parking, payment options. Fill every applicable attribute. Customers filter by these on Google Maps. A missing attribute makes you invisible in filter-based searches.

**Stylist and practitioner bio pages**
Each stylist is a separate local SEO asset: name + city + specialty. "Balayage specialist [city]," "Brazilian blowout [city]," "lash extensions [city]" — one dedicated page per practitioner per specialty. 300 words, the practitioner's name, a real headshot, and their booking link. Most salons have one team page. That's one ranking opportunity where there could be ten.

**Review response timing**
Service disagreements in beauty happen. A low rating from a mismatched expectation is an honest review — Google won't remove it. But a professional public response within 24 hours, acknowledging the concern and offering a correction, restores trust in search results. Future customers read every owner response.
