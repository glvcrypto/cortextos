## LinkedIn Post

I audited a client's blog last month using only GSC. Found 14 posts ranking between position 11 and 25 with real impressions. Six of them had the same problem: the title tag didn't match the query Google was associating with them.

That's a title tag fix. Not a rewrite. Not a new post. A single-line change that often moves a post from page 2 to page 1 in two to three weeks.

Here's the four-step GSC blog audit:

**1. Filter to blog URLs only**
GSC → Performance → Pages. Filter by URL containing "/blog/" (or your blog's path). Sort by Average Position. You want posts in the 11–25 band with 50+ impressions in the last 28 days.

**2. Click each page → Queries tab**
This shows which queries Google associates with that post. Compare the top query to your actual title tag and H1. If they don't match — or if the post's H1 is vague and the query is specific — you've found the fix.

**3. Decide: refresh title tag or write a new page**
If the post covers the topic but has the wrong headline: fix the title tag and H1, re-request indexing in GSC URL Inspection. If GSC shows intent the post doesn't actually address: add it to your new-page list. Don't retrofit a post that was written for a different purpose.

**4. Consolidation signal**
If two or more posts are ranking for the same query, pick the stronger one (higher impressions + position), 301-redirect the weaker one into it, then tighten the winner with a stronger title tag and one internal link from a well-ranked page. Same principle as keyword cannibalisation for service pages — different content type, same fix.

Takes about an hour per 30-post blog. The posts you find in the 11–25 band are already indexed, already have impressions — they just need a signal tighten before you start writing anything new.
