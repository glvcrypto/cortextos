## Slide 2 — Why Competitor GBP Analysis First?

**Headline:** You don't need to outrank the internet. You need to outrank the three businesses in your local pack.

**Body:**
The local pack shows three results.
Your client is competing with two or three specific businesses — not the entire web.
Before I build a strategy, I want to know the exact gap between my client's profile and the profiles that are outranking them.
Is the gap a review count problem? A completeness problem? A recency problem?
Knowing that narrows the first 30 days from "everything" to "the three things that actually matter."

*Visual: Local pack search result showing 3 listings — fictional businesses*

## Slide 3 — Check 1: Review Count and Velocity

**Headline:** Check 1: How many reviews do they have — and when was the last one?

**Body:**
Open a Google Maps search for your client's service + city.
Open the top 3 competitors' profiles.
Note:
→ Total review count for each
→ Date of the most recent review
→ Average rating (4.5+ vs below 4.0)

Review velocity matters as much as total count. A competitor with 120 reviews, the most recent from 8 months ago, is a different threat than a competitor with 40 reviews, all in the past 3 months.

Low velocity = gap you can close in 60 days with a consistent ask system.

*Visual: Google Maps business profile showing reviews section — fictional business*

---

## Slide 4 — Check 2: Photo Count and Recency

**Headline:** Check 2: How many photos do they have — and what category?

**Body:**
Click into each competitor's Photos section.
Note:
→ Total photo count
→ Date of the most recent photo
→ Whether they have "At Work" photos (the category most businesses skip — covered in an earlier post)

A competitor with 15 photos, all from 2 years ago, has a gap you can close in a single afternoon.
A competitor with 200 recent photos in every category is a harder visual signal to beat short-term — plan for it.

Bonus check: are photos geo-tagged? Photos uploaded from a phone retain GPS metadata, which Google uses. Most businesses never think about this.

*Visual: GBP Photos section showing categories — fictional business*

## Slide 5 — Check 3: GBP Completeness

**Headline:** Check 3: How complete is their profile — services, Q&A, business description?

**Body:**
Open each competitor's profile and check:
→ **Services section**: are individual services listed with descriptions, or is it blank?
→ **Q&A section**: have they seeded questions — or is it empty?
→ **Business description**: does it include service + city keywords, or is it generic?
→ **Attributes section**: filled in, or blank? (Accessibility, payment methods, service delivery)

In most local markets, at least one of the top 3 competitors has a half-filled profile.
A half-filled profile is a gap. A gap is a priority.

## Slide 6 — Building the Gap Opportunity List

**Headline:** Turn the 3 checks into a priority list for the first 30 days.

**Body:**
Score each top competitor across the 3 checks:

| Competitor | Reviews (count + velocity) | Photos (count + recency) | Completeness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Competitor A | 140 / 3 months ago | 22 / 8 months ago | Services: no. Q&A: no |
| Competitor B | 38 / 2 weeks ago | 8 / last month | Services: yes. Q&A: no |
| Competitor C | 67 / 5 months ago | 95 / recent | Services: yes. Q&A: yes |

Where your client's gap is smallest against the weakest competitor — that's the 30-day target.
Example: if Competitors A and C have weak review velocity, a review ask system is the first deliverable.

## Slide 7 — The Insight That Changes the Strategy

**Headline:** Most local businesses are not losing to competitors who are doing everything right. They're losing to competitors who did a few things better.

**Body:**
In the majority of local SEO audits I run, the businesses ranking in the top 3 are not technically excellent.
They have more reviews.
Or more recent photos.
Or a more complete services section.
One of those three things, done consistently, is usually the difference.

That changes the strategy. Instead of a 6-month technical overhaul, you have a 60-day momentum play:
1. Fill the completeness gaps
2. Build a review ask system
3. Add recent photos on a cadence

That's the gap your competitor created. That's where you start.
