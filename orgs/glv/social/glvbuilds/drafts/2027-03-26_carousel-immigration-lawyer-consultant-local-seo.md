## Slide 2 — Designation in GBP and Bio Pages

RCIC (Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant) is a protected title. Registration is held by the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (CICC).

Immigration lawyers are registered with their provincial law society.

Your designation, registration number, and regulatory body — in your GBP description and on individual bio pages — is a trust signal for a high-stakes service where clients are actively screening for credentials. Most profiles omit it.

---

## Slide 3 — Service-Specific Landing Pages

"Study permit [city]," "work permit [city]," "permanent residency application [city]," "spousal sponsorship [city]" — each is a different search intent with different urgency and different eligibility criteria.

One "immigration services" page competes against everything and ranks for nothing specific.

Build one page per visa or permit category you handle. 300–400 words, eligibility overview, your contact CTA, your designation prominently placed.

---

## Slide 4 — Language-Specific Pages

Immigration clients often search in their first language — or specifically for practitioners who speak their language.

"Immigration consultant [city] Punjabi," "immigration lawyer [city] Mandarin" — these are low-competition, high-intent queries. A page per language served captures them. A bullet point in your footer doesn't rank.

Page: language name in the title tag, your name + designation, a brief note on language capability, the same service structure as your English pages.

---

## Slide 5 — Citation Consistency with Directories

RCIC registrants appear in the CICC public register. Immigration lawyers appear in provincial law society directories.

Both are high-authority citation sources. The name and address in those directories must match your GBP exactly — same spelling, same address format, same phone number.

A mismatch between the CICC register and your GBP is a local SEO NAP inconsistency on one of your highest-trust citations.

---

## Slide 6 — Review Collection in a Privacy-Sensitive Context

Immigration clients are often cautious about leaving reviews that identify their visa type or nationality.

When asking, prompt: "A general note about the consultation process is helpful — you don't need to mention your visa type or personal details."

Some clients will. Many won't. Ask consistently and make it easy for those who are willing.
