## Slide 2

**What are GBP justifications?**

Justifications are small text snippets Google surfaces under your listing in the local pack. They appear between your star rating and your website link.

They tell searchers why your listing is relevant to their query — before they click.

Examples:
- "From their website: furnace repair in Calgary"
- "In reviews: replaced our hot water tank same-day"
- "✓ Boiler installation" (from your Services tab)

---

## Slide 3

**The 4 types you can influence**

1. **Website** — "From their website: [phrase]" — pulled from indexed page copy
2. **Reviews** — "In reviews: [phrase]" — pulled from customer review text
3. **Services** — checkmark-style, pulled from your GBP Services tab entries
4. **Posts** — occasionally pulled from recent GBP updates

---

## Slide 4

**How to check what you're triggering**

Open an incognito/private browser window.
Search: [your service] [your city].
Find your listing in the local pack.
Look at the line directly below your star rating and review count.

Note what justification shows — and note what your top 2–3 competitors are triggering.

The gap between their justifications and yours is your starting point.

## Slide 5

**Engineering website justifications**

The phrase must appear verbatim (or near-verbatim) on an indexed page.

Add your service + city phrase to:
- H1
- First body paragraph
- Meta description

One focused service page = one justification opportunity.

A homepage that lists every service you offer rarely triggers specific justifications for any single query. Dedicated service pages do.

---

## Slide 6

**Engineering review justifications**

You can influence this at the point of asking for a review.

Prompt customers to mention the specific service: "Could you mention that we [specific service] in your review?" — not just "leave us a Google review."

"Great company, 5 stars" → no justification.
"They replaced our boiler in Etobicoke same day" → justification candidate.

The more specific and service-focused the review language, the higher the chance Google uses it.

## LinkedIn caption

GBP justifications are one of the least-talked-about levers in local pack click-through rate.

When someone searches "[service] [city]" and your listing shows a snippet like "From their website: furnace repair in Calgary" right under your star rating — that snippet is a justification. Google is surfacing it to tell searchers exactly why your listing is relevant to their query.

Justifications don't just affect visibility. They affect click-through. A listing with a sharp, on-topic justification stands out from three generic listing cards showing only a name, a rating, and a business category.

**The 4 types you can influence:**

1. **Website justifications** — Google pulls a phrase from your indexed pages. The phrase must match the query closely. Add your service + city combination to your H1, first paragraph, and meta description. One service page = one justification opportunity. A homepage listing every service you offer rarely triggers specific justifications for any single query.

2. **Review justifications** — Google pulls language from customer reviews. You influence this by prompting customers to name the specific service in their review. "Great company" doesn't trigger a justification. "They replaced our boiler in Etobicoke same-day" might.

3. **Services tab justifications** — If you've filled out GBP Services, Google sometimes surfaces them as checkmark-style justifications ("✓ Furnace replacement"). This is often the easiest win: fill out your Services tab completely, with a short description for each service.

4. **GBP post justifications** — Recent GBP updates can contribute. Less reliable than the first three, but another reason to maintain a consistent posting cadence.

**How to audit where you stand:**

Open a private browser window. Search for your top service + city combination. Look at the line directly under your star rating — that's where justifications appear. Note which type shows (if any), and whether it aligns with your main target keyword.

Then check your top 2–3 competitors. What justifications are they triggering? That gap between their justification coverage and yours is your starting point.
