## Body (3–26s)

**Step 1 (3–9s)**
Open the page. Right-click → View Page Source.
Ctrl+F: search for `<title`.
Does the title tag have the service name AND the city?
"Lawn Mower Repair | Sault Ste. Marie" = pass.
"Home | Smith's Shop" = fail.

**Step 2 (9–17s)**
Back to the page. Find the H1.
It should match the intent of the title tag.
"Lawn Mower Repair in Sault Ste. Marie" = pass.
"Welcome to Our Shop" = fail.
Missing H1 entirely = common problem, immediate fix.

**Step 3 (17–24s)**
Read the first paragraph.
Does the city appear in the first 100 words?
Does it answer what the service is before selling the business?
Most local service pages start with "We've been serving the community for many years."
That's not what a new customer is searching for.

## LinkedIn Caption

Here's the on-page local SEO check I run before touching anything else on a client's service page.

90 seconds. Three things.

**1. Title tag: service + city.**
Right-click → View Page Source → search `<title`.
The title tag should name the specific service and the specific city. Not the business name. Not "Home."
"Lawn Mower Repair Sault Ste. Marie | Reyco Marine" = right direction.
"Welcome | Smith's Equipment" = needs work.

**2. H1: matches the title tag intent.**
The H1 should be the most prominent heading on the page — what the page is "about."
It should contain the service and the city.
If the H1 says "Our Services" or "Welcome," Google doesn't know what to rank the page for.

**3. First paragraph: city in the first 100 words, answers before it sells.**
Most local service pages open with "We've been proudly serving our community for many years."
That tells Google almost nothing about what the page is for.
The first paragraph should tell the reader (and Google) what service you offer, where you offer it, and who it's for — before anything about the business itself.

Those three fixes — title tag, H1, first paragraph — are faster than building a single backlink and more reliably effective for local service pages.
