## LinkedIn post

Every December I block off one afternoon for a conversation I don't bill for.

It's not a strategy session. It's not a quarterly review.

It's a 15-minute call with each client where I ask the same three questions:

1. What did you actually notice this year? (Not what I reported — what *they* noticed.)
2. What did you think we'd accomplish that didn't happen?
3. What's the one thing that would make next year feel successful?

The answers are almost never about rankings.

They're about:
- Getting more calls from people in the right neighbourhood
- Not having to explain what they do every time someone calls
- Feeling like their Google listing actually looks like their business

This conversation is what shapes the January plan.

The metrics report tells me what happened. These questions tell me what it meant to the person paying the invoice.

If you're running a local SEO agency — or you're a business owner working with one — have this call. It takes 15 minutes and it's worth more than the Q4 report.

What do you check in with clients about at year-end? Drop it in the comments.
