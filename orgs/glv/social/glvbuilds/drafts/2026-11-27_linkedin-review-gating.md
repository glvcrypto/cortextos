## LinkedIn Post

A client showed me the tool their last agency used to "manage" their Google reviews.

It was review gating — and it violates Google's Terms of Service.

Here's what review gating is, why it's a problem, and three compliant alternatives.

**What review gating is:**
A system that asks customers for their experience first — privately. Happy customers get sent to Google to leave a review. Unhappy customers get routed to an internal feedback form instead of Google. The goal is to prevent negative reviews from appearing publicly.

On the surface it sounds like good reputation management. Google treats it as manipulation.

**Why it violates Google's policies:**
Google's review policies prohibit "selectively soliciting reviews from customers." That phrase is the specific prohibition — you can't ask only the happy customers to post publicly while steering unhappy ones away. The policy requires that your solicitation process treats all customers the same.

If Google detects the pattern — or if a customer reports it — you can lose your GBP listing's review features, have reviews removed, or receive a warning on your profile. For some categories, it can trigger a suspension.

**Three compliant alternatives:**

1. **Ask everyone.** Send the same review request to every customer. The compliant approach is consistent, not selective. Most customers who had a neutral or even moderately negative experience won't leave a review at all — natural volume skews positive without gating.

2. **Respond to negatives publicly.** A thoughtful, professional response to a negative review often converts more trust than a perfect rating with no reviews. Potential customers read responses. A defensive or absent response tells them more than the negative review does.

3. **Build consistent volume.** Review velocity (Oct 5 post) matters more than a one-time burst. A business with 12 new reviews per month over two years will consistently outrank a business with 200 reviews from a single gated campaign three years ago.

If your agency or review tool is using a two-step "how was your experience?" pre-screen before routing to Google — that's review gating. Ask them to change the flow.
