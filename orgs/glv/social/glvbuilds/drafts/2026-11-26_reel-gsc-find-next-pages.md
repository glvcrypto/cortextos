## LinkedIn Caption (for sharing the reel)

For the first year of running my agency, I chose which local landing pages to write based on gut feel. I'd think: "We don't have a page for HVAC in [city] yet — let's write one."

GSC has a better answer.

Here's the workflow I run now before writing any new local page:

**Step 1: GSC → Performance → Queries.** Filter by a core service keyword. You're looking for queries that combine your service with a location — a city, neighbourhood, or postal code.

**Step 2: Find the gap pattern.** Filter for queries where position is 20 or higher and impressions exist but clicks are zero or near-zero. That pattern tells you something specific: Google is indexing your site for that service+location combination, ranking you — just not high enough for clicks. The demand is confirmed. The ranking isn't there yet.

**Step 3: Check if you have a page for it.** If the query is "furnace repair [city]" and you're appearing at position 24, do you have a dedicated page for furnace repair in that city? If the answer is no — that's your next page. If you do have the page and it's ranking at 24 — it's a rewrite and optimisation problem, not a missing page problem.

The distinction matters. Writing a new page when you already have one creates cannibalisation (Nov 18 post). Optimising when you need a new page wastes the effort.

This workflow is distinct from the Jul 3 external keyword gap tools post (finding keyword opportunities you haven't started ranking for yet) and the Sep 17 competitor gap analysis post (mapping what competitors rank for that you don't). This is the GSC-only internal gap finder — service+location combinations where you're already in Google's index but not ranking well enough to be clicked.
