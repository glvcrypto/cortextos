## LinkedIn Caption

The local SEO audit I do before every strategy call. 7 checks. 15 minutes.

I used to show up to discovery calls and ask clients what they wanted to improve. Now I do a 15-minute pre-call audit so I walk in knowing exactly what's broken.

Here's the checklist:

**1. Google the business name**
Do they control the knowledge panel? Is the info accurate? Any duplicate listings? Duplicates actively hurt rankings and I flag them immediately.

**2. Check the local pack for their primary keyword**
"[service] [city]" — are they in the pack? If not, I note their ranking position or absence. This tells me whether the problem is GBP or the website.

**3. GBP completeness**
Primary category correct? Hours set? Photos uploaded recently? Services filled in? Q&A seeded? Five-minute check that reveals most of the low-hanging fruit.

**4. Run site:domain.com in Google**
How many pages are indexed? Are service pages showing? If a business has 50 services and 3 indexed pages, that's the conversation.

**5. Mobile load speed**
PageSpeed Insights on mobile. Anything below 50 is a problem. Most local business sites I audit are in the 20–35 range. Speed directly affects bounce rate and local pack position.

**6. NAP consistency check**
Copy the business name + address from GBP. Paste it into Google. Check the top 3 directory results — Yelp, Yellow Pages, Facebook. Do the name, address, and phone number match exactly? Mismatches suppress rankings.

**7. Review velocity**
How many reviews do they have? When was the last one posted? A business with 80 reviews and none in the last 6 months looks stale to Google. Review recency matters as much as review count.

By the time the strategy call starts, I know: GBP issues, indexing issues, speed issues, NAP issues, and review issues. The conversation is specific before it begins.
