# 2027-05-06 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Local SEO for personal injury lawyers — search timing, practice area pages, and trust signals
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Personal injury searches don't happen right after the accident. They happen 4 to 6 weeks later — and your site needs to be ready for that timing."

---

**Beat 1 — When PI searches actually happen:**
"Most people who've been in a car accident, a slip and fall, or are dealing with a denied disability claim don't search for a lawyer immediately. They wait — dealing with insurance adjusters, medical appointments, and the initial shock. The research phase peaks 4 to 6 weeks post-incident. By then, they're in comparison mode: reading reviews, comparing firms, checking credentials. A site that answers questions clearly at that stage — rather than just listing practice areas — gets the call."

---

**Beat 2 — Practice area pages:**
"'Personal injury lawyer [city]' is one search. 'Car accident lawyer [city],' 'slip and fall lawyer [city],' 'long-term disability claim [city],' and 'accident benefits denied [city]' are four different searches made by people with four different situations. One practice area page ranks for none of them specifically. Each situation — car accident, LTD denial, slip and fall, pedestrian accident — has a different statute of limitations, a different defendant (employer insurance vs. auto insurer vs. property owner), and different questions the client will ask. A page that addresses those specifics converts better than a general PI overview."

---

**Beat 3 — Trust signal: reviews by practice area:**
"'PI lawyer [city] reviews' is one of the highest-volume trust queries before someone contacts a firm — higher than the firm's own brand name in many markets. Reviews that mention the specific situation — car accident settlement, LTD appeal, slip and fall — carry more weight than generic five-star ratings, because the reader can match their situation to the reviewer's outcome. Most PI firms actively discourage clients from leaving reviews. The ones that rank consistently in competitive markets have a system for requesting them at case close."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Separate pages per practice area. Reviews that name the situation. Content timed to the research window, not the incident date."

---

## Production Notes

- Text overlays per beat — one sentence at a time
- No real case details, client names, settlement amounts, or images that could identify anyone
- Captions on
- Showing a generic Google search result for "car accident lawyer [city]" is a good hook visual
- Law Society of Ontario (LSOC), Law Society of BC (LSBC), and Law Society of Alberta (LSA) all have advertising guidelines for contingency fee claims — no implied guarantees or results
- Do not state specific settlement amounts or outcomes — "favorable outcome" not "won $X"
