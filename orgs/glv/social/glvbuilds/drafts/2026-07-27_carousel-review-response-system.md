## Slide 3 — 5-Star Response Template

**Headline:** 5-star review → Thank + Specific + Invite back.

**Body:**
> "Thank you [name] — so glad [specific service or detail they mentioned] met the mark.
> If you ever need [service type] again, we'd love to see you back at [location/business name]."

One line. One keyword. One invitation.
Don't write an essay. Don't ask for another review. Don't use the same response word-for-word twice.

*Visual: Template with blanks highlighted*

---

## Slide 4 — 3-Star Response Template

**Headline:** 3-star review → Acknowledge + Take it offline.

**Body:**
> "Thanks for the honest feedback, [name]. We'd like to understand what we could have done better — please reach out to us at [phone/email] so we can make it right."

Never argue. Never explain at length.
The goal: show future searchers you take feedback seriously.
Avoid naming the problem publicly — keep it brief and redirect.

*Visual: Template example, cool neutral tone*

---

## Slide 5 — 1-Star Response Template

**Headline:** 1-star review → Short, calm, offline redirect. Always.

**Body:**
> "We're sorry to hear this, [name]. This doesn't reflect the experience we want to provide. Please contact us at [phone/email] — we'd like to understand what happened."

Three sentences maximum.
No defensiveness. No details. No "We don't have a record of your visit."
Future customers are reading your response more closely than the reviewer is.

*Visual: Template with "DO / DON'T" column for tone*

---

## Slide 6 — The Keyword Rule

**Headline:** One keyword per response. Naturally.

**Body:**
5-star: "so glad the marine service was quick" — not "marine service Sault Ste. Marie marine service."
The keyword slot is the service type or city in a natural sentence.
One response, one keyword. That's it.
Stuffing keywords in review responses is visible, looks bad, and likely a net negative.
