## Slide 2 — What Attributes Are (and Aren't)

**Headline:** Attributes aren't the same as categories or services. They answer a different question.

**Body:**
Your GBP category tells Google what type of business you are.
Your services list tells Google what you do.
Attributes tell Google *how* you operate — things searchers filter by when they're deciding whether to call.
"Wheelchair accessible." "Accepts credit cards." "On-site services."
Google displays matching attributes in the knowledge panel. Searchers filter by them in Maps.
Missing attributes = missing filters = fewer qualified impressions.

*Visual: GBP knowledge panel with attribute badges visible*

## Slide 3 — Service Delivery Attributes

**Headline:** Service delivery: on-site, in-store, or online appointments.

**Body:**
These tell Google how a customer accesses your service.
→ **On-site services**: technicians go to the customer's location
→ **In-store/In-shop services**: customer brings equipment or visits
→ **Online appointments**: booking link or form available
→ **Walk-ins welcome**: no appointment needed
Set every attribute that applies. A marine service business that does both in-shop and on-water service should have both flagged.
Searchers specifically filtering "on-site" will miss a business that hasn't set it.

## Slide 4 — Accessibility Attributes

**Headline:** Accessibility: wheelchair, hearing loop, parking.

**Body:**
This category matters more than most local SEO advice acknowledges.
→ **Wheelchair accessible entrance**: ramp or step-free entry
→ **Wheelchair accessible parking lot**: designated accessible spaces
→ **Assistive hearing loop**: available at counter or service desk
→ **Gender-neutral restroom**: where applicable
Google surfaces these in local search filters. Some searchers only consider businesses with specific accessibility confirmed.
If you have accessible facilities, flag them. If you don't, leave them blank — don't mark what isn't true.

## Slide 5 — Payment and Amenity Attributes

**Headline:** Payment methods and on-site amenities.

**Body:**
→ **Payment**: credit cards, debit cards, NFC/tap-to-pay, cash only — flag every method accepted
→ **Parking**: free parking lot, street parking, free garage, paid parking
→ **Wi-Fi**: free Wi-Fi (for waiting rooms or retail spaces)
→ **Restroom**: publicly available
These are the attributes searchers check when comparing two businesses with similar services and similar reviews.
"Accepts cards" and "free parking" in the knowledge panel removes friction before the call is even made.

## Slide 6 — How to Audit Your Current Attributes

**Headline:** Audit your attributes in under 5 minutes.

**Body:**
1. Open your GBP dashboard → Edit profile → Attributes.
2. Scroll through every category. Note anything that applies that's currently blank.
3. Check what competitors in your category have filled in — search your service + city and click into their profiles to see displayed attributes.
4. Fill in everything that's accurate. Remove anything that's checked but no longer true.
One flag: attribute availability varies by business category. A restaurant gets different attribute options than a repair shop. You can only select from what Google makes available for your primary category.

## LinkedIn Caption

Most Google Business Profile optimisation advice covers categories, services, reviews, and photos.

Almost no one covers attributes.

Here's the section I audit on every client profile — and why it keeps showing up blank.

**What GBP attributes are (and what they aren't)**

Attributes aren't categories and they aren't services. They answer a specific question: *how do you operate?*

Things like: do you offer on-site service or in-shop only? Are you wheelchair accessible? Do you accept tap-to-pay? Is parking free?

Google displays confirmed attributes in the knowledge panel. Google Maps lets searchers filter by them. A business that hasn't filled in its attributes is invisible to any filtered search.

**The four attribute categories worth auditing:**

**Service delivery.** On-site services, in-store/in-shop services, online appointments, walk-ins welcome. Flag every option that applies. A business that does both in-shop repair and on-site calls needs both checked.

**Accessibility.** Wheelchair accessible entrance, accessible parking, assistive hearing loop. These appear in filtered searches and in the knowledge panel. If you have the facilities, flag them. Leave blank anything that isn't accurate.

**Payment methods.** Credit cards, debit, NFC/tap, cash-only. Every accepted method. This removes friction before the customer even calls — "do they take cards?" is a decision point.

**Parking and amenities.** Free parking lot, street parking, Wi-Fi, restroom. The attributes that distinguish two otherwise similar businesses when a searcher is comparing them side by side.

**How to audit:**
Open GBP dashboard → Edit profile → Attributes. Scroll every section. Fill in everything accurate. Note: attribute availability varies by primary category — a repair shop sees different options than a restaurant.

Takes about 10 minutes. Most competitors haven't done it.
