# LinkedIn + IG Static: Explaining Local SEO ROI When the Client Has No Historical Data

## LinkedIn Caption (~420 words)

The hardest client conversation I have is with someone who hasn't tracked anything.

No call logs. No GBP Insights history. No GA4 set up. And they want to know if the SEO is working.

"Baseline zero" is a real problem — because without a before state, you can't prove a direction.

Here's what I do when I take on a client with no historical data:

**Step 1: Build a manual baseline on day one**

Before I change anything, I take a screenshot of:
— GBP Insights: searches, profile views, calls, direction requests, website clicks (28-day window)
— GSC Performance: impressions + average position for the top 3–5 service keywords (if connected — note if it isn't yet)
— The client's own rough call count for the previous month ("about 30 calls" is enough)

That screenshot becomes month zero. Every report after references it.

**Step 2: Use ratios, not just absolutes**

"Impressions up 40% since we started" is more honest and more useful than "you got 2,000 impressions" when the client has no context for what 2,000 means. Directional language over raw numbers when there's no baseline to compare.

**Step 3: Three-source truth for call volume**

GBP alone doesn't capture every call source. I use three signals: (1) GBP Insights call count, (2) GSC clicks to service pages in the same 28-day window, (3) client's own records or phone log estimate. Where all three point the same direction, that's a defensible result worth reporting.

**Step 4: Set the 90-day milestone expectation before the 30-day check**

I tell every no-baseline client the same thing upfront: "Month one will show almost no movement — that's expected. Month three is when we look at direction and make decisions." Setting that expectation once eliminates a series of anxious check-in messages.

Clients who've never tracked anything aren't bad clients. They just need the measurement structure set up before the work begins — not retrofitted after they start asking whether it's working.
