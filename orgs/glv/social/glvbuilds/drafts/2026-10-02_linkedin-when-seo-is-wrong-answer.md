# When Local SEO Is the Wrong Answer — LinkedIn text post + IG static

## LinkedIn

**I've turned down local SEO clients because their problem wasn't a Google problem. It was a conversion problem.**

A business can rank first on Google Maps and still not grow. If the phone goes to voicemail during business hours, if there's no follow-up process for missed calls, if the website loads slowly on mobile with no clear call-to-action — Google traffic will arrive and leave. More traffic in that situation just means more wasted opportunity.

Here's the one question I ask now before any engagement:

**"When a customer finds you on Google and calls — what actually happens?"**

If the answer reveals any of these, local SEO alone won't fix the business:

- Calls go to voicemail during the hours listed on GBP
- There is no follow-up system for missed calls or contact form submissions
- Appointments are booked manually with no confirmation sent to the customer
- The website takes more than three seconds to load on mobile and the contact page is buried in the nav

I still run GBP and citation work while clients sort the conversion side. But I set the expectation up front: this part of the work comes before rankings make a meaningful difference.

Ranking higher sends more people to the door. But if the door doesn't open, the problem isn't visibility.

What's the conversion issue you find most often before the SEO work even starts?
