## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
"Google Ads and local SEO are often treated as an either/or choice.

They're not. Here's how I use both — and when to pull back on one."

**Slide 2 — What each system actually does**
Google Ads buys visibility immediately. You pay per click. Pause the campaign, traffic stops the same day.

Local SEO earns visibility over time. Takes months to compound. Pause the work, results fade over quarters — not overnight.

Different mechanisms. Different timelines. Useful for different moments in a business's growth.

**Slide 3 — Use Ads to test keywords before building SEO pages**
Before spending six months writing and optimising a service+city page, I sometimes run a small Ads campaign first.

Two weeks. Targeted to that exact service and city. Watch the click-through rate and what people do when they land.

If the page converts, the SEO investment is justified. If it doesn't convert on paid traffic, it probably won't convert on organic traffic either — and now I know before spending the time.

**Slide 4 — Use local pack rank to decide when to reduce Ads**
If a client is showing consistently in the top three map pack results for their core service+city query, they're getting free local visibility on every search.

That's when it makes sense to reduce ad spend on that exact keyword — not to zero, but to rebalance the budget toward underserved service areas or lower-ranking keywords.

The map pack rank is the signal. Not the ad performance alone.

**Slide 5 — Align GBP and your Ads account**
Location extensions in Google Ads pull from a linked Google Business Profile. That means:

1. The business name, address, and phone shown in your ad must match your GBP exactly
2. Review stars from GBP reviews can appear under your ad (seller rating extension)
3. A misconfigured GBP — wrong phone, wrong hours — shows up in your Ads, not just Maps

Linking the accounts: Google Ads → Assets → Location → link your GBP. Worth 10 minutes.

**Slide 6 — When to prioritise one over the other**
New business (under 6 months): Ads first. Local SEO takes time to work; Ads puts you in front of ready buyers now. Use this window to collect reviews and build GBP completeness.

Established business with weak local rank: both in parallel — Ads maintains visibility while SEO builds momentum.

Established business ranking in top 3 locally: reduce Ads spend on that term, redirect to weaker service areas or seasonal campaigns.

**Slide 7 — CTA**
The most common mistake: running Ads without tracking which keywords convert, then building SEO pages based on assumptions instead of data.

Ads gives you that data fast. Use it.

## LinkedIn Caption

I get asked which is better — Google Ads or local SEO — at least a few times a month.

It's the wrong question. They solve different problems on different timelines.

**Google Ads** puts you in front of ready buyers immediately, at a cost per click. Pause the campaign, traffic stops. Restart it, traffic returns. It's a tap you can turn on and off.

**Local SEO** builds organic visibility over months. Once it compounds, it continues with maintenance rather than ongoing spend. But it takes time to earn.

The more useful question: how do you use both without wasting money?

**Three integration points I use with clients:**

**1. Use Ads to test keywords before building SEO pages.**
Before investing in a service+city page, a two-week targeted Ads campaign tells you whether that keyword actually converts. If it doesn't perform on paid traffic — which arrives immediately with high intent — it's unlikely to perform on organic traffic either. Now you know before you build.

**2. Use local pack rank to reduce Ads spend.**
When a client is consistently ranking in the top three map pack results for a core service+city query, they have free visibility on every search for that term. That's the signal to rebalance Ads budget toward underserved service areas or lower-ranking keywords — not to stop Ads entirely, but to stop paying for visibility you're already earning.

**3. Align GBP and your Ads account.**
Google Ads location extensions pull from a linked GBP profile. That means the name, phone, and address in your ad must match your GBP exactly — and review stars from GBP can appear under the ad. A misconfigured GBP doesn't just hurt your map pack ranking; it creates inconsistency in paid ads too. Link the accounts and check the alignment.
