## LinkedIn Post

Google Local Services Ads show above everything else in search.

Above GBP. Above paid search. Above organic results. Most of my clients don't know they exist — and most of my clients who do think they're only for big companies.

They're not.

I add LSAs for clients when I see three things in place:
– GBP verified and complete (LSAs pull directly from it)
– At least 10 reviews with a 4.0+ rating
– A service area where competitors are already running them

When I hold off:
– New business with fewer than 5 reviews (LSAs surface your rating immediately; a thin profile works against you)
– Offline or by-referral-only business (no search intent to capture)
– Services where LSAs aren't available in the category

The pricing model is pay-per-lead, not pay-per-click. Google screens leads before they reach you. You can dispute leads that don't qualify.

That last part matters: if someone contacts you through an LSA and it clearly doesn't match your service, you can request a credit. Most business owners don't do this.

One thing to watch: your GBP and your LSA profile need to match. Different phone numbers, inconsistent service areas, or a name variation between the two will flag your account.

The businesses getting the most out of LSAs are the ones who already have strong GBP signals. Fix the foundation first — LSAs amplify what's already there.

What do you use for paid local visibility — LSAs, Google Ads, both, or neither?
