**Supporting Context — Key Points for Comments or Follow-Up Content**

**GBP: Service-area business setup for mobile trainers**

An independent trainer who visits clients at home, trains at a park, or works out of rented gym time typically does not have a permanent business address to display publicly. GBP allows a "service area" setup where you define the areas you serve without publishing a home address. Most trainers either skip GBP entirely (losing map visibility) or set up a storefront with a home address (a privacy issue and against GBP guidelines). The correct setup: service-area business, geographic service area defined by city or radius, no physical address shown publicly.

**Per-specialty pages**

- **Pre/postnatal fitness** — "pre-natal personal trainer [city]," "postpartum fitness trainer [city]" — distinct client pool; clients are specifically seeking someone experienced in pregnancy and post-birth fitness; certifications relevant (pre/postnatal specialist credential from organisations like BIRTHFIT or ProNatal Fitness — note these as examples of industry-recognised credentials without fabricating equivalency to regulated health professions)
- **Post-rehab / injury recovery fitness** — "post-rehab personal trainer [city]," "personal trainer after knee surgery [city]" — high-intent, often referred by a physiotherapist; important framing: working alongside a healthcare team, not replacing it; do not make clinical recovery claims
- **Senior fitness / older adult training** — "personal trainer for seniors [city]" — distinct search from general personal training; clients (or adult children of clients) are looking for patience, fall prevention context, and experience with reduced mobility; Canadian physical activity guidelines for older adults (published by CSEP) can be referenced without fabrication
- **Athletic performance / sport-specific** — "hockey training [city]," "sport-specific strength training [city]" — sport-named searches exist and are underserved; NCCP coaching certification can be referenced as a credential for sport-specific training
- **Online coaching** — "online personal trainer [city]" still has local intent — many clients want an online trainer from their area, not just any online trainer; an online coaching page captures this niche without competing with the broad personal trainer search

**Trust signals**

- **CSEP-CPT** — Canadian Society for Exercise Physiology Certified Personal Trainer — the Canadian standard; verifiable at csep.ca; display the credential designation, not just "certified personal trainer" (which anyone can claim)
- **NASM-CPT / ACE-CPT** — US-issued but widely held and recognisable in Canada; state the full organisation name on first use
- **Liability insurance** — personal trainers working outside a gym are personally liable; "insured professional" stated explicitly builds confidence with clients who have never trained outside a gym setting
- **Niche testimonials** — "helped me return to running 8 weeks after my hip replacement" outperforms a five-star rating by a client whose name you don't recognise
- **Session format transparency** — where sessions happen (client's home, outdoor location, rented facility — and which specific facility if applicable); clients have logistics questions before they book
