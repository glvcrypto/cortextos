## LinkedIn Caption

The first backlinks I build for every new local client aren't editorial or guest posts.

They're three things that take less than a week to get and carry genuine local authority.

**1. Chamber of commerce or BIA membership.**
Most towns and cities have one. Annual fees vary widely, but the payoff is a link from the local chamber's member directory — a domain Google treats as a local-authority signal. Plus it's a citation with correct NAP data. One action, two wins.
Cost: membership fee. Time: 1–2 hours to apply and set up the listing correctly.

**2. Local news or community website.**
Not a press release blast to 400 wire services. A real story pitched to the reporter covering local business: a new location, a seasonal service launch, an anniversary milestone. Local papers and community blogs link back when there's a genuine angle.
Cost: zero (unless you pay for a press release service, which I don't). Time: 30–45 minutes to identify the right journalist and pitch a real story.

**3. Supplier or manufacturer dealer directory.**
If a client is an authorised dealer for any brand — power equipment, marine, HVAC, whatever — that manufacturer almost certainly has a "find a dealer" or "dealer locator" page.
Getting listed there means a link from a national brand domain pointing at a local business website. Most clients haven't requested it. Most manufacturers approve the listing within a week.
Cost: zero. Time: about 10 minutes to find the right contact and submit.

These three won't replace a sustained link-building strategy. But they establish a baseline of legitimate local authority that most competitors don't have — and they're available to almost every local service business.
