## Slide 2 — Category Distinctions

**Day Spa**: facials, body wraps, nail services, waxing, lash and brow, relaxation massage. GBP category: "Day Spa."

**Medical Spa**: HydraFacial, laser hair removal, injectables (Botox, fillers), microneedling, chemical peels — services delivered or supervised by a regulated health professional. GBP category: "Medical Spa." In provinces where injectables require physician or NP oversight, the regulated provider should appear on the website.

**Massage Therapy Clinic**: RMT (Registered Massage Therapist) services, direct-billing to extended health. GBP category: "Massage Therapist."

A combined clinic should set its highest-revenue or most-distinctive service category as primary and add the others as secondary. Trying to describe all three in one listing paragraph while using a single generic category is why most multi-service wellness clinics underperform in local search.

## Slide 3 — Service Pages

"Deep tissue massage [city]." "Laser hair removal [city]." "HydraFacial [city]." "Eyelash extensions [city]." "Botox [city]."

Each of these is a separate search. One "services" page listing everything ranks for none of them specifically.

Service-specific pages — describing what the treatment involves, how long it takes, what to expect during and after, who it's for, and cost or "call for pricing" — answer the questions a first-time client has before booking. These are not just SEO assets; they reduce front-desk call time because clients arrive informed.

---

## Slide 4 — Gift Card Page

The gift card page is one of the highest-converting pages on a spa or wellness clinic website — and one of the most neglected from an SEO standpoint.

"Spa gift card [city]." "Massage gift certificate [city]." "Day spa gift ideas [city]."

These searches spike at three predictable times: Mother's Day (April–May), Valentine's Day (January–February), and Christmas (November–December). The gift card page needs to be live year-round, not published three days before a holiday.

The page should include: what denominations are available, how to purchase (online, phone, in person), whether they're redeemable for all services or specific ones, and whether they expire (Ontario: gift cards cannot expire under the Consumer Protection Act).

---

## Slide 5 — RMT Credential Pages

Each Registered Massage Therapist at your clinic is a separate ranking asset.

A named bio page — Registered Massage Therapist designation, provincial registration number (CMTO in Ontario, CMTBC in British Columbia), modalities (Swedish, deep tissue, prenatal, sports, lymphatic drainage, hot stone), languages spoken, insurance direct-billing information — creates one searchable asset per practitioner.

Patients looking for a bilingual RMT or a prenatal massage specialist search by those criteria. A team-grid entry doesn't appear in those searches. A named bio page does.

---

## Slide 6 — Booking and Cancellation Page

Two pieces of information that reduce booking friction and improve client quality: direct-billing information and cancellation policy.

**Direct-billing**: which extended health plans the clinic bills directly (Manulife, Sun Life, Canada Life, Desjardins, Green Shield Canada, etc.). Most clients don't know if their plan covers RMT without checking, and many won't check — they'll book elsewhere if billing clarity is missing.

**Cancellation policy**: a clear 24–48 hour notice policy on the booking page reduces no-show rates and signals professionalism. Clients who read it before booking self-select; those who won't respect the policy often don't book.
