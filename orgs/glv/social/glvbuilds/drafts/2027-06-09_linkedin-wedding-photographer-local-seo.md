**LinkedIn Caption**

Most wedding photographers have one portfolio page on their website. The clients booking them are running twelve searches before they send a single inquiry.

Here's what those searches look like — and why the standard photographer website misses most of them.

**The booking timeline changes everything**

Wedding photography bookings happen 12–18 months in advance of the wedding date. The trigger is almost always the engagement — not a general interest in photography. A couple who got engaged in November is actively searching photographers in November and December for a wedding the following summer or fall.

This matters for local SEO because the volume of "wedding photographer [city]" searches peaks in November–January, drops in summer (when most weddings happen), and spikes again in August–September (couples engaged during summer planning a next-year wedding). Content that is live and indexed before engagement season captures the window. A photographer who publishes content in March for that year's weddings has missed the booking season entirely.

**GBP: right category, right photos**

"Wedding Photographer" as primary GBP category — not "Photographer" alone. The generic category competes with product photographers, headshot photographers, and event photographers in a single bucket. The specific category aligns with the search intent.

GBP photos for a wedding photographer need to show weddings — not just the brand logo and the headshot. A couple shortlisting photographers will look at GBP photos before clicking through to the website. Venue-appropriate photos, diverse couples, and outdoor/indoor variety in the GBP photo set give a visual answer before the website visit.

**Per-format pages beyond "weddings"**

- **Engagement sessions** — booked separately from weddings; clients searching "engagement photographer [city]" are not always the same clients searching "wedding photographer [city]"
- **Elopements** — "elopement photographer [city]" is a distinct search cluster growing in volume; elopement-specific content addresses the logistics that elopement couples need answered (small group, quick ceremony, flexibility on location)
- **Micro/intimate weddings** (under 30 guests) — distinct from elopements; couples planning intimate weddings have different questions than full-service wedding clients
- **Bridal portraits** — regional in demand (more common in certain markets); if offered, a separate page captures the search
- **Second shooter availability** — not a client-facing page, but a B2B page for other photographers seeking a second shooter; a separate traffic source with no competition from the main wedding category

**Venue-specific content**

"[Venue name] wedding photographer [city]" is one of the most targeted, highest-intent searches a wedding client runs. A couple who has booked their venue is searching to see portfolios from that specific location. A photographer who has an album or blog post from a popular local venue ranked for that venue name captures clients who have already made their biggest decision.

This doesn't require a formal partnership. A well-tagged gallery from a wedding at a well-known venue — with the venue name in the page title, H1, alt text, and file names — earns the search without any formal arrangement.

**Style keywords**

Wedding photography style is a real search differentiator:
- "Documentary wedding photographer [city]" — photojournalistic approach; clients who don't want posed shots
- "Editorial wedding photographer [city]" — fashion-influenced; clients who want styled, magazine-feel images
- "Film wedding photographer [city]" — true film or film-look digital; a niche but very loyal client search
- "Light and airy wedding photographer [city]" — editing style as a search term

A photographer who is genuinely known for one style should have that style named in their website copy, their GBP profile description, and their page titles — not buried in a blog post.

**Trust signals**

- Real testimonials naming the wedding date and venue — "shot our wedding at [Venue] in October — not a single posed moment felt staged" converts better than a five-star rating with no context
- Second shooter and backup equipment disclosure — a client question that is rarely answered proactively on photographer websites; answering it builds trust before the inquiry
- Contract transparency — refund and postponement policy is the #1 client anxiety in wedding vendor bookings; a clear, non-threatening policy statement reduces inquiry hesitation
- Response time — photographers who confirm inquiry response time ("I reply within 24 hours") lower the client anxiety around being left waiting during a high-stress planning period
