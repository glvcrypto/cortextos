## LinkedIn Caption

Most local service businesses set up their GBP Categories, Services, and Description — and stop there.

The Products tab is the one that gets skipped almost every time.

Here's why I add it as a standard step for every client.

**It's not just for physical products.**

GBP's Products tab was built with retail in mind, but the functionality works equally well for service businesses. A "product" is anything you offer — and that includes services.

Each product entry has four fields: name, description (up to 1,000 characters), price (optional), and a link (optional). For a service business, that becomes: service name, a description of what it includes, "call for pricing," and a link to the service page.

**It surfaces in the GBP sidebar on search.**

When a searcher finds your profile on Google Maps or Search, the Products section appears in the profile view — in many cases above the website link and phone number. That's prime real estate for searchers who are already on your profile.

**Each entry is indexed text.**

Every product name, description, and associated keyword is text that Google reads within your profile. It acts as a second layer of keyword coverage, supplementing the Business description and Services tab.

**You can add photos.**

Photo + service name + description creates a visual services menu inside Google's own search results. For a client with strong service photography, this is a significant opportunity to differentiate from competitors who have no product entries at all.

**The time investment is low.**

For a business with 5–8 core services, setting up the Products tab takes 30–45 minutes. Once it's built, it requires minimal maintenance unless services change.

If your Services tab is already configured, the Products tab is the next 30-minute move.
