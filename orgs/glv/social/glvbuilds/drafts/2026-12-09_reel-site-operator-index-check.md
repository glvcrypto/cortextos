## LinkedIn Caption (~300 words)

A 10-second check to see if your service page is actually indexed — no Search Console login needed.

**Open an incognito browser. Type this into Google:**

`site:yourdomain.com/your-service-page-url`

**Three outcomes:**

**1. The page appears once**
Indexed. Now check what Google shows as your title tag and meta description — not what your CMS says, but what Google actually reads. If the title is wrong, your click-through rate is wrong.

**2. Two or more pages appear**
Cannibalisation signal. Google is choosing between multiple pages for the same query and likely ranking none well. Identify which URL you want ranking and tighten internal links to it.

**3. Nothing appears**
Not indexed. Head to GSC → URL Inspection. Check for: noindex tags, blocked robots.txt rules, canonical pointing elsewhere, or thin content signals.

This is the gut-check I run before I touch anything on a page. If the page isn't indexed, on-page work is irrelevant until that's resolved.

The site: operator also tells you what your title tag looks like in the wild — what Google actually renders, not what's in your CMS editor. Useful to check after any title tag edit before waiting for GSC to update.

One operator. Ten seconds. Surfaces three different categories of issue before they waste hours of work.
