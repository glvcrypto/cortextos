## LinkedIn caption

I run a 15-minute competitor check every first Monday of the month.

Not a deep audit — just a pulse check to catch what changed since last month. Here's what I look at:

**Review delta**
Note where each competitor sat last month. Flag any that jumped by 5+ reviews in a short window. A sudden burst is worth watching — it may signal a new review-request system, a referral campaign, or (less commonly) a coordinated scheme worth documenting.

**GBP posts**
When did they last post? Competitors going quiet often means they've deprioritised local SEO — an opportunity to widen the gap. Competitors who started posting more consistently: note the topic. If Google is rewarding their posting cadence, that's useful signal.

**Services tab**
Any new services added? New descriptions? If a competitor added a service you also offer but haven't listed yet, their justification and category coverage just expanded. Update your own tab if there's a gap.

**Rank position delta**
I run a quick incognito search for our top 2–3 service + city combinations. Note if any competitor moved from position 4 to 2, or dropped out of the local pack entirely. Rank grid tools (BrightLocal, Local Falcon) give a more precise picture, but manual checks catch the obvious shifts without any setup.

The whole thing takes 15 minutes because the initial competitive gap analysis — the one-time baseline audit — was done in onboarding (see Sep 17). Monthly monitoring is just tracking deltas against that baseline.

The first month you run this, you'll probably find nothing changed. The third month you'll catch something worth acting on. The sixth month you'll have a clear picture of how your competitors operate — which is when monitoring starts compounding into strategy.
