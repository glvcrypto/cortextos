My client's Google Business Profile disappeared in the middle of their busiest month.

No email. No warning. Just gone.

Here's the process I followed to get it reinstated.

**Step 1: Check the suspension type.**
Open the GBP dashboard. Look for the "Suspended" label.
If there's an "Appeal" button, it's a soft suspension — more common, usually fixable.
No appeal option = hard suspension, which takes more documentation and patience.

**Step 2: Read the reinstatement policy before touching anything.**
Most soft suspensions have one of three causes: the address isn't verifiable at street level, the primary category doesn't match the actual business, or Google detected what it thinks is a duplicate listing.
Fix the underlying issue first. Submitting an appeal before fixing the root cause is a waste of a submission.

**Step 3: Submit the appeal with documentation.**
Use the Business Profile Help form (found via Google's support pages) — not the in-dashboard help button, which routes slower.
Document what the profile looked like before, what you changed, and why the listing is legitimate. Screenshots help.

Appeals submitted with a clear explanation of the fix and supporting screenshots move noticeably faster than bare-minimum forms.

The most frustrating part of GBP suspensions is that Google rarely tells you exactly why. Learning the common patterns saves time.
