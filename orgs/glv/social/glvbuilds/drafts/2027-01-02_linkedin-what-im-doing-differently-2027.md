# LinkedIn: What I'm doing differently for local SEO clients in 2027

## LinkedIn Caption (full)

**It's January 2. This isn't a resolutions post.**

Three specific things I'm actually changing about how I work with local SEO clients this year:

**1. Conversion audit before SEO audit.**
I used to start every new engagement by looking at GBP, GSC, and keyword gaps. This year I start one step earlier: "When a customer finds you on Google and calls, what happens?" I audit the conversion path first — call answer rate, response time, follow-up process. If the pipeline is broken, ranking improvement alone won't fix the business.

**2. One content cluster per quarter, not one-off pages.**
I've been building isolated service pages. This year I'm building clusters: one anchor page, a how-it-works page, an FAQ page, a comparison page, and an areas-served page. The internal linking compounds. Five connected pages consistently outperform five unconnected ones.

**3. Monthly competitor monitoring as a fixed calendar event.**
First Monday of the month, 15 minutes, every active client. Review count delta, GBP post activity, Services tab changes, rank shift. I've done this informally for years. This year it's a recurring block. The clients where I catch a shift early have better outcomes.

These aren't new ideas. I just wasn't doing all three consistently. This year I am.

What are you doing differently this year?
