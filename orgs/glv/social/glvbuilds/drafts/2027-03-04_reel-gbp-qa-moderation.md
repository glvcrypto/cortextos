## LinkedIn Caption

Most business owners don't know the Q&A section on their GBP listing exists.

Some of them have found out the hard way — when a customer showed up based on an answer a stranger posted, and the answer was wrong.

Here's how it works: any Google Maps user can post a question on your listing. Any other Google Maps user can answer it. Google notifies the business owner, but those notifications often go unread.

The moderation workflow is straightforward:

**Step 1:** Google Maps → your business listing → scroll to the Q&A tab. Read every answer that came from "a Google user."

**Step 2:** If an answer is inaccurate — three-dot menu on the answer → Report → "Incorrect information." Then post your own correct answer immediately from your GBP Manager account. Don't wait for Google to act on the report before you add the right answer.

**Step 3:** Prevent it from happening in the first place. Seed 3–5 of your most common customer questions in the Q&A tab from a personal Google account. Answer each one from your GBP Manager account the same session. Your controlled answer appears first and reduces the chance a stranger answers it wrong.

The questions to seed: the ones that most often determine whether a customer calls or goes to a competitor. Service area ("do you come to [neighbourhood]?"), pricing ("do you have a call-out fee?"), process ("how long does it take?"), credentials ("are you licensed and insured?").

Audit takes 10 minutes. Monthly check takes 2.
