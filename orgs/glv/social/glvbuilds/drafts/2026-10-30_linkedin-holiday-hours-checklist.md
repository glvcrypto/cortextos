## LinkedIn post

Most local businesses update their hours on one platform when they close for the holidays. The other six places their hours live get forgotten.

A customer searching on Thanksgiving sees the right hours on your GBP but finds nothing updated on Bing. They check Apple Maps before they leave the house — it still shows your regular schedule. They show up anyway, find you closed, and don't come back.

**Every place your holiday hours need to change:**

1. **GBP** — Special hours (not regular hours). GBP Manager → Edit profile → Hours → Special hours. Set them for every affected date. Google flags listings as "may be closed" on statutory holidays if you haven't entered special hours — even if you're actually open.

2. **Website** — Contact page, footer, homepage banner if you have one. If your site has a business hours block (often pulled from a plugin or schema), update the override there too.

3. **Bing Places** — Open hours section has a special/holiday hours option. If your Bing listing is imported from GBP, it may not auto-sync — log in and check.

4. **Apple Maps** — Business Connect → Hours → Special hours. Siri near-me queries pull from Apple Maps. If your hours aren't updated there, anyone asking Siri gets the wrong answer.

5. **Facebook** — If your page has business hours listed, there's a temporary hours option. Most local clients I work with have a Facebook page with hours that haven't been touched since the page was created.

6. **Answering service or voicemail greeting** — Not a platform, but it counts. A customer who calls over the holidays and hears a greeting that doesn't mention holiday hours is going to assume you're open and show up anyway.

**Timing:** Set these two weeks before the holiday, not the day before. Special hours in GBP in particular take a few days to propagate fully across search results.
