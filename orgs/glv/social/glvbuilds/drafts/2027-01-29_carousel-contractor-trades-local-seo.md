## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
"Most contractor GBP profiles are optimised for the wrong search.

Here are the five things I check first on any new trades client."

**Slide 2 — Category specificity: the most common gap**
"Contractor" is a valid GBP category. It ranks for almost no specific service search.

Open Google Maps in a private browser. Search your service in your city. Look at the primary category for the top three results in the map pack.

"Plumber." "Plumbing Contractor." "Emergency Plumber." Pick the most specific category that still accurately describes your business — match or go one level deeper than your competitors.

Primary category is the highest-weight field in the local pack.

**Slide 3 — Service area setup for trades**
Most contractors work from home or a non-public shop. You should not be displaying a home address in GBP.

Two-step fix:
1. Clear the business address in GBP Manager → Edit profile → Business location
2. Set service areas — add cities, towns, or a kilometre radius for the areas you actually work in

Google uses your verified location for proximity ranking internally. Customers never see your home address. You still show in the local pack.

**Slide 4 — HomeStars for Ontario trades**
For HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, and renovation contractors in Ontario — HomeStars is where homeowners are looking before they call.

It's a secondary platform to GBP, not a replacement. Worth investment for those categories in the Ontario market.

Claiming and updating your HomeStars profile with real work photos, service descriptions, and consistent NAP is the minimum. Reviews on HomeStars are a trust signal that compounds with GBP reviews for the kind of decision a homeowner makes before letting a contractor into their house.

**Slide 5 — Licence numbers as E-E-A-T signals**
Red Seal number. Gas-B certificate. Certificate of Qualification.

These don't belong buried in an About page paragraph. They go on a named technician bio page — full name, specific licence type and number, years of experience stated as "more than X years," a real photo.

This is what Google's quality raters look for when evaluating whether a contractor website deserves to rank for high-stakes service queries. It's also what converts a first-time homeowner who doesn't know your business.

**Slide 6 — The "emergency" keyword opportunity**
"Emergency plumber Toronto." "After-hours HVAC repair Ottawa."

High intent. Often lower competition than the general category search. Homeowners searching these are ready to call.

If you actually take emergency calls, two assets capture this:
1. One dedicated page — clear hours, direct tel: click-to-call link, what "emergency" means for your response time
2. A GBP Q&A seed: "Do you offer emergency service?" — asked from your personal Google account, answered immediately from your GBP account

Two assets. One high-value search intent covered.

**Slide 7 — CTA**
The first thing I check on any new contractor client: primary GBP category in a private browser, compared to the top three map pack results for their core service.

Takes 90 seconds. Tells you whether the most important field in local ranking is set correctly before anything else is touched.
