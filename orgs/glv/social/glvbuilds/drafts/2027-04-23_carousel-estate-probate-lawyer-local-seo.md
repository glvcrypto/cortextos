## Slide 2 — GBP Category

"Wills and Estate Attorney" is the correct GBP primary category for estate and probate practices.

This is a distinct category from "Family Lawyer" (which covers divorce and custody) and from "Lawyer" (too generic to carry the right filter signal). Estate and probate searches happen in a specific emotional and logistical context — being invisible to that category filter means losing to firms that have simply set this correctly.

---

## Slide 3 — Practice-Specific Pages

"Probate lawyer [city]," "estate administration lawyer [city]," "will drafting lawyer [city]," "power of attorney lawyer [city]," and "estate litigation lawyer [city]" are separate searches made by people at very different stages — some planning ahead, some in the middle of a crisis.

One "Estate Law" services page ranks for none of them specifically.

Each practice area page should open with a plain-language explanation of what the service involves and what triggers the need for it. Avoid starting with the firm's credentials.

---

## Slide 4 — Named Lawyer Bio Pages

Each lawyer at the firm is a separate local SEO asset.

A dedicated bio page — full name, call year, provincial Law Society number (Law Society of Ontario, Law Society of BC, Law Society of Alberta), practice focus, languages spoken, real headshot — creates one ranking opportunity per named lawyer.

Google quality raters check for bar membership specificity on legal practitioner pages. A team-grid listing four lawyers with headshots is one opportunity where there could be four.

---

## Slide 5 — Empathy-First CTA

Estate and probate searches often happen within days of a death. The person searching may be grieving, handling an unfamiliar process, and under deadline pressure for estate administration filings.

A contact form that says "get in touch" is not built for this moment.

Lead with estimated response time, offer a phone number above the fold, and use "book a confidential consultation" rather than generic language. Showing a named lawyer's contact rather than a general inbox removes a layer of friction for someone already overwhelmed.

---

## Slide 6 — Dual-Phase FAQ

Estate law has two distinct intent clusters: people planning ahead (writing a will, setting up a POA, updating beneficiaries) and people managing an estate after a death (probate filing, estate administration, handling disputes).

These two searchers have completely different questions. One FAQ block mixing both serves neither well.

Build one FAQ section for planning-intent searches and a separate one for administration-intent searches. Use H2 and H3 headings that match the exact phrasing people search. "How long does probate take in Ontario?" is a better heading than "Frequently Asked Questions About Probate."
