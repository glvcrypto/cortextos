## Post Summaries

### Apr 13 — Carousel: Electrical Contractor Local SEO
_"I've audited local SEO for electrical contractors in three provinces. Every one showed up for their company name. Almost none showed up for 'licensed electrician [city]'."_

Five gaps: GBP category precision (Electrician vs. Electrical Contractor vs. Commercial Electrician); provincial licence numbers on service pages (ESA/ECRA/TECA — Ontario, BC Safety Authority — BC, APECB — Alberta, Red Seal); EV charger installation page (growing demand, lower competition, early-mover window still open in most Canadian cities); emergency electrical page (phone above fold, hours visible, GBP Q&A seed); service area setup for home-based operations (hide address, set service areas by city).

CTA: WIRE. ⚠️ New keyword — Aiden to confirm automation or swap to manual DM.

### Apr 14 — LinkedIn text + IG static: GSC Featured Snippet Capture
_"GSC shows you when Google is already reading your page for a question it's answering elsewhere. Most people miss it."_

Workflow: GSC → Performance → Queries → filter question words (how/what/why/cost) → sort by position → look for position 1–8 with significant impressions. Open in private browser, check snippet source. Fix: H2 = exact question, first sentence = direct answer in under 10 words, 40–60 words total. Two things that don't work: long intros before the answer, structured data alone. Distinct from existing GSC posts (weekly check Oct 14, position 11–20 Feb 6, blog audit Mar 10).

CTA: Open comment. No flags.

### Apr 16 — Carousel: Tutoring and Learning Centre Local SEO
_"I've audited local SEO for tutoring centres and private tutors across four cities. Every one appeared when someone searched their name. Almost none appeared for 'math tutor [city] grade 10'."_

Five gaps: GBP category precision (Tutoring Service vs. Test Preparation Center — distinct categories; IELTS/LSAT/standardised test prep have separate filter behaviour); subject + grade level pages (lower competition, higher intent than generic "tutoring [city]"); individual tutor bio pages (one team-grid page = one ranking opportunity; one bio page per tutor = separate ranking per name + subject + city); exam season page timing (Ontario secondary exam windows Jan and Jun, 6–8 weeks lead time; university prep tests have annual cycles); GBP hours accuracy and seasonal closures (academic year vs. summer, PD Days, March Break — inaccurate hours generate "closed when listed as open" reviews).

CTA: EXAM. ⚠️ New keyword — Aiden to confirm automation or swap to manual DM.

### Apr 17 — Reel: Family Lawyer Local SEO
_"Family law has some of the most emotionally charged searches on Google. The way someone searches during a separation is completely different from how they search for most legal services."_

Three beats: dual intent split ("divorce lawyer [city]" = research mode vs. "separation agreement lawyer [city]" = ready to retain — two different pages, CTA above fold on the latter); question-driven content (FAQ pages for "how long does divorce take Ontario," "do I need a lawyer for separation agreement" — answer in first sentence, defensible language, "in Ontario, typically" not "you will"); provincial law society citation check (LSOC Ontario, LSS BC, LSA Alberta — firm name + address + phone must match GBP exactly; almost no accounts audited have verified this).

CTA: Open comment. No flags. Distinct from existing legal posts: Jan 19 (professional services general framework), Mar 26 (immigration lawyer — CICC/RCIC focus). Family law has unique intent-split and emotionally-driven search language.
