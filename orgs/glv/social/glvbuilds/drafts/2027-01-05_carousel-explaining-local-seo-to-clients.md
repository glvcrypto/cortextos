# Carousel: The 60-second explanation I give every new local SEO client

## Slide 1 — Hook

"Most clients don't know what local SEO is.

Here's the explanation I use. 60 seconds. Works every time."

## Slide 2 — The problem it solves

When someone searches '[your service] in [your city]', Google decides who shows up first.

That decision comes down to three signals: how verified and complete the business profile is, how relevant the business is to the search, and how trusted Google considers the business to be.

Local SEO is the work of making sure Google gets all three right.

## Slide 3 — Two separate ranking systems

There's a map pack (the three listings with star ratings and a map) and organic results (the blue links below it).

Both appear on the same search page. Both work differently.

GBP powers the map pack. Your website powers organic. Optimising one doesn't automatically help the other.

## Slide 4 — What the work actually involves

Three categories:

1. **GBP**: Verified, complete, active — photos, services, hours, category, posts
2. **Website**: Service pages that match what people search for — right structure, right local signals, right content
3. **Trust signals**: Consistent reviews, accurate business information across directories, mobile-ready site

## Slide 5 — The timeline expectation

Set this upfront. Clients who understand the timeline don't panic at month 2.

- GBP changes: visible in days to weeks
- Service pages gaining rank momentum: 3–6 months
- Compounding results (ranking + traffic + calls): 6–12 months

Local SEO builds like compound interest. Slow at first. Then it isn't.

## Slide 6 — What success looks like

Not "number 1 for everything."

Specifically:

- GBP appearing in the map pack for 2–3 high-intent service+city searches
- At least one service page receiving organic traffic
- Review count growing consistently, month over month

These are the targets I set at the start of every engagement.

## Slide 7 — Why the explanation matters

Clients who understand what local SEO is and how long it takes:

- Give you better information about their business
- Don't panic when rankings shift after an algorithm update
- Can actually recognise when something is working

The explanation takes 60 seconds. The relationship goes better for all 12 months.
