## Slide 2 — GBP Category Precision

Google has distinct categories: Driving School, Truck Driving School, Defensive Driving School.

Check what your top 3 map pack competitors are using as their primary category. Set yours to match your highest-revenue service, then add relevant secondaries.

Most driving schools set a generic category at registration and never revisit it.

---

## Slide 3 — Course-Specific Landing Pages

"G1 exit test prep [city]," "G2 road test [city]," "highway driving lessons [city]," and "defensive driving course [city]" are four distinct search intents.

One generic "Services" page tries to serve all of them and ranks for none specifically.

Build one dedicated page per highest-demand course. Start with the stage that drives the most new student registrations — not the one you already rank for.

---

## Slide 4 — Instructor Bio Pages

Each instructor has their own search demand: name + city + course type.

A dedicated bio page — full name, MTO-approved instructor number (Ontario) or provincial equivalent, specific courses taught, real headshot — creates one ranking opportunity per instructor.

One team-grid page is one opportunity where there could be four.

---

## Slide 5 — GBP Booking Link

Add your booking system URL to GBP Manager → Edit profile → Booking.

A "Book a lesson" button appears on your mobile Maps listing before a student clicks through to your website. Takes three minutes to add. Almost never set on driving school profiles.

If you use Calendly, Acuity, or a booking page on your own site, this applies.

---

## Slide 6 — Review Specificity

Reviews that name a specific course or licence stage appear as justification text in local pack results.

When asking for a review, prompt: "If you're comfortable, mention the licence stage you were preparing for."

"Helped me pass my G2 road test" in a review shows as a justification snippet in Maps. "Great instructor" doesn't.
