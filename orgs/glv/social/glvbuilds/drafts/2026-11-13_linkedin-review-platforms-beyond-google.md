## LinkedIn Caption (300–500 words)

A new client asked me whether they should pay for Yelp advertising.

Here's what I told them: no. But not for the reason they expected.

The issue isn't cost. It's category fit.

**Yelp in Canada**

Yelp is dominant for restaurants, bars, and personal care services in major Canadian metros — Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary. For those categories, having a claimed, optimised Yelp listing matters.

For home services and trades in most Canadian markets? The data doesn't support paying for it. Organic Yelp presence (claimed profile, accurate info, a handful of reviews) is worth having. Paid Yelp advertising is a harder case to make.

There's also the "not recommended" filter. Yelp's algorithm hides reviews it considers suspicious — including reviews from customers who created accounts specifically to leave you one. If you ask customers to go to Yelp, many of their reviews will be suppressed. That's not a bug; it's how the platform works. Soliciting Yelp reviews tends to backfire.

**Where the platforms actually matter (in Canada)**

HomeStars — if you're in HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, or renovation in Ontario, HomeStars is where homeowners look. A strong HomeStars profile with verified reviews can generate leads Google doesn't. Worth the investment if you're in those trades.

Houzz — interior designers, renovators, architects. High-intent platform for project-based work. Less relevant for commodity services. If you do kitchens and bathrooms, it's worth setting up.

BBB accreditation — not a review platform in the traditional sense, but a trust signal. Some clients specifically check it before hiring. Low volume but high intent.

Google + one other platform. That's the rule I work from.

Get your GBP generating reviews consistently. Then pick the one secondary platform where your customers actually look for businesses like yours. Don't try to be everywhere — you'll produce mediocre profiles on five platforms instead of a strong presence on two.

The question isn't "should I be on Yelp?" It's "where does my customer look when they're deciding who to hire — and am I showing up there?"

What platforms are you managing reviews on beyond Google?
