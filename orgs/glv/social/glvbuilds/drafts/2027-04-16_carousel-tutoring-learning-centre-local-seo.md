## Slide 2 — Gap 1: GBP Category Precision

**"Tutoring Service" is not the same as "Test Preparation Center."**

If you do IELTS, SAT, LSAT, GRE, or university admissions prep, those are distinct Google categories with different filter behaviour.

Check what the top 3 map pack competitors in your city use in private browser.

Most centres default to "Tutoring Service" at setup — even when test prep drives half their revenue.

---

## Slide 3 — Gap 2: Subject + Grade Level Pages

**"Grade 10 math tutor [city]" is lower competition than "tutoring [city]" — and higher intent.**

One page per subject + grade level combination for your highest-revenue offerings.

Examples:
- "grade 12 chemistry tutor [city]"
- "AP calculus tutor [city]"
- "Ontario grade 11 physics tutoring [city]"

Start with the service driving the most bookings. Add one page per month.

---

## Slide 4 — Gap 3: Individual Tutor Bio Pages

**Each tutor is a separate ranking opportunity.**

Most centres have one team-grid page. That's one ranking opportunity where there could be eight.

Individual bio page formula:
- Full name
- Specific subjects and grade levels
- Degree and faculty ("BSc Mathematics, McMaster")
- Language of instruction if applicable (bilingual families search this)
- Real headshot
- Booking link or contact CTA

---

## Slide 5 — Gap 4: Exam Season Page Timing

**Build exam prep pages before the demand wave — not during it.**

Ontario secondary exam windows: January and June. Build 6–8 weeks before each window.

University exam prep: LSAT (February + June deadlines), MCAT, GRE, GMAT — each has a predictable cycle. One page per test, live before registrations peak.

Pages built after the spike can't recover that traffic until next year.

---

## Slide 6 — Gap 5: GBP Hours Accuracy + Seasonal Closures

**Tutoring centres adjust hours more than almost any other business type.**

Academic year hours ≠ summer hours. March Break hours differ. PD Days affect scheduling.

Incorrect hours = "closed when listed as open" reviews. Fast path to local pack ranking loss.

Update GBP hours each September and June.

Add special hours for PD Days, March Break, and winter holidays — any week where your schedule differs.

## LinkedIn Caption

I've audited local SEO for tutoring centres and private tutors across four cities.

Every one appeared when someone searched their name. Almost none appeared for "math tutor [city] grade 10" or "AP calculus tutor [city]" — the searches that run when a student or parent has a specific exam ahead of them and is ready to book.

Here are the five gaps I find on nearly every account.

**1. GBP category precision**

"Tutoring Service" and "Test Preparation Center" are distinct Google categories with different filter behaviour. If your revenue includes IELTS prep, LSAT coaching, or university admissions consulting, those are separate categories. Check the primary category on the top 3 map pack listings in your city via private browser.

**2. Subject + grade level pages**

"Grade 12 chemistry tutor [city]" has lower competition and higher intent than "tutoring [city]." One dedicated page per subject + grade level combination for your highest-revenue services. Google interprets the specificity as relevance. Start with your most-booked subject. Add one page per month.

**3. Individual tutor bio pages**

Each named tutor is a separate ranking opportunity — name + city + subject. A team-grid page with five names gives you one ranking chance. Five individual bio pages give you five. The formula: full name, specific subjects and grade levels, degree and institution, language of instruction, real headshot, booking link. Most tutoring operations don't have these.

**4. Exam season page timing**

Exam prep pages need to be indexed and ranking before the search demand arrives — not during it. For Ontario secondary schools, that's 6–8 weeks before January and June exam windows. For standardised tests (LSAT, MCAT, GRE, GMAT), each has its own annual registration peak. Build before the wave. Pages launched after a spike wait a full year for their next shot.

**5. GBP hours accuracy + seasonal closures**

Tutoring centres have more hours variation than almost any other local business: academic year hours, summer hours, PD Days, March Break, winter holiday closures. Inaccurate hours generate "closed when listed as open" reviews. Those reviews affect local pack ranking and erode trust faster than almost anything else. Update GBP hours each September and June. Add special hours for any week where your schedule differs.
