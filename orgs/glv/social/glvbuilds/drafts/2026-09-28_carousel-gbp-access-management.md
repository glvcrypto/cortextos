# GBP Access Management — Carousel

## LinkedIn caption

**The most expensive GBP mistake I see isn't getting suspended. It's losing access.**

I've taken on clients who couldn't get into their own Google Business Profile because a former marketing agency owned the primary access. The profile was live, receiving calls, collecting reviews — and my client had no control over it. Getting it back took weeks.

GBP access issues are more common than most local SEOs admit. Here's how the system works, and how to protect your clients from it.

**GBP has three access levels:**
- Primary Owner — one only. Full control. Can transfer ownership or delete the profile.
- Owner — nearly full control. Cannot transfer or delete.
- Manager — day-to-day access. Cannot change ownership.

The business itself should always be the Primary Owner. Not the agency. Not the web developer. Not the employee who set it up years ago.

**The rule for adding vendors:** Always add agencies, contractors, and employees as Managers only. If they leave, you remove them. If they become unresponsive, you still have control. If they add them as Primary Owner, you don't.

**If you're already locked out:** GBP has an ownership request process. You submit a request, Google sends an email to the current owner, and if they don't respond within 7 days, Google reviews the request. Be ready to verify you are the legitimate business owner. The process can take more than two weeks — don't wait until you have an emergency.

**The first thing I check on every new audit:** GBP → Settings → Managers. Who is Primary Owner? Is it actually the client?
