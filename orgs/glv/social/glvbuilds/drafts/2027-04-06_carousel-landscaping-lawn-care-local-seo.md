## Slide 2 — GBP Category Precision

Google has distinct categories: Landscaper, Lawn Care Service, Tree Service, Landscape Designer, and Snow Removal Service.

Each is a separate search filter. If your GBP is set to "Landscaper" and someone searches for "lawn care service [city]," you're invisible.

Check what your top 3 map pack competitors are using as their primary category. Set yours to match your highest-revenue service, then add secondaries for the rest.

---

## Slide 3 — Seasonal Service Pages

"Spring cleanup [city]," "lawn aeration [city]," "overseeding [city]," and "fall cleanup [city]" are four distinct seasonal searches — each with different timing and different buyer intent.

One generic "Services" page tries to serve all of them and ranks for none specifically.

Build one dedicated page per seasonal service. Start with the service that drives the most bookings at peak season. Add the page 6–8 weeks before the seasonal search spike — not the week it starts.

---

## Slide 4 — Seasonal Service Area Documentation

Most landscaping companies adjust their service radius by season. In spring and summer they cover more ground; in winter they may shift to snow removal in a tighter radius.

Document both in GBP: update service cities at the start of each season. Google uses the service area list to determine proximity relevance — an unlisted city is a missed search opportunity.

Takes five minutes to update twice a year in GBP Manager → Edit profile → Service area.

---

## Slide 5 — Portfolio Photos Strategy

For landscaping, GBP photo activity is a stronger trust signal than for most service categories. Customers are making a visual judgement before they call.

Upload 2–3 real project photos per week during active season. Use the "At Work" category. No stock images, no graphics — actual before-and-after project shots or work in progress.

Blur any visible house numbers, faces, or identifying details. Google surfaces these in local pack results. Most landscaping profiles I've audited have one photo from two years ago.

---

## Slide 6 — Review Specificity

Reviews that name a specific service appear as justification text in local pack results.

When asking for a review, prompt: "If you're comfortable, mention the service we did and the time of year."

"Great spring cleanup — they handled everything before the season started" shows as a justification snippet. "Excellent work" doesn't.
