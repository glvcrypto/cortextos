## Slide 2

**Why keyword-stuffed GBP names rank**

Google's algorithm treats the business name field as a strong relevance signal.
A listing named "Toronto Emergency Plumber 24hr" will match that query even if the business isn't real.
It's spam. Google's own guidelines prohibit it. That doesn't stop people from doing it.

---

## Slide 3

**How to spot a spam listing**

Four signals:
1. Name includes the city + service (e.g. "Plumber Toronto Fast Service")
2. Few or no reviews — or a cluster of identical 5-star reviews posted on the same day
3. No website or a redirect to a lead gen page
4. Address is a residential area, virtual office, or UPS store

---

## Slide 4

**How to report it**

In Google Maps:
1. Search for the listing
2. Scroll to "Suggest an edit"
3. Change the name to the actual business name (remove keyword stuffing)
4. Mark address as incorrect if it's a fake location
5. In some cases: flag as permanently closed or report as spam via the three-dot menu

Google reviews these reports. Turnaround: days to weeks.

---

## Slide 5

**What to do in parallel**

Don't wait for Google to act. Strengthen your own signals at the same time:
- Increase review velocity (3–4/month minimum)
- Add more relevant service descriptions in the Services tab
- Ensure your primary category matches the keyword the spam listing is targeting
- Add a GBP post referencing that service + city this week

---

## Slide 6

**When to escalate**

If the report is ignored after 2–3 weeks:
- Post in the Google Business Profile Help Community (staff monitor it)
- File a Business Redressal Complaint Form (for listings with suspected fake info)
- Document the spam listing via screenshots with timestamps — useful if you need to escalate further

The community escalation route works. I've seen listings removed within 48 hours after a community post.
