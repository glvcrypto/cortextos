# 2027-04-24 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Mortgage broker local SEO — licence visibility, intent split, and review signals
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Mortgage brokers are competing against the big banks in local search. Most are running the same local SEO setup as a landscaper."

---

**Beat 1 — GBP category and licence visibility:**
"'Mortgage Broker' is the correct GBP primary category — not 'Financial Institution' and not 'Bank.' The provincial licence number goes in the GBP business description: FSRA in Ontario, BCFSA in BC, RECA in Alberta. The banks have their licences visible everywhere. Most independent brokers have a website that doesn't even mention their registration number. That's a trust gap Google's quality raters notice."

---

**Beat 2 — Three intent types, three pages:**
"'Mortgage broker [city]' is someone comparing options. 'Mortgage rates [city]' is someone already shopping and about to call whoever has the best rate displayed. 'First time home buyer mortgage [city]' is someone who doesn't know what they don't know yet. Three different searchers. Three different pages. One generic 'mortgage services' page serves all of them poorly."

---

**Beat 3 — Review language as conversion signal:**
"In financial services, reviews that mention specific loan types — 'helped us close a self-employed mortgage,' 'refinanced after a variable rate spike' — carry more weight with new clients than star ratings alone. Ask recent clients to mention the loan situation if they're comfortable. Those phrases appear as justification text in local pack results and tell the next searcher that you've handled their specific situation before."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Provincial licence visible. One page per intent type. Review language that mentions the loan situation."

---
