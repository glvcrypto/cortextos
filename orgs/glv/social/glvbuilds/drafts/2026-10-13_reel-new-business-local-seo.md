## LinkedIn Caption

A client launched three months ago. No reviews. No ranking. No website traffic.

Here's the exact order I work in for a brand-new business — because the order matters more than the speed.

**Step 1: GBP verification — before anything else**
The GBP listing is the anchor for everything that follows. Don't build citations, don't push directories, until the profile is verified and complete.

**Step 2: Homepage NAP + LocalBusiness schema**
Name, address, and phone visible on the homepage in text (not an image). LocalBusiness structured data so Google can read it without guessing.

**Step 3: First 5 reviews — guided ask, not incentivised**
Friends, past colleagues, anyone who can speak honestly to working with you. Show them exactly how to leave the review. Don't offer anything in return — Google's guidelines are clear on this.

**Step 4: Three core directories**
Bing Places, Apple Maps, one industry-specific directory relevant to the business category. NAP must match GBP exactly — character for character, not close enough.

**Step 5: One city + service page live**
One page. City name + service in the title tag and H1. Properly formatted. Indexed. Then wait before adding a second — let Google establish what the first page is about.

You won't rank in month one. That's not the goal.

The goal is to build the structure that ranking requires — so when Google is ready to trust a new business, yours is the one with the right signals already in place.
