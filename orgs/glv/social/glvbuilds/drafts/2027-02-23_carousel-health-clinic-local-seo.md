**Slide 2 — GBP category precision**
**"Physiotherapist" is not the same as "Physical therapist" on Google.**
Open a private browser. Search your service in your city.
Check the primary category of the top 3 map pack results.
"Physiotherapist," "Chiropractor," "Dentist," "Registered Massage Therapist" — each is a distinct GBP category.
Match or go one level more specific. Generic categories rank for nothing specific.

---

**Slide 3 — Individual practitioner pages**
**Every named practitioner gets their own bio page.**
Full name. Regulated designation (RMT, DC, PT, DDS). Specific focus or patient population. Real headshot.
Not a team page with four names listed.
Google indexes individual bio pages separately.
Each one is a ranking opportunity for [practitioner name] + [city] searches.

---

**Slide 4 — PHIPA-compliant review asks**
**You can't acknowledge a patient relationship when asking for reviews.**
"If you're comfortable, a Google review mentioning the service you received helps us grow" is compliant.
Asking "as a patient" or referencing a specific appointment is not.
Reviews that name a specific service or practitioner carry stronger local SEO signal than generic 5-stars.
Spread asks across all practitioners — concentration on one name signals imbalance.

---

**Slide 5 — Each practitioner ranks independently**
**Four practitioners = four name + city ranking opportunities.**
Google ranks individual bio pages for searches like "Dr. [Name] Toronto physiotherapist."
Each bio page should include: regulated designation, specialisation, clinic address, and a booking link.
New practitioners added to the clinic get their page built early — not after they've been there a year.

---

**Slide 6 — Multi-location consideration**
**If your clinic has two locations, each gets its own GBP profile.**
Reviews don't transfer between profiles.
A weak Location B GBP pulls down the brand's credibility in searches for that area.
Both locations must be independently verified, completely filled out, and actively collecting reviews.
Never consolidate two active GBP profiles — you'll lose all reviews from the merged one.

---

→ Team page with names but no individual bio pages

## LinkedIn Caption

A physiotherapy clinic asked me to audit their local SEO. Four practitioners. One GBP profile — set to "Health" as the primary category. No individual bio pages. No review collection system.

Here's what I found — and what applies to any multi-practitioner health clinic.

**GBP category precision matters more than most clinics realise.**
Open a private browser. Search your service and city. Check the primary category of the top 3 map pack results. "Physiotherapist," "Chiropractor," "Dentist," "Registered Massage Therapist" — each is a distinct Google category. The difference between "Health" and "Physiotherapist" is the difference between ranking for general health searches and ranking for what your patients actually search.

**Each practitioner needs their own bio page.**
A team page listing four names is not the same as four individual pages. Google indexes bio pages separately. Each one can rank for [practitioner name] + [city] and [specialty] + [city] searches. The format: full name, regulated designation (RMT, DC, PT, DDS), specific clinical focus or patient population, real headshot, clinic address, booking link. Build the page before they've been practising there for a year — the indexation timeline starts whenever you publish.

**Review asks require extra care in health settings.**
You can invite patients to leave a review — you can't acknowledge the patient relationship in writing. A compliant ask sounds like: "If you're comfortable, a Google review mentioning the service you received helps us a lot." Not "as a patient" and not referencing a specific appointment or clinical situation. Reviews that name a specific service or practitioner ("the manual therapy sessions with Dr. [Name]") carry stronger SEO signal than generic 5-stars. Spread the collection system across all practitioners, not just the principal.

**If you have two locations, each needs its own fully verified GBP.**
Reviews don't transfer. A GBP at Location B that's half-filled and has three reviews undermines the brand's credibility for searches in that neighbourhood. Both need independent verification, complete profiles, and active review collection.

The gap in most health clinic local SEO is at the foundation: one generic GBP profile, no bio pages, no compliant review system. The opportunity is right there.
