## Slide 2 — Why GSC for Local SEO

Google Search Console shows you what people are searching before they click your site.

GBP Insights shows calls and direction requests.
GA4 shows what happens after they arrive.

GSC shows you the gap in between — the queries that are sending people your way (or not).

For local SEO, it's the most honest signal you have.

---

## Slide 3 — Report 1: Queries

**Performance → Search results → Queries tab.**

Filter by the city name. What service queries show up?

What I'm looking for:
- Queries with impressions but low clicks (CTR under 3%) — your title or meta description is losing the click
- Queries in position 8–15 — you're close enough to push into the top 5 with a content tweak
- Branded queries — customers already know you; don't waste budget on brand terms

---

## Slide 4 — Report 2: Pages

**Performance → Search results → Pages tab.**

Which pages are sending organic traffic?

What I'm looking for:
- Pages with high impressions but low clicks — they're surfacing but not converting in the SERP
- Pages with zero impressions — indexed but invisible (check for thin content or cannibalisation)
- Service pages ranking for the wrong queries — wrong keyword targeting, needs a rewrite

---

## Slide 5 — Report 3: Index Coverage

**Indexing → Pages.**

Are all your service pages actually indexed?

What I'm looking for:
- "Not indexed" with reason "Crawled — currently not indexed" — Google saw the page and chose not to index it. Usually thin content.
- "Excluded by noindex tag" — someone accidentally blocked a page you need indexed
- Any service page missing from the "Valid" list

---

## Slide 6 — The Monthly Cadence

First Monday of the month. 20 minutes.

1. Queries — flag CTR wins and position 8–15 opportunities. Export to a doc.
2. Pages — identify any invisible pages. Add to the task queue.
3. Index coverage — confirm no new exclusions. Any new "not indexed" pages get investigated before next session.

Share the relevant rows from the Queries export with your client so they see which searches are driving results.

## LinkedIn Caption (300–600 words)

Most people open Google Search Console, look at the impressions graph, feel either good or bad about the direction of the line, and close it.

That's not a reporting process. Here are the three reports I actually use every month for local SEO clients.

**Report 1: Queries**

Performance → Search results → Queries tab. Filter by city name.

I'm looking for three things:
- Queries with impressions but CTR under 3%. Your page is showing up, but the title or meta description isn't winning the click. Fix the copy.
- Queries in position 8–15. You're close to page one. One content improvement can push you into the top 5.
- Branded queries. Customers already know you. If brand terms are eating up your visibility, that's a separate conversation from local SEO.

**Report 2: Pages**

Performance → Search results → Pages tab.

Which pages are actually sending organic traffic? Which have high impressions but low clicks? Which have zero impressions at all?

Zero impressions on a service page usually means one of three things: the page is too thin, another page on the site is competing for the same keywords (cannibalisation), or the page hasn't been indexed yet.

**Report 3: Index Coverage**

Indexing → Pages.

This one catches problems the other reports don't show you. If a service page isn't in the "Valid" list, it's not being ranked — full stop.

The most common culprits: "Crawled — currently not indexed" (Google saw the page and decided it wasn't worth indexing — thin content is the usual cause) and accidental noindex tags.

The monthly cadence: first Monday of the month, 20 minutes. Queries → Pages → Coverage. Export the relevant rows from Queries and share with the client so they can see which searches are driving results.

That's the whole process. No paid tool required.
