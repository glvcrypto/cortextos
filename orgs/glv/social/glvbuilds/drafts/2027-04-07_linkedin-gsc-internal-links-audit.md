# 2027-04-07 — LinkedIn Text Post + IG Static
**Topic:** Using GSC's Internal Links section to find orphan pages and high-authority pages
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## LinkedIn Post

GSC has a Links section most people open once, misread, and never return to.

It's not a problem report — it's a map of your site's internal link structure. And for local service sites, it usually shows the same pattern.

---

**Where to find it**

GSC → Links → Internal links.

It shows every page on your site that has at least one internal link pointing to it, sorted by the number of internal links. Pages that don't appear in the list at all have no internal links pointing to them from the rest of your site.

---

**What the pattern looks like on most local service sites**

The top entries are the homepage and sometimes an About or Contact page — with significantly more internal links than anything else.

The service pages, if they're in the list at all, sit near the bottom with one or two links each. Sometimes fewer.

And pages that appear in GSC's Performance tab with real impressions — pages that Google has found and is showing to searchers — are absent from the internal links list entirely. That means Google found them through the sitemap or a direct URL, not through your own content pointing to them.

---

**Why this matters**

Internal links are one of the signals Google uses to understand which pages on your site are important and what those pages are about.

A service page with one internal link from a sidebar is not being positioned as a priority. A service page that you link to from your homepage, your related-service pages, and your blog posts is a different story.

The anchor text of those links matters too. A link that says "click here" or "learn more" carries almost no signal. A link that says "lawn aeration in [city]" tells Google what the page is about.

---

**The five-minute audit**

GSC → Links → Internal links. Find your top three service pages. Count how many internal links each one has.

Then check if those same pages appear in GSC → Performance → Web with meaningful impressions. If they're getting searches but don't show up in the internal links list, that's the first fix: add two or three internal links from stronger pages, using the target service+city phrase as anchor text.

It doesn't require new content. It requires connecting what's already there.

## IG Static — Graphic Text Concept

"GSC → Links → Internal links shows you which of your pages Google can follow — and which it can only find by accident."

**Body:**
Pages missing from the list have no internal links.

**Caption:**
GSC's Internal Links section shows which pages on your site have other pages pointing to them, and how many. Most local service sites have the same pattern: homepage at the top with dozens of links, service pages with one or two.

A service page that ranks in position 15–20 and has only one internal link pointing to it has a fast potential fix: add two or three links from your strongest pages — homepage, related services, or blog posts — using the target service+city phrase as anchor text.

Check your top service pages in GSC → Links → Internal links before your next round of on-page changes.
