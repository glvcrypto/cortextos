## LinkedIn Post

Local SEO sends traffic. Traffic doesn't tell you if it's working. These four numbers do.

Most local SEO reports show impressions and clicks. Those are inputs, not outcomes. The outcome for a local service business is a phone call or a form submission. The gap between clicks and contacts is where most audits get uncomfortable.

**The four conversion points to track:**

— **GBP call button taps** (GBP Insights → Performance → Calls). Calls triggered directly from the listing without a website visit. This number by itself shows whether your GBP profile is converting people who found you on Maps.

— **Website clicks from GBP** (GBP Insights → Performance → Website clicks). How many people needed to see your site before deciding. High here + low calls = something on the website is stopping the conversion.

— **`tel:` link taps on the website** (GA4 → Events, or GTM phone click event). Calls coming from people who went to your website first. Filter by google/organic in Traffic Acquisition to isolate search-driven contacts.

— **Contact form submissions from organic** (GA4 → Events → filter form_submit, attribute to google/organic). The written-inquiry path.

**The diagnostic:**

High GBP impressions + low GBP calls → profile problem. Category, completeness, photo activity, review count.

High GBP website clicks + low tel: taps or form submissions → website problem. Phone number buried, CTA below fold on mobile, form too long, slow load.

Low GBP impressions + low everything → ranking problem. Work on the signals, not the page.

You don't need a benchmark to know if something's wrong. Track your own baseline for 90 days and look at trends, not absolutes. A 20% month-over-month improvement in call taps tells you more than any industry average.
