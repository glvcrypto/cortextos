## Slide 1 — Hook

Most mortgage broker websites have one problem: they sell mortgages.

Not "a first mortgage for a self-employed buyer trying to avoid CMHC insurance." Not "a mortgage renewal where the lender is offering a posted rate that's 1.5% above what's available elsewhere." Just: mortgages.

Here's why that distinction matters for local SEO — and how to fix it.

---

## Slide 2 — GBP Category and Profile Setup

Google's category for mortgage brokers is "Mortgage Broker." That's the one to use — not "Financial Institution," not "Financial Planner," not "Loan Agency." Those categories attract different searches from different intent stages.

Your GBP profile needs two things most broker profiles don't have:

**Lender network size.** Mentioning that you work with 30+ lenders (or whatever is accurate) is a trust signal that distinguishes an independent broker from a single-lender bank. State it in your profile description and on your website.

**FSRA licence number** (Ontario) or equivalent provincial regulatory number. This is a verifiable trust signal. A client searching "licensed mortgage broker [city]" is looking for exactly this. Display it prominently.

---

## Slide 3 — Per-Product Pages: Stop Lumping Everything Under "Mortgages"

"Mortgage [city]" is one search. But the person searching for a pre-approval is not the same person as the one mid-renewal comparing lenders. Each product deserves its own page:

- **Variable rate mortgage** — how it works, who it's suited for (rate-tolerant buyers, shorter holds), current rate context ("contact for today's live rate")
- **Fixed rate mortgage** — stability framing, typical terms (1yr, 2yr, 5yr), suitable buyer profile
- **Mortgage pre-approval** — the step before the search, timeline expectations, what's needed, what it does and doesn't guarantee
- **Mortgage renewal** — why using a broker at renewal instead of auto-renewing with your current lender can reduce your rate; the 120-day window
- **Refinancing** — equity access, debt consolidation, rate improvement; when it makes sense vs. when it doesn't
- **HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit)** — distinct product with distinct searches; secured vs. unsecured context

One "products" page won't rank for any of these specifically.

---

## Slide 4 — Per-Situation Pages: The Client's Problem Is Not "Mortgage"

The searcher doesn't think in product terms. They think in situation terms.

Four situation-based pages that earn traffic a product page won't:

- **First-time home buyer** — FHSA, RRSP Home Buyers' Plan, CMHC insurance thresholds, stress test — the decision-making layer before the product selection
- **Self-employed mortgage** — stated income vs. traditional qualification; the #1 underserved niche for independent brokers; clients who've been turned down by banks and are searching specifically for a broker who can help
- **Investment property mortgage** — rental income qualifying, CMHC non-eligibility, portfolio approach; distinct search, distinct buyer profile
- **Divorce / separation** — disposition of matrimonial home, buy-out qualification on a single income, spousal name removal from title; sensitive niche with a client who is under deadline pressure

These pages answer the question the client is actually asking.

## Slide 5 — Trust Signals: What Actually Closes the Sale

A mortgage is the largest financial transaction most clients will make. The trust signals that matter:

- **Regulatory licence number** (FSRA in Ontario, BCFSA in BC, AMF in Québec) — publicly searchable; display it in the footer and on your About page
- **Lender network** — number of lenders, types (banks, credit unions, monoline lenders, private) — specificity matters more than a generic "access to many lenders"
- **Reviews that name the situation** — "helped me qualify as self-employed" or "got us a rate well below our bank's offer at renewal" — prompt clients to write specific reviews after closing
- **Turnaround language** — pre-approval in 48 hours, renewal comparison in 24 hours — set expectations precisely; do not guarantee approval outcomes

Do not fabricate rate examples or published rate comparisons unless they are current and verifiable at the time of publication.

---

## Slide 6 — The Renewal Opportunity Most Brokers Miss

Mortgage renewal is the highest-intent, lowest-awareness opportunity in the broker market.

Most clients with a renewal coming up receive a letter from their current lender and auto-renew without shopping. They don't know they can use a broker at renewal. They don't know the posted rate is negotiable. They don't know the process takes a few hours and costs them nothing.

A dedicated renewal page — explaining the 120-day early renewal window, the difference between a posted rate and a negotiated rate, and how to submit documents — captures the client who is open to shopping but doesn't know it's an option.

"Mortgage renewal [city]" is a lower-competition keyword than "mortgage broker [city]." The person searching has a specific trigger. The broker with a page for it wins.
