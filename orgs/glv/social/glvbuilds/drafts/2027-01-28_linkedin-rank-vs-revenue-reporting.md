## LinkedIn Post

Every local SEO client I've worked with asks about rank. Barely any of them track calls.

Rank tells you where you appear in the results. It doesn't tell you what happens after someone sees you there.

Here's the shift I make with every client past month three:

**Lead metric: GBP call volume.**
GBP Insights shows call volume month over month. This is the number that tells you whether Google is actually sending people to contact you — not just showing you. If rank is moving up and calls aren't moving, there's a disconnect worth diagnosing.

**Second metric: form submissions from organic.**
With GTM and GA4 connected, you can show how many contact form submissions came from organic search — not just traffic, but intent that turned into action. This is the setup covered in the Nov 1 post if you haven't done it yet.

**Rank as context, not the headline.**
"You moved from position 9 to position 4 on '[service] [city].'" That's the context for why call volume increased. It belongs in the report. It's not the report.

The reporting shift changes the client conversation. When a client understands that rank is the input and calls are the output, they stop refreshing the rank tracker. They start paying attention to the number that tells them whether the work is actually working.

Three-number framework for local SEO reporting after month three:
- GBP call volume (month over month)
- Form submissions from organic (GTM + GA4)
- Rank position for top 2–3 service keywords (context)

Report them in that order. Lead with conversion, close with position.
