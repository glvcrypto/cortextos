## LinkedIn Caption

Most of my clients think keyword research means plugging terms into a paid tool and downloading a spreadsheet.

I start somewhere simpler: I open an incognito tab and type their service name into Google.

Google Autocomplete shows you what real people in your area are actually typing — not estimated volume from a database, but live predictions based on current search behaviour.

Here's how I use it systematically:

**Step 1: Service + city variations**
Type [service] + [city] and stop. Write every suggestion. Then try the next suburb. Then the next service line. You'll find variations you'd never think to target — and some that confirm your existing pages are right on track.

**Step 2: Problem and qualifier phrases**
Try [service] + near, [service] + for, [service] + licensed, [service] + emergency. These map to different pages: problem phrases belong as FAQ answers on your service page; qualifier phrases belong in your title tag.

**Step 3: Seasonal variation**
Autocomplete is dynamic. Run the same search in September and in March — the suggestions change. That tells you what content to have ready before a season peaks, not after.

**Step 4: Use it to audit what you have**
If Google is autocompleting "[service] in [neighbourhood]" and you don't have a page for that neighbourhood, that's a gap. If you do have the page, check whether you're using the exact phrase customers type.

This takes about 15 minutes per service line. No subscription required. No data export needed. Just a browser in incognito mode and a notes document.

I do this before I touch any paid keyword platform because it anchors me in customer language, not tool language.
