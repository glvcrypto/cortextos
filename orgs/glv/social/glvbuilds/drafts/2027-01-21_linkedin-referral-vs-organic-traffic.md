## LinkedIn Post

Most local business owners assume referrals are their strongest lead source.

Sometimes they are. But here's what GA4 shows when you actually look at the split.

**Referrals are high-trust, high-close.** A client who comes in because their neighbour recommended you is already halfway convinced. You didn't have to earn that attention from scratch. The conversion rate is usually better than any other source.

The ceiling is your network. Referrals are bounded by how many people already know you and how often they talk to someone who needs what you do.

**Organic reaches the people outside that circle.** Different postal codes. Different job types. People who searched "plumber in [neighbourhood]" on a Tuesday evening and had never heard of you before. Seasonal spikes in traffic that referrals don't explain — those are usually organic.

How to check your own split in GA4:
- Traffic Acquisition → Session source / medium
- Compare google / organic against direct and referral segments
- Look at: average session duration, landing page, and whether a conversion event fired

Two things to watch for: if your organic sessions have a much shorter session duration than referral, the landing page probably isn't matching search intent. If organic is barely registering as a segment, your GBP or service pages haven't picked up enough traction yet.

Both sources matter. But treating organic as "nice to have" while word-of-mouth is working is a way of capping your reach at the size of your current network.

The referral base is what you have. Organic is the part you can keep building.
