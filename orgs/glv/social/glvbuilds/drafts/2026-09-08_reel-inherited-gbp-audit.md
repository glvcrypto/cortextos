## LinkedIn Caption

When I'm handed access to a new client's Google Business Profile, the first thing I do isn't add content.

It's audit for damage.

Most profiles that come to me have at least one of these four problems — and sometimes all four.

**Wrong primary category**

Category is the single most important ranking signal in GBP. A business listed in the wrong category is targeting the wrong searches. I've seen a plumbing company listed as "Handyman" and a dental clinic listed under "Medical Clinic." Both were confused about why they weren't showing up in local pack results.

First fix, every time: verify the primary category is the most specific match for the core service.

**Outdated information**

Phone numbers change. Businesses move. Hours shift seasonally. But GBP doesn't update itself.

I check every field — address, phone, website URL, hours including holiday hours — and verify against what's currently on the website. Any mismatch gets corrected before anything else is added.

**Third-party photos**

Anyone with a Google account can upload photos to a business's GBP. Customers upload photos — some relevant, some not. Competitors occasionally upload unflattering images.

I audit every photo in the library, check the source, and flag anything that should be reported for removal.

**Duplicate listings**

This is the one that surprises business owners most. A duplicate GBP means Google is splitting signals between two profiles. Reviews may be scattered across both. Rankings are diluted.

Check by searching the business name from a private browser. If two profiles appear — or one unfamiliar listing shows up — that's a priority fix before any optimisation begins.

Clean up first. Then build.
