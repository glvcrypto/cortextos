**Title:** How GLV Marketing Works: A Step-by-Step Look at Our Process

---

Most agencies are pretty vague about their process.

You get a general overview in the sales call. The work starts. Reports show up. Invoices show up. And it's not always clear what the connection is between the two.

Every GLV client goes through the same structured cycle. Not because it's convenient for us. Because it's what actually produces compounding results. Here's exactly what that looks like.

### Step 1: We Research Your Market First

Before we touch your website or write a word of content, we learn your market.

Your competitive landscape. Who's ranking for the terms your customers search, what they're doing well, where the gaps are. Your target search terms at the intent level, not just the volume level. What your customers need to see before they'll contact someone.

That research drives everything that follows. The content strategy, the site structure, the GBP categories, the ad targeting. Get the research right and every decision downstream is better. We don't skip it to get to the visible work faster.

### Step 2: Then We Build the Foundation

Once we know the landscape, we build.

Website structure, content plan, SEO architecture, local presence. In the right order. Foundation before content. Structure before campaigns.

The order matters more than most people realise. A well-structured site makes content work harder. A strong local presence makes paid ads more efficient. You can't stack things that don't have a base under them. We build the base first.

### Step 3: Then We Execute. Consistently.

The foundation is only worth what you put on top of it. And that work has to be consistent.

Content goes out on schedule. SEO work gets done weekly, not in bursts before a quarterly report. Ads get monitored and adjusted on a regular cadence.

No off weeks. No gaps. No stretches where nothing moves forward.

Consistency is one of the most underrated factors in marketing results. Google rewards sites that publish on a cadence. Email lists decay if they go quiet for months. The businesses that compound are the ones where the work doesn't stop.

### Step 4: We Review Before It Ships

Nothing goes out without a review step.

Strategy calls before major decisions. Content sign-off before it's published. Campaign reports before the next period is planned.

That review layer is what keeps the strategy aligned with what's actually happening in your business. You might have a product launch coming, a seasonal push, something that shifts the priority. The review step is where that information gets in and shapes what comes next.

### Step 5: Watch It Compound

It isn't dramatic in any single month.

Month one, you've got a foundation. Month three, a few pages are ranking. Month six, organic traffic is up and you're getting leads you didn't have six months ago.

By month 12 the marketing is working in a fundamentally different way than it was at the start. The content is indexed and earning. The local presence is established. The campaigns are optimised on real data.

This is what it looks like when the work stacks instead of resets. Not a spike. A curve that keeps going up.

### One Team. One Strategy. One Direction.

You work with one team across everything. The person who knows your SEO strategy also knows your content plan and your ad targeting.

No briefing the same context to three different people. No situation where the email work is going one direction and the SEO work is going another.

One relationship. One standard. Consistent across everything we produce for your business.
