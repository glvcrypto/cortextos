## LinkedIn Post

Most local businesses rank #1 for their own name.

And nowhere else.

This isn't a vanity win. It's a signal that the site has no topical authority outside of branded search — and that any new customer who doesn't already know the business name will never find them organically.

Here's how I diagnose it in 5 minutes:

**1. Search the business name → they rank #1.**
Good. That's baseline.

**2. Search "[service] [city]" → they don't appear.**
That's the gap. The site has no service-page authority for the terms actual customers are typing.

**3. Check GBP. Is the primary category correct?**
Wrong category = wrong local pack, regardless of how good the website is.

**4. Check the service pages. Do they exist?**
Many local business sites have a single "Services" page listing everything in one paragraph. Google can't rank a paragraph — it ranks pages.

The fix isn't complicated. It's a separate page per service, with a clear H1, a geographic modifier where it fits naturally, and internal links from related pages.

One client I worked with had 8 services on a single page. We split them into 8 separate pages. Three months later, 5 of those pages were ranking in the top 10 for their primary local keyword.

No backlinks. No technical changes. Just structure.
