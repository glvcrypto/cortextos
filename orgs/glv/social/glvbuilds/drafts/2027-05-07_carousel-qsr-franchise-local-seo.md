## Slide 2 — Claim and Access Your GBP

The first thing to verify: who is listed as Primary Owner on your location's GBP?

If it's the franchisor or a corporate marketing agency, your ability to update location-specific information — hours changes, holiday closures, your phone number — depends on their response time.

The location owner should hold at least Manager access, and ideally should be Primary Owner if the franchise agreement allows it. If your GBP is locked under corporate ownership and your hours are wrong right now, that is a conversion problem happening today.

Check: Google Maps → your location name → Suggest an edit → see who responds to the ownership claim, or log into GBP Manager to see your current access level.

---

## Slide 3 — Review Responses

Corporate social media teams manage brand reputation at scale. They rarely respond to individual location reviews with location-specific context.

A 1-star review mentioning the drive-through wait time at a specific location needs a response from someone who knows that location, can acknowledge the specific complaint, and can speak to what's being done about it. A corporate template response — or no response — tells the reviewer and every future reader that nobody at that location is paying attention.

Location owners who respond to reviews consistently — within 24–48 hours, by name when appropriate, with specifics — show a higher review-to-conversion rate than those using corporate boilerplate or no responses at all.

---

## Slide 4 — Location-Specific Hours and Special Hours

A franchise location that closes early for a local health inspection, has different hours than the corporate template, or is closed for a day that isn't a national holiday needs to update GBP directly.

Corporate calendars do not manage location-specific variations. If GBP shows incorrect hours during a closure and a customer shows up, the resulting 1-star review is your problem, not corporate's.

Set special hours in GBP for every location-specific variation: reduced staffing days, local event impacts, equipment outages, and statutory holidays that differ by province (e.g., Family Day in Ontario vs. Islander Day in PEI).

---

## Slide 5 — Q&A Seeding

The Q&A section of a GBP listing is publicly editable — anyone can post a question, and anyone can answer it.

At the location level, the questions are specific: "Is there accessible parking at this location?" "Do you have a birthday party booking option?" "Is the McCafe equipment working at this location?" "Do you accept the [local sports team] discount card?"

Seeding five to ten location-specific Q&A entries — answers written by you or your staff — fills the section with accurate information before a frustrated customer does it with inaccurate information. Corporate cannot seed location-specific Q&As across hundreds of locations. You can do yours in 20 minutes.

---

## Slide 6 — Real Location Photos

Corporate brand stock photography appears on GBP listings automatically for most major QSR chains. That photography is identical across every location in the country.

Google's algorithm weights recent, real-location photos — exterior, interior, team, food — higher than stock imagery for local relevance signals. Customers looking at photos want to know what your specific location looks like, not what the brand looks like in a studio shoot.

Add 10–15 real photos: exterior (day and evening), dining area, drive-through (if applicable), team member with permission, and current menu board. Update quarterly. This takes less than 30 minutes and almost no franchise locations do it consistently.
