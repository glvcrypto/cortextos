## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
"There's a second Google search result most local businesses completely ignore.

YouTube videos rank in Google Search. Almost no one in local services is using this."

**Slide 2 — Why YouTube works for local search**
Google owns YouTube and returns video results for many service+city and how-to queries.

Search "how to know when your furnace needs replacing" or "HVAC tune-up [city]" — you'll often see a YouTube video in results, sometimes above organic text links.

The competition for these placements in local service categories is almost always lower than for regular organic text results.

**Slide 3 — Three content formats that work**
1. How-to videos — answer the question a customer asks before calling ("how to know when you need a new [X]")
2. Before/after walkthroughs — show the work in progress (blur any identifying client details)
3. Local area context — "the most common [problem] we see in [city] homes"

Real footage, real location, real results. No production budget required.

**Slide 4 — Channel and video setup**
Channel name: exactly your business name — matches your GBP and website name.

Video title formula: [Service/topic] in [City] | [Business Name] — keep it under 60 characters.

Description: the first 200 characters are indexed by Google. Lead with service + city + what the video covers, then include your full NAP.

**Slide 5 — Embed on your service page**
After publishing, embed the video on the relevant service page on your website.

This does two things: (1) the page now has video content, which Google tracks under the Video search type in GSC; (2) the YouTube video gets view signals from your site traffic.

**Slide 6 — What to track**
GSC → Performance → Search type dropdown → Video. This shows impressions and clicks specifically from video results — completely separate from the default Web view. Switch to this tab to see it.

YouTube Analytics: watch time and impression click-through rate per video.

**Slide 7 — CTA**
One video per service area per quarter. In 12 months, you'll have a video presence for every core service — and your competitors won't have noticed you building it.

## LinkedIn Caption

I started looking at the Video search type in Google Search Console for local service clients this year.

Most had zero impressions. Not because they were ranking badly — because they had no videos indexed at all.

The competition for video results in local service categories is almost nonexistent. And Google frequently serves YouTube video results for service+city and how-to queries — sometimes above regular organic results.

**Why YouTube specifically:**
Google owns it. YouTube videos are indexed directly in Google's search engine. A well-optimised video for "how to know when your roof needs replacing" or "plumbing inspection [city]" can appear in Google Search results without touching your website's domain authority at all.

**Three formats that work for local service businesses:**
1. How-to videos that answer the question a customer asks before calling (not "choose us" content — answer the question they actually have)
2. Before/after walkthroughs of real work (blur or exclude any identifying client details)
3. Local area context: "the most common [problem] we see in [city] homes"

**Basic setup:**
- Channel name = exact business name (NAP consistency applies here too)
- Video title: [Service/topic] in [City] | [Business Name] — under 60 characters
- Description first 200 characters: service + city + what the video covers + your NAP

Embed each video on the relevant service page. Then track impressions under GSC → Performance → Search type: Video.

One video per service area per quarter. Four videos per service line per year. In 12 months, you'll have a video presence that your competitors haven't noticed you building.
