## LinkedIn Post

I run local SEO audits for tutoring centres regularly. The gap I see most often isn't a technical issue — it's that the entire website is written as if every student needs the same thing.

Here's what an audit finds almost every time:

**GBP category**
"Tutoring Service" is the primary category. If you offer test prep specifically (IB, AP, OSSLT, EQAO prep), "Test Preparation Center" is a valid secondary category. Both attract distinct searches. Many centres only list one.

**One page per subject**
"Math tutoring [city]," "English tutoring [city]," and "French tutoring [city]" are separate searches with different parents and students searching. A single "subjects we cover" page listing everything in a grid ranks for none of them specifically. The centre that has a math tutoring page — what grade levels are covered, which curriculum (Ontario, IB, AP), which teacher credentials — wins that search.

French tutoring is a particularly high-demand, underserved keyword in Ontario bilingual markets and French immersion catchments. A dedicated page for French language tutoring — core French, French immersion, FSL — is worth building even for centres where it is a secondary service.

**Credential signals**
Ontario-certified teachers (OCT registration) are a verifiable trust signal for parents. List it — it is publicly searchable on the Ontario College of Teachers register. Subject specialists with relevant degrees are also worth naming. Do not fabricate qualifications; list what is accurate.

**Seasonal timing**
Three peaks for tutoring search volume in Ontario:
- September: back-to-school catch-up, new grade adjustment
- February–March: mid-year exam season, OSSLT (Grade 10 literacy test) timing
- May–June: EQAO testing, finals, credit recovery

Pages and GBP posts should go live at least six weeks before each peak. A centre that builds its EQAO prep page in May is too late for the searches happening in April.

**Online vs. in-person**
These are different searches. A parent looking for "online math tutoring" is not the same parent as one searching "math tutor near me." If you offer both, they need separate pages — different format, different booking path, different objection handling.
