# 2027-04-29 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Roofing contractor local SEO — seasonal windows, licence/insurance visibility, photo cadence
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Roofing has three distinct search windows per year. Most roofing companies have content for one of them."

---

**Beat 1 — The three search windows:**
"Spring inspection season runs April–May — the first warm weeks after snow melt, when homeowners notice damage from winter. Fall pre-winter runs September–October — before the first snow, when they want to check condition before it gets worse. And then there's storm damage emergency: no season, high urgency, mobile search, phone call within minutes or the job goes to whoever picks up. Three windows. Three different pages. Three different conversion requirements. One 'roofing services' page handles none of them specifically."

---

**Beat 2 — Licence and insurance visible on the page:**
"Two things a homeowner checks before hiring a roofing company: are you licensed, and are you insured? Provincial contractor licensing varies — Ontario, BC, and Alberta each have different requirements. What doesn't vary: if your liability insurance certificate and WCB or WSIB clearance letter aren't visible on your website, a homeowner who does their due diligence will call someone else who has them posted. 'Licensed and insured' in text is not the same as showing the documents."

---

**Beat 3 — Photo cadence after every job:**
"Before-and-after photos from each project — uploaded to your GBP At Work album the week the job finishes — do two things. They build a local visual record of the neighbourhoods you work in, which reinforces geographic relevance for local search. And they give clients a concrete starting point for reviews: 'replaced our roof after ice dam damage' rather than 'great roofer.' Both signals — the photos and the specific review language — compound over time."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Three seasonal pages. Insurance visible. Before-and-after photos after every job."

---

## Production Notes

- Text overlays per beat — one sentence at a time
- No client addresses, property identifiers, or damage visible in any footage that could identify a home
- Captions on
- Showing a before/after split or a GBP At Work album upload screen works well for Beat 3
- Seasonal timing: April post aligns with spring inspection season — relevant to post immediately
