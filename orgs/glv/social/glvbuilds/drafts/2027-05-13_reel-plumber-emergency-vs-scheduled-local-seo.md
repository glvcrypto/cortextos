# 2027-05-13 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Plumber local SEO — emergency page vs. scheduled service page: two customers, two conversion patterns, two page requirements
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Most plumbing websites have one services page for two completely different customers. Here's why that costs bookings."

---

**Beat 1 — The emergency customer:**
"Someone searching 'emergency plumber [city]' at 11pm with a burst pipe is not browsing. They need a phone number, confirmation you cover their area, and a signal that you're available right now. Load time matters more than any other page on your site — a 4-second load loses this customer before the page is visible. The phone number needs to be above the fold on mobile, tap-to-call, with no form required. '24/7 emergency plumbing' needs to appear in the first three words on the screen. A general services page — with a contact form, portfolio section, and 'about us' paragraph — fails this customer completely."

---

**Beat 2 — The scheduled service customer:**
"Someone searching 'bathroom renovation [city]' or 'water heater replacement [city]' is not in crisis. They're comparing two or three plumbers over a few days. They want to see previous work, understand the process, know approximately what it costs, and read reviews from people who had the same job done. A quick-contact form is fine here — this customer will fill it out. But a gallery, a process section, and reviews that mention the specific job type are what move them from 'considering' to 'calling.' The same urgency-first design that wins the emergency customer actively repels the scheduled-service customer."

---

**Beat 3 — The fix:**
"Two customers. Two conversion patterns. Two pages. One emergency services page with phone above fold, minimal design, fast load, explicit 24/7 statement. One scheduled service page per major job type — bathroom renovation, water heater, drain service, sewer line — with gallery, process, pricing range, and reviews. Neither page needs to exist on the other's terms. Most plumbing sites have one 'services' page that tries to be both and succeeds at neither."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Emergency page: phone, availability, speed. Scheduled page: gallery, process, price, reviews. One site, two pages, two different customers."
