## LinkedIn Post

When I pull GSC for a trades client — plumber, electrician, HVAC tech, roofer — I segment queries into three buckets before I touch anything else on the site.

Each bucket has a different page requirement and a different conversion pattern.

**Bucket 1: Emergency queries**
"Plumber near me emergency." "24 hour electrician [city]." "Furnace not working [city]." "Flooded basement [city]."

The person doing this search is in crisis mode. They will call the first listing that signals it can help right now.

The page requirements for emergency queries are different from every other page on the site: phone number above the fold, no-form-required contact option, explicit statement of 24/7 or after-hours availability, load time under 2 seconds (a 4-second load loses this customer before the page renders), and a clear service area statement so they know you cover their neighbourhood.

Most trades sites have a general "emergency services" line buried in the footer. That is not an emergency page.

**Bucket 2: Scheduled service queries**
"Bathroom reno [city]." "Electrical panel upgrade [city]." "Furnace installation [city]." "Roof replacement quote [city]."

This customer is not in crisis. They're comparing. They want to know who does the best work, what the process looks like, approximately what it costs, and what other customers say.

Page requirements: a gallery or before-and-after section, detailed process walkthrough (so they know what to expect), a pricing page or pricing range (even "starting from X" reduces hesitation significantly), and reviews that name the service type. A quote form is fine here — this customer will fill one out.

**Bucket 3: Seasonal queries**
"Furnace tune-up [city]." "AC installation [city]." "Eavestrough cleaning [city]." "Roof inspection [city]."

These searches spike on a predictable calendar. The 6-week lead time rule applies: content targeting seasonal queries needs to be live and indexed 6 weeks before the season peak, not published the week searches start climbing.

A furnace tune-up page published in October, when people are turning on heat for the first time, misses the indexing window entirely. That same page published in August ranks by the time the volume arrives.

---

Most trades businesses have one "services" page that addresses all three buckets inadequately. The businesses that dominate their local markets have separate pages — different conversion intent, different page design, different content — for each one.

Three GSC query buckets for trades accounts — each needs a different page and a different conversion approach:

**Emergency queries** ("plumber near me emergency," "24 hour electrician [city]"): phone above the fold, no-form-required option, explicit 24/7 signal, under 2-second load time. The person searching has no patience for a slow page or a hidden contact number.

**Scheduled service queries** ("bathroom renovation [city]," "electrical panel upgrade [city]"): gallery, process walkthrough, pricing range, reviews naming the service. This customer is comparing. Give them what they need to choose.

**Seasonal queries** ("furnace tune-up [city]," "AC installation [city]"): 6-week lead time rule. If the page isn't live and indexed 6 weeks before the season starts, it misses the traffic window. Publishing in October for a heat-season query means starting from scratch next year.

Most trades sites handle all three with one "services" page. The ones that rank well have three different pages with three different conversion designs.
