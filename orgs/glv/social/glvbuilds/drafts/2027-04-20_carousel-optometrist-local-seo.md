## Slide 2 — GBP Category Precision

"Optometrist" is the correct primary GBP category for most optometry clinics.

The gap is that "Optometrist," "Eye Care Center," and "Optician" are separate categories in Google's taxonomy with different filter behaviour. A clinic that dispenses eyewear alongside clinical exams often qualifies for both Optometrist and Eye Care Center — using only one leaves patients using the wrong filter unable to find you.

Check what your current primary and secondary categories are set to. Category changes take 24–48 hours to propagate.

---

## Slide 3 — Service-Specific Pages

"Eye exam [city]," "children's eye exam [city]," "contact lens fitting [city]," and "dry eye treatment [city]" are different searches made by different patients at different urgency levels.

One "Services" page listing all of them ranks for none specifically.

A children's exam page can include a note about OHIP coverage for patients under 20 — a distinct purchase driver that adults booking for themselves don't have. That difference in intent justifies a separate page.

Start with the service that drives the most new patient bookings.

---

## Slide 4 — Individual OD Bio Pages

Each optometrist in your practice is a separate local SEO opportunity.

A dedicated bio page — full name, OD designation, provincial registration number (College of Optometrists of Ontario, BCDO, ACOO, or equivalent), clinical interests, languages spoken, and a real headshot — creates one ranking opportunity per practitioner.

Google quality raters check for licence and registration specificity on regulated health practitioner pages. A team-grid with four names is one opportunity where there could be four.

---

## Slide 5 — Insurance and Benefits Information

A significant percentage of patients booking eye exams are driven by extended health benefit renewal windows — not by a clinical need.

"Does [plan name] cover eye exams?" is a real search. A page that answers which major insurance plans are accepted, what coverage typically looks like, and how to confirm benefits before booking removes a purchase objection that otherwise sends patients to a chain.

This page does not need to list specific plan amounts — those change. "We direct-bill most major extended health plans — contact us to confirm your coverage" is defensible language that removes the friction.

---

## Slide 6 — Three Seasonal Windows

Optometry has three distinct demand spikes: back-to-school (August–September), January benefits reset (January–February when annual vision benefits renew), and spring (March–April, lower than the other two but consistent).

Each window has a 6-week lead time for content. Publishing an "eye exam for back to school" page in September has missed the peak.

Build the seasonal page once. Update the publish date and any time-sensitive details. Six weeks before each window, add an internal link from the homepage.
