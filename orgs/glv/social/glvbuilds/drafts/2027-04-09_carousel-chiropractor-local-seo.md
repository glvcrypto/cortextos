## Slide 2 — GBP Category Precision

"Chiropractor" is the correct primary GBP category for most chiropractic clinics.

The issue is at the service page and Services tab level. "Back pain," "sports injury," "disc herniation," and "prenatal chiropractic" are distinct condition searches — each with different urgency, different patient intent, and lower competition than the generic category term.

Check what queries your clinic appears for in GSC Performance → Queries. The condition-specific ones are where the quick movement is.

---

## Slide 3 — Condition-Specific Landing Pages

"Back pain chiropractor [city]," "sports injury chiropractic [city]," "neck pain treatment [city]," and "headache relief chiropractic [city]" are separate searches with different urgency levels.

One "Services" page that lists all conditions ranks for none of them specifically.

Build one dedicated page per highest-demand condition. Start with the condition that drives the most new patient bookings — not the one you already rank for.

---

## Slide 4 — Individual DC Bio Pages

Each chiropractor is a separate local SEO asset.

A dedicated bio page — full name, DC designation, provincial college registration number (College of Chiropractors of Ontario, BCCA, ABCA, or equivalent), specific techniques (ART, Graston, Webster, dry needling where scope permits), real headshot — creates one ranking opportunity per practitioner.

A team-grid page with three names and headshots is one opportunity where there could be three. Google quality raters check for designation and registration specificity on regulated health practitioner pages.

---

## Slide 5 — PHIPA-Compliant Review Asks

You cannot acknowledge a patient relationship in writing. Compliant review-ask framing: "If you're comfortable, a Google review mentioning the area you were treated for helps other patients find our clinic."

Reviews naming a specific condition or technique appear as justification text in local pack results.

"Helped with chronic lower back pain" in a review shows as a justification snippet in Maps. "Great chiropractor" doesn't carry the same local SEO signal.

---

## Slide 6 — GBP Services Tab

Add one entry per condition or technique to the GBP Services tab. Write each description in patient search language, not clinical terminology.

Formula: [condition/technique] + [city] + [one specific detail about the approach].

Google indexes Services tab text. These descriptions become justification text in local pack results — the same as review phrases, but within your full control.
