## LinkedIn Caption

I don't delete old service pages when they stop ranking.

I refresh them — and it's usually faster than writing a new page from scratch.

Here's the framework I use to decide when and how to refresh a local service page.

**Slide 1 — Hook**
Your service pages don't have a shelf life. But they do have a refresh trigger.

**Slide 2 — The 3 refresh triggers**
1. Position 4–15 in GSC for 90+ days with no movement
2. Traffic declined 20%+ year-over-year with no algorithm explanation
3. Outdated specifics: prices, staff names, service areas, or tools mentioned no longer accurate

**Slide 3 — What to update first**
Title tag + H1: make the service + city pairing more specific.
First paragraph: lead with the searcher's problem, not your company intro.
FAQ section: pull the latest People Also Ask (PAA) results and add 2–3 fresh Q&As.

**Slide 4 — Add, don't just swap**
Don't just replace old copy — expand it.
Add a new section covering a service variation or nearby area you now serve.
Google rewards pages that grow in usefulness, not just pages that get reworded.

**Slide 5 — Schema and internal links**
Recheck your LocalBusiness + Service schema after updates — outdated values undermine the copy you just fixed.
Add or tighten one internal link: anchor text should name the exact destination service.

**Slide 6 — Signal the update to Google**
After saving: update the "last updated" date if your theme supports it.
Resubmit the URL in GSC → URL Inspection → Request indexing.
Check crawl status within 48 hours.

**Slide 7 — CTA**
Comment REFRESH and I'll send you the one-page audit template I use before touching any existing page.

I run this refresh audit quarterly on every active client's top-5 service pages.

Most of the time, it's not that the page is bad — it's that it was written once and never touched again.

Google notices.

The four most common gaps I find: title tag still generic from launch day, FAQ section empty, service area not mentioned in body copy, schema values pointing to an old phone number or address.

None of them require a developer. All of them matter.

The refresh trigger I see most often: position 6–12, consistent impressions, but CTR under 2%. That's a title tag and meta description problem, not a content problem. Fix the title first before rewriting everything.

If a page has been stuck at position 11–15 for six months, the problem is usually that it's too thin to overtake a competitor with 600+ words and a full FAQ. Add content.

If traffic dropped but rankings held, the problem is usually seasonal or a SERP feature (featured snippet, map pack, image carousel) now stealing clicks at the top. Different fix.
