# Carousel: You offer multiple services. Which one do you optimise for local SEO first?

## Slide 1 — Hook

"You have one GBP listing and five services to rank for.

Local SEO has a sequencing problem. Here's how I solve it."

## Slide 2 — Why sequence matters

Your GBP primary category is a single choice. It carries significant weight in local ranking.

Your website's authority distributes across every page. Spread it equally across five service pages and none rank well. Focus it on one and it compounds — then you build the next.

Trying to rank for everything at once is one of the most common reasons multi-service businesses rank strongly for nothing.

## Slide 3 — The selection framework

Three criteria, in order:

1. **Revenue** — which service generates the most income?
2. **GSC momentum** — which service keyword already has impressions? A page at position 10–20 is easier to move than building from zero.
3. **Competition gap** — which service has the weakest competition in the local map pack right now?

Start with the service that scores highest across all three. When they point in different directions, revenue wins.

## Slide 4 — Check GSC before you decide

GSC → Performance → Queries. Filter by each service keyword.

If one service already has impressions and is sitting at position 10–20: that page has existing momentum. A title tag improvement and one internal link tighten can move it before anything built from scratch would.

"Protect what's already moving before you push something that isn't."

## Slide 5 — The GBP primary category decision

Set the GBP primary category to match your priority service.

Check: what primary category are the top 3 competitors in your area using for that service search?

Secondary categories: add all remaining services here. They contribute relevance signals without diluting the primary one. Don't change primary category impulsively — it can temporarily affect all rankings. Make the change once, correctly.

## Slide 6 — The build sequence after service 1

Service 1 page indexed and showing rank movement → then add service 2 page.

Link service 2 from service 1 — you're passing authority from an established page to a new one.

Cadence: one new service page every 30–60 days, each linked to and from the primary service page. After 6 months, service 1 still ranks well and services 2 and 3 are building momentum.

## Slide 7 — The mistake to avoid

Thin pages for every service, published at once, with equal internal links and no clear hierarchy.

Google can't determine which service matters most. You can.
