# Google Review Velocity — Carousel

## LinkedIn caption

**Getting 50 reviews in your first month and then nothing for six months is worse than getting three reviews every single month. Google doesn't reward volume. It rewards consistency.**

This is the most common review mistake I see in local SEO audits. A business runs a one-time "please leave us a review" push — friends, family, loyal customers. Thirty reviews in two weeks. Then nothing for a year. The review count looks fine. The signal doesn't.

Review velocity — the rate at which new reviews arrive — is a ranking factor in local search. Consistent incoming reviews indicate an active, trusted business. A long gap followed by a burst can flag as an unnatural pattern. A slow but steady drip reads as a healthy operation.

My target for every client: a minimum of three to four new reviews per month. Not a campaign. A system with three sources.

**Source 1: In-person ask at the moment of satisfaction.** The best time to ask is when the customer expresses satisfaction — at pickup, at the end of a service call, when they say "this looks great." Script it: "I'm glad it worked out. If you have 30 seconds, a Google review would help us a lot." Train anyone on the team who has face-to-face contact to say this out loud.

**Source 2: QR card at checkout or in your location.** A printed card or sticker with a QR link to your GBP review page. No typing required. Works at any physical touchpoint — checkout desk, service counter, delivery packaging.

**Source 3: Same-day follow-up text.** Within a few hours of completing work, a short text: "Hi [name], thanks for choosing us today. If you have a moment, we'd appreciate a Google review — [link]." Keep it to two sentences. One link. No pressure language.

**Slow season:** In off-peak months, cut the target in half. Three reviews in December is better than zero. Don't pause the system entirely — it's harder to restart than to maintain.

**Monitoring cadence:** I check review count and velocity at the start of every monthly report. Two months in a row below target means the system needs a fix — not just a reminder to the client.
