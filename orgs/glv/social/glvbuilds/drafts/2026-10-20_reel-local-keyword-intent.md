## LinkedIn caption

A new client came in ranking #1 for their own business name on Google Maps. Great.

They ranked nowhere for "[their service] [their city]." Not great.

Here's the diagnostic I ran:

**Check GBP Insights → Searches**

The search terms tab in GBP Insights shows what queries are surfacing your listing. If you're seeing mostly brand-name or navigational queries, Google knows you exist — but hasn't strongly associated you with the transactional category in your service area.

These are two separate ranking systems, and you can rank well in one without the other.

**The three fastest fixes for a transactional intent gap:**

1. **Services tab** — add every service you offer with a one-sentence description. Include the service and city in the description text. This is one of the strongest on-profile signals Google uses for category matching.

2. **GBP description** — make sure the first sentence is "[Service] in [City]." Not your tagline. Not your founding year. The service and the city, in that order.

3. **Primary category** — look at what category your top 3 competitors use. If your primary category is broader than theirs (e.g. you're "Contractor" and they're "Roofing Contractor"), that gap matters. Match or beat the specificity.

The client I mentioned: three changes, 28 days, profile views for the service keyword query went up substantially. The navigational ranking stayed intact.
