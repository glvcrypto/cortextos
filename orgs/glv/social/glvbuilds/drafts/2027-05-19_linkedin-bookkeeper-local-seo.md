## LinkedIn Post

When I set up local SEO for an independent bookkeeper, the first thing I check is the Google Business Profile category.

"Bookkeeper" is a GBP category. It is distinct from "Accountant" and "Tax Preparation Service." The three categories have different filter behaviour in Maps and different search intent behind them.

Someone searching "bookkeeper [city]" is looking for ongoing monthly financial support — payroll, bank reconciliation, GST/HST remittances, month-end close. They are not looking for an annual tax filing service. A bookkeeping practice listed as "Accountant" on GBP appears in the wrong filter and speaks to the wrong search intent. The two-minute fix changes who finds the listing.

**Why bookkeeping local SEO is different from accounting local SEO**

Accounting and tax preparation are seasonal. Bookkeeping is not. A business owner looking for a bookkeeper has a need that starts now and continues month to month. The search volume is lower than tax season peaks but it is consistent year-round. Content and GBP management that would work for a tax preparation service — heavy January through April, quiet otherwise — does not match how bookkeeping clients search or decide.

**Software-specific pages**

"QuickBooks bookkeeper [city]." "Xero bookkeeper [city]." "FreshBooks bookkeeper [city]."

Business owners searching these queries have already chosen their accounting software and are looking for a bookkeeper who knows it. A software-specific page — what the software does, what migration from another platform looks like, what the ongoing workflow involves — captures a search that a general "bookkeeping services" page will not rank for specifically.

A bookkeeper proficient in QuickBooks Online, Xero, and Wave should have a page for each. That's three additional ranking opportunities the general services page does not cover.

**Industry specialisation pages**

"Bookkeeper for contractors [city]." "Restaurant bookkeeper [city]." "E-commerce bookkeeper [city]."

Business owners in specialised industries search with those terms because they want someone who understands their specific invoicing patterns, GST/HST implications, payroll setup, or revenue recognition. A bookkeeper who works primarily with contractors — job costing, holdback, WCB remittances — has a knowledge base that a general business owner doesn't need and a contractor specifically wants.

A page for each specialisation answers those searches. A general "we work with all types of businesses" paragraph does not.

**Professional scope: what a bookkeeper does and doesn't do**

Copy should be clear about what a bookkeeper handles and what requires a CPA. Bookkeepers prepare financial records; CPAs provide assurance, file corporate tax returns, and provide professional opinions on financial statements. Conflating the two overstates scope and creates client expectation problems.

"We handle your books so your CPA has clean, organised records at year-end" is accurate and frames the relationship correctly. It also speaks to a client's actual question: "Do I need a CPA or a bookkeeper?" Answering that question clearly and correctly on the website builds trust before the first call.
