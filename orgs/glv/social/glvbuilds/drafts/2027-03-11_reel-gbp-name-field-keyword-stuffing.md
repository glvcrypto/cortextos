**Topic:** GBP name field — what you can and cannot put there
**CTA:** Open comment
**Flags:** None

## Script

**[Hook — text on screen]**
There's a GBP field most businesses are quietly abusing. And it's the first one Google's quality raters check.

**[Cut — on screen]**
The name field. Your legal business name goes here. Nothing else.

**[Step 1 — on screen]**
What I see in audits: "Joe's Plumbing | Trusted Toronto Plumber | 24/7 Emergency Service."

That's not a business name. That's keyword stuffing.

**[Step 2 — on screen]**
Google's guidelines: the name field must match your actual registered or DBA business name. City, service descriptor, ranking keywords — not allowed.

One exception: if the city is legally part of your name ("Toronto Tile and Grout Inc."), it belongs there. If it's not — it doesn't.

**[Step 3 — on screen]**
Consequence: a competitor can flag your listing via "Suggest an edit" → "Business name is inaccurate." Google sometimes auto-applies it. Repeat flags can trigger a profile warning or suspension.

**[Payoff — on screen]**
The name field isn't where you rank. GBP category, Services tab, website copy, and reviews do that work.

Fix the name field before someone else does it for you.

**[CTA — on screen]**
Check your GBP name field right now.
