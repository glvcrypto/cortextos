## LinkedIn Caption

The florist category has one of the sharpest intent gaps I see in local SEO. A customer searching "florist [city]" is browsing. A customer searching "same-day flower delivery [city]" has already decided to buy.

Here's what a florist website audit typically surfaces:

**Same-day delivery: its own page**
"Same-day flower delivery [city]" is the highest-converting search in the flower category. The client is ready to purchase — they need to confirm you can deliver, what the cutoff time is, what the delivery area covers, and whether their occasion is achievable on short notice. A dedicated same-day page that answers those four questions converts. A homepage that mentions delivery in a paragraph does not.

**Occasion-based pages**
Most florist websites have a gallery and a contact form. The occasions that generate the most searches each warrant their own page:

- **Wedding florist** — distinct niche, distinct buyer (often the couple's first purchase with a florist, high consideration), distinct timeline (months in advance vs. same-week); consultation process, style portfolio, whether you handle delivery and setup
- **Funeral flowers** — the buyer is typically a family member under time and emotional pressure; page tone matters; lead time and what's appropriate to order should be answered directly
- **Corporate / office flower delivery** — standing orders, subscription arrangements, invoice billing; B2B page with B2B language
- **Anniversary / birthday** — shorter lead time, gift-card pairing, delivery confirmation options

Each of these is a separate search from a separate buyer at a separate moment.

**Seasonal timing**
Valentine's Day (Feb 14), Mother's Day (second Sunday in May), prom season (May–June), and wedding season (May–Oct) are predictable peaks. A Valentine's Day landing page live in late December captures the January searches. A page built February 10 misses most of the window. The rule is: live six weeks before the peak.

**GBP: "Florist" as primary category**
"Florist" is the correct primary GBP category — not "Flower Shop" (valid but lower search match in most markets), not "Gift Shop." Photos should show real arrangements, not stock images. GBP posts timed to upcoming occasions — a Valentine's post in January, a Mother's Day post in late April — reinforce seasonal intent.
