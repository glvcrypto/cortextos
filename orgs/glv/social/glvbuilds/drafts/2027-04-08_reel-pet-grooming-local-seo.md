# 2027-04-08 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Pet grooming local SEO — the search intent split and the GBP photo gap
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Dog groomers have one of the tightest local search windows I track. And one of the most avoidable GBP gaps."

---

**Beat 1 — The search intent split:**
"'Dog groomer near me' is a same-week search. That person needs an appointment soon. Your phone number and next available slot need to be visible before they scroll. 'Dog grooming salon [city]' is a browse — they're comparing. Both end up on the same page on most grooming sites, and that page isn't built for either intent."

---

**Beat 2 — The breed-specificity gap:**
"'Doodle grooming [city].' 'Double-coat grooming [city].' 'Puppy first groom [city].' These are lower-competition searches with high appointment intent. One page that says 'we groom all breeds' ranks for none of them specifically. One page per service type changes that."

---

**Beat 3 — The GBP photo gap:**
"Before-and-after grooming photos, with the breed noted in the photo description and uploaded in the 'At Work' category, are one of the strongest conversion signals in this category. Customers are making a visual decision. Most grooming profiles I audit have three photos and no updates in months."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Phone number visible on first scroll. One page per service type. Before-and-after photos updated weekly in season."
