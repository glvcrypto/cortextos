## LinkedIn Caption (posted with reel embed)

Most local SEO audits check LCP. Fewer check CLS and INP — the two other Core Web Vitals that Google uses to evaluate page experience.

**CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift):** Measures how much the page moves around while loading. Images without defined dimensions and fonts that load late are the most common causes. Target: under 0.1. Easy to miss until you test on a slow mobile connection.

**INP (Interaction to Next Paint):** Replaced FID (First Input Delay) in March 2024. Measures how quickly your page responds to any click or tap — not just the first one. Target: under 200ms. Heavier JavaScript bundles and render-blocking scripts are the usual causes.

Where to check: PageSpeed Insights → Performance section. Run it on mobile, not just desktop.

The most common CLS fix for small business websites: images without explicit width and height attributes in the HTML. Two-line change. Instant improvement.

Check both in PageSpeed Insights → Performance → Mobile tab.
