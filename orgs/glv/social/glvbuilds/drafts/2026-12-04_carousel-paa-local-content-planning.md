## LinkedIn Caption

Before I write a service page, I search the service in incognito.

Not to check rankings. To read the People Also Ask box.

PAA questions are real customer questions — confirmed by search volume, organised by intent. They're Google telling you what your target customer is still unsure about after they found your listing.

Here's how I turn that into a content plan:

**Step 1: Research**
Search [service] + [city] in incognito. Find the PAA box. Click each question — the box expands to show more. Write down every question that applies to your business. I usually get 15–20 per service.

**Step 2: Categorise by intent**
Cost questions → FAQ answer addressing price range and what affects it (don't give exact prices if they vary — use "call for current pricing" or a range if it's stable).
Process questions → FAQ answer with specific steps or timeline.
Comparison questions → FAQ answer where you take a clear position.
Qualification questions → FAQ answer that builds authority (reference credentials, standards, or regulations where applicable).

**Step 3: Write answers to rank**
Format matters. Open with a direct answer to the exact question. Keep the core answer to 40–60 words. Add a follow-up paragraph for context. Wrap in H3 tags. Add FAQPage schema.

That structure gives Google what it needs to pull your answer into the PAA box — and increasingly, into AI Overviews.

**Step 4: Organise into your page**
A FAQ section on your service page with 6–10 questions covers most of what customers ask before they call. Each question gets its own H3. The schema reinforces the structure.

This is one of the fastest ways to add useful, rankable content to a service page that's already live.

Comment PAA and I'll send you the template I use for the FAQ section structure and schema.
