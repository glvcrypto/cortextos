# Reel: Writing a Case Study for Your Local Service Page

## Script

**Hook:** "A 200-word case study on a service page outperforms 800 words of generic copy. Here's the three-part structure I use."

**Part 1 — Problem.** One sentence. Client situation, no identifying details. "A plumbing company in [city] was showing up in GSC for furnace repair queries — but their service page had no content about the repair process."

**Part 2 — What you did.** Three specific steps. "Added a 200-word process section. Fixed the H1 to include service + city. Added a three-question FAQ block using PAA answers."

**Part 3 — Result.** Measurable if you have the data — defensible if you don't. "Calls from that page went up within six weeks." Don't fabricate a specific number. "Fully booked within two weeks" is defensible if true. "300% increase in leads" requires actual tracking data.

**Where it goes:** Below the FAQ block on the service page. 150–200 words is enough. One case study per service.

## LinkedIn Caption (~340 words)

A 200-word case study placed at the bottom of a service page outperforms 800 words of generic service description copy.

Here's why — and the three-part structure I use to write them.

**Why it works**

Generic copy says "we're experienced, trusted, and committed to quality." A case study says "here's a specific situation, here's what we did, here's what happened." One of those is what a potential customer actually wants to know before they call.

Google also reads it differently. A case study introduces specific terms — the problem type, the service, the location, the process — that generic "we offer [service] in [city]" copy doesn't.

**The structure**

**Part 1 — Problem (one sentence)**

Describe the situation without identifying the client. "A retail business in [neighbourhood] had their GBP primary category set to 'Business' for three years." The specificity is what makes it useful — the vagueness on the client is what makes it publishable.

**Part 2 — What you did (three steps)**

Concrete actions only. "Changed primary category to match the top three competitors. Filled the Services tab with every service + one-sentence description. Added a GBP post schedule — one per week for 30 days."

No vague process language. "Implemented a comprehensive optimisation strategy" is the version no one believes.

**Part 3 — Result (measurable or defensible)**

If you have the data, use it. If you don't, use language that doesn't fabricate precision. "Profile views increased over the following 30 days" — defensible. "250% more calls" without tracking data — not defensible, and risks E-E-A-T concerns.

**Where it goes**

Below the FAQ block on the relevant service page. One per service. 150–200 words is enough. Add a subheading: "Recent work" or "What this looks like in practice."
