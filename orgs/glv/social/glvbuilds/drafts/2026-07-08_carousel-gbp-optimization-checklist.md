## LinkedIn Caption

The GBP optimization checklist I run on every new local client — before I touch anything else.

Most local SEO audits start with keywords. I start with GBP. Because if the profile is wrong, nothing downstream works.

Here's the 7-section checklist I go through in order:

**1. Business name**
Exact legal name. No keyword stuffing. Google has been catching and suppressing keyword-padded names for years. If the client added "Sault Ste. Marie" to their business name, I flag it.

**2. Primary category**
This is the single highest-leverage field in GBP. Wrong primary category = wrong local pack. I check Google's full category list and pick the most specific accurate match. Not "General Contractor" — "Kitchen Remodeler" or "Roofing Contractor."

**3. Service area vs storefront**
Service-area businesses (SABs) should hide their address if they don't receive customers at a physical location. Clients who serve customers at a location should show the address and have it consistent with their website, Yelp, and top 10 directories.

**4. Hours**
Stale hours are a trust killer. I check: are regular hours set? Are holiday hours added for upcoming dates? Are hours consistent with the website footer and Facebook?

**5. Photos**
Most clients have one or two photos from five years ago. I check: exterior (at least 3), interior (at least 3), team/at-work (at least 5). Google prioritises profiles with recent photo activity. I also check for owner-posted vs user-contributed photos — both matter.

**6. Services + products**
Most clients have never touched this section. Adding services with descriptions gives Google structured signals about what you do. I fill in every service offered, with a 2–3 sentence description per service where available.

**7. Q&A**
Empty Q&A is a missed opportunity. I seed 3–5 questions the client gets asked frequently, and answer them as the business. This content shows up directly in the local pack.

This checklist takes about 25 minutes per profile. It's the first thing I bill time to on any new local SEO engagement.
