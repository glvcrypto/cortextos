## Slide 2 — GBP Category

"Accountant," "Tax Preparation Service," and "Bookkeeper" are three separate GBP categories with different filter behaviour.

A firm that offers all three — annual tax preparation, monthly bookkeeping, and CPA-level advisory — should set the category that reflects its primary revenue driver as primary, with the others as secondary categories.

Most firms default to "Accountant" or leave the category at a generic setting. "Tax Preparation Service" captures a high volume of seasonal searches (February through April) that "Accountant" misses. Both secondary categories help for year-round bookkeeping searches.

---

## Slide 3 — Tax-Specific Pages

"Personal tax accountant [city]," "small business tax [city]," "self-employed tax return [city]," "corporate tax return [city]," and "rental property tax [city]" are separate searches made by people with different situations and different price expectations.

One "services" page ranks for none of them specifically.

A self-employed tax page that addresses the specific deductions relevant to sole proprietors, incorporates the T2125 without over-explaining it, and shows estimated turnaround time answers the questions a self-employed person has that a personal tax page doesn't.

---

## Slide 4 — CPA Designation Page

The CPA designation is regulated. Provincial body registration numbers are verifiable.

A page — or a bio section — that includes the CPA designation, provincial registration (CPA Ontario, CPA BC, CPA Alberta), and any specialisation (trust and estate accounting, RRSP and retirement planning, GST/HST advisory) creates a verifiable credential signal that Google quality raters check on financial service practitioner pages.

"Licensed CPA" in a footer bio is not the same as a named accountant with a provincial registration number on a dedicated bio page.

---

## Slide 5 — Seasonal Content Timing

Accounting has three main seasonal windows with different lead times:

Personal tax season runs mid-January through April 30. Content targeting "tax return [city]" needs to be live and indexed by mid-January — not published in March when competition for those searches is highest.

Corporate year-end varies by fiscal year — but a page targeting "corporate tax return [city]" with information about common fiscal year end dates (December 31, March 31, June 30) addresses a year-round search.

Bookkeeping is year-round but spikes in January (year-end clean-up) and September (businesses starting fresh before year-end). These are separate conversion moments.

---

## Slide 6 — Niche Specialisation Pages

"Accountant for real estate investors [city]," "accountant for self-employed [city]," "newcomer to Canada tax return [city]," and "accountant for incorporated professionals [city]" are searches with lower competition and higher conversion intent than generic "accountant [city]."

Each represents a searcher who already knows they have a situation that requires a specialist. A page written for that situation — using the specific terminology they use — converts at a higher rate than a generic services page.

Start with the client type that represents the largest share of your current client mix.
