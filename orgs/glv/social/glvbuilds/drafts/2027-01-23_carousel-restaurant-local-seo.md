## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
"Restaurant local SEO has three rules that don't apply to any other local business.

Most general SEO guides miss all three."

**Slide 2 — GBP category: cuisine beats "Restaurant"**
"Restaurant" is a GBP category.

So is "Italian Restaurant." "Sushi Restaurant." "Breakfast Restaurant."

The cuisine type shows up in Google Maps filters — people narrow by cuisine before they pick a specific place. If your primary category is "Restaurant" when you could be "Thai Restaurant," you're invisible in that filter.

Check what your top 3 competitors in the city are using. Match or go more specific.

**Slide 3 — Attributes matter more for restaurants**
Attributes are where most restaurant GBP profiles leave traffic behind.

Four that show up in Maps filter searches:
- Dine-in / takeout / delivery (three separate filter options)
- Dietary options: vegan-friendly, vegetarian-friendly, gluten-free options
- Outdoor seating (its own search filter in warmer months)
- Accessibility: wheelchair accessible entrance + seating

If it's true for your location, it needs to be checked in GBP. Missing it means missing the filter result.

**Slide 4 — Photos: the highest-weight signal for restaurants**
For restaurants, GBP photos carry more weight than almost any other local business type.

Five photo categories that matter:
1. Food — your most popular dish, well-lit, real photo
2. Interior — the actual atmosphere, not a render
3. Exterior — what it looks like from the street so people recognise it when they arrive
4. Menu board or current specials (updatable)
5. Team (optional, but warmth signal)

One new photo per week. Consistent upload activity signals an active business to Google.

**Slide 5 — Google Food Ordering**
If you use a third-party ordering platform — Uber Eats, DoorDash, SkipTheDishes — confirm your restaurant is linked in GBP.

When correctly connected, an "Order online" button appears directly on your Google listing and Maps result.

Check: go to your GBP profile in Maps (private browser). If there's no Order button, log into GBP Manager → Edit profile → Order links and add the platform URL.

Takes 2 minutes. Shows on the listing before a customer ever clicks through to your site.

**Slide 6 — Reviews: the Yelp exception**
For most local businesses in Canada, Yelp is not worth prioritising.

For restaurants in major metros — Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa — it is.

Two rules for restaurant Yelp:
1. Do not solicit Yelp reviews directly — their "not recommended" filter suppresses reviews from accounts that exist only to leave one review.
2. Do claim, update, and add photos to your Yelp listing. Consistent photo activity helps visibility on the platform.

Google still first. Then Yelp, because your customers are looking there.

If those things are true about your restaurant, they need to be toggled on in GBP. A customer filtering "vegan options near me" won't find your listing if you haven't set it.

For restaurants, it's also how customers make decisions before they walk in the door. They want to see what the food looks like and whether the atmosphere matches the occasion. One new photo per week — real food, real space — signals both Google and the person looking at the profile that this is an active, real business.
