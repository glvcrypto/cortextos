**Topic:** GSC Discover tab — how to read it and what it tells you
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## LinkedIn Post

GSC has five search types. Most local businesses never leave the default Web view.

The Discover tab is the one most people find confusing — because it doesn't show queries.

---

**Why there are no queries**

Discover traffic comes from users who weren't searching. Google surfaced your content in their feed based on past behaviour and topic signals. No one typed a query. That's why the Queries column is blank.

It's not a reporting error. It's a different traffic source.

---

**Where to find it**

GSC → Performance → Search type → Discover.

You'll see impressions and clicks. You won't see queries, average position, or CTR in the same sense as Web search.

---

**Three reads that matter**

A sharp spike immediately after publishing means the topic resonated with Google's Discover algorithm. That signal is worth noting — topics that earn early Discover impressions often perform well in Web search as the page matures.

Low impressions with a published date of more than a few days? The most common fix is the thumbnail image. Discover pulls the Open Graph image — the image in your page's `<meta property="og:image">` tag. The recommended size is 1200×628px. Images that are too small or missing are the most frequent reason a page underperforms in Discover.

Consistent baseline impressions over weeks or months means Google has established trust with that content type. That's a signal to produce more in the same format.

---

**The 90-second monthly check**

GSC → Performance → Discover. Set a 28-day window. Look at your top five pages by impressions. Check if the thumbnail images on those pages are 1200×628px. Note which content types are appearing — it tells you what Google associates your account with in users' feeds.

This isn't a channel to optimise heavily. It's a readout.

**Caption:**
GSC has five search types: Web, Image, Video, News, and Discover. Most local service site owners check the default Web view and nothing else.

The Discover tab shows impressions and clicks for content Google surfaced in user feeds — not in response to a query. No queries shown isn't a reporting problem. It's a different traffic type.

The most common fix when Discover impressions are low: the thumbnail image. GSC pulls the Open Graph image for Discover previews. If it's missing or smaller than 1200×628px, your content won't display properly. Check your top pages in GSC → Performance → Discover and verify the og:image tag is set correctly.
