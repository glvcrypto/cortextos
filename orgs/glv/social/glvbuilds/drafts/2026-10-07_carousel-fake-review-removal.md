Submit — and document the review URL for follow-up

## LinkedIn Caption

Most of my clients have at least one Google review they know is fake.

The person never visited. The description doesn't match anything in their records. Or the same account left four stars, flipped to one after a refund dispute, and the language reads like a competitor wrote it.

Here's what I tell them.

**What Google will actually remove:**
– Spam or fake content (no customer relationship, off-topic)
– Harassment or personal attacks
– Conflict of interest (competitor-posted)
– Prohibited content (hate speech, personal information)

**What it won't remove:**
– A low rating you disagree with
– An honest negative from a verified customer
– A complaint about a service you actually provided

**How to flag it:**
GBP Manager → Reviews → three-dot menu → Report review. Choose the closest policy violation. Submit.

**What to expect:**
Most flags are declined on first pass. If you have strong evidence, post in the Google Business Profile Help Community and submit again with more context.

**While you wait:**
Respond publicly. Keep it factual. "We have no record of this customer and couldn't locate this visit in our system. We'd welcome the chance to connect directly." That response is for every future reader, not the reviewer.

One rule I hold to: the removal process is for fake reviews.

If valid customers are leaving honest negatives — no amount of flagging fixes that. The review isn't the problem.
