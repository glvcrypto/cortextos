## LinkedIn Caption

A client asked me last month whether they needed to do something different to show up in ChatGPT or Perplexity searches.

The honest answer: mostly no. But there's one thing worth understanding about how these tools find local businesses.

AI search tools like Perplexity and ChatGPT Search don't maintain their own database of local businesses. They pull from indexed web content — your GBP listing (which Google indexes from Maps), your website copy, review platforms, third-party directories. The same sources Google has been using.

**What that means practically:**

**NAP consistency matters more, not less.** AI search tools cross-reference multiple sources for local queries. If your GBP says one phone number, your website says another, and your Yelp listing has a third — AI search generates uncertainty about which version is current and may surface a competitor with cleaner signals instead.

**GBP completeness still carries weight.** If your GBP has thin or missing sections (no description, incomplete services, no photos), that's what AI search gets. A complete, accurate profile is a data source that AI tools can actually pull from.

**Service page first paragraphs.** These tools tend to pull structured, plain-language answers. "We are a [service] in [city]. We provide [specific service list]." — that sentence structure gets cited more often than vague brand copy. The first two sentences of every service page should answer who, what, and where without making the reader work for it.

**Review platform presence.** Yelp, HomeStars, BBB, and Google reviews are indexed and cited by AI search tools for reputation and credibility signals. Accurate, up-to-date profiles on the platforms relevant to your category matter here.

What's different from traditional Google SEO: AI search sometimes surfaces one specific answer rather than a list. If your website clearly answers a specific local query — service area, process, typical cost — that page has a better chance of being cited directly.

The foundations don't change. The place where people start searching is.
