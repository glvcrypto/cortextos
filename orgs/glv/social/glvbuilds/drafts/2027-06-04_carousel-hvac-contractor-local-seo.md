An HVAC contractor's local SEO problem isn't a keyword problem. It's a timing problem.

## Slide 2 — GBP: One Profile, Right Category, Both Services

Google's category "HVAC Contractor" covers both heating and cooling. Use it as your primary — not "Air Conditioning Contractor" or "Heating Contractor" unless you genuinely only offer one. Specialised categories limit your reach in the off-season.

Two things most HVAC profiles are missing:

**Emergency availability signal.** "24/7 emergency furnace repair" belongs in your GBP profile description, your services list, and your website homepage — not buried in a footer. A homeowner with a failed furnace in January is not reading your full site. They are calling the first result that confirms someone will answer.

**Seasonal GBP posts.** A post in late September — "furnace tune-up season starts now" — activates heating season before the first cold snap. A post in April for AC tune-ups does the same for cooling. GBP posts expire after seven days; schedule seasonally.

## Slide 3 — Heating Pages: Emergency vs. Scheduled Are Different Sites

Two different searches. Two different conversion paths. Two different pages.

**Emergency furnace repair:**
- Phone number above the fold — not below, not in the nav only
- No form as the primary CTA (a client with no heat in January is not filling out a contact form)
- 24/7 availability stated explicitly
- Response time range ("typically within X hours" — use a range, not a guarantee unless that's your actual SLA)
- No SEER ratings, no efficiency comparisons on this page — this client has one question: how fast can you get here?

**Scheduled furnace installation / maintenance:**
- Longer-form content appropriate — brands, efficiencies, rebate eligibility (Ontario Enbridge rebates, BC Fortis rebates — verify current programs and do not fabricate amounts)
- Process timeline, what to expect at installation, warranty information
- Gallery appropriate (before/after, equipment photos)
- A form CTA is appropriate here

## Slide 4 — Cooling Pages: AC Has Its Own Search Cluster

Heating and cooling share an HVAC brand but split into different search patterns:

- **AC installation** — spring search, homeowners preparing before summer heat; brand questions (Carrier vs. Lennox vs. Trane vs. Goodman — clients search brand names); rebate questions
- **AC repair** — summer emergency; same emergency-page pattern as furnace repair — phone first, fast response stated, no lengthy form
- **AC tune-up / maintenance** — pre-season (May); GBP post timed to April activates this search before competitors; positions you for a relationship that converts to replacement when the unit fails

A single "air conditioning services" page that lists all three will rank for none of the three specifically. A client searching "AC installation [city]" in April and a client searching "AC not working [city]" in August have different needs and different conversion paths.

## Slide 5 — Heat Pump: The Page Most HVAC Sites Are Missing

Heat pumps are a bridge system — heating and cooling in one unit. They are also the fastest-growing search in the HVAC category as Canadian households respond to energy cost and rebate availability.

A dedicated heat pump page earns searches that neither a heating page nor a cooling page captures:

- "Heat pump installation [city]"
- "Heat pump vs. furnace [city]"
- "Heat pump rebate [province]" (federal Greener Homes Grant; Ontario and BC provincial programs — verify current program status and do not fabricate rebate amounts or eligibility thresholds)
- Cold-climate heat pump performance context (a common objection in Canadian winters — address it)

The rebate question is the highest-intent qualifier for heat pump searches in Canada right now. An HVAC contractor with a rebate FAQ on their heat pump page captures the client who has done their research and is ready to move forward.

## Slide 6 — Trust Signals That Differentiate

In a market where most HVAC contractors look the same online:

- **TSSA certification** (Ontario) — publicly verifiable; display it; it signals that your technicians are licensed to work on gas appliances. Not all HVAC websites state this.
- **HRAI membership** (Heating, Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Institute of Canada) — trade association membership; signals industry standing
- **Brand authorisation** — if you are an authorised dealer for Carrier, Lennox, Trane, or another brand, state it. Clients who have researched brands want to find authorised dealers. Do not claim authorisation you do not have.
- **Reviews naming the situation** — "fixed our furnace at 11pm in January" closes more inquiries than five stars with no context. Prompt clients after emergency calls specifically.
- **Manufacturer warranty pass-through** — if your installations carry the full manufacturer warranty (typically conditional on registered installation by an authorised dealer), state the terms clearly.
