# Local SEO for Service Area Businesses — What Actually Changes When You Hide Your Address

## Hook (on screen + voice-over)

Most service area businesses set up their Google Business Profile wrong.
Not because they're hiding their address —
because they don't know what actually changes when they do.

---

## Body (on screen, one line per beat)

**What a service area business is:**
You go to the customer. Plumbers, cleaners, mobile mechanics, landscapers.
You hide your address in GBP — customers don't come to you.

**What changes when you hide your address:**
— Your service area replaces your address in the GBP panel
— Google uses your listed service areas for local pack placement, not a storefront address
— You lose the "Located in [neighbourhood]" proximity signal — reviews and activity carry more weight

**What stays the same:**
— You still need a real physical address to verify (PO boxes don't work)
— All other GBP signals still apply: reviews, photos, posts, categories, Q&A

**The mistake most SABs make:**
Setting service areas too large ("all of Ontario").
Google's local pack is local. City-by-city areas outperform province-wide every time.

---

## Ending (on screen)

Hide the address the right way.
Set service areas by city — not by how far you'll theoretically drive.
