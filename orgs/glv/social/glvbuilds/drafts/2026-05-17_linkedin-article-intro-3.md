**Title:** How GLV Marketing Works: A Step-by-Step Look at Our Process

---

### Here's Exactly How We Work on Your Account

A lot of agencies are vague about their process. You get a general overview in the sales call, and then the work just sort of happens. Reports arrive. Invoices arrive. It's hard to see the connection between what's being done and the results you care about.

GLV works differently. Every client goes through the same structured process. Not because it's the easiest way to run an agency, but because it's the way that produces compounding results. Here's what that looks like, step by step.

---

### Step 1: We Research Your Market

Before we write a word of content or build a single page, we learn your market.

That means your competitive landscape: who else is ranking for the terms your customers search, what their strengths are, and where the gaps are. It means understanding your target search terms at the intent level, not just the volume level. It means knowing where your customers are looking and what they need to see before they contact you.

That research drives every decision that follows. The content strategy, the site structure, the GBP categories, the ad targeting. If the research is solid, the decisions downstream are better. We don't skip this step or rush it to get to the visible work faster.

---

### Step 2: We Build the Foundation

Once we know the landscape, we build.

Website structure, content plan, SEO architecture, local presence. We put the pieces in place in the right order. Foundation before content. Structure before campaigns. Local presence before paid acceleration.

The order matters because each layer supports the next. A well-structured site makes content work harder. A strong local presence makes paid ads more efficient. A compounding marketing system starts with a solid foundation, not with the most visible tactic on the menu.

---

### Step 3: We Execute. Consistently.

The foundation is only as valuable as the work built on top of it, and that work has to happen consistently.

Content goes out on schedule. SEO work gets done weekly, not in bursts before a quarterly report. Ads are monitored and adjusted on a regular cadence. There are no off weeks, no gaps in execution, no stretches where nothing moves forward.

Consistency is one of the most underrated factors in marketing results. Google rewards sites that publish regularly and keep signals active. Email lists decay when they go quiet for months. The businesses that see compounding results are the ones where the work never stops.

---

### Step 4: We Review Before It Ships

Nothing goes out without a review step.

Strategy calls happen before major decisions. Content goes through sign-off before it's published. Campaign reports go to the client before the next period is planned. We're not executing in a vacuum.

That review layer is what keeps the strategy aligned with what's actually happening in the business. Markets change. Competitive landscapes shift. A client might have a product launch or a seasonal push that changes the priority. The review step is where that information enters the system and shapes what comes next.

---

### Step 5: Watch It Compound

The compounding effect isn't dramatic in any single month. Month one, you have a foundation. Month three, a few pages are starting to rank. Month six, organic traffic is measurably up and you're getting leads you didn't have before.

By month 12, the marketing is working in a fundamentally different way than it was at the start. The content is indexed and earning. The local presence is established. The email list is active. The ad campaigns are optimised on a real body of data.

This is what marketing looks like when the work stacks instead of resets. Not a spike, a curve that keeps going up.

---

### One Team. One Strategy. One Direction.

You work with one team across everything. The person who knows your SEO strategy also knows your content plan and your ad targeting. There's no briefing the same context to three different vendors. No situation where the email team did one thing and the SEO team didn't know.

One relationship. One standard. Consistent across everything we produce for your business.
