## LinkedIn Caption (~300 words)

Anyone with a Google account can suggest edits to your Google Business Profile. Google sometimes applies them without asking you first.

Here's what gets changed without your knowledge, and what to do about it:

**What third-party edits look like**

A Maps user flags your hours as wrong. Someone suggests your business name should include a keyword phrase. A competitor submits an incorrect category. These go into a queue — and if your listing has low recent activity, Google may auto-apply them.

**Most common bad edits I've seen**

— Hours changed to incorrect times (usually a confused customer, sometimes a competitor)
— Business name modified to include location keywords (violates GBP guidelines — but Google may apply it before it's reviewed)
— Primary category swapped to something adjacent but less accurate
— Phone number changed (rare, but happens)

**How to protect your listing**

Turn on notifications before this happens, not after.

GBP Manager → Settings → Notifications → Business edits. Enable the toggle. You'll receive an email when a change is applied or pending.

Once a month: open your GBP dashboard and check the edit history under "Manage profile" → "Edits." Look at what Google has accepted vs. what's still pending.

If an edit was applied incorrectly: submit a correction yourself through the "Suggest an edit" flow on your own listing to revert it. Flag it as inaccurate.

If the same incorrect edit keeps coming back: report it as a spam or malicious edit via the three-dot menu. Document with screenshots.

Five minutes of setup now prevents the "my hours were wrong for three weeks and I didn't know" conversation.
