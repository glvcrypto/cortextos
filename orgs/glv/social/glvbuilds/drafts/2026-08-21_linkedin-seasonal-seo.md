# Seasonal SEO for Local Businesses — Why Timing Is the Work

## LinkedIn Post

Most local businesses publish content when they have time.

The ones ranking at the top of the season started months earlier.

---

Google search demand for local services follows predictable patterns.
Boating searches spike in April and May.
HVAC searches spike in June and December.
Lawn care searches climb in March.

If you publish a service page in peak season, you're too late.
New pages can take several weeks — sometimes months — to gain traction in search results.
By the time Google indexes it, ranks it, and serves it to the right searchers, the season is over.

---

Here's the timing approach I use for every seasonal local client:

- **8 weeks before peak**: publish or update the service page with targeted content and FAQ block
- **6 weeks before peak**: start posting to GBP about the seasonal service (offer posts + service updates)
- **At peak**: the page is already indexed, GBP is active, and you're capturing searches your competitors aren't
- **After season**: update the page with what you serviced, brand names, pricing guidance — so it's stronger next year

---

One practical note:
GBP posts don't carry forward the way pages do.
A post from six months ago has no ranking value today.
Pages age and accumulate equity. Posts are short-term.

That's why the combination matters — pages for evergreen ranking, posts for in-season visibility.
