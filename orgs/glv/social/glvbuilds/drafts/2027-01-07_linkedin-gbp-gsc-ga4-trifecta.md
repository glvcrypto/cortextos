# LinkedIn: The three Google tools every local business needs — and most only have one

## LinkedIn Caption (full)

**Most local businesses have a Google Business Profile. Some also have Google Search Console. Very few have all three connected.**

The three tools:

**Google Business Profile (GBP)**
Controls what Google shows about your business in Maps and local search. Phone number, hours, photos, reviews, posts, services — all of it. If GBP isn't verified and accurate, none of the other work matters as much as it should.

**Google Search Console (GSC)**
Shows what people search before they click through to your website. Not traffic — intent. The queries in GSC tell you what pages to write, which title tags to fix, and what service+city combinations are getting impressions but no clicks.

**Google Analytics 4 (GA4)**
Shows what happens after a visitor arrives. Which pages they land on, how long they stay, whether they call or submit a form. The story of what the traffic actually does.

**Why they need to be connected, not just individually set up:**

- Add UTM parameters to the GBP website field → GA4 shows `source = gmb` as its own traffic segment → you see exactly how many visitors came from your GBP listing vs. organic vs. paid
- GSC shows which service page gets impressions → GA4 confirms whether those visits result in calls or form submissions → you know which pages are doing actual work
- GBP Insights shows call volume from the listing → GA4 shows form submissions from the website → together they give you the real lead count, not just traffic numbers

**Five-minute setup check:**
- GBP verified and website field populated? ✓
- GSC verified and showing data? ✓
- GA4 installed and linked in GSC Settings → Associations? ✓
- GBP website field URL UTM-tagged? ✓

Most local businesses are missing one or two of these. Getting all three connected is under an hour of setup. The reporting clarity it unlocks is ongoing.
