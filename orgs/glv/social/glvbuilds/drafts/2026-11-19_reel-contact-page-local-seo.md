## LinkedIn Caption (for sharing the reel)

The contact page is almost always the most neglected page on a local service site.

It's also one of the pages Google looks at most carefully when evaluating local relevance — because it's where NAP (name, address, phone) information lives. If that information is inconsistent, incomplete, or missing, it works against you.

Five things I fix on every new client's contact page:

**1. NAP exact match.** The business name, address, and local phone number on the contact page must match the GBP profile exactly — same format, same number. Not a tracking number, not a toll-free line. The actual local number.

**2. Google Maps embed.** Not a screenshot of the map, not a link to Google Maps — the live interactive embed. It adds a location signal to the page and makes it trivial for visitors to get directions.

**3. Hours on the page.** Written out in plain text. "Monday–Friday, 8am to 5pm. Closed statutory holidays." Not "See our Google listing for hours." A Google crawler and a real customer should both be able to read the hours without leaving the page.

**4. Click-to-call phone link.** Format the phone number as a `tel:` link. On mobile, a tap dials directly. Most contact pages display the number as plain text. Most visitors on mobile won't manually dial a number — they'll leave instead.

**5. One CTA visible above the fold on mobile.** A phone number or a contact button that appears before any scrolling is required. Most local service sites have both buried in the footer. On a contact page — the page a visitor goes to when they're ready to reach out — make it visible before they give up.

None of these require a developer. Most can be done in under 30 minutes with basic CMS access.
