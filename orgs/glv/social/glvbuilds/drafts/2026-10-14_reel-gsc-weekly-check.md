## LinkedIn caption

Most local SEO reports focus on ranking. I focus on GSC every Monday morning — 5 minutes, three filters, before I open anything else.

**Here's the exact sequence:**

**1. Impressions with low CTR (< 3%)**
Filter your Pages tab by impressions descending, then add a CTR < 3% filter.
These are pages Google is already showing. People aren't clicking.
That's a title tag or meta description problem — not a ranking problem. Fix the copy first.

**2. Zero-impression pages**
Any indexed page with zero impressions in the last 28 days isn't being surfaced.
Two causes: not indexed, or targeting intent that doesn't have search volume.
Quick check: paste the URL into a private browser Google search. If it doesn't appear, you have an indexation issue to fix before anything else.

**3. Featured snippet queries**
Filter positions 1–3, then open the SERP for any query where a snippet box appears.
If a competitor owns it, add a structured answer block to the page — a short direct answer to the question, followed by the supporting detail. That's all it takes most of the time.

The whole check takes 5 minutes. I've caught indexation bugs, cannibalisation issues, and title tag problems this way — none of which would show up in a rank tracking report.
