## Slides

**Slide 2 — What a GBP short name is**
A custom URL for your Google Business Profile
Format: g.page/yourbusinessname
Set it once in GBP Manager → Edit profile → Add short name
Up to 32 characters, no spaces

**Slide 3 — Why it matters for reviews**
Your review link without a short name looks like this:
google.com/maps/place/?cid=1234567890123456
That doesn't fit on a business card. It won't scan as a QR code cleanly.
With a short name: g.page/yourbusiness/review
One line. Printable. Scannable.

**Slide 4 — Where I use the short name**
Review request card (QR code → g.page/shortname/review)
Email signature: "Review us on Google: g.page/shortname"
Invoice footer
Vehicle wrap or signage CTA
Same-day follow-up text after job completion

**Slide 5 — How to set it up**
GBP Manager → Edit profile → scroll to "Add short name"
Choose a name close to your business name (check availability)
Save — Google reviews it; usually approved within 24h
After approval: add /review to the URL for the direct review link

**Slide 6 — One thing to check**
Claim your short name before a competitor with a similar name does.
If your business is "Hamilton Plumbing" and a competitor named "Hamilton Plumbing Pro" claims "hamiltonplumbing" first — your options get limited fast.

**Slide 7 — One-time setup, ongoing payoff**
Set it once. Use it everywhere.
I've seen clients go from 2–3 new reviews a month to 8–10 just by making the review link easier to share.
The short name doesn't change that number. Removing friction does.
