## LinkedIn Caption

A physiotherapy clinic that ranks for "physiotherapy [city]" can still be invisible for the searches that come from clients who are ready to book.

Here's the search category most physio websites miss:

**Direct billing page**
"Direct billing physiotherapy [city]" is a separate search from "physiotherapy [city]." Clients who have extended health coverage want to find a clinic that will bill their insurer directly — Blue Cross, Canada Life, Manulife, Sun Life, Desjardins — rather than submitting manually for reimbursement. A direct billing page that lists the insurers you work with is a conversion page. Without it, clients who would choose you based on that convenience are going to the clinic that lists it.

**OHIP coverage FAQ**
"Does OHIP cover physiotherapy?" is one of the highest-volume physio queries in Ontario. The answer is no — OHIP does not cover private physiotherapy (only hospital-based services or specific WSIB/OHIP extended claims). Clients who search this question are often unaware that extended health covers it instead. A page that answers the OHIP question accurately, explains how extended health works, and walks through the direct billing process captures that traffic and closes the knowledge gap before the client calls a competitor.

**MVA and WSIB pages**
Motor vehicle accident and WSIB physiotherapy involve different billing processes than extended health or private pay. Clients in an active claim are searching specifically — "MVA physiotherapy [city]," "WSIB physiotherapy [city]" — for a clinic experienced in that billing path. Accepting MVA and WSIB clients and not building those pages means you are invisible to clients who are already in a claim process and actively looking.

**Search timing**
Physiotherapy searches tend to peak 2–4 weeks post-injury or post-surgery — the point where a client has an assessment, a referral or recommendation, and is researching their options. This is a high-intent search moment. A clinic that answers the insurance question at that moment is positioned to convert.
