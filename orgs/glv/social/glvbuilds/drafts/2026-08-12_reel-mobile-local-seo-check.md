## LinkedIn Caption

Most local service pages were built on a desktop.

Most local searches happen on a phone.

That mismatch costs bookings. Here are the four checks I run on every local service page before touching anything else.

**Check 1: Layout integrity.**
Open the page on a real phone, not a browser emulator. Does the text wrap correctly? Can you read the headline at mobile width without zooming? Layout breaks at 390px are a ranking signal and a usability problem — Google's mobile-first index crawls the mobile version.

**Check 2: Tappable phone number.**
Find the phone number on the page. Tap it. If it doesn't prompt a call, it's a plain text string — not a `tel:` link. A visitor on a phone who wants to call has to manually dial. Most won't. One line of code fixes this: `<a href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX">`.

**Check 3: CTA above the fold on mobile.**
Scroll to the main call-to-action — the booking button, the contact form, the phone number CTA. Is it visible on a 390px screen without scrolling? On a 390px iPhone, "above the fold" is about 844px of vertical space. If the button is below that on first load, a significant share of visitors never see it.

**Check 4: Core Web Vitals — LCP.**
Run the URL through PageSpeed Insights (free, Google). Look at LCP — Largest Contentful Paint. Under 2.5s is passing. Over 4.0s is a ranking problem. For local service pages, the LCP is usually the hero image or the H1 block — compress images, defer non-critical scripts.

Four checks. About two minutes per page.

Most of your competitors haven't done any of them.
