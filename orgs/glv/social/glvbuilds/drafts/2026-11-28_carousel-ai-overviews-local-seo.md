## Slide 2 — When AI Overviews appear in local search

**AI Overviews trigger most for informational queries — not transactional "near me" searches.**

"How much does furnace repair cost?" → likely triggers an AI Overview.
"Furnace repair near me" → usually shows the local pack, not an AI Overview.

AI Overviews are Google's answer to informational intent. When a searcher wants to understand something before they buy, Google synthesises an answer at the top of the results.

For local service businesses, this matters most for cost, comparison, and how-to queries — the ones that appear before a buying decision is made.

---

## Slide 3 — What AI Overviews pull from local business content

**Four content sources I see pulled regularly:**

1. **FAQ sections** on service pages — especially direct Q&A format (Question: ... Answer: ...)
2. **Service page first paragraphs** — concise, specific descriptions of what a service includes
3. **GBP Services tab** — service descriptions with city context
4. **Review text** — customer reviews that describe the service clearly and specifically

What they don't pull: generic filler copy, marketing superlatives ("industry-leading"), or content with no specific information.

---

## Slide 4 — The opportunity: informational queries

**Ranking in an AI Overview doesn't guarantee a click. But not ranking in one means you're invisible for that query.**

For local service businesses, the informational query sequence often looks like:
1. Searcher asks "how much does [service] cost in [city]?"
2. AI Overview appears — potentially citing your service page
3. Searcher is now educated and moves to: "[service] near me"
4. Local pack appears — you need to be there too

The two-query journey means both stages matter. An AI Overview citation at stage 1 builds brand familiarity before the searcher hits the local pack at stage 2.

---

## Slide 5 — Practical action 1: FAQ sections

**Add a concise, specific FAQ block to every service page.**

Format: actual question as the heading (H3), specific answer in 2–4 sentences below.

Pull questions from: GBP Q&A (questions customers have already asked), Google's "People Also Ask" box for your service keywords, and common questions that come up in sales conversations.

FAQ schema (Aug 13 post) helps Google identify and process these sections. But even without schema, a well-structured FAQ block is one of the highest-probability sources for AI Overview citations.

---

## Slide 6 — Practical action 2: Specific service page copy

**Generic service page copy doesn't get cited. Specific copy does.**

What generic looks like: "We provide high-quality furnace repair services to homeowners in the area."

What specific looks like: "We repair gas, electric, and oil furnaces for residential customers in [city]. Service calls are booked within 24 hours. We carry parts for the 12 most common residential furnace brands."

AI Overviews are synthesising answers for searchers who want to know what a service includes and what to expect. Give the answer in the copy, not after a phone call.

---

## Slide 7 — Practical action 3: GBP Services tab descriptions

**The GBP Services tab is one of the most underused local SEO assets — and AI Overviews appear to pull from it.**

Each service entry has a name and a short description field. Most businesses leave the description blank or write one word.

Instead: write one sentence per service that includes the service name, the city, and a specific detail. "Residential furnace repair in [city] — same-day service calls, all major brands."

Takes 10 minutes for a complete service list. (Sep 23 post covered the full Services tab audit.)
