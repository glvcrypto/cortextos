# The One-Page Monthly Local SEO Report — Every Section I Include (and What I Left Out)

## Slide 1 — Hook

The report I send every local SEO client fits on one page.

Six sections.
Fifteen minutes to fill in.
Every client actually reads it.

Here's every section.

---

## Slide 2 — Section 1: GBP Performance

Three numbers from the GBP Performance tab:
- Phone calls this month vs. last month
- Direction requests this month vs. last month
- Website clicks from GBP this month vs. last month

Format: [number] ↑/↓ [%] vs. [last month]

This is the section clients refer to first.
It answers: "Is my Google presence actually sending people to me?"

---

## Slide 3 — Section 2: Service Page Traffic

Two to three service pages from Google Search Console:
- Impressions (how many times the page appeared in search)
- Clicks (how many people visited from search)

Pick the pages you're actively optimising.
Month-over-month trend matters more than the raw number.

---

## Slide 4 — Section 3: Work Completed

Three bullet points. No more.

What I actually did this month — not a task list, not a log.
Examples:
- Updated spring service page with FAQ block and new internal links
- Posted four times to GBP (two offer posts, two service updates)
- Fixed three NAP inconsistencies in top directories

Clients need to know their money is doing something. This section answers that.

---

## Slide 5 — Section 4: One Win

One specific, measurable result.

Not "traffic is up."
"GBP calls up 31% versus last month."

Not "your profile looks better."
"You appeared in 214 local searches this month — up from 160 in March."

One win. Real numbers. Every month.

---

## Slide 6 — Section 5: Priority for Next Month

One sentence.

"Next month: update the snow removal page before the October search spike."
"Next month: resolve the duplicate GBP listing flagged in the audit."

This tells the client the work is planned, not reactive.
It also holds me accountable.

---

## Slide 7 — What I Left Out

Things that used to be on the report. Now they're not.

- Keyword ranking positions — they fluctuate too much to mean anything month-to-month
- Domain Authority — not a Google metric, clients don't need to know about it
- Total website sessions — misleading without conversion context
- Impression-only graphs — looks impressive, tells nothing about calls or leads

The goal is a report the client reads in 90 seconds and understands.

## LinkedIn Caption (300–600 words)

I spent the first year of my agency sending clients five-page reports.

They didn't read them.

Now I send one page. Every client reads it. Most reply within 24 hours.

Here's what changed — and every section I include.

**Why one page works**

Local business owners are not SEO specialists. They don't need to understand every metric. They need to know two things: is this working, and what are you doing about it?

A five-page report buries those answers. One page puts them front and centre.

**The six sections**

**GBP Performance** — Phone calls, direction requests, and website clicks from their Google Business Profile. Month-over-month. These are the closest thing to leads that any free reporting tool gives you.

**Service page traffic** — Two or three key pages from Google Search Console. Impressions and clicks. I focus on the pages we're actively improving — not the full site overview.

**Work completed** — Three bullets. What I actually did this month. Not a task list, not a time log. Three specific actions with real context. Clients need to see activity connected to results.

**One win** — One measurable result with real numbers. This is the section that builds trust. Every month, there should be something you can point to that moved. If there isn't, you have a problem — and the report forces you to confront it.

**Priority for next month** — One sentence. What's the single most important thing you're working toward. This shows the work is planned, not just reactive.

**What I removed** — Keyword ranking reports. Domain Authority. Total sessions without conversion context. Impression graphs with no call data. These are metrics that look impressive in a report and mean almost nothing to a client trying to decide if their local SEO investment is worth it.

The goal of a client report is not to prove you're working hard.
It's to show that the work is producing results.
One page is enough to do that.
