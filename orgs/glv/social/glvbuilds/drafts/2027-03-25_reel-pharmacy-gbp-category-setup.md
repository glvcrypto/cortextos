## Script

**[Hook — text on screen]**
There are two types of pharmacies on Google Maps. One shows up for "pharmacy near me." The other shows up for "walk-in clinic near me."

If you run both — you probably need both setups.

**[Beat 1 — on screen]**
GBP allows one primary category and multiple secondary categories.

A pharmacy with a walk-in clinic that only lists "Pharmacy" is invisible for clinic searches — and vice versa.

Set your primary to whichever service has the higher search intent for your location. Add the other as a secondary category. Both now appear in the relevant map pack.

**[Beat 2 — on screen]**
GBP attributes for pharmacies: Drive-through, Accepts insurance, Wheelchair accessible.

For walk-in clinics: Accepts walk-ins, Open weekends.

These appear as filter options in Maps. Customers use them before they click your listing. If your profile doesn't have them set, you don't show when they filter.

**[Beat 3 — on screen]**
Hours accuracy.

Pharmacy hours and clinic hours often differ. GBP Special Hours lets you set different hours for each. Inaccurate hours generate "business doesn't follow operating hours" reviews — one of the fastest ways to lose local pack position.

**[Payoff — on screen]**
Most pharmacy GBP listings are set up once and never reviewed.

Category, attributes, hours. Fifteen-minute audit. Check them twice a year.

**[CTA — on screen]**
When did you last audit your GBP attributes?
