## LinkedIn Caption

A client runs a trades business in one city and asked how to start getting jobs 40 minutes away — without opening a new location.

Here's the five-step framework I used.

**Step 1 — Check GSC first.** Pull Performance → Queries, filter your service keyword, and scan which cities are already giving you impressions at position 30–50. If a city is returning impressions with no clicks, demand exists. That's where to expand.

**Step 2 — Update GBP service areas.** GBP Manager → Edit profile → Service areas. Add the target city. This doesn't immediately shift rankings, but Google needs the signal that you actually serve that area. Keep it to 3–5 cities you can genuinely service — not 20 you might theoretically reach.

**Step 3 — Build one dedicated landing page.** URL: /service/target-city/. H1: [Service] in [City]. The page needs real content — not a copy-paste from your home city page with the name swapped. Google identifies near-duplicates. Aim for 300–500 words with one specific local detail: the housing stock in that area, common problems you see there, specific districts or postal codes you service.

**Step 4 — Add one citation for that city.** Check BrightLocal, a local chamber directory, or a trade association for that city. Is your business listed there? NAP must match GBP exactly. One solid, accurate citation matters more than five thin directory entries.

**Step 5 — Add internal links from strong pages.** Your existing service pages have link equity. Link 2–3 of them to the new city page using anchor text that includes the service and city name. This gives Google the relevance signal it needs to start ranking the new page.

Realistic timeline: expect first GSC impressions within 60 days, meaningful click volume in months 3–4, and map pack visibility for searchers in that city in months 5–6.

One caveat: you won't own the map pack from 40km away the way a local business with a verified address there will. Proximity is a dominant local pack signal. But organic search doesn't penalise distance — it rewards relevance. That's where the expansion opportunity lives.
