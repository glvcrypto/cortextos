## LinkedIn caption

The most common reason a local business loses Google rankings isn't an algorithm update. It's a website redesign handled without a pre-launch SEO checklist.

The problems are almost always the same: URLs restructured without 301 redirects, GBP pointing to old URLs, schema not updated, GSC not re-verified. None of these are hard to catch — but they need to be done before go-live, not diagnosed after traffic drops.

**The 7-step pre-launch migration checklist:**

1. **URL redirect map** — document every changed URL and the 301 redirect destination. Prioritise: service pages, blog posts with backlinks, any page showing impressions in GSC. A spreadsheet with old URL → new URL → redirect verified is the minimum.

2. **GBP website field** — update to the new URL before the site goes live. GBP crawls the website field; stale URLs create citation inconsistency and a disconnect between your listing and your indexed site.

3. **LocalBusiness schema** — update the URL field in your structured data. While you're there: verify the telephone and address fields still match exactly what's in GBP and your directories.

4. **GSC property verification** — if you're staying on the same domain, confirm the existing property is still verified after any changes to site structure. If the domain is changing, add and verify the new domain as a separate property.

5. **XML sitemap submission** — after go-live, submit the updated sitemap in GSC. This signals to Google that a new URL structure exists and speeds up discovery of the new pages.

6. **Change of Address tool (domain changes only)** — GSC Settings → Change of Address. This tells Google your site has moved to a new domain and helps transfer the ranking signals your old domain built. Skipping it means Google treats the new domain as a brand-new site.

7. **48-hour post-launch monitoring window** — open GSC Coverage tab daily for the first two days. Watch for a 404 spike (missed redirects), a drop in indexed pages, or crawl errors. Catching these in 48 hours means they're fixable before they compound.

This list takes about an hour to run through before a launch. It's saved several clients from discovering significant ranking drops weeks after the migration window had already passed.
