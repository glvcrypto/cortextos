## Slide 2 — Why Most Local SEO Reports Miss the Point

**Headline:** Reporting rankings and domain authority to local clients is a mistake.

**Body:**
Most local SEO reports lead with keyword positions.
The problem: a client ranked #3 for "marine repair Sault Ste. Marie" doesn't tell them whether the phone rang.
What clients care about: did someone call? Did someone ask for directions? Did the site get a lead?
Report the signals that connect to the business outcome — not the signals that are easy to pull.

## Slide 3 — Metric 1: GBP Calls

**Headline:** GBP calls — phone calls triggered directly from the profile.

**Body:**
Found in: Google Business Profile dashboard → Performance → Calls.
What it tells you: how many people clicked the call button on the profile this month.
Why it matters: this is the clearest conversion signal from local search without additional call tracking software.
Trend line matters more than the absolute number. A drop in calls alongside stable rankings = something is wrong on the profile itself — photo quality, hours, category.

## Slide 4 — Metric 2: Direction Requests

**Headline:** Direction requests — people who asked Google Maps to route them to the business.

**Body:**
Found in: GBP Performance → Direction requests.
What it tells you: purchase-intent visitors who planned a physical trip.
Why it matters: for any business with a storefront, this is near-perfect intent. Someone requesting directions is planning to show up.
Seasonal spikes are normal. Compare same month last year — not month-over-month — to avoid misleading the client.

*Visual: Direction requests graph with "same month last year" comparison annotation*

## Slide 5 — Metric 3: GSC Clicks to Service Pages

**Headline:** Search Console clicks to service pages — organic traffic to the pages that should convert.

**Body:**
Found in: Google Search Console → Performance → Pages. Filter to core service pages.
What it tells you: organic clicks from Google search to the pages that are supposed to convert — not the homepage, not the blog.
Why it matters: GBP metrics measure the profile. GSC clicks measure the site. You need both.
A profile that drives calls but near-zero site clicks may mean the site isn't ranking alongside the map pack in organic.

## Slide 6 — Metrics I Stopped Reporting

**Headline:** Stopped: keyword rankings, domain authority, impressions.

**Body:**
→ **Keyword rankings**: volatile, personalised by location and device, and not connected to revenue. Every position move generates a question. Clients don't need the noise.
→ **Domain authority**: a third-party score that clients conflate with Google's ranking opinion. It isn't. Remove it from reports.
→ **Impressions**: useful internally for diagnosing CTR problems. Not a client-facing metric. A business with 80,000 impressions and 40 clicks has a CTR problem — but "80,000 impressions" sounds good and distracts from the real issue.
