**Topic:** Three GSC query language patterns that tell you exactly what to fix for professional services
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

## LinkedIn Post

I pull up GSC for a new professional services client and look at three query patterns before I touch anything else on the site.

Each pattern tells me something different — and points to a different fix.

**Pattern 1: Name-only queries dominating impressions**
When 80%+ of a client's search impressions come from their business name — firm name, practitioner name, branded variations — and almost nothing from service + city queries, that's a brand-only traffic problem.

They rank when someone already knows them. They don't rank for the searches that happen before a patient or client has a referral or a recommendation.

Fix: this is a content gap, not a technical issue. The site needs service-specific pages with city modifiers. The GBP category probably needs attention too. There's no optimisation shortcut for a page that doesn't exist.

**Pattern 2: Service + city queries sitting at 20–50 impressions**
This is the fast-movement zone. The page exists. Google is showing it. But it's ranking at position 12–25 — visible on page 2, invisible in practice.

A small number of targeted changes move these: sharpen the H1 to match the exact query language (not the internal service name — the words a customer types), put the city modifier in the first paragraph, add one internal link from a higher-authority page on the same site.

Most clients with a 20–50 impression query can move it to page 1 in 30–60 days without new content. This is the highest-ROI optimisation work I do.

**Pattern 3: Question queries at 4–12 impressions**
"How long does [service] take [city]." "Is [service] covered by OHIP." "What is the difference between [option A] and [option B]."

These are pre-purchase research queries. Low impressions, but the intent is high — someone asking this question is close to choosing a provider.

If the current page answers the question poorly (buried, buried in dense text, or not answered at all), adding a FAQ section with a 40–60 word direct answer for each question — formatted as H3 + paragraph — moves these into featured snippet territory and AI Overview eligibility.

It's slower than a title tag fix but the conversion quality is different.

---

Check your GSC Performance report, filter by queries, and find one example of each. You'll almost always find all three in a professional services account.
