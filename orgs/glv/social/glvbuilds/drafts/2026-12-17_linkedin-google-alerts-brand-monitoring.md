# LinkedIn Text + IG Static: Google Alerts for Brand Monitoring

## LinkedIn Post (~380 words)

I set up Google Alerts for every new client in week one. It takes five minutes. Costs nothing. Catches things that would otherwise go unnoticed for months.

Here's what it actually surfaces:

**Unlinked press mentions**

A local news site publishes an article that references the business — but doesn't include a link. Google Alerts picks it up within 24 hours.

That's a warm outreach reason: "Noticed you mentioned us in your piece on [topic] — would you be able to add a link to [URL]?" Most say yes, in about five minutes. Same idea as the Dec 5 post on finding unlinked brand mentions via search operators — this just automates the discovery part.

**Review site updates**

New reviews posted on HomeStars, Yelp, or BBB trigger an alert. You find out the same day instead of three months later when a client asks "did you see that review from October?"

**Directory errors**

Some directories scrape or aggregate your business info and get it wrong — old phone number, incorrect address format. An alert surfaces the live listing before the NAP mismatch compounds.

**Competitor monitoring**

Set a separate alert for your top competitor's brand name. When they pick up press, links, or major coverage, you know. Useful context heading into a client reporting conversation.

**Setup**

Go to alerts.google.com.

Create one alert with your exact business name in quotes: "Business Name"

Add a second: "Business Name" + your city

Delivery: weekly digest keeps inbox noise manageable. Daily works if you're monitoring something active.

Takes two minutes per client. I add them during the same onboarding session as GBP access and GSC setup.

The link opportunities this surfaces are ones most clients don't know exist — because they had no system for seeing them. An alert doesn't replace a proactive outreach strategy, but it catches the low-effort wins that would otherwise disappear quietly.
