## LinkedIn Caption (for sharing the reel)

400 impressions for "[service] in [city]" last month. Zero clicks.

This is one of the most common patterns I find when I take on a new client. Google is showing their site in search results. Nobody is clicking on it.

Before touching anything, check one number: position.

If the position is 20 or higher, low CTR is a misleading signal. The page isn't getting clicks because nobody sees it — it's too far down. The fix is ranking improvement, not title tag optimisation. Different problem, different approach.

If the position is 4 to 10, the page is visible. People are seeing the listing and choosing not to click. That's a title tag problem.

Here's the 15-minute fix for the 4–10 case:

**Step 1: Look at the title tag.** Open the page. Check the actual title tag in the HTML — not just the H1. Is it generic ("Services | Business Name")? Is it leading with the business name instead of the service and city? Is it over 60 characters and getting cut off in search results? Any of these will kill CTR on an otherwise well-ranked page.

**Step 2: Rewrite using the formula.** [Service] in [City] | [Differentiator]. Under 60 characters. Lead with the service and city — that's what the searcher typed, that's what should appear first in the result. Add a differentiator that's specific and true: response time, a service detail, a trust signal. Not "best" or "trusted" — something actual.

**Step 3: Save and request indexing.** GSC → URL Inspection → paste the URL → Request Indexing. This speeds up Google's crawl of the updated title tag. Without this, it can take weeks for the change to appear in search results.

**Step 4: Wait and measure.** Check GSC again in 2–3 weeks. CTR should move. If position also improves, the new title tag may also be helping relevance.

This is a narrow fix — it doesn't address the position 20+ problem, and it doesn't help if the page itself is thin or unhelpful. But for a well-ranked page with a generic or over-long title, this 15 minutes regularly produces a measurable CTR lift.

Distinct from the Oct 14 GSC weekly check (which identifies this pattern across all pages) — this is the single-page title tag repair workflow once you've found the problem.
