## LinkedIn Caption

Most financial planning websites describe services: retirement planning, investment management, estate planning. The issue is that clients don't search for services — they search because something happened.

Here's what a local SEO audit of a financial planning practice typically surfaces:

**Per-goal pages**
"Retirement planning [city]," "RESP financial advisor [city]," and "estate planning [city]" are separate searches from people at separate life stages with separate sets of questions. A single "financial planning services" page with a bulleted list of areas ranks for none of them specifically. A page per goal — retirement, education savings (RESP/FHSA), estate planning, insurance review, tax coordination — addresses the specific question the client is already asking.

**Life-event content**
Financial planning decisions are triggered by life events: new baby, promotion, divorce, approaching retirement, inheritance, death of a spouse. "Financial planning for new parents [city]" is a lower-competition search with a higher-intent client than "financial planner [city]." Pages and GBP posts timed to these life stages capture clients at their moment of highest motivation.

**CFP vs. IA distinction**
CFP (Certified Financial Planner) is a designation issued via FP Canada and is publicly verifiable on their registry. Investment Advisors (IAs) are regulated by CIRO. These are different credentials with different scopes of practice — and clients searching for financial help often don't know the difference. A clear explanation on your About or Credentials page of what you hold, what it means, and where to verify it is a trust signal that most competitor sites skip.

**Regulatory language**
No fabricated return claims, no implied guarantees. "Let's talk about your goals" is more credible than a headline rate no one believes. The regulatory environment in financial services means the compliance layer is also your content layer — accurate, qualified language is exactly what a cautious client needs to see before they pick up the phone.
