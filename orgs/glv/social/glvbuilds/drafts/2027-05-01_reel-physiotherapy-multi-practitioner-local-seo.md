# 2027-05-01 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Physiotherapy multi-practitioner local SEO — per-practitioner pages, per-condition pages, and booking friction
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Multi-practitioner physiotherapy clinics have more local SEO assets than almost any other health service. Most are using one of them."

---

**Beat 1 — Per-practitioner pages:**
"Each registered physiotherapist at your clinic is a separate local SEO opportunity. A named bio page — registered physiotherapist designation, FCAMPT or Gunn IMS certification if applicable, provincial registration number, conditions treated, languages spoken, real headshot — creates one ranking opportunity per practitioner. A team-grid page listing six physiotherapists is one opportunity where there could be six. Patients searching for a specific specialty — pelvic health, sports rehab, vestibular physio — often search by credential, not by clinic name."

---

**Beat 2 — Per-condition pages:**
"'Physiotherapy for back pain [city].' 'ACL recovery physio [city].' 'Rotator cuff physiotherapy [city].' 'Pelvic floor physiotherapy [city].' 'TMJ physiotherapy [city].' Each of these is a separate search made by a patient with a specific injury or condition. One 'conditions treated' page listing all of them ranks for none specifically. Per-condition pages — written in patient language with information about the recovery trajectory, what sessions look like, and approximate timelines — convert at a higher rate than generic clinic pages."

---

**Beat 3 — Insurance billing and booking friction:**
"Two questions every physiotherapy patient has before booking: does extended health cover this, and how long is the wait? Most clinic websites don't answer either clearly. 'We direct-bill most extended health plans' on the booking page removes the most common hesitation. An approximate current wait time — or a 'call for current availability' prompt with a phone number visible — tells patients whether to book here or keep looking. If patients have to email to ask, a percentage won't."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"One page per practitioner. One page per condition. Insurance info and wait time on the booking page."

---

## Production Notes

- Text overlays per beat — one sentence at a time
- No patient names, injury images, or treatment footage that identifies an individual
- Captions on
- Showing a clinic GBP listing or a generic search result works well for Beat 1
- "Pelvic health physiotherapy" and "vestibular physiotherapy" are good examples of credential-specific searches — consider text overlay for those phrases
