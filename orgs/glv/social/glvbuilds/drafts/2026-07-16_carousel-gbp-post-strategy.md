## Slide 2 — Why GBP Posts Matter for Local SEO

GBP posts appear in the Knowledge Panel.

They signal to Google that your listing is active.

They also give you a second shot at ranking for a keyword — inside your own panel, not just on the map.

Most businesses ignore this completely.

---

## Slide 3 — The 3 Post Types (and When to Use Each)

**Update** — "We now offer [service] in [area]." Use for new services or hours.

**Offer** — "Book by [date], get [thing]." Use when you have a real incentive.

**Event** — Seasonal: spring tune-up season, winterisation window. Drives bookings ahead of the rush.

**Avoid:** generic "we're open" posts. They don't answer any question.

---

## Slide 4 — The Title Formula

**[Service] + [Location] + one differentiator.**

Bad: "Check out our spring special!"

Good: "Lawn mower tune-up in Sudbury — book before the May rush."

Why: the title is indexed. The keyword in the title has a shot at surfacing your panel for that search.

---

## Slide 5 — The Body Formula

Three sentences max.

1. What the customer gets.
2. Why now (timing, availability, seasonal hook).
3. One CTA — phone, form, or booking link.

Don't write a paragraph. Customers read this on mobile in two seconds.

---

## Slide 6 — Photo or No Photo?

Photo.

Posts with photos get more clicks in the panel. Any photo beats no photo. The "At Work" category I covered last week is ideal.

If you don't have a photo: use a clean graphic with the service name and city. No stock.

---

## Slide 7 — Cadence

Once a week minimum.

**Monday morning** works well — catches the "I need to book something this week" searcher.

Batch-write four posts in one sitting (one per week for the month). Schedule them in GBP or set a phone reminder to post manually.

## LinkedIn Caption (300–600 words)

Most businesses post on Google Business Profile like it's a Facebook status update.

"Happy Monday! Stop by and see us!"

That's not how GBP posts work. Here's what I build instead.

GBP posts show up in your Knowledge Panel — the panel that appears when someone searches your business name, or sometimes when they search your service + city. They also signal to Google that your listing is maintained, which matters for how often you surface in the local pack.

The post structure I use:

**Title:** [Service] + [Location] + one differentiator. "Drain repair Sudbury — same-day slots open." That title is indexed. It gives you a second keyword surface inside your own panel.

**Body:** Three sentences. What the customer gets. Why now. One CTA (phone, form, or booking — pick one).

**Photo:** Always. Posts with photos outperform text-only posts in the panel. If you don't have a good shot of the work, a clean graphic with the service name and city beats nothing.

**Post type:** Update for new services or hours. Offer when you have a real incentive. Event for seasonal timing (spring tune-up season, winterisation booking window). Avoid generic "we're open" posts.

**Cadence:** Once a week minimum. Monday morning catches the "I need to book something this week" searcher. Batch-write a month's posts in one sitting and either schedule them in GBP directly, or set a phone reminder to paste and post manually.

The businesses I see consistently in the local pack do this. They don't do anything exotic. They just post regularly, with a keyword in the title, a clear CTA in the body, and a photo.
