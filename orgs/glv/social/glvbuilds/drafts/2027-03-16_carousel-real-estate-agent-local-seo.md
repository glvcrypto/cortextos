## Slide 2 — The Individual Agent GBP Problem

## Slide 3 — Individual GBP Setup

Primary category: **Real Estate Agent** (not "Real Estate Agency" — that's the brokerage).

Profile name: the agent's full name, not the brokerage brand.

Phone: direct mobile line. Calls routed through a brokerage switchboard are leads that reach whoever answers — not the agent.

---

## Slide 4 — Neighbourhood Landing Pages

"Toronto homes for sale" is national competition. "Forest Hill condos for sale" is not.

One page per neighbourhood you actively work — 300–400 words, recent sold context (defensible language on specifics: "mid-$700,000s" where exact figures require consent), proximity signals, your bio.

---

## Slide 5 — Review Separation

Brokerage reviews rank the brokerage. Agent reviews rank the agent.

Ask every closed client to review your personal GBP profile — not the brokerage's.

"[Agent name] + [city] + realtor" shows up in GSC for active agents. That's a ranking you own when you have reviews on your own profile.

---

## Slide 6 — NAP Alignment

Your individual GBP name must match: personal business cards, personal website bio, CREA/MLS agent directory, real estate board profile.

The brokerage address is fine as your physical office address. The listed name must be the agent's name — not the brokerage's. Mismatched NAP across these sources weakens all of them.

## LinkedIn Caption

I've audited local SEO for real estate agents at three different brokerages. The same mistake keeps showing up: the brokerage GBP is set up correctly. The individual agents aren't on GBP at all.

When someone searches "real estate agent [city]" or "realtor [neighbourhood]," Google's map pack shows individual agents — not brokerages. The brokerage brand does not rank for the agent's name. Two separate visibility opportunities, and most agents are only capturing one.

Here's what individual agent GBP setup looks like:

**Primary category: Real Estate Agent**
Not "Real Estate Agency" — that's the brokerage. "Real Estate Agent" is a distinct GBP category. Check what the top three individual agents in your market are using in their primary category.

**Profile name: the agent's name**
Not the brokerage. Not "Jane Smith @ RE/MAX." Just Jane Smith. The brokerage association goes in the business description.

**Direct phone number**
The agent's mobile, not the brokerage switchboard. A call routed to a receptionist is a lead that doesn't reach the agent.

**Neighbourhood landing pages**
"Toronto homes for sale" is national-volume competition. "Forest Hill condos for sale" is not. One page per neighbourhood you actively work — 300–400 words, recent sold context with defensible language (REBBA advertising guidelines apply — "in the mid-$700,000s" is defensible where an exact figure may not be without explicit consent), a brief bio, and proximity signals.

**Review separation**
Brokerage reviews help the brokerage rank. Agent reviews rank the agent. Ask every closed client to leave a review on your personal GBP — not the brokerage's. "Your name + city + realtor" appears in GSC for active agents. That search becomes a ranking opportunity when you have reviews on your own profile.

**NAP alignment**
Your GBP name must match your personal business cards, personal website bio, CREA/MLS profile, and your real estate board directory listing. Mismatched NAP across these sources dilutes all of them.

Most agents I've audited rely entirely on brokerage visibility. The agents ranking individually in the local pack have done the setup above.
