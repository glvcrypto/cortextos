## LinkedIn Caption

When a local SEO client tells me they're opening a second location, the first thing I ask is: "Do you understand that the two profiles are completely independent?"

Most don't. They assume reviews carry over. They assume the second location will inherit some authority from the first.

Here's what actually happens.

**Each location is a separate entity on Google.**

Different address, different phone number, different hours — all need to be accurate and distinct. Google will flag locations that share the same contact details, and duplicate phone numbers are a common cause of GBP suspension.

**Reviews start at zero.**

Whatever review count Location A has built — that stays with Location A. Location B goes live with nothing. That's not a bug, it's by design. Google's review system is tied to the physical location, not the business name.

**Both profiles surface in search simultaneously.**

This is the one that surprises business owners most. A search for your business name can return both profiles. If Location B has a thin profile — incomplete categories, no reviews, outdated photos — it can outrank Location A for a location-adjacent search.

Your weakest location becomes someone's first impression of your brand.

The fix: treat the second profile exactly like you treated the first. Full category selection. Accurate hours. At Work photos on day one. Description written to the 750-character limit.

Before you open a second location: run a Google search for your business name from a private browser. See what shows. Then build the second profile to the same standard as your best one.
