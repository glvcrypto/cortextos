# Franchise Local SEO — What You Actually Control

## Slide 1 (Hook)

Franchise owners ask me this more than anyone:

"The corporate site ranks for our service. My location doesn't. What can I actually do?"

Here's the honest answer.

---

## Slide 2

**First, understand the split.**

What corporate controls: the main domain, national site structure, brand messaging, sometimes your GBP primary category.

What you control: your individual GBP profile, your local reviews, any local pages if you have your own domain.

This matters because the fix depends entirely on which side the problem is on.

---

## Slide 3

**Your GBP is your primary lever.**

Verify it's in your name — or at minimum, that you have Owner-level access, not just Manager. One verified GBP per physical location.

The national corporate site can rank in organic. Only your location-specific GBP can rank in the map pack for searches near your address.

---

## Slide 4

**Reviews are your strongest differentiator.**

Corporate can't generate local reviews for your location. You can.

A franchise location with 80 reviews at 4.7 will outrank a competing franchise location with 12 at 4.1 in the map pack — even if they share the same brand.

Start here. Reviews are the one local signal no one can give you except your actual customers.

---

## Slide 5

**Local pages — if you have your own domain.**

If corporate has given you a subdirectory (yourbrand.com/your-city/), work within it: fill out the service descriptions, add real photos of your location, localise the content.

If you have your own domain: build a dedicated [service] in [your city] page. Internal link from your homepage. Get one local citation pointing to it.

---

## Slide 6

**What you cannot outrank directly:**

For generic national keywords ("brand name + service"), the corporate domain almost always wins on organic. Domain authority + age + internal links — you can't replicate that at a franchise level.

Don't try to compete on those terms. Compete on "[service] in [your city]" where proximity is the dominant ranking signal and your local GBP is the asset that matters most.

---

## Slide 7

**The realistic outcome:**

You may never rank above the corporate site for broad terms.

You can rank for the searches that matter most to you — the ones where someone near your location is ready to buy.

That's the map pack. It runs on GBP, reviews, and proximity. Not on the national domain's authority.
