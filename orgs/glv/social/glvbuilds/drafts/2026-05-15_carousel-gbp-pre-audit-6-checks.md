## LinkedIn Caption

The 6-point pre-audit I run before making any change to a client's Google Business Profile.

Most people jump straight into optimising. I learned to stop and look first.

I've inherited profiles from bad agencies, found suspended listings clients didn't know about, and made the mistake of editing something before I had a baseline screenshot. These 6 checks now take 10 minutes at the start of every new engagement. They've saved me from making things worse more times than I can count.

Here's what I look at:

**1. Who is the Primary Owner?**
Every GBP profile has one Primary Owner with full control, including the ability to transfer or delete the listing. If a previous agency is Primary Owner and the relationship ended badly, your client has a serious problem. Check this first. If it's an agency account, you'll need to request ownership transfer before you do anything else.

**2. Is there a duplicate listing?**
Open a private browser and search the business name. If two profiles come up, the duplicate will suppress the primary listing. I've found duplicate listings on clients who had no idea they existed. One listing gets the review traffic, the other shows up in Maps and confuses customers.

**3. Is the profile verified, flagged, or suspended?**
GBP doesn't always email businesses when there's a problem. Check the status in GBP Manager before assuming the profile is active and visible. A profile showing as unverified or suspended has limited visibility in local search. If it's suspended, there's a specific reinstatement process to work through before any optimisation makes sense.

**4. Screenshot the current state before changing anything.**
Primary category, hours, description, photo count. I take screenshots of everything before I change a thing. When clients ask "did it always say that?" I can show them. When Google adds a suggested edit I didn't approve, I can prove it wasn't me. Baseline documentation takes 5 minutes.

**5. Are there pending user-suggested edits?**
Google lets anyone suggest edits to a business listing. If a past customer or a competitor suggested an incorrect address, wrong hours, or a category change, it may be sitting in the queue unreviewed. Check this before making your own edits so you know what you're working with.

**6. Does the NAP match the website exactly?**
Business name, address, and local phone number in GBP need to match the website footer, the LocalBusiness schema, and the top citation directories. If there's a mismatch, anything you build on top of it will produce inconsistent signals. This check takes 2 minutes with a browser window open beside GBP Manager.

10 minutes. Every new client. Every time.
