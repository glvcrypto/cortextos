## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Immigration consultants have one of the most intent-specific local search profiles I work with. The problem is that most websites are built for someone who has already decided to hire them."

---

**Beat 1 — Per-application-type pages:**
"'Express Entry consultant [city].' 'Family sponsorship consultant [city].' 'Study permit consultant [city].' 'Work permit [city].' 'Visitor visa [city].'

Each of these searches is made by someone at a specific stage with a specific application in mind. They are not browsing. They are comparing consultants who can handle exactly their situation. A general 'immigration services' page that lists all application types covers all of them insufficiently. A page for each application type — what the process involves, what documents are typically required, approximately how long the process takes at current IRCC processing times, and what the consultant's role is at each stage — converts at a higher rate because it answers the specific questions the searcher is already asking."

---

**Beat 2 — RCIC credential as trust signal:**
"Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants are licensed by the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants. Every licensed RCIC has a public registration number. That registration number on every page of the website — not just a footer line, but stated clearly on service pages and in the bio — is simultaneously a legal requirement in advertising and a trust signal to prospective clients. Clients searching for immigration help are often in a high-stakes situation. An RCIC number that can be verified in seconds on the CICC public registry is the fastest trust signal available. Most RCIC websites bury it."

---

**Beat 3 — Consultation model matters for lead quality:**
"'Free immigration consultation [city]' and 'immigration consultant [city]' attract different clients. A 'free 15-minute consultation' may attract high volume and low commitment. A 'paid initial assessment' signals that the consultant's time and expertise have value and tends to attract clients who are ready to proceed.

Neither is wrong — but the consultation model should be stated clearly on the booking page, because the client who books a free call expecting a full assessment and receives a brief intake screen feels misled. Copy that sets expectations correctly before the booking — what the consultation covers, what happens afterward, what the next step looks like — reduces that friction and produces better-fit bookings."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Per-application-type pages. RCIC number visible on every service page. A booking page that sets expectations before the client books."

## Production Notes

- Text overlays per beat — one key point per screen
- No client names, file numbers, or immigration case details
- Captions on
- Good hook visual: search results page showing "express entry consultant [city]" — high specificity
- CICC = College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (formerly ICCRC — name changed 2021; use CICC throughout)
- RCIC = Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (the credential designation)
- Do NOT reference specific IRCC processing times — use "current IRCC processing times" language only; processing times change frequently
- Do NOT reference specific success rates or approval statistics — inadmissible as advertising under CICC rules
- Immigration lawyers (regulated by provincial law societies) are distinct from RCICs — do not conflate
