## LinkedIn Caption

Most of my clients have never claimed their Nextdoor Business Page.

It takes less than 20 minutes and it's free. Here's what it actually does — and when it's worth your time.

**What Nextdoor Business is**

A verified local listing visible to residents in your service neighbourhood. Different from your GBP — it lives inside a platform where people actively ask neighbours for recommendations. "Anyone know a good plumber?" posts get real engagement from real locals.

**Who it's actually for**

Trades and home services: plumbers, electricians, HVAC, landscapers, roofers, cleaners.
Local retail and food: bakeries, hardware stores, coffee shops.
Health and wellness: physio, dental, massage, optometrists.

Not a strong fit for: B2B services, e-commerce, or businesses with a service area larger than a city.

**The 3-step setup**

1. Claim your page at nextdoor.com/business — verify with your business address.
2. Add your services, website, phone, and a photo that shows your work, not a logo.
3. Post once or twice a week: local tips, seasonal reminders, a before-and-after — not sales pitches.

**What not to do**

Don't post promotional content constantly. Nextdoor members flag businesses that spam the feed.
Don't ignore recommendations — when someone mentions you in a "who's good for X?" thread, respond in the comments.

**The honest ROI conversation**

Nextdoor won't replace GBP. The audience is smaller and the intent is lower than a "plumber near me" search.

But for businesses where word-of-mouth drives most new clients — trades, home services, personal care — it's one more place residents find you when asking their neighbours. Low effort once set up. Worth the 20 minutes to claim.

Have you seen any referrals come in from Nextdoor? I'm curious what service types are getting the most traction.
