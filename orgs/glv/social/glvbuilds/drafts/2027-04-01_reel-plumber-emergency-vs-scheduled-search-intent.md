# 2027-04-01 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Emergency vs. scheduled search intent for plumbers
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"There are two types of people searching for a plumber. They search completely differently."

---

**Beat 1 — Scheduled intent:**
"Scheduled search: 'plumber [city].' Comparison mode. They read reviews, check the website, maybe call two numbers. They're not in a rush."

---

**Beat 2 — Emergency intent:**
"Emergency search: 'burst pipe [city],' '24-hour plumber [city],' 'emergency plumber near me.' Phone-first. Decision window under 30 seconds. If your phone number isn't visible immediately, they've already called someone else."

---

**Beat 3 — The gap:**
"Most plumbing sites have one 'Services' page that tries to serve both. It doesn't work for either. Emergency searches want a phone number above the fold and hours that say 24/7. They don't want to read about your team."

---

**Beat 4 — The fix:**
"One dedicated emergency page. URL: /emergency-plumber/[city]/. H1 includes 'emergency.' Phone number above the fold. Hours listed prominently. GBP Q&A seeded: 'Do you offer emergency plumbing service?' — answered with your hours and direct line."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Two search intents. Two pages. Each capturing the right customer at the right moment."

---
