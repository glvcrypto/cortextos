## Slide 2 — What "near me" actually is

**Google doesn't rank you for the phrase "near me." It ranks you for proximity to the searcher.**

When someone types "[service] near me," Google isn't looking for pages that say "near me." It uses the searcher's device location and your verified GBP address to calculate physical distance.

Writing "near me" in your title tag adds nothing. Google already knows where your business is and where the searcher is.

---

## Slide 3 — Signal 1: Proximity (GBP address)

**Proximity is determined by your verified GBP address vs. the searcher's GPS location.**

If your GBP address is verified and accurate, and the searcher is physically close to you, you have a proximity advantage.

If your GBP address is wrong, incomplete, or you're using a virtual office address in a market you don't physically serve — you lose proximity entirely. Google's confidence in your location drops.

Fix: verify GBP address, make it match your actual service location, ensure it's complete and consistent with your website and citations.

---

## Slide 4 — Signal 2: Category relevance

**Your primary GBP category must match what the searcher is looking for.**

Google needs to be confident your business type matches the query. If someone searches "plumber near me" and your primary category is "Contractor," Google isn't sure you're a plumber.

Category relevance is a prerequisite. Without it, proximity doesn't matter — Google won't surface you for a query type it doesn't associate with your profile.

Fix: research what primary category your top-ranking local competitors use. Match their specificity level. (Extends Oct 9 primary category post.)

---

## Slide 5 — Signal 3: Prominence

**Proximity and category get you considered. Prominence determines whether you rank above the others who are also close.**

Prominence factors include:
- Review count and average rating
- Profile completeness (photos, services, description, hours)
- GBP post activity (signals the profile is actively managed)
- Mentions and links from other local websites

A business two blocks away with 150 reviews and a complete profile will outrank a business one block away with 12 reviews and a half-filled profile.

---

## Slide 6 — The one legitimate "near me" opportunity

**Review justifications — not your copy.**

The one place "near me" legitimately appears in Google's local pack results is in review justifications — snippets pulled from customer reviews where the reviewer mentioned your service area.

You can't engineer this directly. But you can prompt customers to be specific when they leave reviews: "mention the service you had done and where you're located." Some will naturally write "best [service] near [neighbourhood]" — and Google surfaces those phrases.

---

## Slide 7 — What to do instead

**Stop writing "near me." Fix the three signals instead.**

Checklist:
1. Verify and confirm GBP address is accurate and complete
2. Check primary category against top 3 local competitors — match specificity
3. Review count: is it growing consistently? (Velocity post, Oct 5)
4. Profile completeness: every section filled, photos current, GBP posts active
5. GSC → Performance: search "near me" queries to see how you're already appearing
