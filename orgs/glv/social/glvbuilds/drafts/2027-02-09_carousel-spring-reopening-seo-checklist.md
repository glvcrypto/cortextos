## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
"Every spring I run through the same checklist before a seasonal client reopens.

It takes 25 minutes. Most businesses open the doors without doing any of it."

**Slide 2 — Remove "Temporarily Closed" early**
Last fall you marked your GBP Temporarily Closed. Today, go back in and remove that status before your opening date — not on it.

Google can take several days to propagate changes. If you flip it the morning you open, customers are still seeing "closed" that afternoon. Start three weeks out.

**Slide 3 — Update regular hours and clear old special hours**
Set your new season hours as regular hours.

Then check Special Hours and delete any fall/winter overrides. While you're there: if spring statutory holidays are coming up — Good Friday, Victoria Day — set them now. Propagation lag means doing it on the weekend before is already too late.

**Slide 4 — Refresh your GBP description**
If you left a "see you in spring" note in your description last October, replace it with your current service offering and accurate dates.

"Open [Month] through [Month]" should reflect this year's real window. Current, accurate descriptions signal an active, maintained listing.

**Slide 5 — Update your website**
Check: homepage hero, contact page hours, footer.

If any of them still show last season's dates or "returning in spring" messaging, update them before search traffic picks up. Then: GSC → URL Inspection → Request Indexing on every page you changed.

**Slide 6 — Seed the Q&A**
Before you open, ask and answer "Are you open for the season?" in your GBP Q&A — ask from your personal Google account, answer from the GBP account.

If you don't seed it, customers (or strangers who don't know your hours) answer it for you. That guess gets indexed.

**Slide 7 — CTA**

## LinkedIn Caption

I work with a handful of seasonal businesses that close every winter. Every spring, the same problems come up — customers find them on Google and see "Temporarily Closed" even though the doors are open.

Most of it is fixable in 25 minutes if you do it before you open, not after the complaints arrive.

**The spring reopening checklist I run every year:**

**1. Remove "Temporarily Closed" three weeks early.** GBP propagation takes days, not hours. Don't wait until opening day — by then, customers have already moved on or called a competitor.

**2. Update regular hours and clear old special hours.** Last fall's override hours are still sitting there. Replace them with your actual spring/summer hours, then set any upcoming statutory holiday hours while you're already in the panel.

**3. Refresh the GBP description.** If you left a "see you in spring" note last October, replace it with your current service offering and accurate seasonal dates. Current descriptions signal an active, maintained listing.

**4. Update the website.** Homepage hero, contact page, footer. If any of these still say "returning in spring" — change them and Request Indexing in Google Search Console for every page you touch.

**5. Seed the Q&A.** Ask and answer "Are you open for the season?" from your personal account right now, before your customers come in and random strangers answer it incorrectly.

It's not complicated. It's the kind of thing that gets skipped when you're busy with everything else that comes with reopening.
