## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
"Local SEO month 2 looks like nothing is happening.

It isn't. Here's what to look for at 30, 60, and 90 days — and what each signal actually means."

**Slide 2 — Why timelines matter more than tactics**
Most local SEO concerns I hear in the first 90 days come from not knowing what signals to expect and when.

Month 2 looks slow from the outside. That's normal. It's also when most people make reactive changes that interrupt the work before it can compound.

Understanding the timeline doesn't require patience — it requires knowing what you're waiting for.

**Slide 3 — Day 30: structure signals**
By day 30, these should be true:

— GBP is verified and appears when you search the exact business name in Google Maps
— All primary GBP fields are complete: category, services, hours, website URL, photos, description
— Website has a contact page with matching NAP (name, address, phone) and a live interactive Google Maps embed
— GSC is verified and showing crawl data — any coverage errors identified

If GBP isn't verified at day 30, everything else waits.

**Slide 4 — Day 60: first organic signals**
By day 60:

— GSC should show first impressions for service+city queries — even if position is 30–50, the page is being evaluated
— GBP should be appearing in map pack results when you search the business name (brand searches first, then service searches follow)
— First 3–5 reviews collected via guided ask — not incentivised, just asked at the right moment

These are signals that Google has found and indexed the work. Position 30–50 at day 60 is expected and on track.

**Slide 5 — Day 90: momentum signals**
By day 90:

— Service pages ranking in position 20–50 for target service+city queries in GSC
— Map pack appearance on some searches for core service+city (not all, not consistently — that comes later)
— At least one citation live in each of the three core directories: Bing Places, Apple Maps, one industry-specific
— GBP Insights showing upward trend in profile views and website clicks from the listing

Position 20–50 at day 90 is not failure — it's early momentum. The question at 90 days is direction, not destination.

**Slide 6 — What slow at 90 days actually means**
If none of the day-90 signals have appeared, three things to check before changing tactics:

1. Is the GBP fully verified, with category, services, and photos complete? Incomplete profiles rank poorly before anything else.
2. Are the service pages indexed? Run `site:yourdomain.com/service-page` in incognito to confirm.
3. Is the NAP consistent across GBP, website, and any live citations? Inconsistencies slow down trust signals.

Fix the foundation before adding new work.

**Slide 7 — CTA**
The businesses that get the most out of local SEO are the ones who understand what to look for — and don't pull the plug in month 2 because it looks quiet.
