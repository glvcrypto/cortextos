## Slide 2 — GBP: Right Category, Emergency Signal Front and Centre

"Locksmith" is the correct primary GBP category. Not "Security System Supplier," not "Safe and Vault Shop" — unless those are genuinely your core business. Broad category = broader map visibility.

Two signals most locksmith GBP profiles are missing:

**24/7 availability.** This belongs in your profile description, your services list, and your website above the fold. A client locked out at midnight has one question: are you available right now? If your profile and homepage don't confirm that immediately, you lose the call.

**Response time phrasing.** State a range, not a guarantee — "typically within 30–60 minutes" is safe and credible; "guaranteed 20-minute arrival" is a liability unless that's your verified SLA. Use a range you actually achieve.

## Slide 3 — Emergency Lockout Page: Phone First, Nothing Else

The lockout page is the highest-converting page on a locksmith website. It needs to do one thing: get the phone to ring.

What this page requires:
- Phone number above the fold — in the header, in a sticky bar, and in a click-to-call button visible without scrolling
- "24/7" stated explicitly in the headline or subhead, not in a paragraph the client will never read
- Service area confirmation — "serving [city], [neighbourhood], [city]" — so the client knows you cover their location before calling
- Response time range stated near the CTA (not in the footer)
- No contact form as the primary CTA — a client standing outside in the rain is not filling out a form

What this page does not need: a gallery, a long history section, or a navigation to ten other services. Strip it to the conversion.

## Slide 4 — Per-Service Pages Beyond Lockout

Lockout calls pay the bills but are not your only search traffic. Each service below has its own search cluster and needs its own page:

- **Lock change / rekey** — high-intent post-move-in search ("rekey locks after moving [city]")
- **Deadbolt installation** — homeowner upgrade search; safety-driven, not emergency
- **Key cutting / duplication** — transactional, local, competitive; clients search by key type (car key cutting, transponder key, fob programming)
- **Master key system** — commercial and multi-unit residential; distinct buyer (property manager, not homeowner)
- **Access control installation** — commercial upgrade; leads to longer-term relationships than residential lockout

One "services" page listing all of these ranks for none of them specifically. Each page needs its own H1, its own context paragraph, and its own conversion path.

## Slide 5 — Commercial vs. Residential: Different Buyers, Different Pages

Residential and commercial locksmith clients have different buying timelines, different decision-makers, and different trust signals.

**Residential:** fast decision, individual homeowner, price-sensitive, urgency-driven. Trust signals: reviews, response time, local presence.

**Commercial:** longer cycle, facilities manager or property manager as decision-maker, contract-based relationships (ongoing maintenance, master key systems, access control), invoice-required billing. Trust signals: business registration, insurance certificate, references from comparable commercial clients (e.g., multi-unit residential, retail, office).

A commercial locksmith page separate from your residential homepage captures property managers who are not in emergency mode — they are evaluating vendors for an ongoing relationship. This page can convert a $150 lockout into a $2,000 annual contract.

## Slide 6 — Trust Signals That Differentiate

In a market where locksmith scam operations inflate Google results with fake listings:

- **Licensing where required** — BC: Security Services Act licence (publicly searchable through the BC government registry); Alberta: Security Services and Investigators Act registration. If your province has a licensing regime, display the number. If it doesn't, state what you have instead.
- **ALOA membership** — Associated Locksmiths of America and Canada; voluntary but publicly verifiable at aloa.org; signals professional standing in a market where trust is scarce
- **Business insurance** — bonded and insured should be explicitly stated, not implied
- **Google verified** — a GBP "Google Guaranteed" badge (Local Services Ads) signals vetting to clients who know what it means; the screening process includes background and insurance checks
- **Reviews naming the situation** — "called at midnight, arrived within 40 minutes, no upsell pressure" closes more calls than any credential
