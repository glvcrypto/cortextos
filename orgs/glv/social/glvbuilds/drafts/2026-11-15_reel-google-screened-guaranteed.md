## LinkedIn Caption (for sharing the reel)

Most business owners I work with have heard of Google Ads. Fewer have heard of Local Services Ads. And almost none have heard of the trust badges that come with them.

Here's the short version.

**Google Guaranteed**

For licensed home service businesses: plumbers, electricians, HVAC technicians, locksmiths, pest control, window cleaners. If you're a licensed trade that serves residential customers, this is probably available to you.

The badge shows up on your Local Services Ad — the green checkmark with "Google Guaranteed" below your business name. The guarantee means Google will back the job up to a set amount if a customer is unsatisfied and you can't resolve it. The specific amount varies by market; check the LSA dashboard for current terms in your area.

**Google Screened**

For professional services: lawyers, real estate agents, financial advisers, tax specialists, therapists. The verification is licence-based, not job-completion-based. The badge signals that Google has confirmed your professional credentials.

**How to qualify for either**

You need an active Local Services Ads account first. Once your account is approved, you pass a background check and licence verification. The badge is added to your listing automatically.

**Why this matters for local SEO**

LSAs and their badges appear above everything else in local search — above standard Google Ads, above the local pack, above organic results. Not every query triggers them, and eligibility varies by category and market. But if you're in an eligible category, the badge is one of the highest-visibility trust signals Google surfaces in search.

The catch: you need reviews to make it work. Google recommends at least 10 verified reviews before running LSAs. The badge increases visibility; reviews determine whether the click converts.
