# First Session With a New Local SEO Client — What I Check Before I Touch Anything

## Hook (on screen + voice-over)

When I take over a new local SEO client's website,
I run through the same checklist before I change anything.

Here's what I look at in the first session.

---

## Body (on screen, one line per beat)

**Why diagnose first:**
Optimising a site without knowing its current state is how you make things worse.
Five checks. All free. All take under 10 minutes total.

**Check 1 — GSC connected and verified?**
Many sites have GSC set up but the wrong property type selected or the verification expired.
No GSC = no baseline data.

**Check 2 — Any Manual Actions in GSC?**
A Manual Action from Google means something is actively penalising the site.
Fix this before any other work — optimisation on a penalised site doesn't help.

**Check 3 — Is the site actually indexed?**
Type "site:[domain]" in Google.
How many pages are showing? Is the homepage in results?
Indexation problems are common on older sites and recent rebuilds.

**Check 4 — Redirect chains or broken 301s?**
Run Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs).
Chains (301 → 301 → 301) dilute link equity and slow load time.

**Check 5 — Is the GBP linked to the correct website URL?**
Mismatch between the GBP website URL and the actual site is more common than you'd think.
Especially after a domain change or site rebuild.

---

## Ending (on screen)

Don't start optimising until you know what you're working with.
Diagnose first. Then build.
