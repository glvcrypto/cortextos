## LinkedIn Caption (posted with reel embed)

There's a GSC filter most local SEO audits skip entirely.

By default, the GSC Performance report shows "Search type: Web." But Google surfaces your pages in four other places — and each one has its own impression and click data hiding in that dropdown.

**Image search:** If your business has before/after photos, job site images, or product shots on your website, you may be getting meaningful impressions from image search every month. This traffic doesn't appear in your Web default — you have to switch to Image to see it. High impressions with low clicks usually means the image is being found but isn't compelling enough to click. Better file names, descriptive alt text, and image compression (the Oct 24 post covers the full framework) address this directly.

**Video search:** For clients who've embedded YouTube videos or Reels on their site, video search impressions may already be appearing. Most local service businesses have zero video impressions — which means early movers have an uncrowded channel.

**Discover:** Appears for sites with an active blog. Mostly content and media plays, but worth checking if your client does any article-format content.

**News:** Relevant for clients with press coverage or a news-format blog.

Monthly habit: switch through all five search types in GSC before pulling the standard monthly report. The Web tab tells you one story. The others fill in what it leaves out.
