## LinkedIn Post

The most common local SEO mistake I see at the franchise level isn't made by franchisees. It's made by franchisors.

It goes like this: corporate invests in national SEO, achieves strong brand rankings, and assumes local pack visibility follows automatically for each location.

It doesn't.

Local pack ranking runs on proximity, GBP completeness, and reviews. None of those are controlled at the national level. All three are controlled at the location level.

**What franchisors can do centrally:**
— Build a repeatable GBP setup playbook: verified address, correct primary category for each service type, fully completed Services tab, cover photo specs and guidelines
— Give franchise owners Owner-level GBP access — not just Manager. Owner access allows profile transfer and recovery from suspension; Manager access does not. This matters when a GBP profile gets incorrectly suspended
— Create a review collection template franchisees can actually use with real customers in their market
— Provide a location page framework: a structure with a required unique paragraph per city, not just a name-swap from a master template

**What franchisors cannot do:**
— Rank franchise locations in local pack through national domain authority
— Substitute a generic corporate phone number for a local number in GBP (the proximity signal is tied to the verified location — a corporate number mismatch weakens it)
— Transfer reviews between locations (they don't carry across GBP profiles — Location A's reviews don't help Location B)
— Manage local pack visibility in bulk from a central dashboard

The accountability question for any franchisor: when a customer searches "[service] in [city]" in a market where you have a location, do you know if that location appears in the local pack?

If the answer is no, that's the starting point — not the national rankings report.

## IG Caption

Franchisors often assume strong national SEO rankings cover local search for each location.

They don't.

Local pack visibility runs on proximity, GBP completeness, and reviews — all controlled at the location level, not centrally.

What you can do from corporate: a GBP setup playbook, Owner-level access for each franchisee (not just Manager), a review collection template, a location page framework with a required unique paragraph per city.

What you can't control centrally: the proximity signal, local review volume, or whether each location's GBP is actually complete and current.

The question every franchisor should be able to answer: which of your locations are appearing in local pack for your top service category in their city — and which aren't?
