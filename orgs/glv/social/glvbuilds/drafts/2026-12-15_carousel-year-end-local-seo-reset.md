## LinkedIn Caption (~420 words)

Every December I run the same five-check reset for every client.

Takes about 20 minutes. Most businesses skip it — then spend January wondering why rankings softened when they weren't watching.

Here are the five checks:

**Check 1: GBP info audit**

Log in to GBP Manager. Verify the basics: business name (make sure it's your actual business name, not a keyword-stuffed version), local phone number, website URL pointing to the right page, address format matching your schema and citations, and holiday hours set correctly.

Fix anything that has drifted. This sounds simple — and it is — but drifts happen and few people check.

**Check 2: Citation spot-check**

Pick five random directories: Yelp, YP.ca, Bing Places, Apple Maps, and one industry-specific. Search your business name in a private browser. Compare NAP against your GBP exactly — name spelling, address format including suite numbers, phone number format.

Flag every mismatch. Even small inconsistencies in the address format can create citation noise heading into Q1.

**Check 3: Low-performer page scan**

GSC → Performance → Pages. Sort by Impressions, descending. Find pages that had meaningful impressions in Q3 but have lost position heading into Q4.

Add those pages to a January refresh list — title tag, H1, first paragraph, internal link tighten. Don't delete and rewrite. Refresh. Preserves authority, addresses drift.

**Check 4: Competitor delta check**

Private browser. Check the top 2–3 competitors in your main city. How many reviews have they added since mid-year? Are they posting to GBP? Are they running Local Services Ads now?

Log what changed. Not to stress — to know what landscape you're heading into January facing. Surprises hurt more than known shifts.

**Check 5: January baseline reset**

Before December 31st: take a screenshot of GBP Insights (28-day view) and note your GSC impressions for the month. This becomes your Q1 baseline.

Proving growth to a client is much easier when you have a documented starting point from the first week of January — not an estimate made in March about what things probably were.

**Bonus:** Write your Q4 report now while the data is fresh. Six sections, one page: GBP wins, citation fixes, pages live, rank shifts, traffic benchmarks, Q1 goals. It goes from fresh recall to vague effort in a week.

Comment RESET and I'll DM you the checklist doc I use — one page, five checks, built for a 20-minute year-end review.
