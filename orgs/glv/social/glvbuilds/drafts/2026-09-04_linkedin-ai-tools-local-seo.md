# AI Tools in Local SEO — What I Use and What I Won't

## LinkedIn Post

I use AI tools in local SEO every week.

I also have a specific list of things I won't use them for.

**What I use AI for:**

— **First-draft copy.** Service page intros, GBP descriptions, review response templates. AI drafts, I edit, I publish. Cuts the blank-page problem in half.

— **FAQ question generation.** Given a service and a city, AI generates questions customers actually ask. I verify them against real search data, then write the answers myself.

— **Review response templates.** Five to seven base templates — positive review, neutral review, complaint. AI drafts the structure. I personalise before posting.

— **Data formatting.** Cleaning up keyword lists, reformatting exports from GSC or GBP. Tasks that are repetitive and low-stakes.

**What I won't use AI for:**

— **Service history claims.** "Serving the community for over 20 years." AI doesn't know. I verify with the client or I don't write it.

— **Certifications and brand authorisations.** If a business claims to be an authorised dealer or a certified installer, that needs to come from the source. Not a language model.

— **Statistics and benchmark claims.** Any figure cited needs a traceable source. AI generates plausible-sounding numbers. I don't publish unverifiable statistics.

— **Anything requiring a client-specific fact.** If the content sounds the same for every business in the category, it's not local SEO copy. It's a template.

**The guardrail:**

AI drafts. I verify. I publish.

If I can't verify a claim in under two minutes, it doesn't go in the copy.

That's the standard I apply to every piece of client content.
