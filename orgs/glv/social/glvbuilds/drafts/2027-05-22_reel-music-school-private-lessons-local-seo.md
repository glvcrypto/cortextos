## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Music schools and private lesson teachers have a hyperlocal search profile. Almost none of them are set up to capture it."

---

**Beat 1 — GBP category and per-instrument pages:**
"'Piano lessons [city].' 'Guitar teacher [neighbourhood].' 'Violin lessons for kids [city].' 'Adult singing lessons [city].'

These searches are instrument-specific and often neighbourhood-specific. A music school that teaches piano, guitar, violin, voice, drums, and flute — but only has one 'lessons' page — ranks for none of these queries specifically.

A page for each instrument, covering what learning that instrument involves at beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels, what the first lesson typically covers, what age groups the teacher works with, and how scheduling works — answers the questions a parent or adult learner has before they call. Per-instrument pages aren't a luxury. They're how you show up when someone searches for a specific instrument in your area."

---

**Beat 2 — Instructor bio pages:**
"Each named instructor is a local SEO asset.

A bio page — instruments taught, RCM teaching certification level if applicable, years of teaching experience, age groups (children, teens, adults, seniors), languages of instruction, teaching philosophy in one clear sentence — creates a page a student or parent can search by name and a page that ranks for things a staff grid never will.

In multilingual markets, an instructor who teaches in Mandarin, Cantonese, Korean, or Hindi and has that language signal on their bio page captures the search from a family who specifically wants lessons in that language. That search is real, it's consistent, and almost no music school bio page is set up for it."

---

**Beat 3 — Seasonal peaks and the trial lesson page:**
"Music schools have two predictable traffic peaks: September — back to school, parents researching activities for kids — and November through December, when gift certificates for lessons and new-year-new-hobby intent spike.

Content and GBP updates need to be in place before these peaks, not during them.

The highest-converting single page a music school can have is a trial lesson page. One lesson. Low commitment. Clearly described: what happens in the lesson, what to bring, whether the teacher provides the instrument for the first session, and how to book. A family on the fence about whether their child will stick with piano books a trial much more readily than they book a month of lessons. That page, with a clear booking flow, converts browsers into students."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Per-instrument pages. Named instructor bios with credential and language signals. A trial lesson page. Content live before the September and December peaks."
