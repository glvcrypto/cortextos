# 2027-04-10 — Reel (~30–35s)
**Topic:** Home inspection local SEO — the two searcher types and the service page gap
**CTA:** Open comment (no automation)

---

## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Home inspectors have a narrow, high-urgency search window. Most are only capturing one of the two searcher types."

---

**Beat 1 — The two searcher types:**
"Searcher one: the buyer's agent refers them. That's a brand search — they already know your name. Searcher two: the buyer goes straight to Google. Usually mobile, usually at night, often under time pressure because they just got an accepted offer. If your phone number and availability aren't visible in the first five seconds, that second searcher is gone."

---

**Beat 2 — The service page split:**
"'Pre-purchase home inspection [city].' 'Pre-listing inspection [city].' 'New construction inspection [city].' 'Condo inspection [city].' Four distinct searches with different buyer situations. One 'home inspection services' page ranks for none of them specifically. One page per service type — starting with the one that drives the most bookings."

---

**Beat 3 — Provincial designation on the bio page:**
"Buyers increasingly check credentials before booking. OAHI or PHI designation in Ontario, ASTTBC in BC — registration number on your bio page, next to your name and a real headshot. Not buried in an About page. The buyer who finds two equally-reviewed inspectors on Google calls the one whose credentials are verifiable in thirty seconds."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Phone number above the fold. One page per service type. Designation visible on your bio page."
