## LinkedIn Post

Every accountant and bookkeeper I've audited has the same GSC pattern in late January: a spike in impressions they weren't set up to capture.

Tax season search intent doesn't start in April. It starts when T4 slips arrive — mid-to-late January — and it peaks in March. By the time most accounting firms notice the traffic, the window is already closing.

Here's what to have in place six to eight weeks before the spike:

**Event-specific service pages, not blog posts**
"Personal tax return [city]," "self-employed tax filing [city]," "corporate tax filing [city]" — each is a different search intent with different urgency. These should be dedicated service pages with a phone number or booking CTA above the fold, not articles. Blog content ranks for information-seeking queries. Service pages rank for intent-to-hire queries.

**PAA content built off GSC**
GSC → Performance → search queries your site is getting impressions for but not ranking on. "What documents do I need to file taxes in Canada?" "Can I deduct home office expenses?" These are questions your clients ask in the consultation. If you haven't answered them on the site, someone else's blog is ranking for them. A focused 400–600 word post per question, with an internal link to the relevant service page, captures the research phase of the client journey.

**GBP Offer post before the peak**
GBP → Add update → Offer. Set an end date matching the personal filing deadline. "Accepting new personal tax clients — book a 15-minute call." Add the Booking attribute (GBP → Edit profile → More) if you use Calendly or a similar scheduler. Most accounting firms don't have this set up. The Offer badge appears on your listing in Maps during the active window.

The pattern repeats: January (T4 release), March–April (personal filing), June (corporate and trust filing, depending on year-end). Building these pages once means they're already indexed and accumulating authority before next season.
