## LinkedIn Post

A client texted me at 7am: "Rankings dropped overnight. What happened?"

It happens a few times a year — a Google update rolls out, traffic shifts, and the phone starts ringing with panicked clients.

Here's the framework I use. Step one is almost always: don't touch anything yet.

**Check if it's a real update.** Go to Semrush Sensor or Search Engine Roundtable. If volatility scores are spiking across industries, an update is in progress. If the sensor is calm, the drop may be page-specific — a different diagnostic applies.

**Determine the scope.** Site-wide ranking drops usually point to an algorithmic signal (content quality, E-E-A-T, link profile). Page-specific drops more often point to a technical issue, a title tag change, or a new competitor. Pull GSC and check whether the drop affects one URL or dozens.

**Compare competitor movement.** Check the top 3 competitors ranking for your main keywords. If they also moved — some up, some down — it's the algorithm reshuffling things. If your competitors held position and you dropped, the problem may be yours specifically.

**Wait two weeks before making changes.** This is the hardest part to explain to clients. Google updates typically roll out over 1–2 weeks, and the first position is often not the final position. Reactive changes made mid-rollout frequently make things worse by introducing new variables while the algorithm is still settling.

**After two weeks, if still down:** review content quality signals first — E-E-A-T, thin content, pages that haven't been updated in over a year. Then technical signals: coverage errors in GSC, crawl issues, Core Web Vitals. Backlink changes last — unless you can see a clear toxic link pattern in GSC Security & Manual Actions.

The clients who handle updates best are the ones who've built a documented baseline. If you have 90-day position averages for your top queries in GSC before the update, you have something to measure against. If you've been tracking at all, you're ahead of most.

Document the before state. Wait two weeks. Then act.
