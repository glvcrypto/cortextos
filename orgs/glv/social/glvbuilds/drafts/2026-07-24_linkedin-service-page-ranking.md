## LinkedIn Post

Most local service businesses have the same problem with their website.

They have one page called "Services."

It lists everything they do. Sometimes it has a photo. Almost always it has a "Contact Us" button at the bottom.

And Google mostly ignores it.

Not because the work is bad. Not because the business isn't legitimate.

Because the page is trying to rank for everything, so it ranks for almost nothing.

Here's what I change first:

**One dedicated page per service + city.**

Not "Lawn Care." A page called "Lawn Mower Repair in Sault Ste. Marie."

Not "Marine." A page called "Marine Engine Service Sault Ste. Marie."

Each page has:
- An H1 that includes the service and the city
- A title tag that matches what someone actually searches (not the business name)
- A first paragraph that answers the question before explaining anything about the business
- Specific details — equipment types, brands, what's included, what's not

That's it. No tricks. No backlinks required.

Google is trying to match a search to a page. The more specifically your page answers a specific search, the better it ranks for that search.

A "Services" page that covers ten things ranks for none of them reliably.

A page about one service in one city ranks for that.
