# Bing Places + Apple Maps — LinkedIn text post + IG static

## LinkedIn

**I add every new client to Bing Places and Apple Maps. It takes less than an hour. Most local SEOs skip both.**

This isn't about Bing vs. Google. It's about where local data ends up.

Bing's local index feeds Microsoft Copilot's local recommendations. If your business isn't in Bing Places, you're missing visibility with anyone who asks a Copilot-powered assistant for a local service recommendation — and that number is growing.

Apple Maps is simpler. The Apple Maps Connect listing takes 20 minutes. iPhone users see it in Maps, Siri uses it for "near me" queries, and it's a citation signal that feeds into aggregators.

Neither one will move your Google Maps rank. That's not the point. Local search is becoming multi-platform. A client who invests in local SEO should show up everywhere a potential customer might look.

Here's what I do for every new client in their first week:

- Claim or create Bing Places (you can import direct from GBP — takes five minutes)
- Verify business details match GBP exactly (NAP consistency)
- Claim or create Apple Maps Connect listing
- Add photos to each
- Note both in the client onboarding doc so they don't get forgotten at the next review

Less than an hour total. I've never had a client who had both set up correctly when I found them.

Where do you think local businesses are losing visibility because customers aren't only searching on Google? Let me know in the comments.
