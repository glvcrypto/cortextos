## LinkedIn Post

Local SEO advice is written almost entirely for B2C service businesses — plumbers, dentists, restaurants.

If you're a B2B service business (IT provider, commercial contractor, accountant serving businesses, consultant, staffing agency), the same fundamentals apply. But some signals matter differently.

**What still works:**

— GBP is still the anchor. "Commercial IT support Toronto" has lower search volume than "plumber Toronto" — but the intent is sharper. Set it up. Fill the Services tab with client-language terms, not your internal service names.

— Service pages with city + service + specific business problem still rank. "Bookkeeping for small businesses in [City]" is a different page from "bookkeeping services [city]" and converts differently.

— Reviews still matter. B2B buyers are more likely to read them carefully before committing. Three detailed reviews from other business owners outperform ten generic 5-stars.

**What's different:**

— GSC query language shifts. B2B queries are longer, more specific, often include industry terms or business size signals ("for restaurants," "for trades," "for clinics"). Pull your actual GSC queries before writing any content.

— Volume is lower; intent is higher. Don't compare impression counts to a consumer business. Ten calls from qualified decision-makers is a better month than 200 calls from consumers who can't use your service.

— The buying cycle is longer. Someone searching "commercial HVAC maintenance Toronto" is probably three conversations away from signing — but they started on Google. Your site needs to answer the commercial-specific questions that residential content doesn't.

B2B local SEO is under-optimised. That's the opportunity for the businesses that do it properly.
