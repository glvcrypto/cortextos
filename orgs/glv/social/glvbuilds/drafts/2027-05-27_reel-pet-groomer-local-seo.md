## LinkedIn Caption

I audit a lot of pet groomer websites. The most common issue: one "grooming services" page that lists every service in a bullet list. It ranks for none of them specifically.

Four things I look at first:

**GBP category.** "Pet Groomer" is the correct primary category — not "Animal Care Service" or "Dog Walker." This affects which filtered map searches your listing appears in. Confirm it on every inherited listing before doing anything else.

**Per-service pages.** "Full groom [city]," "dog bath [city]," "nail trim [city]," and "mobile dog grooming [city]" are different searches at different points in a client's decision process. A client looking for a nail trim only is not in the same buying mode as one researching a first full groom for a new puppy. A page per service gives each search something to land on.

**Mobile grooming.** If you offer it, it needs its own page. "Mobile dog groomer [city]" has consistent search volume and lower competition than "dog groomer [city]" in most local markets. Groomers who offer it and bury it under a general services page are missing a disproportionate share of the traffic relative to the effort of adding one page.

**Cat grooming.** "Cat groomer [city]" and "lion cut [city]" are common searches that many grooming websites do not address at all. If you groom cats, a cat-specific page — what you offer, how it differs from dog grooming, how to book a first appointment — captures searches that no one else in most markets is building pages for.

Groomer credentials worth listing on bios: IPG (International Professional Groomers) and NCMG (National Certified Master Groomer) are recognisable North American certifications. Completed training school and specialisation (breed expertise, hand-stripping, specific coat types) are legitimate trust signals for clients making an unfamiliar booking.
