**Topic:** Childcare and daycare local SEO — trust signals and category precision

## Script

**[Hook — text on screen]**
Childcare is one of the most trust-dependent local searches Google handles. Parents don't scroll past page 1.

**[Cut — on screen]**
First thing I check in any childcare audit: GBP primary category.

**[Step 1 — on screen]**
"Child Care Centre" is fine for a general daycare. But if you specialise — Montessori School, Preschool, Infant Care Centre, After-School Programme — there's a more specific category. Set the one that matches how parents in your city actually search.

**[Step 2 — on screen]**
Provincial licence and capacity. Add it to your GBP description: licensed capacity, the regulatory body (Ontario Ministry of Education, BC CCOF, Alberta's Child Care Licensing), age range served.

Parents search for it. It's also a trust signal Google weights on high-stakes categories.

**[Step 3 — on screen]**
Review specificity. "Great daycare, our kids love it" is fine. A review that mentions the age group, programme type, or a specific educator carries more signal in the local pack.

Ask parents: "mention the age group or programme if you're comfortable."

**[Payoff — on screen]**
These signals matter more in childcare than almost any other local category. The decision is higher stakes. Parents read everything.

**[CTA — on screen]**
Check your GBP category and description. That's where the audit starts.
