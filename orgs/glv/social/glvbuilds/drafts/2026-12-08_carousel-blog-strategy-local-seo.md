## LinkedIn Caption (~400 words)

I get asked "should I start blogging for local SEO?" at least once a week.

The honest answer: it depends entirely on where you are in the process — and most businesses blog at the wrong time.

If your core service+city pages aren't live, verified, and indexed, blogging won't move your local rankings. The sequence is: GBP optimised → service pages indexed → citations corrected → then blog. Posts added before that foundation is solid can compete with your own service pages for crawl budget and create cannibalisation risk.

Once the foundation is in place, blogging makes sense — but only the right kind.

**Four formats that work for local service businesses:**

1. **City-specific roundup** — "What to expect from [service] in [city]", "Best time to [service] in [city]" — targets informational queries with local intent
2. **PAA answer post** — pick one customer question from the People Also Ask box, write a full useful answer, build the page around it
3. **Before/after case study** — real project: what the problem was, what you did, what the result was; specific enough to be genuinely different from any other page
4. **Seasonal guide** — practical advice tied to your local climate and service timing; outranks generic national content because it names your area

**Finding topics:**

Don't guess. Check your GBP Q&A — customers have already told you what they want answered. Pull GSC queries with impressions but no clicks — there's your confirmed demand. PAA boxes for your service+city query show exactly what people ask before hiring someone.

**What makes it fail:**

Thin generic posts ("5 tips for homeowners") with no local angle, no specific examples, and no internal links underperform. Writing for a broad audience instead of someone searching your service in your city undercuts everything.

**Structure that works:**

— H1 states the specific question or local topic
— First paragraph answers directly (no slow wind-up)
— One PAA block: H3 + 40–60 word answer + follow-up paragraph
— Internal link to your relevant service page
— One real local example — an actual job, an actual neighbourhood, an actual client type

One genuinely useful post per month outperforms eight thin ones.
