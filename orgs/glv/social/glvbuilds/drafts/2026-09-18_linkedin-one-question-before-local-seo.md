## LinkedIn post

Before I start local SEO for any client, I ask one question.

Not "what's your budget?" Not "what keywords do you want to rank for?"

I ask: **"When a customer calls you from Google, what happens next?"**

Most businesses can't answer it cleanly.

The phone isn't answered consistently. Or it goes to voicemail between noon and 2pm. Or the person who answers doesn't know the service backlog. Or there's no follow-up if the first call goes to voicemail.

That's a lead pipeline problem, not an SEO problem.

If I drive more calls and the conversion rate is near zero, all I've done is generate noise.

So before I touch a GBP or build a service page, I map the conversion path:

- How does a call get answered?
- What's the response time on form submissions?
- Is there a follow-up if the first contact attempt fails?
- What's the actual close rate on inbound leads right now?

If there are obvious gaps in the path, I flag them in session one. I don't refuse the work — but I document the constraint so the client understands why rankings alone won't move revenue.

The question tells me whether I'm building on a solid foundation or patching a roof while the walls are wet.

What's your answer to it?
