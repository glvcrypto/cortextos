# Local Rank Grid Tools — Carousel

## LinkedIn caption

**I don't just check where my client ranks on Google Maps. I check where they rank across their entire city.**

Checking rank at one address gives you one data point. But your clients' customers are searching from home, from work, from the other side of town. A single rank position doesn't tell you what they're seeing.

A local rank grid maps your GBP position at dozens of grid points across your service area. Each point shows your ranking at that GPS coordinate. Green = top 3. Orange = positions 4–10. Red = you're not on the radar.

I run this diagnostic at the start of every new engagement and again at 90-day reviews. Here's what I look for:

**Strong core, weak periphery** — you rank well near your address but fade at the edges. Usually a combination of service area settings and missing local content pages for those zones.

**Uneven distribution** — strong on one side of town, invisible on the other. Usually a competitor's physical location advantage. The fix is review presence and review velocity in the weak zone, not just category optimisation.

**Scattered pattern** — inconsistent ranking with no clear shape. Often signals a NAP consistency issue or a GBP that hasn't built enough history yet.

Tools I use: BrightLocal Local Search Grid and Local Falcon. Both are paid. Whitespark has some limited free rank-check features. Pick one tool and use it consistently so you're comparing like for like over time.

One rule: don't chase green everywhere. Focus on the zones where your highest-value customers actually search, and work outward from your strongest positions.
