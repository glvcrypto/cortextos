## Slide 2 — GBP Category

"Optician" is the correct primary GBP category for independent eyewear dispensers.

This is a distinct category from "Optometrist" (medical eye exams, OD designation) and "Eye Care Center." Many independent opticians have this set incorrectly — often because the store also offers in-store eye exams through a visiting optometrist. If exams are offered but optician services are the primary business, "Optician" is the correct primary, with secondary categories added for any exam services.

Getting this wrong means filtering out in local searches that your most likely customers are using.

---

## Slide 3 — Lens-Type and Product Pages

"Progressive lenses [city]," "blue light glasses [city]," "prescription sunglasses [city]," "sports glasses [city]," and "children's glasses [city]" are different searches made by different buyers with different urgency and price expectations.

One "eyewear services" page ranks for none of them specifically.

A page about progressive lenses that explains the adjustment period, the fitting process, and why in-store fitting matters — rather than simply listing that you offer them — is the content that differentiates an independent optician from a chain where the product arrives in a box.

---

## Slide 4 — Insurance Direct Billing

Direct billing to extended health plans is one of the highest-converting pieces of information an independent optician can put on their website.

A page — or a clear section on the homepage — showing which plans are accepted for direct billing removes the most common "I'll call around first" hesitation. Most patients don't know if their plan covers optician services separately from optometrist exams.

Defensible language: "We direct-bill most major extended health plans. Contact us to confirm your coverage before your visit."

This page also attracts "does [insurance] cover glasses [city]" searches — a real query type that chains capture and most independents don't.

---

## Slide 5 — The Independent Advantage Page

Chains offer price and convenience. Independent opticians offer something chains structurally can't: the same person who helped you pick your frames will adjust them six months later when they've shifted.

That isn't always communicated on the website. A dedicated "Why choose an independent optician" or "Our fitting process" page — describing what a proper frame fitting involves, how adjustments work, and what the follow-up looks like — is content that speaks directly to the searcher who has been burned by a chain once.

It also ranks for longer-tail searches that chains don't produce pages for.

---

## Slide 6 — Review Specificity

Reviews that mention the fitting experience carry a different signal than "great glasses" or "fast service."

Ask clients to mention: the frame style they chose and why, the lens type (progressives, bifocals), any adjustment visits, and the staff member by name if they're comfortable.

"Helped me find progressives that actually work at my desk" appears as justification text in local pack results. It tells the next progressive lens searcher that you've solved their specific problem before.
