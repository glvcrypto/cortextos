## Slides

**Slide 1 — Hook**
> "The basic Merchant Center setup gets your products into Google Shopping. The local inventory layer shows your in-stock items to people searching nearby. Almost no small retailers have it set up."

---

**Slide 2 — What local inventory feeds are**
**A separate feed that tells Google what you have in stock at each physical location — updated daily.**

When someone searches "[product] near me" or "[product] in [city]," Google can surface your store with current in-store availability shown.

The result: an "Available nearby" badge on Shopping results.
Not just a listing — confirmation that the item is in stock before they drive over.

---

**Slide 3 — The 5 fields Google needs**
**Feed accuracy is a requirement, not a suggestion.**

1. **Store code** — must match your GBP location ID exactly
2. **Item ID** — linking back to your base product feed
3. **Availability** — "in_stock" / "out_of_stock" / "limited" (not "yes" or "1")
4. **Price** — must match your in-store price exactly (Google audits this against your website)
5. **Quantity** — optional, but "limited" triggers an urgency indicator in results

Mismatches between feed data and what's actually in the store → Google pauses the programme.

---

**Slide 4 — What customers see**
**This is what shows up for a nearby searcher:**

→ "Available nearby" badge on Shopping results
→ Store-level availability when they click through
→ "Pick up today" option if you offer in-store pickup
→ Your inventory appearing in the Google Maps "products" layer for nearby searches

It's the difference between "here's a product" and "here's the product, it's at a store 2km away, they have it right now."

---

**Slide 5 — Setup path**
**Merchant Center → Manage programs → Local inventory ads → Enrol.**

Then upload your local product inventory feed:
— Google Sheets (manual or script-connected to your POS)
— Automated feed via your e-commerce platform (Shopify and WooCommerce both have feed plugins that can include store-level inventory data)

Your store code in the feed must match the location ID in your linked GBP profile.

---

**Slide 6 — The accuracy requirement**
**This is the part most businesses underestimate.**

Your feed needs to update at least daily to stay accurate.

Items listed as in-stock when they aren't = frustrated customers at the counter + Google pausing your access to the programme.

If you don't have a system to export daily inventory counts automatically, set that up before enabling local inventory feeds. Manual updates don't scale once you have more than a handful of SKUs.

---

**Slide 7 — Who this is for**
**Physical retail with real inventory — and a system to track it.**

Best fit: hardware, sporting goods, auto parts, home furnishings, specialty retail.

Not for service businesses. Not for businesses that don't have a POS or inventory management system that can produce daily feed data.

Check Merchant Center eligibility by business category before setting up — not every retail category qualifies.
