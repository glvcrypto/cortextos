## Reel Script

**Hook (on screen):**
"Most driving school websites mix up two completely different searches — and rank well for neither."

---

**Beat 1 — Instructional vs. transactional intent:**
"Someone searching 'how to parallel park' or 'G2 road test prep [city]' is looking for information. They're not ready to book lessons yet. Someone searching 'beginner driver education [city]' or 'BDE package [city]' is ready to sign up. These are separate searches with separate conversion expectations — and they need separate pages. A single 'services' page that tries to answer both converts at a fraction of what a dedicated BDE enrolment page and a dedicated road test prep resource page would do separately. Most driving schools have one. Some have neither."

---

**Beat 2 — Per-DriveTest-centre pages:**
"A student choosing a driving school often cares which DriveTest centre they'll be testing at. They want familiarity with the test routes. A driving school that operates near multiple DriveTest locations — Brampton, Mississauga, Hamilton, for example — has a ranking opportunity at each one. A page for each location: which DriveTest centre students typically test at, what the local test routes look like, any location-specific notes, and instructor availability in that area. This is a near-me search where 'near' means the DriveTest centre, not just the school's registered address."

---

**Beat 3 — Instructor bio pages:**
"A driving school with five MTO-certified instructors has five local SEO assets that most schools never use. A named bio page — MTO certified driving instructor, years of experience, languages spoken (major differentiator in immigrant-dense markets), specialty if applicable (nervous drivers, teen learners, mature drivers returning after a gap) — creates a searchable page that a 'meet the team' section never will. Families looking for a bilingual instructor search by language. They need a page to find."

---

**Payoff + CTA (on screen):**
"Separate pages for information and booking. A page per DriveTest centre. One bio per instructor."
