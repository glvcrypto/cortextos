## Slide 2 — GBP Category Precision

Google has distinct categories: Veterinarian, Animal Hospital, Emergency Veterinarian Service, Exotic Animal Veterinarian, Veterinary Pharmacy.

"Veterinarian" doesn't rank for emergency searches. "Animal Hospital" doesn't rank for exotic pet searches.

Check what you actually offer. Set the category that matches your highest-search-intent service.

---

## Slide 3 — Emergency vs. Appointment Page

"Emergency vet [city]" and "24-hour vet [city]" are high-urgency, high-intent searches. They deserve their own landing page — not a paragraph buried in your Services section.

Emergency page: phone number above the fold, hours prominently displayed, brief description of what you handle. One page, one purpose.

---

## Slide 4 — Provincial Licensing on Bio Pages

Each veterinarian has a registration number with their provincial regulatory college (CVBC in BC, CVO in Ontario, ABVMA in Alberta).

Adding the DVM designation, registration college, and areas of focus to individual bio pages — not a team-grid page — creates one ranking opportunity per vet rather than one for the whole clinic.

---

## Slide 5 — Review Specificity for Pet Type

Reviews that mention the animal species ("our rabbit," "our senior lab mix") and the condition treated appear as justification text in local pack results.

When asking for a review, prompt: "If you're comfortable, mention your pet's species and the care they received."

Most clinics ask for reviews without this prompt. The difference in review text quality is significant.

---

## Slide 6 — Attribute Signals on Maps

GBP has veterinary-specific attributes: appointment required, accepts new patients, on-site pharmacy.

Pet owners filter on these. Most clinic profiles haven't touched the attributes section since the profile was set up.

Attributes → Edit. Takes five minutes. Appears on your Maps listing immediately.
