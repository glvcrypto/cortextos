## Instagram
*(Full carousel style research: `ig-carousel-style-research-2026-05-16.md`)*

### Format Specs
- Carousel: 1080x1350px (4:5), up to 10 slides
- Reels: 1080x1920px (9:16), 15-90 seconds optimal
- Single image: 1080x1350px (4:5)
- Hashtags: 3-5 (hashtag following removed Dec 2024 — keywords in caption now matter more for search)

### Cadence
4-5x/week. Mix: 2 Reels + 2-3 carousels or feed posts. Stories separately if capacity allows.

### Hook Pattern
Visual hook at feed thumbnail size — headline must be readable on a 270x340px thumbnail before anyone taps. For Reels: hook-hold-reward in first 3 seconds, 70%+ retention target past 3 seconds.

### Algorithm Levers (ranked)
1. Save rate (strongest signal for carousels)
2. Watch completion (for Reels)
3. Shares
4. Comments
5. Likes (weakest)

**Key behavior:** Instagram re-surfaces a carousel to users who saw slide 1 but didn't swipe — confirmed algorithm behavior. The cover slide is also the re-serve hook. This makes carousel design disproportionately important.

### Unique Edge
Nothing else re-serves content to people who partially engaged with it. Carousel algorithm is the most forgiving second-chance mechanism on any platform.

## LinkedIn

### Reference Accounts
- **Aleyda Solis** (linkedin.com/in/aleyda) — ~102K followers. SEO consultant, carousel-forward, educational. Migrated primary posting priority to LinkedIn after outperforming her larger Twitter following here. Model for a small expert-led agency.
- **Neil Patel** (linkedin.com/in/neilkpatel) — 1M+ followers. Data-heavy insight posts, near-daily, accessible tone. Reference for reach — not depth.
- **Ross Simmonds** (linkedin.com/in/rosssimmonds) — ~60K followers. B2B content strategy, Framework-first PDFs. Best model for a Canadian founder-led agency.

### Format Specs

| Format | Avg Engagement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| PDF/Document carousel | 6.60% | **Highest ROI format on the platform** |
| Native video 30-90s | 5.60% | Strong; live video 24x more |
| Image + text | 3.20% | Solid workhorse |
| Text only | 2.00% | Works for personal profiles |
| External link in body | ~60% reach penalty | Never — link goes in first comment |

- Character limit: 3,000 chars. "See more" cutoff at ~210-220 chars. Hook must land before the fold.
- Image: 1200x1200px (1:1) or 1200x627px (1.91:1)
- Video: 30-90s optimal, up to 10 min max, 9:16 or 16:9 or 1:1
- PDF carousel: 6-10 slides optimal; PDF renders most consistently

### Cadence
3-5x/week on **personal profiles** (Aiden + Ben). Company page: 2-3x/week — but personal profiles get ~62% feed reach vs ~5% for pages. Post thought leadership personally, amplify on company page.

Best times: Tuesday–Thursday, 8am–12pm local. 12-hour minimum gap between posts.

### Hook Patterns
LinkedIn cuts off at 210-220 chars. Everything hinges on the first 2-3 lines.

Top patterns:
1. **Specific number:** "I analyzed 22,000 Google Ads accounts. Here's the mistake in 80% of them." — specificity = credibility
2. **Contrarian claim:** "Stop chasing Google reviews. Here's what actually moves local rankings."
3. **Admission/vulnerability:** "I made a $15,000 mistake on a client campaign. Here's what I'd do differently."
4. **Incomplete information:** "Most local businesses are wasting their Google Ads budget. Here's the exact reason why."

**Never start with "I" on LinkedIn** — penalized. Lead with value, not self.

### Hashtag Strategy
3-5 hashtags. 1-2 broad (#digitalmarketing, #seo) + 2-3 niche (#localSEO, #canadianmarketing). Post body keywords matter more than hashtags for search.

### Algorithm Levers (ranked)
1. **Dwell time** — 60s+ reads see 15.6% engagement vs 1.2% for 0-3s views. Carousels win here by nature.
2. **Quality comments** — 15x weight of likes. Meaningful replies from professionals >> generic "great post!"
3. **First-hour velocity** — Post gets test-distributed; strong early engagement triggers expansion. Reply to comments within 30 minutes: +64% total comments, 2.3x more views.
4. **Saves/bookmarks** — "I'll return to this" signal; treated as high-value
5. **Shares/reposts**
6. **Likes** (weakest)

**What destroys reach:** External links in post body (26.5% reach penalty per 900K-post study). Put links in first comment.

### Cross-Post vs Native
- LinkedIn personal profiles dramatically outperform company pages (5% reach vs 62%)
- Native-feel content outperforms obvious reposts by ~20%
- PDF carousel ≠ Instagram carousel — same content, different format, different density
- Never: TikTok/IG watermarks on video, 15+ hashtags, casual entertainment hooks

### Unique Edge Worth Using
**LinkedIn Newsletter:** 49.3% median open rate (vs 20-25% for email newsletters). Delivers directly to subscriber inboxes, bypasses the feed algorithm. Build a bi-weekly newsletter list now — it becomes an email-equivalent owned audience on-platform.

## TikTok

### Reference Accounts
- **@nogood.io** (NoGood) — campaign breakdowns + brand strategy with personality. Best agency model.
- **@ashwinnabego** (Ashwinn) — 294K followers. "Brand autopsy" format: punchy strategic teardowns of real brand decisions.
- **@jtbarnett** (JT Barnett) — 307K followers. Storytelling strategy for creators + service businesses. Conversational, high trust.

### Format Specs
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 mandatory. No borders. No letterboxing.
- Optimal length: 30-60 seconds for educational/business content
- Caption: 100-150 characters, keyword-first opening clause
- Hashtags: 3-5. Structure: 1 broad + 2 medium-niche + 2 specific (e.g., #MarketingTips + #ContentStrategy + #LocalSEO + #CanadianMarketing + #B2BMarketing)
- Never use #FYP or #viral — they send mixed signals about content category

### Cadence
3-5x/week (Buffer 11.4M post analysis confirms 2-5x/week is highest ROI zone). Daily posting gives more views per post but the production burden is extreme. Consistency matters more than volume.

Best times: Thursday 6-9am, Saturday midday 10am-6pm.

### Hook Patterns
63% of highest-CTR videos hook the audience in the first 3 seconds. New in 2025: if users swipe away before 3 seconds, content enters reduced distribution ("view jail"). The opening 3 seconds are non-negotiable.

Top patterns for business/educational content:
1. **Bold contrarian:** "Most marketing agencies are killing their clients' reach and don't even know it."
2. **Curiosity gap:** "Here's the one thing your competitor's GBP has that yours doesn't."
3. **Specific result:** "This 3-second GBP change doubled our client's call volume."
4. **Provocative question:** "Why do 80% of local businesses fail at Google Ads?"

Effective hooks combine 2 of 3: pattern interruption + curiosity gap + social proof. Target: 35-45% watch rate past 3 seconds; top performers hit 50-60%.

### Algorithm Levers (ranked)
1. **Watch time / total minutes viewed** — dominant signal in 2025. 60%+ completion is "good"; 80%+ triggers viral distribution
2. **Saves** — "worth returning to" signal; outweighs shares
3. **Shares** — recommendation signal
4. **Comments** (quality-weighted — substantive beats emoji-only)
5. **Likes** — significantly reduced weight in 2025

TikTok tests each post in the first 3 hours with a 200-500 user cohort. Their response determines expansion. The distribution window is narrow and front-loaded.

### TikTok SEO
40%+ of users search TikTok before Google. Massive underutilized channel. TikTok's AI reads spoken audio, on-screen text, and captions. Say "Google Business Profile" on camera + write it in caption + overlay it on screen = you appear in TikTok search for that term. Use TikTok Creator Center keyword tool before filming. Caption keyword priority: opening clause > on-screen text > spoken audio > hashtags.

### Cross-Post vs Native
TikTok penalizes visible watermarks from other platforms. Always export from original camera roll/editing project, never from another platform's save function. The correct workflow: shoot 9:16 once → edit → export clean → upload natively to each platform with platform-specific captions.

### Unique Edge Worth Using
**Stitch (not Duet):** Reusing 1-5 seconds from another video + your response. 35% higher engagement than standalone content. For an agency: stitch trending "bad marketing advice" videos to position yourself as the authoritative correction. This is the fastest path to thought leadership on TikTok.

## Facebook

### Reference Accounts
- **Jon Loomer Digital** (facebook.com/jonloomer) — ~170K followers. Facebook ads specialist. Personal brand + companion Group. Most replicable model for a small agency.
- **LocaliQ / WordStream** — educational graphics for local business owners. Similar positioning to GLV.

### Format Specs

| Format | Reach Performance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Native Reels | Highest reach | Meta's priority format, 2-3x more organic reach |
| Albums (multi-image) | Best static engagement (1.6%) | Underused |
| Native video | 1.5% engagement | Algorithmic preference |
| Photo posts | 1.3% | Declining but works |
| Link posts | ~0.1-0.2% | Heavily deprioritized |
| Text only | 0.20% | Low but non-zero |

- Reels: 1080x1920px (9:16), 15-30 seconds optimal (highest replay rate), 60s max before significant drop-off
- Photo: 1080x1350px (4:5) or 1080x1080px (1:1). Landscape is deprioritized.
- "See more" collapses at ~477 chars on mobile. Hook must be in first 2-3 sentences.

### Cadence
**Honest reality:** Facebook organic page reach averages 1.37% in 2025. Median engagement: 0.2%. This is structural, not fixable with frequency.

Recommended: 4-5x/week. Mix: 2 native Reels + 2-3 image/text posts. Reels have their own distribution pipeline separate from the feed — they reach non-followers through the Reels tab. This is the only meaningful organic reach lever available.

Over-posting note: Unlike LinkedIn, Facebook does penalize over-posting — your posts compete against each other for limited organic distribution.

### Hook Patterns
Facebook audience is in social/entertainment mode (vs LinkedIn's professional discovery mode).

What works:
1. **Question with personal stake:** "Is your local business showing up when someone Googles [city] + [service]? Most aren't."
2. **Local social proof:** "A Sault Ste. Marie contractor was spending $1,400/month on Google Ads getting 2 leads. Here's what we changed."
3. **Story opening:** "Last Tuesday I got a call from a restaurant owner who was about to give up on her Google profile..."
4. **Reels opening frame:** Text on screen + spoken hook simultaneously within 2 seconds.

### Algorithm Levers (ranked)
1. Reels completion rate
2. Comments + shares
3. Group engagement (Groups see substantially more organic reach than Pages)
4. Saves/private shares (via Messenger)
5. Likes (weakest)

**What kills Facebook reach:** Engagement bait ("tag a friend"), TikTok watermarks, horizontal video, link-only posts, repeated low-performing content.

### Cross-Post vs Native
**Never auto-cross-post Instagram Reels to Facebook.** Controlled study (Agorapulse, 3 accounts): native Facebook posts got **72% higher reach** vs Instagram cross-posts. Meta's cross-post pipeline causes technical failures (audio muting, wrong feed surface placement). Upload separately even if the video file is identical.

### Unique Edge Worth Using
**Facebook Groups — highest ROI organic move on the platform.** Groups see significantly more organic reach than Pages. Members receive notifications by default. Create a local-audience Group ("Local Business Marketing Tips — SSM" or broader "Small Business Growth") and position as the expert facilitator. Never sell inside the Group. Leads come from DMs, not pitches. The Group becomes a warm lead pipeline.

**Honest verdict:** Facebook is worth maintaining for Reels reach (non-followers via Reels tab), local audience credibility signal, and the Group play. Do not invest primary content creation effort here — cross-post from Instagram with native uploads, run the Group separately.

## YouTube Shorts

### Reference Accounts
- **Ahrefs** (youtube.com/@Ahrefs) — 620K+ subs. Distilled SEO tutorials in Shorts. "Teach one thing per Short" model. Best-in-class for the SEO/marketing education niche.
- **Neil Patel** (youtube.com/@neilpatel) — 1M+ subs. High-volume, punchy tips, talking-head + bold text overlays.
- **vidIQ** (youtube.com/@vidiq) — 200K+ subs. YouTube growth tips in 30-60 second format. Their Shorts are proof-of-concept for their own advice.

### Format Specs
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 mandatory (1080x1920px)
- Max length: 3 minutes (expanded from 60s in 2025) — but optimal is 45-60 seconds
- Sweet spot data: 50-60 second range earns 22x more views than clips under 10 seconds (Inflow Network, 5,400 Shorts)
- Completion rate: 75-85% for 15-30s; 60-75% for 60-90s; drops to 40-60% for 90s+
- **Title is the primary discovery mechanism** — write it as a search query: "How to get more Google reviews (fast)" not "Growth Tip #47"
- Hashtags: 2-3, secondary to title and description for discoverability

### Cadence
2-3 Shorts/week. Unlike TikTok, YouTube doesn't reward high-frequency posting with proportional distribution gains. Consistency matters; bursts do not.

Channels posting 12+ times/month grow views 53% faster and gain 66% more subscribers (VidIQ, 5M channel dataset).

### Hook Patterns
YouTube Shorts hooks optimize to earn the watch and keep viewers **in the Shorts ecosystem session** (session time affects distribution, not just individual video metrics).

What works:
1. **"Watch until the end" premise:** "I'm going to show you the one GBP setting most local businesses never turn on." — Sets up what they get if they stay.
2. **Strong arc:** Intrigue → build curiosity → satisfying conclusion. Loopable Shorts that viewers replay score higher.
3. **Visual change every 3-4 seconds:** Sustains attention without TikTok-level pattern interrupts.
4. **No intros.** No logo, no name, no "hey guys welcome back." Every second before the hook is a swipe risk.

### Algorithm Levers (ranked)
1. Watch completion + replay rate ("loopable" Shorts rewatched multiple times)
2. Viewer satisfaction / swipe behavior (measures if your Short contributed to a good session)
3. Comments, shares, likes
4. Subscribe button click-through (tracked while video plays; verbal CTA to subscribe measurably improves conversion)
5. Search metadata (titles, descriptions, hashtags — indexed for YouTube search; unique to YouTube)

### Shorts vs Long-Form
You don't need a long-form YouTube strategy to benefit from Shorts. But treat Shorts as a **search-indexed content library**, not a social feed. A well-optimized Shorts title can drive relevant traffic for 12-24 months from YouTube and Google search. TikTok has a 48-72 hour algorithmic lifespan; YouTube Shorts compounds indefinitely via search.

Channels integrating both formats grow 41% faster than single-format channels — but this is a future option, not a prerequisite.

### Cross-Post vs Native
Same watermark rules as TikTok: YouTube penalizes TikTok watermarks. Export from original project file. Title and description must be rewritten for YouTube search — an Instagram caption is useless as a YouTube Shorts title.

### Unique Edge
**Search longevity.** This is YouTube's differentiator vs every other short-form platform. A Short titled "How to set up Google Business Profile in 2025" will surface in YouTube and Google search results for 12-24+ months. For an agency whose value proposition is expertise, building a search-indexed video library is a long-term lead generation asset that TikTok cannot replicate.

## Threads

### Reference Accounts
- **Matt Navarra (@mattnav)** — social media consultant, Geekout Newsletter founder. Active Threads presence covering platform news. Model for industry-insider voice.
- **Rand Fishkin (@randfish)** — SparkToro founder. Data-backed contrarian takes. Builds authority through opinion, not link dumps.
- **HubSpot** — breaks complex concepts into actionable text posts. Strong educational professional voice.

**Niche gap note:** The marketing/SEO consultant niche is thin on Threads. Early-mover advantage is real. Most reference accounts are large consumer brands (Wendy's, Poppi) — not small professional service firms. There's almost no direct competition in the Canadian marketing/agency space.

### Format Specs
- Character limit: 500 per post (2x Twitter)
- Images: 1080x1350px (4:5)
- Carousels: Up to 20 images/videos (as of Dec 2024) — underused for educational content
- Video: Up to 5 minutes
- Links: Clickable in post body (unlike Instagram)
- Topic tags: 1 per post (Threads' hashtag equivalent — minor discoverability lever)
- Thread chains: Supported; chain multiple posts for longer content

### Cadence
3-5x/week original posts + active replies to others. Threads rewards **conversation frequency**, not just publishing frequency. Budget time for replies, not just posting. A post with no replies from you dies quickly.

Peak times: 8-10am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm. Weekends viable (unlike X).

### Hook Patterns
Threads is **more conversational, less clickbait** than X. Algorithm deprioritizes broadcast posts and rewards genuine replies.

What works:
1. **Opinion that invites pushback:** "Everyone is telling local businesses to be on every platform. Here's why that's bad advice."
2. **Direct questions:** "What's the biggest time-waster you see in small business marketing?" — generates 5-10x more replies than statements
3. **Hot takes:** "Posting 3x a week consistently beats posting daily and burning out. Change my mind."
4. **Short insight + thread signal:** "3 things I'd do differently setting up a GBP client in 2025. 1/"

**What doesn't work:** Promotional content, link-first posts, long-form broadcast essays with no invitation to respond, cross-posted LinkedIn copy.

### Algorithm Levers (ranked)
1. Replies (especially deep conversation threads — replies-to-replies)
2. Reposts + quote-threads
3. Likes (weaker than Instagram)
4. Early engagement (first 30-60 minutes)

Operational insight: If your posts get likes but no replies, the algorithm will plateau your reach. Posts generating 5-10 genuine back-and-forth replies get expanded to non-followers via "For You."

### Unique Edge
**IG audience portability.** Threads is the only text platform where an existing Instagram following pre-seeds your audience instantly. Your IG followers see your Threads launch immediately. For an agency that already has Instagram history, this is the fastest new-audience bootstrap on any text platform.

**Honest assessment:** 320M+ MAU and growing. 4.51% average engagement rate (vs X's ~0.3% for large accounts). Fully organic — no paid requirement. Not a short-term lead gen engine. Think: 6-12 month brand authority play that earns inbound referrals. The Canadian marketing/agency niche on Threads is nearly unclaimed.

## Google Business Profile

### What GBP Posts Actually Do
**Bottom line upfront (confirmed by Sterling Sky's controlled study):** GBP posts have **no measurable direct impact on Local Pack rankings.** Sterling Sky tested weekly posting for 9 weeks across 3 listings and found zero keyword ranking movement. Whitespark places posts in their "C tier" — nice to have, not a ranking driver.

**What they DO:** GBP posts are a **conversion tool** for visitors who already found your profile. They signal active management, surface offers, and influence whether a prospect calls or books. Think of them as landing page optimization, not SEO.

### Post Types

| Type | Best For | Expiry |
|---|---|---|
| **Update (What's New)** | Service highlights, completed jobs, team news | 6 months (then archived, not deleted) |
| **Offer** | Discounts, seasonal promotions | After your set end date (max 12 months) |
| **Event** | Open houses, demos, workshops | After event end date |

Offer posts outperform Update and Event posts on click-through. A "Free GBP audit — 5 spots this month" Offer beats a generic "We're here for all your needs" Update every time.

### Format Specs
- Character limit: 1,500 chars. **Visible before truncation: ~150-250 chars.** First 150 chars = your full ad.
- Images: 720x720px (1:1) recommended. Non-stock images get **5.6x more clicks** than stock images.
- Video: Up to 30 seconds, 75MB, 720p minimum
- CTA buttons: Book, Order online, Buy, Learn more, Sign up, Call now
- No native hashtag support. No bullet points (use dashes/numbers if needed).
- Emojis: Allowed and slightly improve performance (BrightLocal data)

### Cadence
1-3x/week. Posts older than 6 months move to archive (don't disappear — just deprioritized). Recent posts surface first, so weekly cadence keeps the profile appearing active.

**Practical schedule:**
- 1x/week Update post (service highlight, completed job, local tip)
- 1x/month Offer post (seasonal discount, promo)
- Event posts as they occur

### Hook Patterns
GBP visitors are in **transactional mode** — they searched, found you, and are deciding whether to call. This is the opposite of social scrolling. Hook patterns that work here are different from every other platform:

What works:
- Service + location + urgency: "Same-day marine engine repair in Sault Ste. Marie — call before noon."
- Specific offer: "Spring tune-up special — 20% off through May 31."
- Job result as social proof: "Just rebuilt a Mercury 150hp for a client in Algoma District. Back on the water same week."
- Problem/solution: "Prop strike last weekend? We diagnose and repair prop damage, usually same week."

What does NOT work on GBP:
- Generic "we're open!" posts
- Educational how-to content (wrong intent — teaches them to DIY)
- Company news with no customer benefit
- Hashtags, @mentions, or social-style hooks
- Long posts (nobody reads past 250 chars on GBP)
- Stock images

### GBP-Unique Edges Worth Maintaining

**Q&A Section — seed it yourself proactively.** Anyone can answer your Q&As (including strangers, incorrectly). Write the 5-8 questions your clients most commonly ask, then answer them yourself before strangers do it wrong. Individual Q&A answers surface in search results for specific queries.

**Review responses — high conversion value.** 88% of consumers prefer businesses that respond to all reviews. Include service keywords and location naturally: "Glad we could help with your outboard service in the Sault — see you next season." Responds to negative reviews especially.

**Photos — ongoing, not one-time.** Photo recency signals active management. Minimum 1 new photo/week alongside posts. Job-site photos, team photos, equipment. Non-stock images only.

**Do NOT connect Instagram or Facebook to GBP.** If you connect social accounts, the social feed may replace your native Update posts in the profile view. If you connect anything, LinkedIn or YouTube only (commercial-intent content). Per Whitespark's explicit guidance.

**GBP Messaging — only enable if monitored.** Google disables messaging if you don't respond within 24 hours. A disabled-by-Google messaging feature looks worse than no messaging at all.

## Cross-Channel Synthesis

### 1. Content Format Routing

**Carousels / Slides:**
- **Instagram:** 1080x1350px (4:5), up to 10 slides, image format
- **LinkedIn:** Same content as PDF, 6-10 pages, text density can be higher per slide
- **Facebook:** Same images as Instagram, 1080x1080px (1:1) safer for Facebook's crop; native upload required (not IG cross-post)
- **Design master:** Build at 1080x1350px with a centered safe zone for all text. Renders well on IG and LinkedIn; Facebook crops to square — design text centered to survive the crop.

**Short-form video (9:16 Reels):**
- Same source video works on all four platforms
- **What changes per platform:** Caption (length, tone, keywords), hashtags, CTA, hook text overlay
- **What never changes:** The video file itself — exported clean with no watermarks from original project
- Upload **natively to each platform** — never auto-cross-post from one to another

| Element | TikTok | Instagram Reels | YouTube Shorts | Facebook Reels |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Caption | 100-150 chars, conversational, keyword-first | 100-150 words, keyword-rich | Not the focus — title matters | Short, casual |
| Hashtags | 3-5 targeted | 3-5 targeted | 2-3 (secondary to title) | 1-3 |
| Hook phrase | Pattern interrupt, 3s | Hook-hold-reward, 3s | Earn the watch, 2-3s | Same as IG |
| CTA | "Comment" / "Save" | "Save" / "Follow" | "Subscribe" | "Share" / "Follow" |
| Title field | N/A | N/A | Write as search query | N/A |

**Text content:**
- Same core idea can run on LinkedIn, Threads, X, Facebook — but each needs rewriting, not copy-paste
- LinkedIn: professional framing, 3,000 chars, external links in first comment only
- Threads: conversational, 500 chars, ends in a question or invitation to reply
- X: sharp, opinionated, 280 chars, punchy
- Facebook text: warmer community tone, short, include image for any meaningful reach

### 2. Confirmed Cross-Posting Penalties

| Behavior | Status | Impact |
|---|---|---|
