## 2. Niches

### Current Niches — Continue, Drop, or Adjust

**HVAC — DOUBLE DOWN**
- Highest revenue-per-business of any niche we've tested. An HVAC company running 3–5 techs does $500K–$1.5M annually. A $2K/mo retainer is 2–5% of revenue — completely reasonable.
- Year-round revenue (furnace in winter, AC in summer) = no seasonal budget drop-off.
- Northern Ontario = furnace country. Digital gap is massive — most HVAC operators have Wix sites and 20–30 Google reviews while their top competitor has 100+ reviews and 1,500+/mo organic traffic.
- Decision-maker is almost always the owner directly.
- **Expand HVAC to every active city.**

**Plumbing — DOUBLE DOWN**
- Similar owner-operator profile to HVAC. Revenue $300K–$1M+ for a 3-person shop.
- Emergency service = year-round revenue, no seasonal constraint.
- Digitally underserved: most plumbers rely on word-of-mouth and Yellow Pages remnants.
- Strong SEO gap in every tested city.
- **Expand plumbing to Sudbury (untested).**

**Roofing — ADD (new)**
- High-ticket installs ($10K–$30K per job). A roofing company doing 50 roofs/year is at $750K–$1.5M revenue.
- Concentrated spring/summer season creates urgency for lead generation.
- Northern Ontario roofing is underserved digitally — same pattern as HVAC/plumbing.
- Positive framing angle: "You're going to be busier than ever this spring — are you capturing every lead?"
- **Start with Sudbury and Thunder Bay roofing as first test.**

**Electricians — MAINTAIN (SSM in-person only, email in Sudbury/Thunder Bay)**
- SSM batch drafted (briefing pack format). Good fit for in-person.
- In Sudbury and Thunder Bay, electricians can receive email. Commercially licensed electricians running crews of 3–10 have strong revenue.
- Lower urgency than HVAC/plumbing because electricians often have more commercial B2B word-of-mouth. Email reply rate may be lower.
- **Keep at current scale; expand if roofing validates.**

**Golf Courses — DEPRIORITIZE**
- Small pool (3–6 courses per city). Already contacted several.
- Seasonal revenue concentrated May–October. In a bad year (cold May, early September), the marketing budget is the first cut.
- Committee-driven decisions at clubs = longer sales cycle.
- Lead-to-retainer conversion probability is lower than HVAC/plumbing.
- Exception: **indoor golf simulators** (year-round, owner-operator, growing trend) — treat as a separate niche if any appear in research.
- **Finish the existing batch; do not run new golf scouting rounds.**

**Construction/General Contractors — DEPRIORITIZE**
- Project-based revenue model makes ongoing retainer a harder sell ("we're busy" = no need for marketing; "we're slow" = no budget).
- Longer sales cycle, less predictable renewal.
- Exception: **specialty contractors with recurring residential services** (e.g. foundation repair, insulation) may fit. Evaluate case-by-case.
- **Finish existing Sudbury construction batch; do not start new construction scouting.**

## 3. Resource-Fit Heuristic

**Important:** This is a **priority tier**, not a gate. Every business that passes scout triage gets outreach eventually. Resources determine *when* they get outreach and *how much effort* goes into personalization — not whether they get outreach at all.

**The logic:** A solo plumber billing $120K/year is a harder close than a 5-person HVAC shop billing $900K/year. Both are worth a cold email. The HVAC shop gets a hand-crafted, fully researched pitch sent first. The solo plumber gets a well-written templated send queued after Tier A is exhausted.

### Scoring Model (apply at scout stage)

Score each signal. **Tier A: ≥ 7/15. Tier B: < 7/15.** Both tiers get outreach.

| Signal | 0 points | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|--------|----------|---------|----------|----------|
| Google reviews | < 10 | 10–24 | 25–74 | 75+ |
| Years in operation | < 3 | 3–7 | 8–15 | 16+ |
| Estimated team size | Solo (1) | 2 | 3–5 | 6+ |
| Incorporated (Ltd/Inc vs registered name) | No / Unknown | — | Yes | — |
| Service area breadth | Single city, residential only | Single city + some commercial | Multi-area OR commercial client base | Multi-area AND commercial |
| Any visible marketing spend (ads/agency) | None visible | — | Any evidence | — |

**Max score: 15. Tier A: ≥ 7. Tier B: < 7. Tier C: only if actively disqualifying (see below).**

### Tier Definitions

| Tier | Score | Outreach approach |
|------|-------|------------------|
| **A — Priority** | ≥ 7/15 | Fully researched dossier, hand-crafted hook referencing specific business details, sent first in batch. |
| **B — Opportunistic** | < 7/15 | Lighter dossier (NAP + basic gap check), well-written but less personalized hook, queued after Tier A in same batch. |
| **C — Skip** | Any | Only applies to actively harmful cases: fake/closed business, do_not_contact flag, franchise (parent controls budget), or permanent digital absence (no website + GBP permanently closed). |

### Signal Sources (where to find each)

| Signal | Where to find |
|--------|--------------|
| Google reviews | GBP listing |
| Years in operation | Website "about" or footer, NUANS/Ontario Business Registry, LinkedIn |
| Team size | LinkedIn company page, ZoomInfo (treat as rough proxy), About page staff photos |
| Incorporated | Ontario Business Registry lookup; "Ltd" or "Inc" in legal name = incorporated |
| Service area | Website service area page; testimonials mentioning cities |
| Marketing spend | Semrush paid traffic > 0; Facebook Ad Library results; "Google Guaranteed" badge |

### Tier C Disqualifiers (skip entirely)

- GBP permanently closed or website returns 404/dead
- `do_not_contact` flag in prospect registry
- Franchise location (corporate marketing controls budget)
- No website AND no GBP AND no directory listing — cannot craft a relevant hook

## Addendum: National Expansion Map
*Added: 2026-04-20 | Trigger: user directive — TAM is all of Canada, not Northern Ontario*

### Scope Correction

The original v1 framing treated Northern Ontario as the market. It is the **starting point**, not the ceiling. Pipeline executes Northern Ontario first because that is where scouted leads live. Expansion is phased but national in scope.

The "Barrie deprioritized because it loses Northern Ontario differentiator" reasoning in v1 is **withdrawn**. That framing does not hold at national scope. See positioning section below for the replacement logic.

---

### Positioning Language (updated)

**Original framing:** "Northern Ontario local fluency big-city competitors don't have."

**Problem at national scale:** This framing expires the moment we email a business in Barrie, Kingston, or Halifax. We are not local there.

**Option A — Canadian-owned agency specializing in trade SMBs**
- Works nationally, positions against US-owned agencies (relevant — many large agencies are US-headquartered)
- Weak as a standalone: "Canadian-owned" is table stakes, not a differentiator. Lots of Canadian agencies exist.

**Option B — City-by-city researched fluency (demonstrated, not claimed)**
- What we already do: every email references the specific business, their top competitor, their actual digital gap
- The hook IS the local fluency — a plumber in Halifax sees that we know Halifax HVAC keyword volumes and their #1 competitor's review count
- Scalable: the research process doesn't depend on where we're based
- Stronger because it is demonstrable in the email itself, not a brand claim

**Recommendation: Option B.** Drop the geography-specific positioning. The differentiator is: *"We research your specific market before contacting you — we know who your top competitors are, where your digital gap is, and what your city's search landscape looks like."* This works in SSM, Barrie, Halifax, or Winnipeg without modification.

Tagline update to consider: "Built for Canadian trades." (Flag for user/Ben to decide.)
