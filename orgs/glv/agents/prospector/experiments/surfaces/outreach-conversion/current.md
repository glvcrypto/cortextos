# Outreach Conversion — Surface (Email Copy and Hook Structure)

## Current Approach (v5 — locked 2026-04-22)

**Voice:** Passive discovery framing. "We were mapping the X market in Y and you came up." Not "I noticed this about you." The researcher/analyst frame, not the observer frame.

**Emotional rule:** Positive levers only. Curiosity, aspiration, pride, validation, opportunity FOMO. No fear, no loss framing, no negativity.

**Hook style:** Lead with what we found while mapping the market — specific, verifiable, and connected to an opportunity. The hook should make them think "huh, that's interesting" not "oh no, I'm behind."

**Structure:** HOOK -> WHY -> WHAT (S1-validate-opportunity-diagnostic)

- Hook (2-3 sentences): Passive discovery opener + one specific verifiable finding from the dossier. Never generic.
- Why (3-5 sentences): Plain language. What the finding means for their business. One clear opportunity + what capturing it looks like. No jargon.
- What (1-2 sentences): Low-commitment CTA. Warm, short, zero pressure.

**Subject line:** "Something we came across mapping [city] [niche]s" — 8-9 words, passive discovery framing, curiosity-gap format.

**CTA:** "Worth a quick call?" or "Worth 15 minutes?" — never "Book a 30-minute call."

**Tone:** Casual. Plain language a tradesman would use. Must read like a real person wrote it, not a marketing agency. Canadian English. No em-dashes.

**Length:** 80-125 words. Trim aggressively — every sentence must earn its place.

**Banned words:** genuinely, excited, thrilled, leverage, navigate, landscape, unlock, elevate, harness, delve, robust, seamless, synergy. No marketing jargon ("Local 3-Pack", "NAP", "GBP", "Local SEO") — use plain English equivalents.
