## Personal Story

Doug and Sara Hicks built Klimit Mechanical from the ground up starting around 2015 in Thunder Bay, Ontario -- a city where -40C winters make HVAC service a matter of survival, not convenience. They positioned the company around two differentiators that most HVAC outfits in Northwestern Ontario cannot match: a Red Seal Journeyperson Sheet Metal Worker (308A) doing custom fabrication in-house, and a service area stretching from Nipigon to Atikokan -- a radius of over 300 km.

The "Klimt" branding (with the macron over the "i") is deliberate and distinctive -- it stands out in a market full of generic trade names. Doug is the face of the operation, personally visiting homes to assess jobs and explain options. Reviews consistently describe him as patient, knowledgeable, and responsive -- including one where the owners arrived within an hour of a condemned furnace call and had a new unit installed the next day.

Over a decade in, they have built a solid word-of-mouth reputation (5.0 on Facebook, 4.3 on Google) and earned serious trade credentials (TSSA, G1-G3/GP gas tickets, 313A/D refrigeration). They also expanded into creative custom metalwork -- fire pits, commercial kitchen hoods, stainless steel cladding -- showing ambition beyond standard residential HVAC.

**Key motivator:** Doug and Sara appear to be craftspeople first, business operators second. The sheet metal fabrication specialty, the personal house calls, the wide service area -- these are the moves of people who take pride in the work itself. Marketing has clearly never been a priority, and the website going down without apparent urgency to fix it confirms this. They likely have more work than they can handle from referrals alone -- but that referral pipeline is fragile and invisible to Google.

## Niche-Marketing Misalignment

The Thunder Bay HVAC target audience (homeowners 35-65, median income $77,500, extreme winters -40C) wants:

1. **Immediate emergency response** -- When a furnace dies at -40C, homeowners need same-day service. Klimit's website (when live) listed Mon-Fri 8-4:30 with no emergency messaging. Reviews show they DO respond to emergencies (one review: "called within an hour when furnace was condemned") -- but this capability is invisible online.

2. **Trust signals before calling** -- 90%+ of homeowners research online before choosing an HVAC contractor. Klimit's website is DOWN. Their Google rating is 4.3 (below the 4.5 trust threshold). Two reviews mention reckless driving in a branded truck. There is no counter-narrative -- no testimonials on site, no case studies, no "about us" story.

3. **Verifiable credentials** -- Klimit has TSSA, Red Seal 308A, G1-G3/GP, 313A/D -- an impressive credential stack that most Thunder Bay HVAC companies cannot match. None of this is visible because the website is down. Even when it was live, this was buried on the "Our Team" page rather than prominently displayed.

4. **Custom fabrication capability** -- The in-house 308A sheet metal worker is a genuine competitive moat. Custom ductwork, commercial kitchen hoods, fire pits -- this differentiator is completely invisible online. No portfolio, no photos on the website, no video content showing the fabrication process.

5. **Local, family-owned preference** -- Northwestern Ontario homeowners strongly prefer local trades over national chains (Reliance, Enercare, Aire Serv all operate in Thunder Bay). Doug and Sara ARE the local, family-owned story -- but it is not being told anywhere. Sara's LinkedIn says "Small Business Owner" with minimal detail.

6. **Wide service area advantage** -- Serving Nipigon to Atikokan (300+ km radius) is unusual for a small HVAC shop. Homeowners in outlying communities have few options. This geographic reach is not being leveraged with location-specific landing pages or content.

## Opportunities Identified

### 1. Website Revival + SEO Foundation -- From 1 Keyword to 50+ in 6 Months

**The data:** Website is DOWN. Semrush shows 1 keyword, ~2/mo traffic. Clow Darling has 153 keywords and ~1,401/mo traffic. There are 1,110+ monthly searches for HVAC keywords in Thunder Bay that Klimit captures zero of.

**The play:** Rebuild or restore the website with proper hosting (the current site is unreachable, likely a hosting/SSL failure). Optimize service pages for high-intent keywords. Add location pages for each service area community (Nipigon, Kakabeka Falls, Atikokan, etc.). Implement LocalBusiness + Service schema. Launch a blog targeting "furnace installation thunder bay" (70/mo, KD moderate), "furnace repair thunder bay" (210/mo), and "ac repair thunder bay" (90/mo).

**Revenue impact:** At 3-5% conversion and $5,000-$10,000 average install value, capturing just 10 organic leads/month from currently invisible keywords = $50,000-$100,000 in new annual revenue. The SEO retainer pays for itself with a single furnace install lead.

### 2. NAP Cleanup + Review Recovery -- From 4.3 to 4.7+ Stars

**The data:** Three different addresses across listings. Two different phone numbers. 4.3 stars with negative reviews about reckless driving and ductwork quality that are unanswered (assumed, based on typical patterns for businesses without marketing support).

**The play:** Audit and correct all directory listings to one canonical address and phone number. Respond professionally to every negative review (especially the driving complaints -- shows accountability). Implement a systematic review request process. With ~2-5 jobs per week, generating 8-12 new reviews/month is achievable. Target: 75+ reviews at 4.7+ within 12 months.

**Revenue impact:** Local Pack visibility is directly tied to review volume and rating. Moving from 31 reviews (4.3) to 75+ reviews (4.7+) could 2-3x their Local Pack impressions for "hvac thunder bay" and related terms.

### 3. Sheet Metal Fabrication Content -- Untapped Differentiation

**The data:** Klimit has an in-house Red Seal 308A journeyperson sheet metal worker -- a credential most competitors cannot match. Custom ductwork, fire pits, commercial kitchen hoods, stainless steel cladding. This is visually compelling, highly differentiated work that is completely invisible online.

**The play:** Photo/video portfolio of custom fabrication projects. Before/after gallery. "Why custom ductwork matters" educational content. Instagram Reels and TikTok showing the fabrication process (this content performs exceptionally well on social media). Case studies of commercial kitchen hood installations. This positions Klimit as craftspeople, not just installers.

**Revenue impact:** Custom sheet metal work commands premium pricing ($2,000-$10,000+ per project) with higher margins than standard furnace installs. A visible portfolio attracts both residential and commercial clients who are specifically seeking custom work.

### 4. Emergency Service Messaging -- Capture the -40C Panic Search

**The data:** Thunder Bay experiences some of the coldest winters in Ontario. "Furnace repair thunder bay" gets 210 searches/month. Reviews prove Klimit responds to emergencies within an hour. But their listed hours are Mon-Fri 8-4:30 with zero emergency messaging.

**The play:** Add emergency/after-hours messaging prominently on the website, GBP, and all social profiles. Create a dedicated "Emergency Furnace Repair" landing page targeting "emergency furnace repair thunder bay" and "furnace not working thunder bay." Even if they don't offer 24/7 service, clearly stating response times and emergency contact options captures leads that competitors lose.

**Revenue impact:** Emergency furnace calls are the highest-margin HVAC work -- homeowners in crisis pay premium rates and rarely shop around. Capturing even 2-3 emergency leads per month during winter could add $10,000-$15,000 in seasonal revenue.
