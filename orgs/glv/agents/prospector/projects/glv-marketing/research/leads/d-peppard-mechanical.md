## Niche-Marketing Misalignment

**Target audience:** Homeowners aged 35-65+, median household income $77,500, Thunder Bay and surrounding rural communities. Trust and reliability are the #1 purchase drivers. Harsh winters (-30C+) make HVAC not a luxury but a safety essential.

**The misalignment:**

1. **David has 36 years of trade experience, NATE certification, and 128 five-star Google reviews -- his website communicates none of this authority.** The audience (35-65+ homeowners) buys on trust and credentials. His website buries the strongest selling points. There are no "Why Choose Us" proof points on service pages, no certification badges, no "30+ years experience" in page titles or meta descriptions, and no review count displayed anywhere.

2. **The mobile experience is broken for the exact moment customers need it most.** When a furnace fails at -30C, homeowners search on their phone. The hero text clips, the form overflows, and the chat widget covers content. This is a conversion killer for emergency searches.

3. **128 Google reviews at 4.9 stars vs. Clow Darling's 78 at 4.1 -- and zero visibility in search.** Peppard has the best review profile in Thunder Bay HVAC but can't be found for any non-branded search. The review strength should translate to Google 3-Pack dominance for "hvac near me" and "furnace repair thunder bay." The fact that it doesn't suggests missing on-page signals, thin GBP categories, or poor keyword-to-page mapping.

4. **Keyword cannibalization is actively suppressing rankings.** "Furnace thunder bay" triggers 5 different URLs. "HRV system" triggers 3. "2 stage furnace" triggers 3. Google can't pick a winner, so none of them rank well. This is the single biggest technical SEO issue.

5. **Email domain (peppardmechanical.com) differs from website domain (dpeppardmechanical.com).** While not a crisis, this creates brand confusion and splits any domain authority. Ideally, both domains should resolve to the same site.

6. **GE Pro Dealer with 12-year warranty -- invisible in search and on homepage.** This is a massive differentiator for the 35-65+ homeowner comparing options. It should be in title tags, meta descriptions, and hero sections.

7. **No emergency-hour presence.** Hours listed as Mon-Fri 8-4 PM. For an HVAC company in Thunder Bay, "furnace not working" searches spike evenings and weekends. No after-hours messaging, no emergency page. Meanwhile, Clow Darling prominently features "24/7 EMERGENCY SERVICE" and "AFTER HOURS CALL?" on their homepage.
