## Personal Story

Benji Dittrich started his construction company on January 5, 2009 -- right in the teeth of the 2008-2009 recession. Over 17 years, he and his wife Bonnie have built it into Greater Sudbury's go-to foundation repair and waterproofing specialist, working as a tight husband-and-wife team.

The company positions itself with the tagline "A Reputation built on Trust and Expert Service" and emphasizes a diagnostic, investigative approach to foundation problems rather than cookie-cutter solutions. Their website states they "come highly recommended from suppliers and past clients" and describes their team as "leak and drainage sleuths" -- a brand identity built around expertise and thoroughness.

Multiple Sudbury.com sponsored spotlight articles (at least 3 found) suggest the company has invested in local PR/advertorial content, which is more marketing sophistication than most trades businesses in the area demonstrate. They describe themselves as proudly local: "we live in Sudbury, play in Sudbury and understand the needs of our fellow Sudburians."

Reviews consistently highlight Benji personally as professional, honest, thorough, and easy to deal with. The office side (Bonnie) is noted for fast, friendly response times. This is a company where both the work quality AND the customer experience get praised -- which makes the 3.7-star rating particularly notable (see Red Flags).

## Red Flags

1. **3.7-star Google rating is below the trust threshold.** Industry research shows 87% of consumers avoid contractors rated below 4 stars. With only ~5 reviews, one negative review has a disproportionate effect. This is the single biggest barrier to converting their #1 organic ranking into leads.

2. **Gmail address as business email.** benjidittrichconstruction@gmail.com signals low digital maturity. For a 17-year-old business handling $5K-$30K+ foundation projects, a branded email (info@benjidittrichconstruction.com) would significantly increase trust.

3. **Content being built on someone else's domain.** At least 3 paid Sudbury.com spotlight articles exist -- these are quality foundation repair content pieces building Sudbury.com's authority and backlink profile instead of Benji's own site. This is a common mistake and a clear coaching opportunity.

4. **Small team may limit budget appetite.** ~3 employees, husband-and-wife operation. Marketing budget may be constrained. However, they are clearly willing to spend (Sudbury.com spotlights cost money), just spending inefficiently.

5. **Old business name on Yellow Pages.** "Benji-Dittrich-Custom-Renovation-Contracting" suggests the business originally offered general renovation/contracting before specialising in foundation work. The old YP listing was never updated, which splits local SEO signals.

6. **GBP website points to old domain.** www.benjidittrich.com instead of benjidittrichconstruction.com. While the redirect works for users, search engines may split authority between the two domains.

7. **No response to negative Google review.** The "not pleased with quality of work" review from Sam appears to be unanswered. Unanswered negative reviews signal indifference to prospective clients and are a red flag to Google's algorithm.
