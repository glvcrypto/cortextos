## Personal Story

Centennial Golf Course has been a Thunder Bay fixture since 1967 — nearly 60 years serving the community. In February 2013, Gerald Breukelman, already the owner of Northern Lights Golf Complex, purchased Centennial and rebranded it as "Centennial Pines Golf Course." At the time, he described the acquisition as "a new challenge" and introduced dual membership packages ($800 for access to both courses) to attract city golfers.

Breukelman said golfers shouldn't expect major changes beyond maintenance on the clubhouse, bunkers, and water system, though he expressed a vision to someday expand the nine-hole course to 18 holes. The two courses are only five minutes apart, creating a unique dual-course experience in the Thunder Bay market.

The course sits within city limits on Thompson Road, offering a par 36, 3,316-yard layout. Reviews describe it as a "forgiving course" with greens "better than Whitewater and half the price." Amenities include a driving range, putting green, practice hole, club rentals, golf carts, a bar, banquet facilities, and a teaching pro on staff. Green fees run $25-$45 for 9 holes. Metal spikes are not allowed and proper golf attire is required.

There are active community groups including the "Wednesday Centennial Pines Ladies Golf" league and a "Centennial Pines Men's Club," both with their own Facebook pages — indicating loyal, engaged membership despite zero digital marketing.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Domain recovery + website build from scratch** — centennialgolfcourse.ca has fully lapsed. Either re-register it or register an alternative (e.g., centennialpinesgolf.ca). Build a mobile-responsive site with hours, rates, course info, photos, and online tee time booking. Even a basic 3-5 page site would go from 0 to immediately capturing some of the ~6,335/mo local golf searches.

2. **Claim and optimize the GBP listing** — The GBP has 4.5 stars and 38 reviews, which is a strong foundation, but it is UNCLAIMED. Claiming it would allow: linking a website, responding to reviews, posting seasonal updates, adding photos, correcting hours, and appearing in the "near me" map pack. This is the single highest-ROI action.

3. **Online tee time booking** — Competitors like Whitewater Golf Club offer online booking. For a seasonal business, online booking reduces phone call overhead and captures after-hours bookings. Platforms like GolfNow, Chronogolf, or a simple embedded booking form would immediately modernize the operation.

4. **Dual-property marketing** — Breukelman owns both Centennial Pines and Northern Lights (~5 min apart). Neither has a functional website. A unified web presence or cross-marketing strategy could promote the dual membership ($800 for both courses) and position the pair as a unique Thunder Bay golf package. One website investment covers two properties.

5. **NAP cleanup** — Fix the 5+ inconsistencies across directories (wrong postal code on GolfPass, wrong hole count on GolfLink, wrong category on Yellow Pages, truncated phone on Golf Canada, broken website links on 6+ directories). This would improve local search signals even before a website exists.

6. **Community marketing** — The Ladies Golf league and Men's Club already have Facebook pages with engaged members. These are untapped marketing channels that could drive referrals, event bookings, and membership sales with minimal effort.

7. **Review response strategy** — 38 Google reviews with no owner responses. A review from "kat trin" specifically called out poor signage and wayfinding — responding publicly would show the business is active and attentive.

- **Domain completely dead (not just down — DNS non-existent)** — This indicates either deliberate abandonment of the web presence or significant neglect. The domain registration was allowed to fully lapse.
- **GBP unclaimed** — The owner has never verified the Google Business Profile, or has lost access. This suggests very low digital awareness/priority.
- **Same owner as Northern Lights Golf Complex** — Not a red flag per se, but outreach must be coordinated. One consolidated pitch for both properties is appropriate. Pitching separately would appear uninformed.
- **Budget concern** — A 9-hole course at $25-$45 green fees with seasonal operation has limited revenue. However, Breukelman owns TWO courses, so combined revenue potential is higher.
- **Possible succession in progress** — Nathan Breukelman (LinkedIn: "Golf Course management") may indicate a generational transition. This could be either an opportunity (younger generation more receptive to digital) or a complication (uncertain decision-making authority).
