## Personal Story

Thunder Bay Country Club is a genuine piece of Canadian golf history. Laid out in 1910 by Tom Bendelow — the "Johnny Appleseed of American Golf" who designed over 600 courses across North America — it is the oldest golf course in Thunder Bay and one of the oldest in Northwestern Ontario. The course sits on hilly terrain surrounded by boreal forest with stunning views of Mount McKay, and meanders alongside creeks that come into play on nearly every hole.

What makes the course architecturally clever is that despite having only 9 greens, each hole features two sets of teeing areas positioned differently, providing a genuinely different playing experience when going around twice for an 18-hole round. This Bendelow innovation gives members the feel of an 18-hole course on a 9-hole footprint.

The club has weathered significant change. In December 2022, Di Gregorio Developments Inc. purchased the property — both the golf course portion and a parcel containing an unfinished 109-unit condominium project that had stalled since 2012. Silvio Di Gregorio, president of the development company (which also purchased the former Municipal Golf Course on Rosslyn Road in 2014), has committed to letting the membership continue golf operations "for the foreseeable future." As of 2026, the condo project has pivoted to apartments, with shell construction underway.

Joe Valente has navigated the club through this ownership transition, balancing the uncertainty of development encroaching on club grounds with maintaining the 116-year tradition. The club runs on membership loyalty and community connection — this is not a corporate operation but a community institution.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Heritage content strategy** (observational) — Thunder Bay Country Club has a story that money cannot buy: 116 years of history, designed by Tom Bendelow (one of golf's most prolific architects), oldest course in Thunder Bay, views of Mount McKay, boreal forest setting. ZERO of this is on the website. A content strategy built around the club's heritage ("Play where Thunder Bay has played since 1910") would differentiate from Whitewater and capture long-tail search traffic around golf history, Tom Bendelow courses, heritage golf in Ontario, and Northwestern Ontario golf tourism.

2. **Non-member online booking** (data-backed) — Currently non-members must phone to book. The club is on GolfNow and TeeOff but doesn't promote these on their site. Opening up Tee-On to non-member online booking (or prominently featuring GolfNow) would capture the estimated ~6,335/mo golf-related searches in the Thunder Bay market. Every competitor offers online booking to the public.

3. **The Residence revival** (data-backed) — theresidencetbcc.com is completely down. This accommodation facility could be a major revenue driver for golf packages, tournament guests, and destination players. A proper landing page with booking integration, photos, and golf-stay packages would create a revenue stream no other Thunder Bay golf course offers (Whitewater has no on-site accommodation).

4. **Wedding and event marketing** (data-backed) — Fort William Country Club dominates Thunder Bay wedding venue searches with detailed pages, menus, and 200+ guest capacity. TBCC has event space but zero wedding marketing. Even capturing 10% of this market would mean significant off-peak revenue. The heritage angle ("Your wedding at Thunder Bay's oldest club") is powerful positioning.

5. **Keyword gap exploitation** (data-backed) — TBCC has 109 keywords generating 391/mo traffic. Whitewater has 121 keywords generating 1,778/mo — a **4.5x traffic-per-keyword advantage**. Key gaps likely include: "golf thunder bay," "tee times thunder bay," "golf course near me thunder bay," "golf events thunder bay." Service-specific landing pages for tournaments, corporate events, and membership would close this gap.

6. **Review consolidation and generation** (observational) — Reviews are scattered across 5+ platforms (GolfNow, GolfPass, Hole19, 18Birdies, TripAdvisor, Google) with low counts on each. A Google review generation campaign targeting members and recent players could build a strong GBP presence. Whitewater has ~83 reviews at 4.4 stars — TBCC needs to match this.

7. **Rates and membership page overhaul** (data-backed) — Rates page shows 2024 pricing, membership page shows 2023 pricing. For a seasonal business where golfers compare options in April-May, having outdated pricing is a conversion killer. Updated, well-designed pricing pages with membership benefits comparison, early-bird deals, and online application would drive membership growth.

## Red Flags

- **Property ownership uncertainty** — Di Gregorio Developments owns the land and has a development project (109-unit apartment building) adjacent to the course. While they have committed to keeping golf operations running "for the foreseeable future," the club operates on someone else's property. This creates long-term uncertainty about the club's future and may make Joe Valente hesitant about major marketing investments.
- **The Residence is a dead asset** — theresidencetbcc.com is completely down. This was an accommodation facility that could differentiate TBCC from every other Thunder Bay golf course. Its current state (dead website, no booking, no information) suggests it may not be operational at all.
- **Stale pricing across the board** — 2024 rates, 2023 membership pricing. For a seasonal business where golfers make decisions in April-May, this signals either neglect or that someone forgot to update. Either way, it loses potential members and casual players.
- **9-hole limitation** — While the dual-tee-box design is clever, serious golfers and tournament organizers may prefer Whitewater's true 18-hole layout. This is a structural limitation that marketing cannot fully overcome.
- **Development construction on property** — The shell for the 109-unit apartment building is now being constructed on the club grounds. Construction noise, visual disruption, and parking/access issues during the golf season could impact the playing experience and member satisfaction.
