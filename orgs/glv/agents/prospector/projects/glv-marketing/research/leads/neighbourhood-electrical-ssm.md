## Their Story

**What we know (confirmed from multiple directories):**

Neighbourhood Electrical Services lists its service scope as: residential, commercial, industrial, infrared scanning, service upgrades, pools and hot tubs. That is an unusually broad service menu for a contractor with this thin a profile — it signals either genuine multi-sector capacity or aspirational listing copy.

**Infrared scanning — what it means for this pitch:**

Infrared/thermal imaging of electrical panels, switchgear, and distribution boards is a preventive maintenance service sold primarily to commercial property owners and managers, strata/condo corporations, industrial facilities, and property insurers. It is not a residential consumer service. If Neighbourhood Electrical is legitimately offering this, they have commercial and industrial clients — the kind of clients who have actual marketing budgets and care about credibility, certifications, and online presence.

In Northern Ontario, thermal imaging electricians often serve:
- Commercial landlords (strip malls, office buildings)
- Industrial clients (algoma steel supply chain, Essar-era legacy industry)
- Property management companies
- Insurance underwriters requiring annual electrical certification

**What we cannot confirm:** Whether the infrared scanning listing is genuine operational capacity or a legacy/aspirational service entry in a directory. This is one of the two key questions for the visit.

**Founding / history:** No information found. No news mentions, no spotlight articles, no Chamber of Commerce membership, no SSM Construction Association membership. Permanent Electric (founded 1981, spotlight article in SooToday) and Guzzo (founded 1990, 16 staff) are both SSM institutions by comparison. Neighbourhood Electrical appears to be a newer or very deliberately low-profile operation.

## Retainer Viability

**The case FOR $2K/month capacity:**

1. **Infrared scanning = B2B clientele.** If real, this service requires capital investment (a quality thermal camera runs $3K–$15K+), certification training, and commercial relationships. Clients paying for annual infrared scans are property managers and commercial operators with ongoing maintenance budgets. These clients can also buy digital credibility — they want to find a qualified contractor online before they hire.

2. **Mon/Wed/Fri hours.** The 3-day admin week suggests either field days (Tue/Thu) or a deliberately managed workload. If field days are Tue/Thu, the owner is billing — which means revenue is coming in, even if the profile is invisible.

3. **Residential + commercial + industrial scope.** The full-service menu, if real, means Neighbourhood Electrical is not a single-trade handyman. They are positioned (even if not marketed) for project-level work.

**The case AGAINST / risks:**

1. **Sole proprietor size risk.** If this is one person, $2K/month for marketing is a significant commitment. A one-person shop running 3 admin days a week has limited capacity to take on new business from inbound leads.

2. **No evidence of commercial project history.** No project photos, no case studies, no news mentions of commercial work. The infrared scanning claim is unverified.

3. **Unconfirmed ESA number.** Until the correct ESA authorization is confirmed, we cannot verify the scope of work they're licensed for.

**Verification questions for the meeting:**
- "How many guys are you running right now?"
- "Do you do a lot of commercial work or mostly residential?"
- "The infrared scanning — are you doing those regularly? What kind of clients?"

## What GLV Would Do (Month 1)

**1. Claim and build the Google Business Profile (Week 1)**
GBP is confirmed unclaimed or dormant — it is the single highest-ROI action for any local service business. Set up categories (Electrician, Electrical Contractor), add service list (including infrared scanning as a callout), add photos of the owner, truck, and any completed work, set correct hours, add the phone number. This is free real estate on the most-used local search platform in Canada.

**2. Build a 3-page website with infrared scanning as a hero service (Weeks 2–3)**
Home page (residential + commercial credibility), Infrared Scanning landing page (B2B focused — "Is your building's electrical system at risk? Annual infrared inspections for commercial properties"), and a Contact/Quote page. Infrared scanning is the differentiator that separates Neighbourhood Electrical from every other SSM residential electrician. It deserves its own page optimized for terms like "infrared electrical inspection Sault Ste Marie" and "commercial electrical inspection SSM."

**3. Launch a Google review acquisition campaign (Week 3–4)**
Send a simple ask to every past client with a direct GBP review link. Target: 10 reviews in Month 1. For a contractor with real client relationships and zero current reviews, this is achievable and transforms the credibility gap fast. At 10+ Google reviews with 4.5+ stars, they start surfacing in the "electricians near me" map pack — the pack that Tombari, Guzzo, and Permanent Electric own right now.
