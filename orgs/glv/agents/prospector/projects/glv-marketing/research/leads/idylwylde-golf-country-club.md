## Personal Story

Idylwylde Golf & Country Club was founded in 1922, making it 104 years old and one of Northern Ontario's most historic private clubs. Developed from the old Howey Farm on the south shore of Ramsey Lake, early golfers had to reach the club by water taxi until a proper road was built.

The course sits on a stunning peninsula overlooking Lake Nepahwin, with the lake coming into play on several holes. It is a parkland-style course with bent grass greens and fairways, designed over the decades by five notable course architects including George Cumming (original) and Doug Carrick.

The club celebrated its centennial in 2022 with "Celebrating 100 Years" branding still featured prominently on their GBP cover photo. It boasts a five-sheet curling rink, professional catering facility, pro shop, and dining room.

**Breaking news (August 2025):** Members voted 77.5% in favour of selling 10.6 acres (holes 6 and 7) to Health Sciences North for hospital expansion. HSN covers course redesign costs. The front nine will be redesigned, with construction beginning second half of 2026 golf season, continuing through 2027-2028. Proceeds fund a clubhouse redevelopment. Idylwylde initiated the deal. This is a club in active transformation.

**Financial Context:** The club owes the City of Greater Sudbury $862,354 in deferred taxes (as of Oct 2022), stemming from a 1966 tax deferral agreement that fixed assessed value at $71,490 while the actual assessed value is $4.08 million. The club pays current taxes but the deferred balance grows annually.

## Red Flags

1. **Board governance = slow decisions.** This is a member-owned private club. The GM reports to a Board of Directors with a rotating president. Marketing vendor decisions may require board approval, adding weeks or months to the sales cycle. The land sale required a member vote (77.5% approval), showing the club operates democratically -- which means slow.

2. **Financial pressure.** The $862K deferred tax debt, combined with selling land to a hospital, suggests the club faces financial constraints. The land sale proceeds are earmarked for clubhouse redevelopment, not marketing. Budget for digital marketing may be tight.

3. **Active transformation period (2026-2028).** The front nine redesign beginning mid-2026 means disruption to golf operations, potential membership churn, and competing priorities. Marketing could be deprioritized during construction. Conversely, this is exactly when they NEED marketing most.

4. **Wix lock-in.** Migrating from Wix requires buy-in for a full website rebuild. If they are attached to Wix (ease of use, existing content), the conversation shifts from "build a new site" to "optimize what you have" -- which limits scope.

5. **Private club culture.** Private clubs can be insular. Marketing is sometimes viewed as "not what we do" because membership was historically word-of-mouth. Paul Schweyer's 25-year tenure means deep institutional knowledge but potentially resistance to change.
