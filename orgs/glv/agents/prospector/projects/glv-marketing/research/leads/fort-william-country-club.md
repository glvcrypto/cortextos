## Personal Story

Fort William Country Club is one of Northwestern Ontario's most storied golf institutions. Founded in 1923 after an organizational meeting led by Dr. M.B. Dean with Dr. R.J. Manion (local MP) as chairman, the club purchased the John Garrity 100-acre farm for $7,500 and hired legendary Canadian golf architect **Stanley Thompson** to design the course. The original 9-hole layout opened in spring 1926.

The club expanded to its current 18-hole championship layout in 1959-1964 under the vision of R.W. Anderson, with Vancouver architect Norman H. Woods handling the redesign. Norman Owens drove the first ball on August 29, 1964. A new clubhouse followed in 1965 with support from Great Lakes Paper Company. In 2002, five additional acres were acquired for a driving range and practice facility.

Nestled at the foot of the Nor'Wester Mountain Range with views of vertical rock faces, the course is widely considered one of the most scenic in Northern Ontario. The **Stanley Thompson design pedigree** puts FWCC in the company of Banff Springs, St. George's, and Highlands Links — a heritage marketing angle that is completely underutilized online.

The club's food and beverage operations are now handled by **Seler Restaurant & Wine** (an established Thunder Bay restaurant at 555 Dunlop Street), operating the "Caddy Shack" on-site daily from 10am-10pm with a summer patio overlooking the course.

Craig Morton has been Head Professional for 25+ years — an unusual tenure that speaks to stability and deep community roots. The transition from Stacey Hutton to Michael Bruce as GM appears recent.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Content/SEO Gap — Capture $6,335/mo in golf search volume** (data-backed)
   FWCC ranks for 166 keywords generating only $8/mo in organic value. This means they rank almost exclusively for brand terms. The total Thunder Bay golf search volume is ~6,335/mo, and FWCC captures a fraction. A blog + landing page strategy targeting "golf courses thunder bay," "best golf course thunder bay," "thunder bay tee times," and "stanley thompson golf courses ontario" could capture 500-1,000 new monthly visitors within 6 months.

2. **Wedding Venue SEO — Untapped $40/mo keyword with high conversion value** (data-backed)
   "Wedding venue thunder bay" gets 40 searches/month. FWCC has dedicated wedding pages but they are thin, unoptimized, and have no testimonials. WeddingWire listing exists but FWCC's own pages do not rank. A proper wedding landing page with photos, pricing, testimonials, and schema markup could capture this high-intent traffic. Average wedding spend at a venue = $5,000-$15,000 — even 2-3 extra bookings/year = $10K-$45K additional revenue.

3. **Stanley Thompson Heritage Marketing — Unique national differentiator** (observational + data-backed)
   FWCC is one of only ~140 Stanley Thompson-designed courses in existence. Thompson enthusiasts actively seek these courses (there's a Stanley Thompson Society with a dedicated page for FWCC). This heritage is barely mentioned on the website and generates zero search traffic. A content strategy around "stanley thompson golf courses," "historic golf courses ontario," and "best golf courses northern ontario" would attract destination golfers who spend more (green fees + dining + pro shop + potentially accommodation).

4. **Google Reviews vs Whitewater — Close the gap** (data-backed)
   FWCC has 74 Google reviews at 4.6 stars. Whitewater has ~83 reviews at 4.4 stars. FWCC has better ratings but fewer reviews. An active review solicitation campaign (QR codes at checkout, follow-up emails after rounds) could push FWCC past 100 reviews, strengthening GBP ranking and local pack visibility.

## Red Flags

- **Board-run club structure:** As a member-owned country club with a 10-person board of directors, marketing decisions likely require board approval. This means longer sales cycles and potential committee-driven decision-making. The GM (Michael Bruce) would need to champion any marketing spend to the board.
- **Outsourced F&B:** Seler Restaurant running the food operation means dining revenue may be partially outside FWCC's control. Marketing the restaurant experience requires coordination with a separate business.
- **Recent GM change:** Transition from Stacey Hutton to Michael Bruce suggests organizational change. Could be positive (new GM open to new ideas) or negative (GM still getting feet wet, may not want to commit budget).
- **Tee-On CMS limitation:** The website runs on Tee-On's platform, which is designed for golf operations, not marketing. Any serious SEO/content strategy may require migrating to WordPress or building a separate marketing site alongside Tee-On.
- **No red flags on reputation** — 4.6 stars with 74 reviews is strong. Only 2 negative reviews (1x 2-star, 1x 1-star) out of 74 total.

## Recommended Outreach Angle

**Primary Why:** FWCC is a 100-year-old Stanley Thompson-designed course — one of the most prestigious pedigrees in Canadian golf — but generates only $8/mo in organic search value. Meanwhile, Whitewater (a 2005 course with no historical significance) captures 28% more organic traffic. The heritage and scenery are world-class; the digital presence does not match.

**Data Point:** FWCC ranks for 166 keywords but generates only $8/mo in organic value — meaning almost zero commercially valuable traffic. With 6,335 monthly golf-related searches in Thunder Bay, there is a massive untapped audience.

**Competitor Comparison:** Whitewater Golf Club gets ~1,778 monthly organic visitors vs. FWCC's ~1,391 — a 28% traffic advantage — despite being a younger course with fewer Google reviews (FWCC: 4.6 stars/74 reviews vs. Whitewater's ~4.4 stars/~83 reviews). FWCC has the better product and reputation but weaker digital presence.

**HOOK question for outreach:** "Did you know that Whitewater gets 28% more website traffic than Fort William, even though your course has a Stanley Thompson pedigree and better Google ratings?"
