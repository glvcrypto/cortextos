## Personal Story / Finnish Heritage

**St. Urho** is a fictional Finnish folk saint, created by Finnish Americans in 1950s Minnesota. The legend says St. Urho drove grasshoppers out of Finland to save the grape harvest — a humorous parallel to St. Patrick driving snakes from Ireland. St. Urho's Day is celebrated March 16.

**Nolalu has deep Finnish roots.** Finnish homesteaders settled the area at the turn of the 20th century. The name "Nolalu" comes from "The NOrthern LAnd & LUmber Co." In 1909, Finnish farmers formed the "Farmers' Mercantile" co-op store — the first communal business in Nolalu. Key founding families include Pelto, Maki, Arola, Oja, Rajala, and Seppala.

The golf course name is a direct **celebration of Finnish-Canadian heritage** in Nolalu. The "Flying Finn Racing" merchandise at Hopper's Variety reinforces this identity. Any marketing should honour, not erase, this cultural connection.
