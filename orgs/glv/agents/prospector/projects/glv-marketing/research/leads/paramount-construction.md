## Red Flags

1. **Employee/conduct reviews are concerning.** The Indeed review (1.5 stars) and multiple Google reviews mention worker misconduct (drinking on the job, sleeping in vehicles, aggressive driving). Even if exaggerated, these are public and unaddressed. Any digital marketing push needs a reputation cleanup component first.

2. **Multiple domains create confusion.** paramount-constructionon.ca, paramount-construction.ca, paramount-construction.net -- which is canonical? The "constructionon" domain is confusing (looks like a typo). Old domains may be leaking authority or creating duplicate content.

3. **3.0-star Google rating is below the trust threshold.** Most consumers filter for 4+ stars. Without a review strategy, increasing visibility could actually backfire -- more people seeing 3.0 stars means more choosing competitors.

4. **Contractual red flag in public reviews.** The $200/hr minimum 8 hours insurance clause mentioned in a review could deter prospects who read reviews carefully.
