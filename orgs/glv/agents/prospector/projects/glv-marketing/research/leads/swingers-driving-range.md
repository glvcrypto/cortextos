## Personal Story

Swingers Driving Range is a practice-focused golf facility that recently underwent an ownership transition. A new ownership group including Ryan Barber and Brian Hobson took over, suggesting fresh energy and potential appetite for growth.

The facility differentiates itself through its **all-grass teeing ground** — most driving ranges use artificial mats, but Swingers lets golfers practice on real grass, replicating actual course conditions. Features include:
- Full-length practice range (all clubs including drivers)
- 800 sq ft putting green
- Practice bunker
- Clubhouse with free coffee and scenic views
- **24/7 automated ball dispenser** (accepts debit/credit/cash tokens) — golfers can practice any hour

Pricing is straightforward: $10 for a medium basket (45-50 balls) or $20 for a large basket (95-100 balls). They also host birthday parties, corporate events, and private bookings.

The facility earned some local attention in 2024 when thousands of golf balls were stolen from the range — a story Tania Corbin (manager/groundskeeper) dealt with publicly via TBNewsWatch.

## Big-Picture Assessment

Swingers Driving Range is a small, seasonal operation with a new ownership group that likely has appetite for growth but may have limited marketing budget. The 4.5-star rating and positive reviews show good customer satisfaction, but the business is under-marketed across every digital channel.

**Strengths:**
- Genuine differentiator (all-grass teeing ground, 24/7 auto-dispenser)
- Good reputation (4.5 stars)
- New ownership = potential appetite for change
- Chamber of Commerce member = engaged in local business community
- Multi-platform social media presence (even if under-utilized)

**Weaknesses:**
- Very small operation with seasonal revenue
- Website abandoned since 2019
- Low digital visibility (~33/mo traffic, 16 keywords)
- NAP inconsistencies hurting local SEO
- No online booking, no email capture, no content strategy

**Verdict:** TIER 3 LEAD. Good business with real digital marketing needs, but the revenue ceiling and seasonal nature make this a lower-priority prospect for GLV. Best approach is a smaller, focused package (GBP optimization + NAP cleanup + review generation) rather than a full retainer. If the new ownership group has growth ambitions (e.g., adding lessons, expanding events, extending season with indoor facility), the opportunity grows significantly.
