## Personal Story

James Scanlon is a Northern Ontario native who has called North Bay home since age eleven. He originally studied health sciences because he genuinely loves helping people, but found that the clinical work itself didn't inspire him the way the human connection did. He pivoted to the trades, and over 12+ years built Connected Plumbing into what ZoomInfo estimates is a 10-19 employee operation.

James is a father of two daughters. He's an outdoors enthusiast who enjoys fishing, hockey, and soccer — a deeply Northern Ontario lifestyle. His business philosophy is rooted in the same reason he entered health sciences: he wants to help people. His Alignable network shows connections to complementary trades businesses (HVAC, controls), suggesting he is embedded in the local contractor community.

The fact that he built a team of 10-19 from scratch using word-of-mouth (virtually no online marketing) speaks to the quality of his work and reputation.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Website revival + local SEO** (data-backed) — With only 8-10 of 28 plumbers having websites, simply getting a site live puts them in the top third. ~1,070 monthly plumbing searches in North Bay, low difficulty across all keywords.

2. **Google review blitz** (competitor comparison) — Perrotta's has 84 reviews. Harwood has 131. Connected has ZERO. With a 10-19 person team doing work daily, 30-50 reviews in 60-90 days is realistic. Even 20 reviews at 5 stars would make them competitive for Maps Pack.

3. **GBP optimization** (data-backed) — GBP exists as a shell. Fill out categories, add team photos, post weekly updates, add services. Free and high-impact. 42% of local searches result in Maps Pack clicks.

4. **Emergency plumber positioning** (observational) — "Emergency plumber North Bay" has low competition. With their 24/7 availability and real team to back it up, they could own this segment.

5. **HomeStars + BBB listings** (competitor comparison) — Perrotta's is on BBB. Connected is on neither HomeStars nor BBB. Trust signals that matter to the 35-65 demographic.
