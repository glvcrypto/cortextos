## Personal Story

Forest Ridge Golf & Country Club exists because of one man's ambition and one construction company's capability. In the early 1990s, Ron Belanger -- owner of R.M. Belanger Limited, a construction company in Greater Sudbury -- agreed to bring his company's assets and building expertise to the table to carve an 18-hole golf course out of the rugged Canadian Shield landscape near Chelmsford. Course designers Howard and Wayne MacDonald faced a formidable challenge: rock outcrops, swampland, varying elevations, and water ponds -- the quintessential Northern Ontario terrain that resists being tamed.

The course opened in 1994/1995 and has operated continuously for over 30 years. It sits on a dramatic property where the natural rock formations and water features were not removed but integrated into the course design, creating a playing experience that photographers describe as "breathtaking" and golfers call a "hidden gem."

Alice Aupin came to help with a single event 19 years ago and essentially never left. She now manages the restaurant, bar, and all special occasion bookings, while also running three weekly leagues and the club championship. Her trajectory from one-time volunteer to indispensable manager speaks to the kind of place Forest Ridge is -- it draws people in and keeps them.

The club hosts three massive league nights weekly: Monday men's night draws 140-170 players, Wednesday ladies night brings ~130, and Thursday mixed night attracts 50-65 couples. The Belanger Ford 2-Man Scramble (named for the owner's dealership connection) is a signature tournament with a $10,000 purse.

In 2023, they had one of their busiest tournament years on record, with events running well into October. They host upward of 15 weddings every year, with a scenic gazebo and water fountain for outdoor ceremonies and a banquet hall that accommodates 250 guests.

The 2018 workplace fatality at the course (a construction worker from R.M. Belanger Limited was killed while offloading a utility pole) is a sensitive chapter in the property's history. The Ministry of Labour issued six orders to R.M. Belanger Limited following the incident.

## Three Opportunities

### 1. Wedding Venue SEO + Directory Blitz (Highest-Margin Revenue Opportunity)

**The math:** Forest Ridge hosts 15+ weddings/year entirely through word of mouth. "Wedding venues sudbury ontario" gets 260 searches/month. Each wedding booking is worth $5,000-$20,000+. Forest Ridge is NOT listed on WeddingWire, TheKnot, EventSource, or any of the 3+ comprehensive Sudbury wedding venue guides.

**The fix:** Build out the wedding page on forestridgegolf.ca with real content: professional photos (the photographers who shoot there already have stunning images), packages and pricing, testimonials from past couples, ceremony location descriptions (gazebo, water fountain, Canadian Shield backdrops), reception details (250-capacity hall, buffet service, head table plate service). Claim listings on WeddingWire, TheKnot, EventSource. Add wedding-specific schema markup. Create a dedicated landing page optimized for "wedding venues sudbury" and related terms.

**Conservative estimate:** Capturing just 3-5 additional weddings per year from improved online visibility = $15,000-$100,000+ in incremental annual revenue. This is the single highest-ROI marketing investment Forest Ridge can make.

**Third-party validation:** Photographers Brooke Murray and Daniel Nunez already rank Forest Ridge among Sudbury's top wedding venues in their blog content. The product is proven -- the marketing just does not exist.

### 2. Fix the Golf Website + Booking Conversion Path

**The problem:** 720 people/month search for "forest ridge golf course" and land on a site where the rates page returns a 404 error and a GolfPass reviewer says the booking system is broken. The homepage content reads like placeholder copy. There is no course guide, no hole descriptions, no photo gallery, no blog.

**The fix:** Populate every page with real content. Fix the rates page. Verify and fix the online booking system. Add a hole-by-hole course guide (the Canadian Shield landscape is the selling point). Build a photo/video gallery showcasing the rock outcrops, water features, and fall colours. Start a seasonal blog (tournament recaps, course condition updates, league results). Add schema markup for golf course, local business, and event venue.

**Revenue impact:** Converting even 5% more of the 720 monthly branded searchers into green fee visits = 36 additional rounds/month at ~$50 = $1,800/month during the 6-month season = $10,800/year in traceable new golf revenue alone.

### 3. Review Generation + GBP Optimization

**The disconnect:** Forest Ridge has 140-170 players on Monday night alone, yet only 5 TripAdvisor reviews and 11 Facebook recommendations across the entire internet. This course has hundreds of loyal regulars who love the place -- the reviews just have not been asked for.

**The fix:** Implement a systematic review generation campaign. QR codes at the pro shop and 19th hole. Post-round email/text follow-ups. Google Business Profile optimization with regular posts, wedding-specific photos, and response to every review. Target 50+ Google reviews in the first 3 months.

**Impact:** Higher review volume + rating improves local pack ranking for "golf courses sudbury," "wedding venues sudbury," and all related local searches. Social proof drives conversion on the wedding side especially -- couples read reviews before booking a $10K+ event.

4. **Acknowledge the golf side.** The rates page is broken (404). A GolfPass reviewer publicly says the booking system does not work. 720 people search for the course by name every month. These are easy wins that build trust before the bigger wedding conversation.
5. **Target Dave Carlisle first.** PGA professional with Maui/Aspen/Texas experience. He understands what professional operations look like. He is most likely to see the gap between Forest Ridge's potential and its current digital presence. Alice Aupin is the alternative contact for the wedding-specific conversation.
