## The Big Picture

One Source Home Services is the **residential arm of a major Northern Ontario industrial conglomerate**. The Patrick Group operates 8-9 companies spanning mechanical contracting, mining services, concrete, metal fabrication, and boat building -- with 125+ employees at Patrick Mechanical alone. One Source was spun up in 2018 to capture the residential market using the Group's existing trade expertise.

This is not a scrappy startup. One Source has institutional backing, industrial-grade trade talent, and access to capital. Yet their digital presence tells a completely different story -- they market like a small owner-operator with a WordPress template site and minimal content strategy.

They sit at **#5 in the Sudbury HVAC market** with 289 keywords and ~292 monthly organic visits. That is dramatically underperforming given their resources. The top competitor, Campeau Heating, pulls ~1,507 visits with a 75-year-old brand. 669 Heat, a company of similar size and trade scope, pulls ~1,259 visits with 544 keywords.

The gap between One Source's operational capability and their digital visibility is the single biggest opportunity here.

## Niche-Marketing Misalignment

The core misalignment is stark: **One Source operates like a $5M+ enterprise but markets like a $500K sole operator.**

1. **Industrial parent, residential marketing void.** The Patrick Group has 125+ skilled tradespeople, multiple companies, and a 35-year reputation in Northern Ontario industrial contracting. None of that credibility, scale, or trust transfers to the One Source website. There is no mention of the Patrick Group on their consumer-facing site. They are hiding their biggest asset.

2. **Multi-trade breadth, single-trade depth problem.** One Source offers HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pressure washing, and carpentry. But their SEO only competes (weakly) in HVAC. They have almost zero keyword presence for "plumber sudbury", "electrician sudbury ontario", or "pressure washing sudbury". Each trade vertical is an untapped content opportunity.

3. **Enercare partnership is underexploited.** They rank #7 for "enercare advantage" (1,600 monthly searches nationally). This is their single highest-volume non-branded keyword, yet it is served by a single page with no supporting content. No comparison guides, no FAQ, no blog posts about rental vs. purchase. This keyword alone could drive meaningful traffic with proper content support.

4. **Review velocity does not match service volume.** A company backed by 125+ tradespeople should be generating far more than 75-127 Google reviews over 8 years of operation. This suggests no systematic review collection process.

## Three Opportunities (with Data)

### 1. Multi-Trade Service Page Expansion + Local Content Strategy

**The data:** One Source has pages for each trade but ranks for almost none of their plumbing, electrical, or pressure washing local keywords. Meanwhile:
- "electrician sudbury" = 320 monthly searches, they rank #7
- "plumber sudbury" = high volume, they do not appear in top 30
- "pressure washing sudbury" = they rank for national variants but not the local term

**The play:** Build dedicated, content-rich service pages for each trade + each sub-service (e.g., "emergency plumber sudbury", "electrical panel upgrade sudbury", "furnace installation sudbury"). Add a blog with 2-4 posts/month targeting the quick-win keywords identified in niche research (KD 0-10, 300+/mo combined volume). Within 6-12 months, they could realistically capture 500-800 monthly organic visits -- nearly tripling current traffic.

**Revenue impact:** At 3-5% conversion rate on 500 additional monthly visits = 15-25 new leads/month. At average $4,000-$9,500 per install job, even 2-3 conversions/month = $8K-$28K monthly revenue from SEO alone.

### 2. Enercare Content Hub

**The data:** "enercare advantage" has 1,600 monthly searches nationally. One Source ranks #7 with a single thin page. No supporting content exists.

**The play:** Build an Enercare content cluster -- comparison guide (rental vs. purchase), FAQ page, equipment-specific landing pages (water heater rental, furnace rental, AC rental), blog posts addressing common Enercare questions. Target long-tail Enercare keywords to build topical authority. This positions One Source as THE Enercare expert in Northern Ontario.

**Revenue impact:** Moving from position #7 to position 1-3 for "enercare advantage" alone could capture 160-480 monthly visits from a single keyword. Enercare rentals generate recurring monthly revenue -- every conversion is a long-term customer.

### 3. Google Review Acceleration + Local Pack Dominance

**The data:** One Source has 75-127 Google reviews. 669 Heat has 2,840+. Castle Plumbing, Campeau Heating, and other top competitors all have significantly more. Google Local Pack rankings are heavily influenced by review count, recency, and rating.

**The play:** Implement automated post-service review requests (SMS/email), train technicians on the ask, and set a target of 10-20 new reviews/month. Combined with improved local SEO (GBP optimization, local citations, service-area pages), One Source can break into the Local Pack for their core service keywords within 3-6 months.

**Revenue impact:** Local Pack listings receive 42% of clicks for local service searches. For "furnace repair sudbury" alone (210 monthly searches), a Local Pack position could generate 25-40 clicks/month of high-intent emergency traffic.

## Red Flags

1. **Corporate decision-making.** One Source is a subsidiary of the Patrick Group. Ian LaLonde is Director of Sales, not owner. Marketing decisions may require approval from Doug Patrick or a group-level executive. This could mean longer sales cycles and multi-stakeholder approval. Ask directly: "Who signs off on marketing investments?"

2. **Possible existing marketing vendor.** The website was built on Divi (a professional theme), has proper schema markup, and includes a blog post authored by "Marketing OSHS." This suggests someone -- internal or external -- has done marketing work. They may already have a relationship with an agency or freelancer. Ask: "Are you currently working with anyone on your website or marketing?"

3. **Enercare partnership dynamics.** As an Enercare partner, One Source may receive some marketing support or co-op funds from Enercare. Their Enercare page may be contractually required content. Understand the partnership terms before proposing changes to Enercare-related content.

4. **Multi-trade scope creep.** One Source does HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pressure washing, carpentry, and home construction. An SEO engagement could easily balloon in scope. Start focused (HVAC + one secondary trade) and expand. Do not promise to dominate all 5 trade verticals simultaneously.

5. **Review count discrepancy.** Google shows 75-127 reviews while Birdeye aggregates 202. Some reviews may be on Yelp, Facebook, or HomeStars rather than Google. Clarify where their reviews are strongest and prioritise Google.

## Summary

One Source Home Services is a **well-resourced, multi-trade home services company** backed by one of Northern Ontario's largest industrial groups -- but their digital marketing does not reflect their operational scale. They sit at #5 in Sudbury HVAC with ~292 monthly organic visits, stuck in positions 5-9 for every high-intent local keyword, with thin website content, almost no blog, and a review count that is 20-37x behind the market leader.

The opportunity is clear: this is a company with the capacity to handle increased lead volume, the financial backing to invest in marketing, and a massive gap between where they are digitally and where they should be. A focused SEO engagement ($2-3K/mo) targeting their multi-trade keyword gaps, Enercare content authority, and review velocity could realistically triple their organic traffic and generate 15-25 new monthly leads within 6-12 months.

**Outreach priority:** High. Contact Ian LaLonde. Lead with the 669 Heat comparison -- same services, same market, but 669 Heat gets 4x the organic traffic and has 20x the reviews. That is the hook.
