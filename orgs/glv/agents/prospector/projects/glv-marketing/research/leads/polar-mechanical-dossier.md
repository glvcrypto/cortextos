## Personal Story

George McKay is the owner of Polar Mechanical, operating under the legal entity Lakehead Polar Air Limited. The company has been in continuous operation since 1976 — making it a 50-year-old Thunder Bay institution. The legal name "Lakehead Polar Air Limited" suggests the original business may predate the Polar Mechanical brand, pointing to a long-standing local ownership structure rather than a recent purchase or rebrand.

The company services a massive geographic footprint: Thunder Bay east to Marathon and Manitouwadge, west to Kenora, and north to James Bay. This is not a small residential shop — it handles residential, commercial, institutional, and industrial sectors across Northwestern Ontario. They also operate a sheet metal fabrication shop on-site, indicating significant capital investment and operational depth.

No press mentions, community sponsorships, or award records were surfaced. No personal LinkedIn presence for George McKay. He is not active publicly online, which is consistent with the old-school trades operator profile — runs on referrals and reputation, not digital marketing.

A reviewer by name called him out directly: *"I've tried to get George out a few times... He says he'll drop by for a quote and never calls."* This suggests George is personally involved in sales/quoting — a solopreneur-style decision-making bottleneck for a 50-year-old commercial shop.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Review Crisis — Unmanaged and Unanswered** — Both Google reviews are 1 star and neither has a response from George or the business. A 50-year-old company with a 1.0-star Google rating is actively losing jobs to competitors. Responding to negative reviews, generating new positive ones, and building a review solicitation system would have an immediate revenue impact. This is the sharpest outreach angle: *you're invisible on Google, and the one thing people do see is damaging you.*

2. **Website With Lorem Ipsum on Key Pages** — The /services page contains dummy placeholder text. This means the site is either broken or abandoned. A business doing commercial/industrial HVAC work across Northwestern Ontario deserves a website that reflects that scale. Rebuilding or properly populating the site creates an immediate SEO foundation and trust signal for commercial clients researching contractors.

3. **Zero Local SEO Presence for High-Value Keywords** — "HVAC Thunder Bay," "commercial refrigeration Thunder Bay," "sheet metal contractor Thunder Bay," "gas fitter Thunder Bay" — none of these are being captured by Polar Mechanical's website. A competitor with even basic SEO is winning those searches. For a commercial contractor with a large geographic footprint, ranking locally is a significant revenue multiplier.

4. **No Content or Authority Building** — A 50-year-old company with licensed journeymen, a fabrication shop, and James Bay–to–Kenora service range has tremendous credibility to build on. A simple content strategy (service area pages, trade credential pages, project spotlights) would position them as the dominant mechanical authority in Northwestern Ontario — something no competitor in the region appears to be doing effectively.
