## Personal Story

STONY was founded in 2020 by Daniel Terrill and Matthew Kaustinen — two friends of nearly 20 years from Thunder Bay, Ontario. They started the brand because they "couldn't find a brand that spoke to that wild, rugged spirit" of Northern Canadian golf, "the kind of place where fairways are carved through granite and pine, and a round of golf can feel like an expedition."

The pair sources and researches technical fabric blends intended for athletes, admitting the process from design to refined fit and finish is lengthy. Their most popular polo, the Hazelwood, is named after Hazelwood Lake in the Thunder Bay area — reflecting the local roots woven into every product.

The brand has grown from a Thunder Bay CEDC-supported startup into a nationally distributed label with significant reach:
- **50+ pro shop locations** across 8 Canadian provinces and 1 US state (South Dakota)
- **Golf Town** carries STONY products nationally across Canada
- **Hudson's Bay (TheBay)** vendor listing
- **Golf PEI partnership** (2023) — joint vendor booth at Port Charlottetown targeting 212,000+ cruise passengers
- **Featured in Pro Golf Now** alongside Radmor and Rhoback as an essential golf brand
- **Club Customs** B2B service — custom-designed polos, hats, and outerwear for golf courses across Canada, contact: daniel@stonywilds.com
- Local retail: JB Evans Fashions, Goods & Co Market (Thunder Bay)

Product categories include men's and women's polos ($55-$95), headwear (snapbacks), outerwear, casual wear, youth apparel, and accessories. The brand emphasizes "cold weather comfort" and gear that handles the unpredictable Northern Ontario climate while remaining stylish enough for everyday wear. Free shipping on orders over $100.

The retail store at 251 Red River Rd in Thunder Bay's Waterfront District serves as both a shop and a brand experience space. Instagram following: ~5,642 (@stony.apparel).

## Opportunities Identified

1. **SEO content strategy** — STONY has a professional e-commerce platform but appears to lack content marketing. Blog content targeting "golf apparel canada," "cold weather golf gear," "northern ontario golf," and course feature stories could drive significant organic traffic and establish STONY as a thought leader in the Canadian golf apparel niche.

2. **Local SEO for retail store** — The physical store at 251 Red River Rd needs GBP optimization. Tourists visiting Thunder Bay searching for "golf shops thunder bay" or "golf gear thunder bay" should find STONY. GBP posts showcasing new arrivals, seasonal collections, and the Club Customs service would drive foot traffic.

3. **Club Customs growth marketing** — The custom apparel service for golf courses is a high-margin B2B revenue stream. Targeted outreach to golf courses across Ontario and Canada (especially Northern Ontario courses that align with the brand story) could significantly grow this segment. A dedicated landing page with case studies of courses they have outfitted would support this.
