## Red Flags

- **Team size unknown:** If Martin is entirely solo, the $2K/month retainer math is tight. A one-person shop doing 40 hours/week at ~$85–100/hour residential rates grosses roughly $170K–$200K/year. $24K/year in marketing (the $2K/month retainer) would be ~12–14% of gross — aggressive for a solo operator. Verify in meeting whether he has apprentices, subcontractors, or steady commercial clients that push revenue higher.
- **Referral-dependent business:** Martin's network (Finishline Carpentry, Staite Builders) may be generating enough work that he doesn't feel urgency. He may not see a problem to solve. The pitch needs to frame this around growth and industrial positioning, not fear of dying.
- **Live.ca email address:** kjelect@live.ca is a consumer Hotmail/Outlook address used as the business contact. This signals low digital sophistication — he may not think about marketing infrastructure at all. Do not lead with jargon.
- **No BBB, no HomeStars, no SSMCA:** The absence of trade association membership and consumer protection platforms is a mild flag — could indicate he has always been referral-only and never needed to play the directory game. Not a dealbreaker, but worth understanding.
- **Website test page indexed:** The page "test-2" is publicly indexed on Google. This is a minor technical housekeeping issue but signals the website was set up without professional guidance and may have other indexing or technical SEO problems.
