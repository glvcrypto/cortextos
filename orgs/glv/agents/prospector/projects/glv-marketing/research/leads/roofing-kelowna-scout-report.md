## Niche Research — Kelowna Roofing

**Climate and material demand drivers.** Kelowna sits in the BC Interior's semi-arid zone, which produces a climate profile unlike anywhere else in Canada: intense summer UV exposure, pronounced day-night thermal cycling, low precipitation but periodic heavy snow at elevation, and moderate (not Prairie-severe) winters. This cycling stresses roofing membranes and shingle adhesive strips faster than coastal climates. Ice damming at higher elevations — particularly properties with Big White access or rural hillside locations — remains a genuine winter concern despite milder valley temps. Flat and low-slope roofs (common on commercial strip retail and industrial) are especially prone to ponding from spring snowmelt.

**Wildfire ember exposure — the region's defining sales angle.** The 2023 McDougall Creek Wildfire destroyed hundreds of structures in West Kelowna and the surrounding hillsides. Documented cases of metal-roofed homes surviving while neighbouring shingle roofs burned are now actively referenced in contractor marketing. Homeowners in wildfire-interface zones (West Kelowna, Lake Country hillsides, Black Mountain, South Slopes) are increasingly requesting Class A fire-rated materials, ember-resistant ridge and soffit details, and full metal roofing systems. This demand is not merely theoretical — metal roofing specialists have made fire resistance their primary Okanagan marketing angle since 2023. For GLV clients who do not currently market this angle, there is a clear content and GBP gap to close.

**Secondary/vacation home and resort economy demand.** Kelowna is one of Canada's fastest-growing resort cities. A significant share of the residential housing stock is owned by Lower Mainland, Alberta, and US absentee owners who visit seasonally. These owners often discover roofing failures (leaks, flashing failures, wind damage) late — after a season of non-occupancy — creating emergency replacement demand with compressed decision timelines. Roofing contractors who appear prominently in Google search and have strong review velocity win a disproportionate share of these "call three companies, pick the first to reply" jobs.

**New construction and development boom.** With 38 projects currently under construction representing nearly 7,000 new units as of early 2026, Kelowna's residential and commercial development activity is driving sustained new-build roofing contract volume. Contractors with builder relationships (GC-preferred vendor lists, commercial flat-roof capability for strata podium decks) are capturing recurring project pipelines. Roofing + siding bundling is common in BC's interior — multiple Kelowna contractors market combined roofing and exterior cladding services. GLV prospects who do roofing-only face more substitution pressure than all-exterior operators.

**Organic search landscape.** Dominant organic and map-pack positions for "roofing Kelowna," "roof replacement Kelowna," and "metal roofing Kelowna" appear to be held by Archer Roofing (118 reviews, 4.9★, ThreeBestRated #1 2024), Pinnacle Roofing (10-year KelownaNow award winner, IKO's only BC Craftsman Premier designation), and TI Roof Rescue (multi-certified, Platinum winner 2020–2024). Companies without a claimed, keyword-optimized GBP and sub-50 reviews are effectively invisible for any competitive Kelowna roofing query.
