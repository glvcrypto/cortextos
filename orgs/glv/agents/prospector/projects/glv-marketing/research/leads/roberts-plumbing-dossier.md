### Squitti Family Legacy (Verified via Multiple Sources)

The Squitti family is one of Thunder Bay's oldest Italian-Canadian families, with roots on Secord Street since 1914. The business address (286 Secord St) sits in the historic "Little Italy" neighbourhood (Bay, Secord, and Algoma Streets -- the "South Side").

**Key family history:**
- **1914:** Three sisters from Simbario, Catanzaro (Italy) opened grocery stores on Secord Street -- Bertucci's, Franks, and Squitti's
- **1915:** Arthur Natale Squitti born on Secord Street (Port Arthur)
- **1919:** Marianne Berlucci Rossa born in Simbario
- **1957:** Robert's Plumbing & Sheet Metal founded (same year Caesar J.B. Squitti was born)
- **1967:** Arthur and Marianne opened "Squitti's 325 Bay" grocery store with fine Italian imports
- **1987:** Squitti's specialty department store (319 Bay Street) established by Caesar and Teresa Squitti
- **2021:** Caesar J.B. Squitti passed away (Oct 21, 1957 - Dec 18, 2021, age 64)

**The plumbing business has been operating from Secord Street for 69 years** -- the same street where the Squitti family opened their first grocery store over a century ago. This is a deep, multigenerational Italian-Canadian trade family story.

**Separate family business:** Squitti's at 319 Bay Street (corner of Bay and Secord) is a well-known specialty department store and gallery -- still operating. Robert's Plumbing and Squitti's store are distinct businesses owned by different family members but share the same Secord Street neighbourhood heritage.

- Affordable pricing

**Sample positive reviews:**

**Negative themes (CRITICAL -- recurring pattern):**
- **Phone responsiveness** is the #1 complaint -- "Called 6 times and left six messages. No calls."
- Rude front desk/phone answering
- Repeat visits without fixing the issue ("attempted to fix furnace 4 times and it never stays fixed")
- Billing disputes (surprise invoices)
- Unethical work allegations ("removing tenant showers to get a paycheck")

**BBB complaint highlights:**
- Customer telephoned for a week straight, only got answering machine each time
- Another customer requested furnace service in September 2024, never got a callback, unresolved by February 2025

> **Key Insight:** The service technicians get good reviews. The front office and follow-up get terrible reviews. This is an operations/systems problem, not a skills problem. A CRM, online booking, and review management strategy would transform this business.

## Outreach Angle

### The Hook

Robert's Plumbing has 69 years of trust in Thunder Bay -- but right now, when someone searches "plumbers Thunder Bay," they find Clow Darling, not Robert's. Their competitor gets 1,400 visits a month from Google. Robert's gets zero. That's not because Clow Darling is better at plumbing -- it's because they have a website and Robert's doesn't.

### Personalization Points

- **The Secord Street legacy** -- "Your family has been on Secord Street since before most Thunder Bay businesses existed. That story deserves to be told online."
- **The 69-year mark** -- "You're approaching 70 years in business. That's extraordinary. But if someone Googles you right now, they find a parked domain."
- **The phone problem** -- Frame it as a SOLUTION: "We noticed some reviews mention difficulty reaching you by phone. An online booking form means customers can request service 24/7 -- even when you're on a job."
- **Lennox dealer status** -- "You carry Lennox furnaces. Lennox has a co-op advertising program that could offset your marketing costs."
- **The competitor gap** -- "Clow Darling ranks #1 for 'HVAC Thunder Bay' and #2 for 'plumbing Thunder Bay.' They've been in business since 1937 -- you've been here since 1957. Same era, same neighbourhood roots. The only difference is they invested in a website."

### Tone for This Lead

Respectful of legacy. Not pushy. This is a family patriarch who has done things one way for decades. The pitch isn't "you're doing it wrong" -- it's "you've built something remarkable, and we can make sure people find it."
