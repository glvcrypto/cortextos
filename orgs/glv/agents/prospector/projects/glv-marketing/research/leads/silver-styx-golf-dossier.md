## Personal Story

Dave Svab is a superintendent-turned-manager -- a greenskeeper at heart who likely built Silver Styx into a legitimate 18-hole championship layout in the Northern Ontario bush. His participation in the GCSAA national tournament in 2017 (competing against top superintendents from across North America) signals someone who takes course maintenance seriously as both a craft and a profession. Multiple reviewers noted the greens were "fabulous," "super fast," and "in great shape" -- the hallmark of a superintendent who pours their skill into the putting surfaces. The disconnect between the praised greens and the criticized fairways suggests a resource/labour constraint rather than a lack of knowledge. Silver Styx sits on Ratter Lake Road in Markstay-Warren, roughly 30 minutes east of Sudbury on Highway 17 -- a beautiful but remote location that makes attracting consistent foot traffic a challenge. The course layout (6,719 yards, slope 130) is genuinely challenging and respected by golfers who have played it. The "Want a Challenge?" TripAdvisor review title captures the course's identity. Tyler McVey's appearance on RocketReach as a superintendent suggests a possible transition in management, or that Dave has passed operational duties forward. Either way, this is a small operation -- RocketReach lists 1 employee.

## 3 Marketing Opportunities

### 1. Website Revival + Direct Booking (IF the course is operating)
**The Pitch:** "Your website is completely down right now -- anyone who Googles 'Silver Styx Golf' and clicks your site gets nothing. Meanwhile, GolfNow is taking a cut of every tee time booked through their platform. A modern one-page site with direct booking, your rates, and a photo gallery would pay for itself in saved commissions within a season."

**What GLV Would Do:** Build a fast, clean WordPress or static site with embedded ChronoGolf/direct booking, rates, photo gallery, course map, and contact info. Mobile-first (golfers search on phones). Add LocalBusiness schema markup and connect to GBP.

**Estimated Impact:** Recover 100% of lost direct traffic. Eliminate GolfNow commission on direct bookings ($3-5 per round adds up fast over a season).

### 2. Google Business Profile Recovery + Review Management
**The Pitch:** "Right now, someone searching for your course on Google might see 'permanently closed.' There is a review from 2024 saying your course is an overgrown field -- and no response from you. Every golfer who reads that chooses somewhere else. We can fix the GBP, respond to reviews, and start building your Google presence so the Map Pack works FOR you, not against you."

**What GLV Would Do:** Claim/verify GBP, mark as operational (if true), upload photos, add posts, set hours, respond to every review. Implement a review collection system (QR code at pro shop / 19th hole). Target "golf near Sudbury" and "golf courses Markstay" in GBP categories.

**Estimated Impact:** GBP is the #1 discovery channel for local golf. A healthy profile with 20+ reviews and accurate info could drive 30-50% more walk-in/call-in rounds.

### 3. Seasonal Content + Social Presence
**The Pitch:** "The golf season in Northern Ontario is 5 months. Every week your course is open and not being promoted is lost revenue you cannot recover. A simple social presence -- course condition photos, tournament announcements, early-bird specials -- keeps your course top of mind for the Sudbury golf crowd."

**What GLV Would Do:** Set up or revive Facebook + Instagram. Weekly course condition posts (greens photos -- the course's strongest asset), event promotions, early-bird / twilight specials. Create 2-3 blog posts targeting "best golf courses near Sudbury," "affordable golf Northern Ontario," and "challenging golf courses Ontario."

**Estimated Impact:** Golf is seasonal and social. One viral course-condition photo can fill a weekend. Consistent posting keeps Silver Styx in the conversation alongside Golf Sudbury, Timberwolf, and Idylwylde.
