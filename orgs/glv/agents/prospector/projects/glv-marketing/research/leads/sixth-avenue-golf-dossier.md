## Personal Story

Rod and Mary Jouppi built Sixth Avenue Golf & Country Club from scratch in 1997 on an 80-acre property in Lively, just outside Sudbury. This was never a typical golf course — Rod, a veterinarian of 40+ years, was acutely aware of the negative environmental impact of conventional golf course maintenance and set out to prove it could be done differently. The course operated 90% chemical-free, earned Audubon Cooperative Sanctuary Program certification, and became a genuine haven for at-risk wildlife including turtles, foxes, and birds. Monica Jouppi, the general manager, described it as "a great place to see and hear wildlife."

Rod's environmental passion extended beyond golf. He founded Wild at Heart, a wildlife rehabilitation centre on land he donated adjacent to his veterinary practice. The centre treated roughly 1,000 animals per year and attracted national media attention (CBC featured his plan for public viewing windows during animal surgeries). Wild at Heart closed in 2019 due to unsustainable funding, with Rod transferring assets to Pet Save.

The golf course also hosted local food growers' markets and became known for its restaurant (fish and chips praised in reviews), wedding venue (tent accommodating 250 guests with full bar service), and as a peaceful retreat from the mining-town bustle. Multiple TripAdvisor reviews praise weddings held there.

By approximately 2020-2021, the golf course appears to have ceased operations. Rod publicly stated a community park would be "the best use of this property" and listed it for ~$2M. The City of Greater Sudbury explored purchasing it with funds from the $4M Meatbird Lake Park sale to Vale, but ultimately chose Anderson Farm Museum instead (Nov 2025 decision). The property's current status is uncertain.
