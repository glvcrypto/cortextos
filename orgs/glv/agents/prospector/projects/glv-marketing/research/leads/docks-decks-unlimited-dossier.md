## Personal Story

Roger Gainer has been running Docks & Decks Unlimited Inc. since 1987 — that is 39 years of building docks and decks across Northern Ontario. He founded the company in Sudbury and built it into the region's go-to outdoor construction firm, covering a massive territory from Sault Ste. Marie in the west to North Bay in the east, and up to Timmins in the north. The company's tagline — "You Should See What We Saw" — hints at a tradesperson's pride in finished work.

The Kinsmen connection is the most telling detail about Roger's character. Docks & Decks has been the deck builder of choice for the Sudbury Kinsmen Home Sweepstakes Draw since 1990 — 36 consecutive years of building the showcase deck for Sudbury's biggest charitable home lottery. The Kinsmen Club has given back over $15 million to the Sudbury community through this program, and Roger's been part of it for nearly the entire run. This is not a man chasing marketing exposure — this is a craftsman who has earned his reputation one deck at a time through community involvement and word of mouth.

With approximately 8 employees, the company is a proper small business — not a one-man show, but not a corporate operation either. Facebook posts from 2021-2023 show recurring hiring notices for dock installation crews, suggesting seasonal demand keeps them busy. A May 2023 post asked for "another dock installation crew" with their own truck, indicating subcontractor relationships. An April 2023 post referenced a "60th anniversary contest" with three $20,000 prizes — likely a Castle Building Centres promotion rather than the company's own anniversary (the business is 39, not 60).

Roger appears to be the kind of owner who shows up on job sites, builds relationships with suppliers, and lets referrals carry the business. He has been doing this longer than most of his competitors have existed. The challenge is that his marketing has not evolved with him.

## Opportunities Identified

### 1. Website Rebuild + Cottage Country SEO Strategy

The current website is beyond repair — it needs a complete rebuild. A modern, mobile-first site with service-area landing pages would unlock the cottage country audience that currently cannot find Docks & Decks online.

**Target keywords (from Semrush data):**
- "dock builders" (390/mo, currently pos 9-10 — realistic to push to top 5)
- "docks and decks sudbury" (50/mo, already #1 — protect and expand)
- "saunas sudbury ontario" (110/mo, pos 5 — can reach #1-3)
- "deck builder sudbury" (70/mo combined variants — pos 7, should be #1)
- "dock installation French River" / "dock builders Manitoulin" / "waterfront decks Lake Nipissing" — ZERO competition, zero current ranking

The 208 referring domains and 2,010 backlinks represent real link equity. A proper site rebuild would inherit this authority and see immediate ranking improvements. Current authority score is 8 — a rebuilt site with content strategy could realistically reach 15-20 within 6 months.

**Revenue impact:** Cottage dock installations are $5,000-$25,000+ projects. Capturing even 5 additional cottage-country leads per season through SEO would represent $25,000-$125,000 in new revenue.

### 2. Review Generation + GBP Overhaul

5 reviews at 3.0 stars after 39 years is the single most damaging metric for this business. A review generation campaign is the fastest-impact initiative available.

**Actionable plan:**
- Systematic post-job review requests (SMS/email with direct Google review link)
- QR code on invoices and business cards linking to Google review
- GBP optimization: weekly Google Posts showcasing completed projects, service listings, Q&A
- Target: 50+ reviews within 6 months, 100+ within 12 months
- Goal: push rating from 3.0 to 4.5+ (likely achievable — the 3.0 is based on only 5 reviews, so volume will naturally correct)

**Why this matters:** 91% of homeowners check reviews. A 3.0-star rating with 5 reviews loses to any competitor with 4.0+ and 20+ reviews. Roger's 39-year reputation is not reflected online — fixing this is the highest-ROI move available.

### 3. Visual Portfolio + Instagram Launch

Decks and docks are inherently visual products. Every completed project is a portfolio piece, yet Docks & Decks has:
- 0 Houzz project photos
- No Instagram account
- Facebook content focused on hiring, not showcase

**Actionable plan:**
- Launch Instagram account with professional photos of past and current projects
- Before/after sequences of dock installations, deck builds, cottage waterfront transformations
- Tag locations (French River, Lake Nipissing, Manitoulin Island) to reach cottage owners
- Cross-post to Facebook with consistent branding
- Upload project photos to Houzz profile (0 currently — easy differentiation)
- Embed Instagram feed on rebuilt website

**Why this matters:** Cottage owners browsing Instagram and searching hashtags like #CottageLife, #MuskokaLiving, #FrenchRiverCottage are a direct audience for waterfront dock and deck services. This audience skews higher income and makes buying decisions based on visual quality.

## Red Flags

- **3.0-star Google rating:** Only 5 reviews, but the low score suggests at least one or two negative reviews are pulling the average down. Need to understand if there are legitimate service complaints or just low sample size. A 3.0 rating actively repels potential customers.
- **Three different addresses in circulation:** 1794 Pioneer Rd (website), 1332 Pioneer Rd (Ontario Business Dir office), 2421 Desloges Rd (Ontario Business Dir mailing). This NAP inconsistency confuses Google and hurts local SEO rankings.
- **Website last modified March 2020:** Six years without a website update. Google+ link still active. Copyright 2014. This signals either a business in maintenance mode or an owner who does not prioritize digital presence.
- **Facebook activity dried up mid-2023:** Most recent visible posts are from mid-2023. The "60th anniversary contest" (April 2023) and hiring posts suggest the business was active, but the social silence since then is concerning.
- **"Over twenty five years" not updated:** The website still says 25+ years when the actual figure is 39. Small detail, but it reinforces the sense that the online presence is neglected.
- **Crates/pallets business:** Manufacturing shipping crates and pallets is a completely different market from residential deck/dock construction. While it diversifies revenue, it muddies the brand online and could confuse homeowners visiting the site.
- **Owner age consideration:** If Roger founded the business in 1987 (likely in his 20s-30s), he may be in his 60s-70s now. Outreach should be sensitive to potential succession planning or wind-down considerations. The lack of digital investment could signal retirement-mode thinking.

The goal is not to change what Docks & Decks is — it is to make sure the internet reflects the business Roger actually built.
