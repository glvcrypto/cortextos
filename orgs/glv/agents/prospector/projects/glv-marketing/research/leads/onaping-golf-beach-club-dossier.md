## Personal Story

The Onaping Golf & Beach Club is one of Northern Ontario's most genuinely community-built institutions. In the mid-1950s, Falconbridge Nickel Mines Limited owned large tracts of land on the northeast shore of Windy Lake, previously held by the Fish and Hunt Club. The government expropriated 102 acres for what became Windy Lake Provincial Park, but the remaining land became the foundation for the golf course.

Around 1955, a work party of roughly 25 volunteers began clearing land — cutting, brushing, and burning on the east side of the area below what is now the Windy Lake Park main gate. Chain saws were becoming more available, and the community effort grew. A letter was written to H.J. Fraser at Falconbridge Nickel Mines Ltd. requesting a $16,000 loan to build a clubhouse and complete the proposed nine holes. The money was granted, the clubhouse site was chosen, and by summer 1957, golfing began at the Onaping Golf and Beach Club — built almost entirely by voluntary labour.

Nearly 70 years later, the course is now owned by Cindy Cornett, who also runs Good-Cor Mining Services Ltd., a mine design planning firm based in nearby Chelmsford. The dual-business ownership is telling — this is not a commercially-driven golf resort. It is a community institution on Windy Lake that happens to also have a golf course, a private beach, a sauna, a cabin rental, a fully licensed clubhouse, Sunday brunch service, night golf events, fireworks displays, and league nights. The green fees ($12–$23) are among the lowest in the Greater Sudbury region, reinforcing its identity as accessible, community-first recreation.

## Red Flags

- **Website is completely down (HTTP 500):** This is the biggest red flag. It suggests neglected hosting, expired SSL, or abandoned web maintenance. If the owner doesn't know it's down, that indicates zero monitoring. If they know and haven't fixed it, it indicates limited technical capacity or budget constraints.
- **Dual-business owner:** Cindy Cornett runs a mining services company alongside the golf course. This likely means the golf course gets secondary attention and limited marketing budget. The course may operate more as a community asset than a profit-driven business.
- **Two domains (golfwindy.com / golfonaping.ca):** Domain consolidation needed. Old domain listings create NAP confusion and split any link equity.
- **Address inconsistency:** 725 Lochhead Dr (most sources) vs. 10 Copper St (GolfPass). Needs cleanup.
- **Very low green fees ($12–$23):** While great for accessibility, this suggests thin margins. Marketing spend may be seen as unaffordable unless framed as ROI on membership growth and event bookings (cabin, brunch, tournaments).
- **Seasonal operation (April–October):** ~6-month revenue window. Marketing investment needs to show returns quickly within the season.
- **Community/volunteer heritage:** The course was literally built by volunteers. There may be resistance to "commercializing" it with marketing — approach must respect the community identity.
