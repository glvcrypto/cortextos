## Personal Story

Chris "Mav" MacDonald started his roofing career **over 30 years ago** — meaning he was in the trade since the early-to-mid 1990s before founding the company in 1999. He built the business from scratch with a small crew of 5 employees and grew it into one of the largest commercial roofing operations in the Algoma region with 20 full-time staff.

**Key values that define him:**
- **Anti-subcontracting philosophy:** He deliberately chose to hire full-time employees rather than use subcontractors, believing this ensures quality and protects clients from "negative third-party experiences." This is a strong quality-first, reputation-driven mindset.
- **Client satisfaction over profit:** His own words: "Since we work in an industry that is based mostly on referrals and tendered projects, we value client satisfaction above all else."
- **Employee loyalty as a competitive advantage:** He attributes the company's success to employee loyalty driving "a first-class work ethic and commitment to the client."

**The "& Son" element:** The company name suggests family succession planning. While the specific son isn't clearly identified on the website, the "Maverick" name likely refers to his son (the business name could be a play on "Mav" + his son). This family legacy angle is significant — it signals long-term thinking and a business built to last.

**Personality read:** Mav is a hands-on tradesman who values quality, loyalty, and reputation above growth-at-all-costs. He's likely skeptical of slick marketing pitches but would respond to someone who understands the trades and speaks his language.

## Opportunities Identified

### 1. SEO — Massive Untapped Potential
- Zero organic traffic for a 26-year-old company is a huge missed opportunity.
- Commercial roofing keywords in Northern Ontario are low competition — easy wins.
- Target keywords: "commercial roofing Sault Ste Marie," "industrial roof replacement Algoma," "metal siding contractor Ontario," "flat roof repair SSM."
- A basic SEO strategy with 6-8 service pages and a blog could dominate local commercial roofing search within 3-6 months.

### 2. Content Marketing — Authority Building
- No blog means no thought leadership despite 30+ years of expertise.
- Blog topics that write themselves: roof maintenance tips for commercial property managers, seasonal roofing checklists, case studies of institutional projects (school boards already provide testimonials).
- Content would feed both SEO and tender/RFP credibility.

### 3. Google Business Profile Optimization
- A commercial roofer with school board testimonials should be showcasing those prominently.
- GBP posts, photos of projects, and review generation would boost local visibility.

### 4. Social Media — Project Showcasing
- Facebook page exists but likely underutilized.
- Commercial roofing projects are visually compelling — drone shots, before/after, time-lapses.
- LinkedIn would be ideal for reaching commercial property managers and institutional buyers.

### 5. Tender/RFP Support
- Mav explicitly mentions the business runs on "referrals and tendered projects."
- A polished digital presence strengthens tender applications (evaluators Google you).
- Case study pages and a professional portfolio would differentiate from competitors.
