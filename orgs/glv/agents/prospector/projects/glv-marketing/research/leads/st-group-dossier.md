## Personal Story

The classic Northern Ontario entrepreneurship narrative. Two licensed electricians — Andrew Sarlo and Dennis Tatasciore — started with "2 guys and a truck" in 1984. Over 40 years they built S.&T. into the Sault's dominant multi-trade contractor through strategic acquisitions:

- **1996:** Added a technologies division (structured cabling, security), winning contracts with IBM, RBC, Bell Canada
- **1998:** Expanded into Northern Michigan with S.&T. Electrical International
- **2003:** Acquired a local mechanical company, adding plumbing/HVAC/sheet metal — rebranded as "S.&T. Group"
- **2013:** Created S.&T. Industrial Inc. to service Algoma Steel
- **2019:** Hit 1 million man-hours without a lost-time incident (second time achieving this)
- **2022:** Won the R.H. Carroll Safety Award for excellence in electrical contracting
- **2024:** Sold to TEAM Group (Markham) but founders stayed on

They are deeply community-rooted — the company runs an annual "12 Days of Kindness" program each December. They are members of the Sault Ste. Marie Construction Association.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Reputation Management** — A 40-year company with a 1.7 TrustedPros rating and virtually no Google reviews is a massive disconnect. A review generation campaign could transform their online presence.

2. **Content Marketing / SEO** — Zero blog content for a company with 40 years of stories, safety awards, community involvement, and multi-trade expertise. Huge untapped potential for local SEO dominance in trades keywords.

3. **Lead Generation Modernization** — Moving beyond phone/email to online booking, quote request forms, and chat would capture leads they are currently losing to competitors with better digital funnels.

4. **Google Business Profile Optimization** — With their service breadth (electrical + HVAC + plumbing + security + general contracting), a properly optimized GBP could rank for dozens of local service queries.

5. **Post-Acquisition Brand Story** — The TEAM Group acquisition is a story that needs to be told publicly to reassure existing customers and attract new ones. Content about "same local team, expanded capabilities" would be valuable.

## Red Flags

1. **Recently Acquired (Sept 2024)** — TEAM Group (Markham) now owns S.&T. Marketing budget decisions may be made at the corporate level, not locally. Sarlo and Tatasciore may have reduced authority over spending.

2. **May Already Have Corporate Marketing** — TEAM Group likely has its own marketing team or agency. S.&T. may be folded into a corporate marketing strategy that GLV cannot influence.

3. **Poor Employer Reviews** — "Extremely toxic work environment" on Indeed suggests internal culture issues. Marketing improvements cannot fix operational problems, and unhappy employees can undermine marketing efforts.

4. **Low Review Scores** — The 1.7 TrustedPros rating, while based on only 2 reviews, could indicate genuine service quality issues or a customer satisfaction gap.

5. **Large Company = Slow Decision Making** — With corporate ownership, multiple divisions, and cross-border operations, getting a marketing contract signed could take months.

6. **They Already Have a Marketing Coordinator** — Jillian Church holds a Marketing and Communications role, meaning they have in-house marketing capacity. GLV would need to position as a specialist complement, not a replacement.
