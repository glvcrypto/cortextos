**The Algoma cottage market context:**
The Algoma District is Lake Huron shoreline country — Mississagi Strait, Blind River corridor, and the north shore communities. The cottage market here is the affordable cousin of Muskoka: average Lake Huron cottage selling price was $672,690 in Q1 2024. These are not shacks — they are properties owned by Sault Ste. Marie professionals, Sudbury families, and southern Ontario buyers priced out of Muskoka. The buyer profile (Gen X families, remote-work professionals) is exactly the demographic that installs EV chargers, upgrades panels for hot tubs, and pays for quality electrical work without blinking.

However, there are no project photos, no portfolio, no case studies, and no reviews that confirm the depth of that niche.

**Founding estimate:** 30 years claimed on Yellow Pages = approximately 1994–1996 founding. Pre-internet, pre-GBP, pre-social — entirely built on local reputation.

## Retainer Viability

**Evidence of cottage-grade work:**
- Cottage renovations and hookups explicitly listed as a service — one of only two SSM electricians (of 37 on YP) to list this
- Generator installation listed — cottage staple (power outages are common in Algoma)
- Panel upgrades listed — required for any serious cottage renovation or EV charger install
- 30 years in SSM = has almost certainly done cottage work; SSM is the primary service city for the Algoma shore

**The EV charger angle — why it matters now:**
Level 2 EV charger installation in Ontario runs $2,000–$4,500 per install (including panel assessment, permit, materials, labor). Cottage owners with EVs need chargers at the cottage — this is already happening. Sovereign Electric in SSM has already added "EV Charger Installations" to their listing. The demand is arriving and no cottage-focused electrician in SSM has staked a content/SEO position on it.

**Ticket size validation:**
- Panel upgrade (100A to 200A): $3,500–$5,000 in Ontario
- EV charger + panel upgrade combo: $5,000–$8,000+
- Full cottage rewire/reno: $8,000–$30,000+
- Generator install: $5,000–$15,000

Cottage clients are discretionary high-spend. The Algoma Lake Huron market's average cottage price of $672K means buyers have equity and income to support $5K–$30K electrical work. If Wayne does 8–12 cottage jobs per season (May–October), the annual revenue from that segment alone could be $80K–$200K+ — making a $2K/month retainer ($24K/year) a rational 12–30% marketing investment in a proven revenue stream.

**Red flag on scale:** Wayne's operation almost certainly runs lean — likely 1–3 people including himself. He may not have capacity to absorb a flood of new leads without stretching. The pitch needs to position GLV as helping him pick better clients, not just generate more volume.

**Seasonality risk:** Cottage electrical work is April–October. January–March is slow. A retainer structure should acknowledge this — consider a 10-month structure or a reduced-rate winter hold.

## Red Flags

**1. Likely solo or micro-crew operator.** 70 Idaho Drive is a residential address — this is a home-based business. No commercial premises, no indication of office staff. Wayne may be a one-person operation with a helper. If so, lead volume needs to be calibrated — too many leads too fast burns a small operator and kills the relationship.

**2. Hours listed as "Closed" on all days.** The SaultSteMarieDirect.ca listing shows all hours as closed. This is either a data entry artifact or the listing is no longer actively maintained. Either way, it signals low engagement with digital presence — which is the norm for a referral-only tradesperson, not necessarily a business in trouble.

**3. No photos, no portfolio, no social proof.** Without project photos, it is harder to build compelling content. Aiden needs to ask at the meeting: does Wayne have any job photos on his phone? Even 10 decent iPhone shots of a panel upgrade or cottage connection are enough to launch a content calendar.

**4. 30 years experience = possible retirement horizon.** A 50-something or 60-something sole operator may not be thinking about growth — he may be thinking about winding down. If that is the vibe in the meeting, pivot the pitch: "GLV helps you build the brand so the business has value when you're ready to hand it off or sell it."

**5. Seasonal ceiling.** Cottage electrical is May–October. Winter months will be slow. $2K/month retainer needs to be justified year-round — residential SSM work (panel upgrades, basement renos, code compliance) fills the off-season gap, but Aiden should probe whether Wayne actively markets to year-round residential clients or coasts on referrals.
