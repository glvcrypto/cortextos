The Sudbury HVAC/plumbing market rewards:
1. **Emergency responsiveness** (74% expect service within 24 hours) -- Nauss has no emergency landing pages
2. **Trust through reviews** (90% check online before calling) -- Nauss's 3.3 stars actively repel customers
3. **Educational content** (heat pump confusion, rebate programs) -- Nauss has zero blog content
4. **Local trust signals** (family-owned, community roots) -- Nauss has receivership baggage and inconsistent listings

The business has the service breadth to compete with 669 Heat and One Source Home Services, but zero digital infrastructure to capture that demand. Every month without action, 669 Heat (2,840+ reviews) and Campeau (1,120+ reviews) widen the gap.

## Three Opportunities

### 1. Website Security + SEO Foundation (URGENT)
**The hacked casino content must be removed immediately.** This is not optional -- it is actively poisoning the domain in Google's eyes. Once cleaned:
- Proper service pages for each trade (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, pools)
- Emergency service landing pages targeting "emergency plumber sudbury," "emergency furnace repair sudbury" (KD 0)
- Quick-win keyword pages: "heat pump sudbury" (50 vol, KD 8), "furnace installation sudbury" (40 vol, KD 0), "sudbury heating and cooling" (70 vol, KD 0)
- **Realistic outcome:** 200-400 organic visits/month within 6-12 months from a clean, optimised site

### 2. Reputation Recovery Program (HIGH IMPACT)
**3.3 stars to 4.5+ stars is achievable within 6-12 months** with a systematic review generation program:
- Automated post-service review requests (text/email)
- Google review response strategy (respond to ALL reviews, especially negative ones -- shows care)
- Showcase positive reviews on the new site
- **Why it matters:** In this niche, the difference between 3.3 and 4.5 stars is the difference between being skipped and being called. With only ~50 reviews, even 30-40 new 5-star reviews would shift the average dramatically.

### 3. Multi-Trade Content Strategy
Nauss is one of the few Sudbury businesses offering plumbing + HVAC + electrical + pools under one roof. This is a genuine competitive advantage that is completely unmarked:
- "One-call" positioning: one contractor for your entire home
- Cross-service content: "Should you upgrade your furnace and water heater at the same time?"
- Pool + HVAC seasonal content (pool heater service is already their best-ranking niche at #12-13)
- **Total addressable volume:** ~3,400/month across all four trades combined in Sudbury

## Outreach Recommendation

**Approach with caution but genuine value.** This is not a standard "you need more SEO" pitch. The lead-in must acknowledge the website security issue -- it is visible, verifiable, and urgent. That creates a reason to reach out that feels helpful rather than salesy.

**Recommended angle:** "We noticed your website has been compromised with injected casino spam content. This is actively hurting your Google rankings for Sudbury plumbing and HVAC searches. We help Northern Ontario trades businesses fix exactly this kind of problem."

**Do NOT mention:** The receivership, the bankruptcy, the financial history. Let Denis bring that up if relevant.

**Ideal first step:** A free, no-obligation website security scan showing the specific compromised pages and their impact on rankings. This positions GLV as the expert who found the problem, not the salesperson who wants money.

**Budget sensitivity:** Given the financial history, consider offering a smaller entry point (website cleanup + basic SEO audit as a one-time project, $1,500-$2,500) rather than leading with a $2K/mo retainer. Let the results speak and upsell from there.
