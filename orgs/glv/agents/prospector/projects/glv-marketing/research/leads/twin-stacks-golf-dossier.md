## Personal Story

Twin Stacks Golf Club sits on one of Northern Ontario's most remarkable reclamation stories. The property was once the Coniston Smelter — operated by the Mond Nickel Company starting in 1913, later absorbed by International Nickel Company (Inco) in 1929. For decades, the smelter stripped the surrounding landscape bare, removing all trees and vegetation to feed roast beds. In 1972, Inco shut the smelter down due to environmental restrictions. The site sat desolate until it was transformed into a lush 9-hole golf course that opened in 1998, giving new life to Coniston's iconic twin smokestacks — which still stand as a backdrop to the course.

Philippe Nave took ownership roughly a decade ago and has poured himself into the operation. He runs the course, manages the clubhouse restaurant (described as a "hidden gem" by Sudbury.com — serving homemade food including lasagna, pasta, burgers, porchetta sandwiches, chicken wings, and full breakfast/lunch service 7 AM - 3 PM daily), and oversees league nights. In 2025, he made a significant bet on the future by partnering with Nickel City Disc Golf (NCDG) to build an 18-hole championship disc golf course on the property, designed by PDGA professionals Thomas Hums and Thomas Gilbert. The disc golf course opened May 12, 2025, and Twin Stacks hosted "The Nickel City Open" — a PDGA-sanctioned tournament.

Philippe is a tradesperson-turned-entrepreneur — the type who would rather be out on the grounds than behind a desk. His restaurant serves all homemade food. He keeps green fees affordable ($23 for 9 holes). He is clearly invested in Coniston's identity and the environmental rebirth story. This is someone who cares about legacy, not just revenue — which makes him both an ideal client (emotionally connected to the business) and a harder sell (may view marketing as an unnecessary expense for a business that "runs on word of mouth").

## Three Opportunities

### 1. Own the Reclamation Story — Content + Local SEO ($$$)
**What:** Create a dedicated "Our Story" page (2,000+ words) telling the full Mond Nickel → Inco smelter → 1972 closure → 1998 golf course rebirth narrative. Add historical photos, a timeline, embed the CBC coverage. Create 5-10 blog posts around the history, the environmental recovery, "things to do in Coniston," and seasonal course guides.

**Why it works:** This story is genuinely remarkable — it is the kind of content that earns backlinks from tourism sites, local media, and environmental publications. It differentiates Twin Stacks from every other golf course in Ontario. It creates content depth that moves non-branded keywords from page 2 to page 1. "Golf course on reclaimed mine site" is a story that CBC, Sudbury.com, and TripAdvisor have already told — Twin Stacks should be the primary source.

**Impact:** Could move "golf courses in sudbury" from position 7 to top 3. Creates linkable assets. Supports tourism directory listings. Gives media a reason to feature Twin Stacks.

### 2. Disc Golf Digital Presence — Capture a Growing Niche ($$$)
**What:** Build a dedicated /disc-golf section on twinstacks.ca with course map, pricing ($15 daily, $300 annual), Monday league info, UDisc embed, photo gallery, and tournament calendar. Optimize for "disc golf Sudbury," "disc golf courses near me," and "disc golf Coniston." Create a blog series: "beginner's guide to disc golf," "Twin Stacks disc golf course review," "disc golf tournaments Sudbury 2026."

**Why it works:** Disc golf is exploding in popularity. Twin Stacks has a PDGA-sanctioned championship course but zero digital footprint on their own domain. Currently, every "disc golf Sudbury" search drives traffic to nickelcitydiscgolf.com or UDisc — not twinstacks.ca. This is free revenue being gifted to third-party platforms. The disc golf audience skews younger (25-40), digitally savvy, and active on social media — perfect for building an Instagram presence.

**Impact:** Opens an entirely new keyword cluster. Attracts a younger demographic. Creates year-round content opportunities. Cross-sells restaurant and traditional golf.

### 3. Restaurant + Visual Content Strategy — Instagram + Google ($$$)
**What:** Create a /restaurant page on the website with menu, hours, and food photography. Launch an Instagram account featuring course beauty shots (the twin stacks backdrop is iconic), food photos, league highlights, and disc golf action. Implement a UGC hashtag (#TwinStacksGolf). Optimize GBP with Google Posts, menu, and seasonal updates.

**Why it works:** The restaurant was featured by Sudbury.com as a "hidden gem" — but it is hidden because it has no online presence. Golf courses are inherently visual businesses, and Twin Stacks has one of the most photogenic backdrops in Northern Ontario (literal industrial smokestacks rising behind manicured fairways). Instagram would cost nothing but time and could drive significant awareness among the 25-45 demographic.

**Impact:** Drives restaurant revenue (currently breakfast/lunch, could expand). Creates shareable content that earns organic reach. Builds the visual brand. Supports GBP rankings through activity signals.

**Best channel:** Phone call to the pro shop (705-694-2131) or email to info@twinstacks.ca. Philippe is an owner-operator — he is likely answering the phone during business hours, especially early season (April-May).
