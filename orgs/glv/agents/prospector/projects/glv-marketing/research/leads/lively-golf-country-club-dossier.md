## Personal Story

Lively Golf & Country Club is a genuine community institution, born from volunteer grit in Northern Ontario mining country. In the late 1950s, a group of Lively residents associated with the Lively Athletic Association obtained permission from Inco (now Vale) to build a par-3 course on a fire break. The course was constructed entirely by volunteers and opened for play in late 1959. During the early years, members ran the course with practically no paid staff, gradually clearing land and extending holes.

In August 1970, a tornado tore through Lively, destroying all buildings, equipment, and many mature trees. Rather than folding, the membership rallied — 250 members each contributed $100 to help rebuild. A new clubhouse was erected off Sixth Avenue, and in 1972 the club was reorganized and renamed the Lively Golf & Country Club. In 1977, a Wintario grant funded construction of the back nine, which opened in August 1978.

Through a series of financial transactions with Inco/Vale over the decades, LGCC eventually acquired full ownership of its buildings and property. Today it operates as a family-oriented, not-for-profit facility governed by a 9-member Board of Directors, dedicated to providing quality golf at reasonable rates.

Mark Taylor has served as General Manager for over 14 years — meaning he has steered the club through more than half its modern history. The club describes itself as "Sudbury's Finest Golf" and emphasizes its family-friendly environment, lush fairways, and consistent greens. The restaurant is well-regarded (reviews praise "some of the best Caesars in the city"), and the venue hosts weddings, tournaments, and community events.

This is a heritage club that has survived a tornado, corporate ownership transitions, and 67 Northern Ontario winters. Its identity is rooted in community, resilience, and affordability — values that should be front and centre in its marketing but are currently buried behind a broken Wix website.

## Opportunities Identified

### 1. Emergency Website Replacement (Immediate Revenue Protection)

The current Wix site is actively losing the club money. Confirmed issues:
- **SEO spam injection** — "slot gacor" gambling pages indexed under livelygolf.com
- **JavaScript redirects** — visitors being sent to Idylwylde, Onaping Golf, Google Search, and about:blank
- **Outdated content** — 2020 green fees, 2022 membership form, no current rates

A professional WordPress site (matching or exceeding Golf Sudbury's standard) with:
- Current 2026 rates and membership info prominently displayed
- Integrated tee time booking (not fragmented across two platforms)
- Dedicated pages: Memberships, Green Fees, Events/Weddings, Restaurant, Course History, Pro Shop
- Mobile-optimized booking flow (critical for on-the-go golfers)
- Blog/news section for tournament recaps, course conditions, seasonal content

**Revenue impact:** Even modest — if the redirects are losing 5 tee times per week at ~$50/round during a 26-week season, that's $6,500+ in directly lost green fee revenue, not counting membership inquiries and event bookings that bounce.

### 2. Local SEO + GBP Domination (Search Visibility)

Golf-related searches in Greater Sudbury total ~13,800/month. LGCC captures almost none of this traffic. Quick wins:

- **Claim and optimize Google Business Profile** — correct postal code, add photos, enable Google Posts, populate services, respond to reviews
- **Standardize NAP** — fix "Sixth Avenue Golf & Country Club" on TripAdvisor, unify name across all 9+ directory listings
- **Create local landing pages** — "Golf Course in Lively," "Golf Near Sudbury," "Affordable Golf Greater Sudbury"
- **Target low-competition keywords** — "golf course lively ontario," "public golf sudbury," "cheap golf sudbury," "golf lessons sudbury," "wedding venue sudbury"
- **Seasonal content** — "Best Time to Golf in Sudbury," "Golf Season Opening Sudbury 2026," course condition updates

With Golf Sudbury at ~2,800/mo traffic, even capturing 10-15% of the total golf search market (1,400-2,000 visits/mo) would represent a transformational increase from the current near-zero baseline.

### 3. Heritage Storytelling + Event Marketing (Differentiation)

LGCC's story is unique in the Sudbury golf market — no other course was volunteer-built, survived a tornado, and gained independence from a mining company. This is content gold:

- **"Our Story" page** — the full history with photos from the 1959 founding, 1970 tornado, 1978 back nine opening
- **Community identity content** — member spotlights, junior program features, tournament traditions
- **Event/Wedding showcase** — professional photos, testimonials, packages, capacity info. The restaurant is praised in reviews but invisible online.
- **Email marketing** — seasonal membership renewal reminders, early bird green fee offers, event invitations. Build a list from every website visitor, tee time booker, and event inquiry.

This positions LGCC as the *community* course vs. Golf Sudbury's *corporate* network — a genuine competitive advantage in a Northern Ontario market that values local over chain.

## Red Flags

- **Not-for-profit board structure:** Marketing expenditures require board approval. Mark Taylor is the GM but may not have unilateral budget authority. Pitch needs to be framed as a necessary operational expense (website is broken, losing revenue) rather than a discretionary marketing investment. The board meets periodically — decision timelines may be longer than owner-operated businesses.
- **Website compromise severity unknown:** The spam injection and redirects could indicate a deeper compromise of the Wix account. If login credentials are compromised, migration to a new platform is the only safe option (which works in our favour, but adds urgency to the conversation).
- **Seasonal revenue model:** 6-month operating season means cash flow is concentrated May-October. Marketing spend needs to align with revenue timing. Monthly retainer may need seasonal adjustment or front-loaded investment pre-season with reduced off-season rate.
- **Budget sensitivity:** As a non-profit offering "reasonable rates," LGCC likely operates on thin margins. The pitch must demonstrate clear ROI — not aspirational growth. Frame everything as revenue protection (fixing what's broken) before revenue growth (expanding reach).
- **GolfPass value rating of 1.0/5:** One reviewer noted "no senior discount" — in a market with a large retiree/senior golfing population, this could be a friction point. The Super Senior category (75+) exists but starts at $1,381 — only $343 less than Gold. Perceived value may be an issue beyond marketing.
- **Stale online information:** The most recent publicly available rates are from 2022. If the club hasn't updated its website in 4+ years, there may be limited internal capacity or interest in digital operations. Mark may need hand-holding through the transition.
