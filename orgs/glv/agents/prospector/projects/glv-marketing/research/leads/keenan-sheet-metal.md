### Personal Story / Hook

Steve Kubinec runs a business that has been in Thunder Bay since 1977 -- nearly 50 years. He is personally doing the service calls, personally calling customers back within minutes, personally showing up within hours. Multiple customers mention him by name. He is the kind of tradesperson who does not upsell unnecessary parts.

This is a man who built his reputation on handshake trust and word-of-mouth over five decades. But his digital presence does not reflect that legacy at all. His 49-year reputation is invisible online.

**The core problem:** Steve has built a rock-solid 49-year reputation through personal service, but his digital footprint is paper-thin. ### Misalignment

The fundamental misalignment is between **offline reputation and online visibility**. Steve Kubinec has spent 49 years building a name that means reliability and honesty in Thunder Bay's HVAC market. But online:

1. **His website looks like it was built in 2015 and never touched** -- a Wix template with 9 pages does not communicate "49 years of trust"
2. **He ranks #1 for ONE keyword** but is invisible for the other 20+ high-intent searches his customers are making
3. **No Google reviews visible** -- in a market where Clow Darling has 71 reviews, Steve's personal reputation is not being captured digitally
4. **No content strategy** -- a 49-year-old HVAC company in Northern Ontario should have dozens of pages about winter furnace tips, energy efficiency, Carrier products, emergency repair guides
5. **No social proof loop** -- happy customers (and there are clearly many) have no easy way to leave Google reviews, no Facebook page to share recommendations

### Red Flags

1. **Owner-operator model** -- Steve appears to be doing everything himself (calls, service, billing). He may not have time or budget for marketing.
2. **Shaw email** -- suggests limited tech sophistication. May be resistant to digital change.
3. **Wix platform** -- migrating off Wix is a bigger conversation. Some SEO can be done within Wix, but it has hard ceilings.
4. **No social presence at all** -- building from zero is harder than optimizing existing.
5. **Age of business** -- at 49 years, Steve may be nearing retirement. Need to assess if he is growing or winding down.
6. **Budget sensitivity** -- sole-operator HVAC companies often have tight margins. However, do NOT auto-exclude. Flag budget, but a business this established likely has healthy cash flow.
