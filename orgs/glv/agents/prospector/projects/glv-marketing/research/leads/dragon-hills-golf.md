## Personal Story

Dragon Hills Golf Course is the story of one man's dream built with his own hands. Mike Komar, a golfer frustrated with the difficulty of getting tee times and the quality of other local courses, got the idea to build his own course in 1999. He started on ten acres of land he owned at the corner of Onion Lake and Melbourne Roads, clearing land and building holes year by year. The course operated first as a 3-hole executive course, then 5 holes, growing organically.

Originally called "Komar Greens," the course was renamed Dragon Hills when it reached nine holes -- named for the many elevation changes that resemble the points on a dragon's back. Mike secured the services of Grant Golf and worked closely with architect David Grant to build a course that maximized the natural beauty of the land: rolling hills, 10 ponds, 2 lakes, views of the Sleeping Giant, Lake Superior, and Mount McKay.

In 2018, an 18-hole championship disc golf course was added, co-designed by Cale Leiviska, Chris Wakefield, and Michael Thompson. It became one of the top-rated disc golf courses in Canada (ranked #62 nationally) and hosted the 2023 Canadian National Disc Golf Championships -- a PDGA A-tier event. In 2021, during COVID, Mike pivoted again and created a "Winter Wonderland" -- a 1.4km skating track, 6km of cross-country ski trails, and snowshoe paths, turning the golf course into a year-round recreation venue.

Mike also owned Dragon's Den Sports Bar at the clubhouse and ran for Thunder Bay city council twice (McIntyre Ward, 2010 and 2014) on a platform of job creation and business incentives.

Mike Komar passed away on February 27, 2026, at the age of 71. His celebration of life was held at the Italian Hall and Dragon's Den on March 16. The business appears to be continuing under family management.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **SSL certificate fix + website modernization** (data-backed) -- The broken SSL certificate means every visitor sees a "Not Secure" warning in Chrome. For a business that wants customers to book online, this is immediately trust-destroying. Fixing SSL is a quick win. Rebuilding the outdated ASP site into a modern, mobile-responsive platform would capture more of the ~6,335/mo total golf-related search traffic in Thunder Bay.

2. **Disc golf marketing -- untapped goldmine** (data-backed) -- Dragon Hills has a nationally-ranked disc golf course (4.3 stars, 154 UDisc reviews, ranked #62 in Canada) that hosted the 2023 Canadian National Championships. Yet the website and social media contain virtually zero disc golf content. Disc golf is growing 20-25% per year in Ontario with 160,000+ players. Dedicated disc golf landing pages targeting "disc golf Thunder Bay," "disc golf course near me," and "best disc golf Ontario" could capture a completely new audience. The disc golf demographic (18-40, tech-savvy) is also highly valuable for social media engagement.

3. **Claim and optimize Google Business Profile** (data-backed, VERIFIED) -- The GBP is UNCLAIMED with 35 reviews at 4.2 stars that nobody is managing. Claiming GBP is FREE and immediately gives access to: respond to reviews, add photos, update hours, post offers, and access Google's analytics showing how customers find the business. Whitewater has ~83 reviews at 4.4 stars. Claiming GBP alone could significantly improve map pack visibility for the ~6,335/mo golf searches in Thunder Bay. This is the single highest-ROI action available.

4. **Online tee time booking integration** (observational) -- The GolfNow listing is dormant (no tee times available) and the tee-on.com integration appears inactive. Working online booking would capture impulse bookings and out-of-town golfers who plan digitally. GolfNow alone drives significant booking volume nationally.

5. **Winter Wonderland year-round marketing** (observational) -- The Winter Wonderland (1.4km skating track, 6km cross-country ski trails, snowshoeing) is a brilliant revenue extension but has no SEO content or social media presence. "Cross-country skiing Thunder Bay" and "outdoor skating Thunder Bay" are searchable terms that could drive winter traffic with minimal content investment.

6. **Keyword gap vs Whitewater** (data-backed) -- Dragon Hills has 87 keywords vs Whitewater's 121. More importantly, traffic is ~646/mo vs ~1,778/mo -- a 2.75x gap. Content targeting "Thunder Bay golf course," "golf Thunder Bay," "driving range Thunder Bay," and course-specific terms could close this gap. Whitewater also has a stronger domain due to HTTPS and modern design.

## Red Flags

- **OWNER RECENTLY DECEASED** -- Mike Komar, the founder and sole owner who built Dragon Hills from scratch starting in 1999, passed away on February 27, 2026 (5 weeks ago). The business appears to be continuing (Facebook post March 10 about memberships), but the current decision maker is unknown. Outreach to this business must be handled with extreme care and empathy. A hard sales pitch would be tone-deaf and potentially damaging to GLV's reputation.
- **Business continuity uncertain** -- Dragon Hills was built by Mike Komar as a passion project over 25 years. Without him, it is unclear whether the family intends to keep operating, sell, or wind down. The March 10 membership post suggests operations continue, but long-term plans are unknown.
- **Website is now completely DOWN** -- As of 2026-04-08, both HTTP (ERR_ABORTED) and HTTPS (ERR_CERT_COMMON_NAME_INVALID) fail. The website is inaccessible to anyone. This is worse than the Apr 6 finding when HTTP still worked. Google's "Website" link in the knowledge panel leads to a dead page.
- **GBP UNCLAIMED** -- The Google Business Profile has never been claimed. Nobody is responding to the 35 Google reviews, managing photos, updating hours, or accessing Google's free analytics. This is a massive missed opportunity but also explains why no one has fixed the website link.
- **Outdated technology stack** -- Classic ASP (.asp pages) is a legacy technology from the early 2000s. Finding developers who can maintain ASP sites is increasingly difficult and expensive. A full rebuild would be needed.
- **Seasonal revenue dependency** -- Golf in Thunder Bay runs May through October at best. Winter Wonderland and disc golf extend the season but the business is heavily seasonal. Marketing ROI must be calculated against seasonal revenue windows.
- **Small budget signal** -- As a 9-hole public course built by hand over 20 years by a single owner, this is not a high-budget operation. Monthly retainer pricing would need to be appropriate for the business size.
