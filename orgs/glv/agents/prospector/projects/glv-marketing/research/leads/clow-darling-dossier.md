## Personal Story

Clow Darling has one of the strongest origin stories in Northwestern Ontario's trades sector. In 1937, Bill Darling and Emerson Clow purchased the Thunder Bay (then Fort William) branch of Darling Plumbing & Heating of Winnipeg — and never looked back. Over nearly nine decades they've grown through contracts tied to the Pine Tree Radar network, McKellar Hospital construction in the 1960s, major paper mill work through the 1980s, biomass boiler installations for Abitibi Bowater (2008), high-efficiency boiler conversions for local school boards, and a propane-to-natural-gas conversion for Goldcorp Red Lake (2015).

The business is now employee-owned by eight partners: Barry Leochko, Gerald Kelly, Eric Niemi, John Cava, Mark Sandberg, Mark Lagrange, Andrew Ramsay, and Paul Maydo. This is a management-led partnership structure — no outside corporate ownership. The Ramsay name (Ken + Andrew) suggests a generational handoff from the previous president is either in progress or recently completed.

They hold union signatory status with nine trades unions including Plumbers and Pipe Fitters Local 628 and Air Conditioning Local 787 — a significant credential in the industrial sector.

**Recognition:**
- 2017 Thunder Bay Chamber of Commerce Commitment to Quality Award (23rd Annual Business Excellence Awards)
- 2024 Chamber of Commerce BizAwards nominee — Customer Service category
- 2025 WSIB Health & Safety Excellence Member

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Content Gap for an 89-Year Brand** — The most recognizable mechanical contractor in Northwestern Ontario has zero content marketing presence. No blog, no project showcases, no "ask the expert" columns. A company with Pine Tree Radar contracts, mining industry work, and hospital builds has incredible story material sitting unused. A local SEO content strategy (targeting Thunder Bay HVAC, industrial plumbing NW Ontario, boiler maintenance Thunder Bay, etc.) would dominate thin competition quickly given their domain authority and brand trust.

2. **Review Management + Reputation Infrastructure** — 78 Google reviews is solid but their Birdeye profile is unclaimed, suggesting no centralized reputation management. With 152 employees and hundreds of service calls per year, they likely generate reviews sporadically. A structured review generation and response process would lift them from 4.1 to 4.5+ and make their residential division more competitive against smaller nimble plumbing/HVAC shops that currently look better online.

3. **Residential Division Digital Presence is Weak vs. Their Scale** — The residential HVAC and plumbing division (managed by John Cava) is competing against local shops that are more digitally active. Homeowners searching "Thunder Bay furnace repair" or "Thunder Bay plumber" are not necessarily loyal to the Clow Darling brand the way commercial and industrial clients are. Targeted Google Ads + a residential landing page refresh would capture demand they're currently invisible for.

## Red Flags

- **Size and Internal Capacity:** At 152 employees and eight owner-partners, this is the largest mechanical contractor in NW Ontario. Their accounting is managed by a dedicated Controller (Karen Boote). Operational complexity is high. The question is whether *any* of the eight partners has appetite for a marketing conversation — or whether they've decided marketing is word-of-mouth and B2B relationships.
- **No Confirmed Marketing Agency:** No evidence of a current agency relationship was found. No structured ads, no content, no review management — points to no active agency.
- **Decision-Making May Be Distributed:** Eight partners means no single person owns the marketing budget. Paul Maydo as President is the right first contact, but expect him to defer to the group on any spend commitment.
- **Industrial/Commercial Revenue Dominates:** The high-margin revenue likely comes from industrial and institutional contracts (mining, hospitals, paper mills) that are won through relationship and tender, not marketing. Residential is a smaller share. They may not *feel* the pain of weak digital presence acutely.
- **No Corporate Acquisition Found:** Clean local ownership confirmed. No national chain or private equity involvement detected.

## Recommended Outreach Angle

**Primary Why:** An 89-year-old brand with the largest workforce in NW Ontario's mechanical trades sector has no content presence, no review management system, and no residential digital strategy — while smaller, newer competitors actively market online. They are losing residential calls to inferior operators simply due to digital invisibility.

**Opening Hook:** The 89-year milestone is a gift. Frame it as: "Clow Darling has built more of Thunder Bay's infrastructure than any mechanical contractor in the region's history — but when a homeowner searches for a furnace repair at midnight, they're not seeing your name." Lead with the legacy, then the gap.

**How GLV Helps:** Local SEO + content strategy as the entry point (low-friction, no ad spend required). Add review management as a quick win. Upsell to residential Google Ads once trust is established.
