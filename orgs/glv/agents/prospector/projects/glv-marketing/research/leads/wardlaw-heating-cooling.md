## Personal Story

### The Wardlaw Family Legacy
Brian Wardlaw founded the company in 1981 as a home heating oil delivery service with a gas station. The business evolved over 40 years into a full-service HVAC company. In 1996, they relocated to the current Lake Street location and expanded HVAC offerings.

In 2012, Brian's children — **Greg Wardlaw and Kelly Jackson** — acquired the business and grew the installation and service divisions significantly.

Kelly Jackson started working at the family business right out of high school, managing the oil and service divisions before co-owning with her brother. She is married (30+ years), has children and granddaughters, and enjoys camping and fine dining in the Sault. She was featured in Sault Business Matters' "Sault Smiles" profile series in April 2021.

Greg expressed pride in the community legacy: *"We are proud of the company we have built and our commitment to the community we served for the past 40 years."* He chose Right Time specifically because they *"maintain local brands and reputations."*

### The Sale (April 2022)
Right Time's 14th acquisition. Terms undisclosed. Staff and management stayed on. The Wardlaw brand was retained for local recognition. Right Time is backed by Gryphon Investors (US-based PE firm).

## Red Flags

1. **CORPORATE OWNERSHIP (DISQUALIFYING):** Wardlaw is owned by Right Time Group (PE-backed, 25+ locations, 1,400+ employees). Marketing decisions are made at the corporate level, not locally. GLV cannot sell to a local branch manager — the decision maker is a corporate marketing team in St. Catharines/Hamilton.

2. **No local decision maker identified:** The former owners (Greg & Kelly) sold in 2022. No publicly visible local GM or marketing contact exists for the SSM branch in 2026.

3. **Template website:** The site follows Right Time's corporate template. Changes require corporate approval, not local autonomy.

4. **Budget mismatch:** Right Time likely has an in-house marketing team or agency relationship at the national level. A local agency pitch would be competing against an established corporate vendor.

5. **Some negative review patterns:** Reports of upselling (red-tagging appliances for expensive replacements when cheaper fixes exist) and service delays (10-day part ordering delay). These are manageable reputation issues but suggest corporate cost/efficiency pressures.

## Recommended Outreach Angle

### Verdict: DO NOT PURSUE

Wardlaw Heating & Cooling is **not a viable lead** for GLV Marketing. The business was acquired by Right Time Group of Companies (a PE-backed national HVAC consolidator) in April 2022. Marketing decisions are made at the corporate level, not locally. There is no identifiable local decision maker with authority to engage a local marketing agency.

### If Pursuing Anyway (low probability of success):
- **Primary Why:** Your 40-year local brand is being diluted by corporate-template marketing. A local agency could produce Sault-specific content that actually ranks and resonates.
- **How GLV Helps:** Hyper-local content strategy, local SEO, community-focused social media that a national corporate team can't replicate.
- **Relevant Blog Post:** "Digital Marketing for Contractors" (glvmarketing.ca/blog/digital-marketing-for-contractors)

Focus outreach on **independently owned** HVAC/trades businesses in SSM where the owner IS the decision maker. The original lead list (KC Roofing, DNM Group, Northern Son Contracting) contains better-qualified prospects.
