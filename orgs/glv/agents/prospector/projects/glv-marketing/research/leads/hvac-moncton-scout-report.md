## Niche Research — Moncton HVAC

**Climate and Heating Demand.** Moncton sits in Atlantic Canada's humid continental zone with cold, snowy winters (January averages around -10°C) and warm, humid summers. Heating season runs roughly October through April, creating high demand for furnace repair, heat pump service, and oil-to-electric conversions. Cooling season is shorter but increasingly relevant as summer heat events grow more common. The combination of a long heating season and growing cooling expectation makes full-service HVAC the dominant trade in the market.

**Rebate Programs and Heat Pump Adoption.** New Brunswick runs two major incentive programs generating significant contractor demand. The Enhanced Energy Savings Program (EESP), administered by NB Power, provides free mini-split heat pump upgrades to qualifying households at no charge. The Oil to Heat Pump Affordability (OHPA) Program helps lower-income homeowners (HHI ≤ $70K) switch from oil to heat pumps, with higher-income households able to access up to $15,000 advance funding through the Total Home Energy Savings stream. OHPA registration closes June 30, 2026 — creating a near-term surge of installs. These programs are a direct revenue multiplier for any accredited contractor who can position around them.

**Oil-to-Heat-Pump Conversion Market.** Roughly 25% of Atlantic Canadian homes still heat with oil, compared to ~6% nationally — the highest residential oil dependency in Canada outside rural Quebec. New Brunswick specifically has high oil heating penetration in Moncton's surrounding communities (Riverview, Memramcook, Shediac). Contractors who handle both heat pump install and oil tank removal/decommissioning have meaningful competitive advantage. This conversion pipeline will run at least 5–7 more years as provincial policy presses toward electrification.

**Bilingual Market and SEO Implications.** Moncton became Canada's first officially bilingual city in 2002. Approximately 36% of residents claim French as their mother tongue, and ~46% of the population is functionally bilingual. The city is the commercial hub of the Acadian Peninsula region. This creates a genuine bilingual SEO opportunity that virtually no Moncton HVAC contractor is actively exploiting: French-language service pages for "pompe à chaleur Moncton," "réparation fournaise Moncton," and "climatisation Moncton" have low competition and high relevance. Any contractor who builds bilingual content would gain meaningful organic advantage in the francophone suburbs (Dieppe, Memramcook, Bouctouche).

**Organic Search Landscape.** The "heat pump Moncton" and "HVAC Moncton" SERPs are actively contested but not yet locked up. Greenfoot Energy (Tier C — national chain) dominates with a dedicated city landing page backed by national SEO resources. Behind them, Bob's Cool Air, Classic Heating, TR Mechanical, Ultra Air, and One Touch all appear on page one organically. The notable gap: most competitors have thin or no service-specific pages, no bilingual content, and weak backlink profiles. A contractor with a properly built local SEO foundation could enter the top-3 Map Pack within 3–4 months.
