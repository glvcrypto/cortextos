**Client base characteristics:**
- BAS clients are commercial property owners/managers, institutional facilities (hospitals, universities, labs), municipalities, and industrial operators — not homeowners

## Red Flags

**1. Company may be in transition:** Brian Bonne's LinkedIn lists him as President of E.C.S. Integrated Limited — a separate entity. It is possible Dennco is dormant or semi-retired. Confirm this directly before pitching.

**2. Very lean operation:** If Dennco is a 1–3 person shop with Brian as sole business development, they may not have capacity to take on new clients even if marketing generates leads. Small BAS teams are bottlenecked by licensed installer time, not demand.

**3. Niche is narrow in SSM:** BAS is a specialized market. SSM has perhaps 20–40 buildings complex enough to need what Dennco offers (hospitals, institutions, industrial facilities). This is a referral and relationship business — cold inbound leads from SEO may be secondary to warm referrals from GCs and property managers. Marketing ROI may take 6–12 months to show.

**4. No social proof to amplify:** Zero reviews, no case studies, no project photos publicly available. Month 1 requires building from absolute zero — this is more work than a competitor who already has 20 reviews and a site that just needs updating.

**5. denncoelectric.com domain is listed in YP.ca** — if Brian paid for or set up a website at some point, he knows digital presence matters. The fact that the site lapsed (or was never launched) suggests either a budget constraint, a time constraint, or he just never prioritized it.
