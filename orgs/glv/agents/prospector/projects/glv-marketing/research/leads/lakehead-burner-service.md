## Personal Story

### The Grasley Family — 36 Years of Thunder Bay Heating

Larry and Sandi Grasley have lived in Thunder Bay their entire lives. In 1990, they founded Lakehead Burner Service to provide families with reliable HVAC services — a direct response to Northern Ontario's brutal winters where a failed furnace is not an inconvenience but a genuine emergency.

Starting as an oil burner service company (hence the name "Burner Service"), they built the business into a full-service HVAC contractor handling gas, oil, and electric systems. The company name reflects their roots — oil heating was the backbone of Northern Ontario homes when they started, and they've evolved with the market while staying true to that heritage.

Larry is active on LinkedIn with interest in heat pump technology and HVAC education in northern climates, suggesting he's technically engaged and forward-thinking about industry trends. The couple are also community supporters — Larry and Sandi Grasley appear on the Thunder Bay Symphony Orchestra donor recognition list, indicating genuine community investment beyond just business.

The "Burner" in the name is both their strength (brand recognition, 36 years of trust) and a subtle liability (it signals oil/gas in an era shifting toward heat pumps and electrification). This is a family that built something real through honest work, and they're likely watching competitors with slicker websites and more reviews wonder how to keep up.

## The Big-Picture Misalignment

**Lakehead Burner Service is a 36-year-old trusted HVAC contractor with #2-#5 rankings on several high-value keywords — but they're capturing almost NONE of that traffic because their Wix website can't convert visitors and they have no content strategy to expand their footprint.**

The numbers tell the story:
- They rank #2 for "furnace repair thunder bay" (210 searches/mo) but only capture ~24 total monthly visitors across ALL keywords
- Clow Darling captures 58x more traffic with only 2.8x more keywords
- The gap isn't about keywords — it's about **website quality, content depth, and conversion infrastructure**

This is a business that has earned Google's trust through longevity and consistent NAP citations, but is wasting that trust on a Wix site that search engines can barely render. Their domain has genuine authority (#2 for the highest-value local keyword) but no system to convert that authority into leads.

**The irony:** Larry and Sandi's 36-year reputation is their competitive moat. But in 2026, that moat is being drained by competitors with $5K websites and basic SEO. Clow Darling — founded in 1937, also a legacy business — invested in a proper WordPress site with team pages, service pages, and content. The result: 58x more organic traffic.

## Opportunities with Numbers

### 1. Website Rebuild (WordPress) — Est. +300-500 visits/mo
Replace Wix with a fast, SEO-optimised WordPress site. Their existing #2-#5 rankings would immediately generate more clicks with proper title tags, meta descriptions, and page speed. A proper site with 15-20 service pages (vs. current 10 Wix pages) could capture the ~1,000/mo in HVAC searches they currently rank for but don't convert.

**Revenue impact:** At 3% conversion rate and $5,000 average job value, 400 extra monthly visitors = 12 leads/mo = $60,000/mo potential.

### 2. Content Marketing (Blog) — Est. +200-400 visits/mo in 6 months
Zero blog posts currently. Thunder Bay homeowners search for "furnace maintenance tips," "oil vs gas furnace," "heat pump rebates Ontario," "how to prepare furnace for winter." Each post is a new ranking opportunity. Target 2 posts/month for 12 months = 24 posts capturing long-tail traffic.

**Estimated keyword opportunity:** 500-800 monthly searches in untouched long-tail HVAC queries.

### 3. Google Business Profile Optimisation — Est. +50-100% more GBP calls
13 reviews in 36 years is dangerously low. Clow Darling has 71 reviews. A review generation campaign targeting 5 reviews/month would reach 73 reviews within 12 months, overtaking Clow Darling. Combined with weekly GBP posts, Q&A optimisation, and photo uploads.

**Review velocity benchmark:** 5/month is achievable with post-service email/text follow-up.

### 4. NAP Cleanup & Schema Markup — Quick Win
Fix the postal code inconsistency (P7E 3S7 vs P7E 2C5), remove the duplicate Yelp listing, claim missing directory profiles (BBB, HomeStars, TrustedPros). Add LocalBusiness + HVAC schema to the new site. This is a 1-week project with immediate local pack impact.

### 5. Social Media Presence — Brand Building
No Facebook page for a local HVAC company in 2026 is a critical gap. Facebook is where Thunder Bay homeowners ask for contractor recommendations. A basic Facebook Business page with seasonal tips, job photos, and review sharing would generate referral traffic and build trust signals.

### 6. Lennox Dealer Content — Competitive Advantage
They're an authorized Lennox dealer but barely mention it. Dedicated Lennox product pages, rebate calculators, and comparison content ("Lennox vs. Carrier for Northern Ontario") would capture branded searches and differentiate from competitors.

## Red Flags

1. **Extremely low review count (13 in 36 years)** — This suggests either a reluctance to ask for reviews, a very small customer base, or both. Could indicate the business is semi-retired or winding down.

2. **Mixed review sentiment** — Multiple reviews cite scheduling delays, technicians not showing up, and broken promises about service timing. One reviewer said they "gave up after 2 months" waiting for a furnace cleaning. This suggests capacity or operations issues.

3. **Wix website with zero content** — The fact that the website hasn't been upgraded in years (Wix free/low-tier) may indicate limited willingness to invest in marketing.

4. **No social media at all** — Complete absence of Facebook/Instagram suggests either tech aversion or disinterest in marketing growth.

5. **Possible second location or old address** — Yelp shows a listing at 819 McLaughlin Street in addition to 626 Waterloo St S. This could be an old address creating NAP confusion.

6. **Age of owners** — Founded in 1990 with "lived in Thunder Bay their whole lives" — Larry and Sandi are likely in their 60s. There's a possibility they're planning to retire or sell rather than invest in growth. This needs to be assessed in the outreach conversation.

7. **No visible employees or team page** — The "team of trained and licensed technicians" mentioned on the site may be very small. Budget capacity for marketing spend is uncertain.
