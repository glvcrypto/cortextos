## Personal Story

Northern Lights Golf Complex was built in 1994 by Gerald Breukelman, positioned just outside Thunder Bay on Twin City Crossroad. The complex is unique in the Thunder Bay market for offering THREE golf experiences in one location: a regulation 9-hole course (par 36, 3,341 yards from blue tees, slope 124), a 9-hole par-3 course ideal for beginners and families, and a driving range. The licensed clubhouse with restaurant serves as a gathering spot for golfers.

Breukelman expanded his golf portfolio in February 2013 by purchasing Centennial Golf Course on Thompson Road (renaming it Centennial Pines Golf Course), offering $800 dual memberships for both properties. He has invested in improvements including double greens on every hole at Northern Lights.

The Breukelman family appears to be a multi-generational golf operation in Thunder Bay. Gerald built the course from scratch; Nadeen Breukelman (likely daughter or wife) is now referenced as the owner in 2024 articles, suggesting a family succession. During COVID (2021), local news covered golf courses remaining open citing confusion over provincial orders -- Northern Lights was part of this story, indicating they stayed active and engaged with the community throughout.

Reviews consistently praise staff friendliness and the course's suitability for all skill levels. The par-3 course fills a unique niche for new golfers and families. With 92 Google reviews at 4.5 stars, they have the **highest review count** of any 9-hole course in Thunder Bay -- proof of customer loyalty despite zero digital marketing investment.

Amenities: licensed clubhouse, restaurant, beverage cart, driving range, putting green, golf cart rentals, pro shop, pull-carts available, walking permitted, free parking. Season: May through October.

## Niche-Marketing Misalignment

This is the textbook case of a business that has earned tremendous real-world reputation (92 reviews, 4.5 stars, 32 years in business) but captures exactly **zero** of the ~6,335 monthly golf-related searches in Thunder Bay online. Key disconnects:

- **71% of golfers book tee times online** (industry standard). Northern Lights has no online booking and no website to host one.
- **"northern lights golf course" gets ~590 branded searches/month** but there is no website to capture this traffic. Searchers land on directory listings and competitor sites instead.
- **The business has 1,303 Facebook check-ins** proving foot traffic, but no way to convert that interest into repeat bookings, email signups, or off-season engagement.
- **Tourists using Superior Country and Visit Thunder Bay directories** find the listing but have no website to click for green fees, hours, or booking. Phone-only means lost after-hours inquiries.
- **Two courses, one owner, zero websites** -- the Centennial Pines connection doubles the pitch value.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Website creation for both properties** — Neither Northern Lights nor Centennial Pines (same owner) has a functioning website. A single website or two linked sites would capture the significant "golf courses thunder bay" search volume (~6,335/mo total niche). Currently, 100% of this traffic goes to competitors with websites. "northern lights golf course" alone gets ~590 searches/mo with no website to capture it.

2. **Online tee time booking** — With two courses and a driving range, an online booking system would reduce phone overhead, capture after-hours bookings, and allow golfers to easily book at either property. This is especially important for tourists using Superior Country tourism listings to find courses.

3. **Family/beginner marketing** — Northern Lights' par-3 course is a genuine differentiator. No other Thunder Bay golf facility specifically targets new golfers and families with a dedicated par-3 layout. Content marketing around "learn to golf Thunder Bay," "family golf Thunder Bay," and "beginner golf lessons" could capture an underserved audience segment.

4. **GBP optimization** — With 92 reviews already, GBP is their strongest digital asset. Adding a website link, posts, photos, and seasonal updates would significantly improve local pack visibility.

5. **Email list building** — 1,303 Facebook check-ins and 847 likes represent an existing audience with zero email capture. A simple website with email signup could build an off-season communication channel for membership renewals, early bird specials, and event announcements.

6. **Dual-property cross-marketing** — Dual memberships between Northern Lights and Centennial Pines could be promoted online, creating a unique value proposition no other Thunder Bay golf business offers.

## Red Flags

- **Same owner as Centennial Pines** — Outreach should be combined. One pitch covering both properties is more appropriate and more compelling.
- **Rural location** — Located outside Thunder Bay city limits on Twin City Crossroad, which may limit walk-in traffic. Website presence is even more critical for a rural course.
- **Not a Golf Canada member facility** — Golf Canada listing carries a disclaimer. Rounds played there cannot count toward handicap calculation. May indicate budget constraints or philosophical disagreement with federation fees.
- **Budget signal is low-medium** — Owner-operated, seasonal, rural. May be price-sensitive. However, owning two courses suggests some capital.
- **Seasonal business (May-Oct)** — 5-6 month operating window means marketing ROI must be concentrated. Website value persists year-round for pre-season bookings.
