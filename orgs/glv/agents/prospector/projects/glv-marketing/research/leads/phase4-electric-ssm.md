## Their Story

Phase Four Electrical Contractor Ltd has operated in Sault Ste. Marie for nearly five decades. Founded in 1977 (or 1978 per BBB), it predates most of SSM's commercial development boom and has been wired into the city's institutional and industrial fabric ever since.

The project portfolio visible on their website — and confirmed through directory snippets — tells the story clearly:

- **F.J. Davey Home** (2004): 374-bed long-term care nursing home. This is a major institutional electrical job — complex life-safety systems, fire alarm integration, scale beyond almost any residential contractor.
- **International Customs Plaza / Sault Ste. Marie Bridge**: The Canada-US border crossing. A federal infrastructure project.
- **Alzheimer Society Sault Ste. Marie & Algoma District**: Institutional non-profit.
- **Boston Pizza Sault Ste. Marie** (2008): Commercial restaurant build-out.
- **Ontario Northland New Depot**: Crown agency transit infrastructure.
- **New Northside Volkswagen Dealership**: Commercial auto retail.
- **Magino Gold Mine**: Mining-sector project, Dubreuilville region north of SSM — the kind of remote industrial work that requires substantial crew capacity.
- **New Holy Cross Elementary School** (SSM, 2023): Recent institutional project, confirming they are still actively winning large commercial tenders.

The succession story: Anthony Barban (founder-era, likely 60s–70s) + Victor Barban (MBA, Operations Manager) = a business in active succession. Victor is running operations. This is a business transitioning from founder to second generation — a motivated window for external support, not a retiring one.

47 years in SSM. Their reputation is built entirely on word-of-mouth and institutional relationships. They have never needed to market. That era is ending.

## Retainer Viability

Evidence that $2,000/month is well within capacity:

1. **Project scale**: Nursing homes, border crossings, and gold mines are multi-hundred-thousand-dollar electrical contracts. Phase 4 is not a two-man shop doing panel upgrades.
2. **47 years of operation**: Sustained operation over this timeline in a competitive trade = proven cash flow. Only profitable companies last this long in Sault Ste. Marie.
3. **ESA licensed with active service scope**: Fire alarms, security systems, solar design, infrared scanning, data cabling — these are not commodity services. They indicate certification costs, trained staff, and premium billing.
4. **24/7 emergency service**: Maintaining this requires staff availability beyond a 1–10 person team. ZoomInfo's count is likely wrong; the project scope demands more.
5. **New Holy Cross Elementary (2023)**: They won a school build-out as recently as 2023, confirming they are still bidding and winning institutional work at volume.
6. **Italian-language listing on all directories**: Suggests continuity with the founding family's client base — the SSM Italian community is substantial and networked, meaning referral pipelines from 1977 may still be active.

**Assessment:** $2,000/month represents well under 1% of annual revenue for a company operating at this project scale. This is a realistic ask. The resistance will be unfamiliarity with marketing, not budget.

## Red Flags

- **Succession uncertainty**: Victor Barban's MBA and Operations Manager title suggest he is positioned to take over — but if Anthony is in his 70s and still runs relationships, Victor may not have budget authority. You may need to reach Anthony directly.
- **Zero appetite for marketing (by history)**: 47 years with no reviews, no PPC, no social activity = deeply rooted belief that marketing is unnecessary. Expect "we've never needed it" as an objection.
- **Barban Construction overlap**: A separate "Barban Construction & Project Management" entity exists in SSM (13 Caddy Avenue). This may be a related family business or a conflict of interest consideration — but it could also be an upsell (serve both entities).
- **Too busy to care**: If they are winning gold mine and school contracts, they may have all the work they want. The pitch needs to be about defensibility and the pipeline 5–10 years out, not current revenue.
- **No signs of agency relationship**: No tracked PPC, no obvious SEO footprint, no social presence — they are not currently working with anyone. Clean slate, but also means you are the first sales conversation they have had.
