## Personal Story

LDR Heating & Cooling is a true family business. Ljubo Jankovic spent over two decades working for a large national HVAC firm before deciding to go independent. On May 1, 2009, he launched LDR alongside his two sons, Darren and Robert. The company name "LDR" almost certainly stands for the first initials of Ljubo, Darren, and Robert.

The family collectively brings over 50 years of HVAC experience. They hold the highest TSSA certification available (G1 Gas Technician 1) and are Red Seal Journeyperson Sheet Metal Workers (308A through the Ontario College of Trades) -- these are serious credentials that demonstrate deep technical expertise.

Robert Jankovic is particularly community-minded. In 2018 (at age 35), he ran for Thunder Bay city council in the McKellar Ward, campaigning on crime prevention and infrastructure improvement. He was described as an army reservist and regional sales manager. His willingness to run for office shows civic engagement beyond the business.

The family operates out of 125 Hardisty Street South in Thunder Bay and services a wide geographic area stretching from Greenstone in the east to Upsala in the west -- covering hundreds of kilometres of Northwestern Ontario.

## Opportunities Identified

1. **Content gap = massive traffic upside.** LDR has 74 keywords vs Clow Darling's 153. With targeted content (blog posts, service area pages, FAQ content), LDR could realistically double their keyword count and 5-10x their traffic from ~152 to 750-1,500/mo. Clow Darling proves this market supports 1,401/mo of organic traffic.

2. **Emergency/repair landing pages.** Repair revenue grew from 21.6% to 31.3% industry-wide. Creating dedicated pages for "emergency furnace repair Thunder Bay" and "AC repair Thunder Bay" (where they already rank #1) with schema markup and click-to-call CTAs could convert more of this growing segment.

3. **Review generation campaign.** With only 25 reviews (vs Clow Darling's 71), a systematic review request process post-service could add 20-30 reviews within 6 months. Moving from 25 to 55+ reviews would significantly boost GBP visibility and trust signals.

4. **Citation building.** Missing from BBB, HomeStars, Yelp, Facebook. Adding 10-15 quality citations would improve local SEO authority and provide additional lead channels. HomeStars alone is a major HVAC discovery platform in Ontario.

5. **Social media launch (Facebook priority).** Thunder Bay homeowners 35-65 are heavy Facebook users. A Facebook business page with seasonal tips, job photos, and emergency alerts would create a new lead channel at minimal cost.

6. **Keyword cannibalization fix.** "Heating and cooling thunder bay" has 7 different LDR URLs in Semrush results (positions 1, 28, 33, 44, 66, 84, 89). Consolidating these with proper canonical tags and internal linking could boost the #1 URL's authority.

7. **Service area expansion pages.** LDR serves Greenstone, Upsala, Nipigon, Red Rock, and Kakabeka Falls. Dedicated service area pages for each community would capture geo-specific searches (e.g., "furnace repair Nipigon").

8. **Google Ads for high-intent keywords.** "Furnace repair thunder bay" has a $4.06 CPC. With zero ad competition from LDR, even a small $500/mo budget could capture emergency repair searches that convert at high rates.

## Red Flags

- **Domain redirect anomaly:** During Playwright browser testing, ldrhc.ca redirected to lakeheadburner.ca (Lakehead Burner Service Ltd / A. Villeneuve Mechanical). However, WebFetch loaded the correct LDR site on all pages. This could indicate a DNS issue, a Squarespace hosting problem, or an intermittent redirect. This warrants verification before outreach -- the domain may be compromised or the business may have sold/merged.
- **Zero digital investment since 2018:** The website was built by Sociable Thunder Bay in 2018 and appears untouched. No social media, no content, no ads. Either they don't need more work (booked solid from reputation alone) or they've deprioritized growth.
- **Only 25 reviews in 17 years:** This is unusually low for an HVAC company in business since 2009. Could signal low volume, lack of review solicitation, or customer base that skews older/less digital.
- **Houzz domain mismatch (ldrhc.com vs ldrhc.ca):** Suggests someone may own ldrhc.com separately, or it was an old domain. Could cause SEO confusion.
- **Robert as "regional sales manager":** His 2018 political bio described him as a regional sales manager, not as LDR owner/operator. He may have a day job outside LDR, which would suggest LDR is primarily Ljubo's operation (now 17 years in, Ljubo would be in his 60s+).
- **No succession/growth signals:** No new hires, no expansion news, no new service announcements visible online.

## Recommended Outreach Angle

**Primary Why:** LDR dominates HVAC-specific keywords in Thunder Bay (#1 for furnace repair, AC repair, air conditioning) but captures only ~152 visits/month out of a ~1,952/month total addressable search market. Meanwhile, their main competitor Clow Darling gets nearly 10x the traffic (1,401/mo) with just 2x the keywords. LDR has earned the rankings but is leaving massive traffic on the table because they have no content, no service area pages, and no digital marketing beyond a static 2018 Squarespace site.

**Data Point:** LDR ranks for 74 keywords vs Clow Darling's 153. With targeted content marketing and proper local SEO, LDR could realistically 5-10x their organic traffic -- the market demand is proven, and they already have Google's trust.

**Competitor Comparison:** Clow Darling has 3x more Google reviews (71 vs 25), 2x more keywords (153 vs 74), and nearly 10x more organic traffic (~1,401 vs ~152). LDR's technical expertise and customer satisfaction (4.8 stars) are clearly strong -- they just have zero digital marketing amplifying it.

**HOOK question:** "You're already #1 in Thunder Bay for furnace repair and AC repair -- but did you know you're only capturing about 8% of the HVAC searches happening in your market every month?"
