## Niche Pain Points

- **Frozen pipe emergencies dominate winter revenue.** Thunder Bay averages 8 nights per year below -30C, with frost reaching 2.5 metres underground in severe winters. Plumbers who can respond within 2 hours to a frozen/burst pipe call have enormous pricing power.
- **Aging housing stock creates perpetual demand.** With 73% of homes built before 1980, Thunder Bay homeowners face ongoing issues with galvanized pipe replacement, outdated fixtures, and inadequate insulation around plumbing.
- **Geographic isolation limits competition but also talent.** Thunder Bay is 700 km from Winnipeg and 1,400 km from Toronto. New competitors rarely enter the market, but existing plumbers struggle to recruit apprentices and journeymen.
- **National franchises are capturing market share by default**, not by quality. Reliance and Enercare rank for local keywords because they have proper websites and SEO -- not because customers prefer them. Local plumbers could reclaim this traffic with basic digital presence.
- **The commercial and industrial segment is underserved online.** Clow Darling dominates, but smaller mechanical contractors (B. Gibson, G4, Villeneuve) have zero web presence for commercial plumbing, leaving a gap for any local business willing to invest in content.
