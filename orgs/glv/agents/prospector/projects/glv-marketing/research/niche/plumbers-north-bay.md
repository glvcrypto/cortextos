## Target Audience Profile

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age Range** | 35-65; homeowners skew older due to North Bay's median age of 43.6 and 20.9% seniors population |
| **Gender Split** | Primary decision-maker roughly 55% male / 45% female; women increasingly manage household service bookings |
| **Household Income** | Median $72,000/year (2021 Census); comfortable middle class but price-sensitive compared to GTA |
| **Homeowner/Renter** | ~65-70% homeowners (48.7% single-detached, 42.1% attached); renters less likely to hire directly |
| **Local Context** | CFB North Bay (22 Wing, ~500 military/civilian personnel) creates transient homeowner segment. Lake Nipissing cottage properties require seasonal plumbing (winterization, well pumps). ~40% of housing stock built before 1980 — aging pipes, galvanized plumbing, outdated water heaters common. Two post-secondary institutions (Nipissing University, Canadore College) drive rental market. Mining supply sector (65+ companies, ~3,000 workers) represents commercial plumbing demand. |

**How they buy:**
- **Search behaviour:** Google is dominant. "Plumber North Bay" and "plumber near me" are primary queries. Word-of-mouth and Facebook community groups (North Bay Buy & Sell, etc.) are strong secondary channels. Yellow Pages still has traction with 55+ demographic.
- **Purchase triggers:** Emergencies (burst pipe, backed-up drain, no hot water) drive 60-70% of initial contact. Seasonal triggers include frozen pipes (Nov-Mar), cottage opening/closing (May/Oct), pre-winter checks. Planned triggers include bathroom renovations, water heater replacement (10-12 year lifecycle), home sales requiring inspections.
- **Average transaction:** Emergency service call $225-450. Water heater replacement $1,700-2,500. Bathroom renovation plumbing $2,000-5,000. Drain cleaning $150-350. Annual average customer value for a retained client: $800-1,200.
- **Decision timeline:** Emergency = same day, no comparison shopping. Planned work = 1-3 weeks, typically contact 2-3 plumbers for quotes. Renovation = 2-8 weeks of research and comparison.

**What motivates them:**
- Trust and reliability — they want someone who shows up when promised and does honest work
- Local reputation — "who does your neighbour use?" carries enormous weight in a city of 52,000
- Fair, transparent pricing — no hidden fees, no upselling; quote = final price

**What frustrates them:**
- Plumbers who don't return calls or show up late — the #1 complaint across all review platforms
- Sticker shock — getting a bill significantly higher than the estimate without explanation
- Difficulty finding availability — most local plumbers are booked 1-2 weeks out for non-emergency work

## Niche Pain Points

- **Most competitors have weak or non-existent websites.** Of 28 businesses identified, only 8-10 have functional websites. At least 10 have zero web presence beyond a Yellow Pages listing.

- **Review counts are low across the board.** The market leader (Perrotta's) has ~84 reviews. Most competitors have fewer than 15. In markets where 47% of consumers skip businesses with under 20 reviews, this represents a major trust gap.

- **Nobody owns the local SEO keywords.** With low keyword difficulty across nearly every local plumbing term, the first business to build even a basic content + SEO strategy would dominate. No competitor appears to have a blog, content strategy, or meaningful backlink profile.

- **Seasonal demand spikes are underserved.** Frozen pipe searches spike 200-400% above baseline in winter. No local competitor has content targeting "frozen pipes North Bay" or similar seasonal terms.

- **The cottage/seasonal market is untapped.** North Bay sits at the gateway to Lake Nipissing cottage country. Winterization, well pump service, and seasonal opening/closing represent recurring revenue that no plumber is marketing for specifically.
