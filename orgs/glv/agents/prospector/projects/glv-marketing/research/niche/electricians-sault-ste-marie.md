## 5. Industry Stats

1. **Search-first buyer behaviour:** According to national contractor research, over 85% of homeowners search online before hiring an electrician — yet most SSM electrical businesses have fewer than 15 Google reviews, meaning virtually none are capturing this intent digitally.

2. **Panel upgrade demand accelerating:** In Ontario, homes with 60-amp or 100-amp panels face increasing pressure to upgrade as EV chargers, heat pumps, and induction ranges require 200-amp service. The Ontario Construction Secretariat's 2024 survey reported Northern Ontario as having the most positive construction outlook among all regions, driven by mining, institutional, and infrastructure projects — all of which require licensed electrical contractors.

3. **Review conversion gap:** Electricians nationally average 12–20 Google reviews in mid-size markets; the average SSM electrician outside the top 3 has fewer than 5. A client who reaches 50+ reviews with a 4.7+ rating in this market effectively becomes the default choice for any homeowner who searches — the bar is genuinely low.

4. **Regulatory tailwind:** As of 2025, Ontario law requires Licensed Electrical Contractors for all new wiring, panel upgrades, EV chargers, and rental property work. This funnels demand away from handymen and towards licensed firms — any LEC that can be found online captures a structurally growing, legally mandated market.

## 6. Target Audience Profile

### Primary Segment: SSM Homeowners

- **Age:** 35–65 (home ownership skews older in SSM vs. urban Ontario)
- **Trigger events:** Panel upgrade (older home stock — much of SSM housing built pre-1980), renovation, basement suite conversion, EV charger, hot tub, generator installation, emergency repair
- **Search behaviour:** Google "electrician near me" or "electrician Sault Ste Marie" — overwhelmingly mobile; reviews and proximity are the #1 decision factors
- **Pain points:** Can't find an electrician who answers the phone, uncertain about permits, worried about price
- **Budget:** $800–$5,000 per job depending on scope

### Secondary Segment: Commercial Property Owners and Managers

- **Profile:** Owners of strip malls, rental buildings, restaurants, retail, and light industrial properties in SSM
- **Trigger events:** Code compliance, renovation, fire alarm install, commercial panel work, lighting retrofits
- **Search behaviour:** Less likely to search cold — more likely to ask for referrals, but will Google a contractor name before calling; LinkedIn and Chamber of Commerce presence matters
- **Budget:** $5,000–$50,000+ per project; retainer relationships possible for multi-property owners

### Tertiary Segment: Construction Project Managers

- **Profile:** GCs, developers, and site supervisors overseeing new residential or light commercial builds in Algoma
- **Needs:** Reliable sub-trade with on-time scheduling, proper permits, ESA inspections
- **Decision driver:** Reputation, availability, and responsiveness — not lowest price
- **Budget:** Project-based; $10,000–$100,000+ per build contract
