## 4. Customer Psychology

### Common Pain Points for Plumbing Customers in Northern Ontario

1. **Emergency panic** — Burst pipes at -30°C, backed-up sewer in the basement, no hot water in January. Customers are stressed, desperate, and willing to pay a premium for immediate response.

2. **Hard water / water quality concerns** — Northern Ontario has mineral-rich water. Limescale buildup, rusty pipes, and water softener needs are ongoing pain points.

3. **Aging home infrastructure** — 55%+ of SSM homes built before 1970. Galvanized pipes corroding, clay sewer lines cracking, outdated fixtures needing full replacement.

4. **Cost anxiety** — Median household income is $73K (14% below national average). Homeowners worry about surprise bills and feel powerless against "plumber pricing."

5. **Difficulty finding available plumbers** — Multiple review mentions of companies "not taking new customers" or "weeks-long wait times." Capacity is constrained.

6. **Seasonal system strain** — Water heaters working 15-20% harder in winter, sump pumps running constantly during spring thaw, exterior hose bibs freezing.

7. **Renovation overwhelm** — Homeowners wanting bathroom/kitchen updates but unsure who to trust, what it costs, or how long it takes.

### Buying Behaviour

| Behaviour | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Emergency vs. planned split** | ~40% emergency (burst pipes, clogs, no heat), ~35% planned maintenance/replacement, ~25% renovation projects |
| **Seasonal patterns** | Peak demand: Nov-Mar (frozen pipes, furnace failures, water heater strain). Secondary peak: Apr-May (spring thaw, sump pump issues, reno season starts). Slowest: Jul-Aug. |
| **Decision speed** | Emergency: immediate (first available plumber wins). Planned: 1-3 weeks research. Renovation: 1-3 months planning. |
| **Research channels** | Google search → Google Maps/reviews → ask neighbours/family → Facebook community groups → call 2-3 for quotes |
| **Price sensitivity** | High for planned work (get 2-3 quotes). Low for emergencies (pay whatever, just fix it now). |

### Objections to Hiring a Plumber

1. **"It's too expensive"** — #1 objection. Hourly rates feel opaque. Customers want flat-rate or at least a range before committing.
2. **"I can do it myself"** — YouTube DIY culture. Often leads to bigger problems and eventual emergency call.
3. **"I don't know who to trust"** — No licensing transparency. Fear of being overcharged by someone unqualified.
4. **"They'll find more problems"** — Distrust that plumbers will upsell unnecessary work.
5. **"I can't get anyone to come"** — Capacity constraints mean some customers give up and live with minor issues until they become emergencies.

### Trust Drivers

| Factor | Importance |
|--------|-----------|
| Google reviews (4.5+ stars, 50+ reviews) | Critical — first filter for most customers |
| "Locally owned" / "born and raised" | Very high in SSM — strong community identity |
| Licensed and insured (visible on website) | High — legal requirement but customers don't always verify |
| Years in business | Moderate — established = reliable |
| Before-and-after photos of work | Moderate — especially for renovations |
| Flat-rate / upfront pricing | High — reduces cost anxiety |
| 24/7 availability | High for emergency customers |
| Word-of-mouth / personal referral | Very high — SSM is a tight-knit community |

**Key insight:** ~75% of SSM homes were built before 1980. This aging housing stock drives consistent demand for re-piping, fixture replacement, water heater upgrades, and sewer line repair — a structural advantage for any plumber with a strong web presence.

**Winter is king:** Average January temperature in SSM is -14°C, with frequent dips below -25°C. Frozen pipe calls spike dramatically. Burst pipe insurance claims average $20,000+ in damage. This creates urgent, high-value emergency demand from November through March.
