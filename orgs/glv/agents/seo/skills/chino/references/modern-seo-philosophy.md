# Modern SEO Philosophy — Ben Pelta Framework

> Google's goal: give the best result in the minimum time. Every SEO decision must serve this.

## Core Principles

### 1. SEO = PR, Not Keywords
- Promote the BUSINESS holistically, not individual keywords
- Build reputation with Google the way you'd build reputation with people
- Google trusts businesses that look like real businesses (social proof, UGC, directories, mentions)

### 2. Entity First, Keywords Second
- Google must understand WHO you are before it can rank you
- NAP consistency is foundational — one wrong phone number kills trust
- Signals (mentions, directories, social profiles) > backlinks
- A "signal" doesn't need to be a link — just a mention of business name, phone, or address

### 3. Content Must Be Personal
- Never write generic "10 things about X" content
- Always frame as: "Our expert [Name] explains..." or "Our team at [Business]..."
- Content needs real people, real photos, author pages
- Google rewards E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

### 4. Above the Fold = Priority Signal
- First scroll of every page gets highest Google priority
- Reduce dead space, optimize font sizes (14.5-15px body minimum)
- Above-the-fold content tells Google what the page is ABOUT
- Hero banners with no text = wasted priority signal

### 5. Website Tree = Priority Declaration
- Nav order tells Google what matters most
- About Us should be FIRST for local businesses (trust before product)
- Keyword-to-page mapping: "dealer" → About Us, NOT homepage
- Category → Subcategory → Product (help Google understand hierarchy)

### 6. Directories: Drip, Don't Bulk
- 1-2 directory submissions per day, not 20 in one sitting
- Mimics real business owner behaviour
- Builds natural-looking signal pattern over time
- Foundation of local SEO — every directory is a signal

### 7. Local SEO ≠ Regular SEO
- Small cities (50-80K) have delayed algorithm rollouts (months behind US)
- Google has fewer options in small markets — easier to rank but harder to build reputation
- Near-me keywords still matter in small/non-US markets even though Google says they don't
- Product pages: go broad FIRST (e.g., "Yamaha dealer"), then narrow to local

### 8. Brand Product Pages Strategy
- When selling branded products (Yamaha, Mercury, etc.), separate brand from business identity
- Google can confuse you as a "promoter" of the brand vs. an independent dealer
- Product pages: start broad (national), add local signals
- Business pages: start local, build outward

### 9. Internal Linking: The Holy Triangle
- For product businesses with financing: Finance Directory ↔ Brand Finance Page ↔ Product Page
- Use BUTTONS (not text links) for loop-back links — Google treats buttons as optional, avoiding infinite crawl loops
- Text links get higher crawl priority than button links — use strategically

### 10. Social Signals Are Mandatory
- Reddit UGC is ranked #1 by Google since 2025 updates
- LinkedIn posts for team members create authority signals
- Automate: article → LinkedIn summary → post
- Take over inactive local subreddits if possible
- Build local "hubs" (community news sites) to compete with established local media

## The Funnel Nuance

Standard funnel (TOFU → MOFU → BOFU) has SUB-FUNNELS within each stage:

- **Price search** = top of BOFU (researching, not buying yet)
- **Comparison search** (X vs Y) = middle of BOFU
- **Finance search** = bottom of BOFU (ready to buy, looking for payment solution)

"Super-intent" keywords (finance, apply, order) convert highest even with near-zero search volume. Fight for every single one.

## Google Ads + SEO Symbiosis

Running Google Ads alongside SEO is not optional for serious projects. Removing ads can tank organic rankings within weeks (Ben's Karma & Luck case: 350K → 80K organic traffic after ads shutdown). Google uses ad data as a trust/relevancy signal.
