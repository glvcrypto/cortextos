    |--> Log post to tracking sheet

### Google Sheet Structure (Content Queue)

| Column | Purpose |
|--------|---------|

| Image/Video | Link to asset or "none" |
| Status | Draft / Ready / Sent for Approval / Approved / Published / Rejected |
| Approved By | Aiden / -- |
| Published At | Timestamp |
| Engagement | Likes / Comments / Shares (manual or API pull) |

### Workflow Steps to Build

1. **Content Sheet** -- Create the Google Sheet with the structure above
2. **n8n Workflow: Daily Check** -- Runs at 7 AM ET, checks for "Ready" posts due today
3. **n8n Workflow: Approval Email** -- Sends formatted email with post preview + Approve/Edit buttons
4. **n8n Workflow: Webhook Receiver** -- Handles approve/edit responses
5. **n8n Workflow: Publisher** -- Posts to LinkedIn (via API) and Facebook (via Graph API)

## Appendix: Writing Standards (For All Content)

### Banned Phrases

Do not use any of these in blog posts, social posts, or any written content:

- leverage / leveraging
- delve / delving
- landscape (as in "digital landscape" or "marketing landscape")
- harness / harnessing
- streamline / streamlining
- game-changer / game-changing
- not just X, it's Y
- in today's digital age / in today's world
- look no further
- at the end of the day
- navigate the complexities
- unlock (as in "unlock your potential")
- elevate (as in "elevate your brand")
- cutting-edge
- robust (unless describing actual physical strength)
- synergy / synergize
- actionable insights (use "specific steps" or "clear next steps")
- deep dive (use "detailed guide" or "full breakdown")
- empower / empowering
- seamless / seamlessly
- paradigm shift
- holistic approach
- best-in-class
- next-level

### Formatting Rules

- No em-dashes (--). Use periods, commas, or restructure the sentence.
- Canadian English: colour, favour, centre, defence, analyse, catalogue, licence (noun)
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max in blog posts, 1-3 in social)
- Use "you" and "your" -- write to the reader, not about them
- Include specific numbers, examples, and local references where possible
- Every blog post ends with a single, clear CTA
- Headings in sentence case, not title case
