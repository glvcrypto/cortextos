### How to Choose the Right Boat Trailer for Northern Ontario

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

A boat trailer is not an afterthought.

In Northern Ontario, it takes more abuse than most people anticipate. The road from the Sault out to your camp might be paved, but the last two kilometres to the launch ramp often are not. Seasonal frost heaves, gravel access roads, and the constant cycle of dipping a steel frame into fresh water and pulling it back out put real stress on a trailer. Buy the wrong one and you will know it by the end of your second season.

At Reyco Marine, we carry Easy Hauler boat trailers. This post is a practical guide to choosing the right trailer for the boats, roads, and launch conditions Northern Ontario puts in front of you.

---

#### Why the Trailer Matters as Much as the Boat

A boat without the right trailer is a liability on the road. The wrong trailer affects:

**Tongue weight and vehicle load.** Every trailer has a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) and a tongue weight limit. Exceed either and you are creating a handling and safety problem before you reach the highway. Know your boat's loaded weight — hull, motor, fuel, and gear — before you choose a trailer.

**Corrosion.** Northern Ontario's fresh water is gentler on trailers than salt water, but that does not mean corrosion is not a factor. Road salt in early spring and late fall is the real enemy. A trailer that sits in a wet garage through freeze-up without proper maintenance will rust out faster than one used on the coast.

**Fit.** A trailer built for a 14-foot aluminum fishing boat does not fit a 20-foot bowrider. Bunks, rollers, and keel supports all need to be matched to your hull shape. An ill-fitting trailer damages your boat.

---

#### About Easy Hauler Trailers

Easy Hauler is a North American boat trailer manufacturer that has been producing trailering systems for recreational and utility use for decades. Their lineup covers small aluminum fishing boats up to heavier fibreglass runabouts and pontoons.

Key features found across the Easy Hauler lineup:

- **Galvanized steel frames** — hot-dip galvanizing on main frame members provides corrosion resistance suited to Northern Ontario's fresh water and road-salt exposure
- **Bunk and roller configurations** — bunk trailers cradle the hull along the keel and chines; roller trailers allow the boat to load and unload more easily in shallow ramps, which matters at some of the launch sites around the Sault and the Algoma district
- **Adjustable bunks and supports** — accommodates different hull shapes without modification
- **LED lighting packages** — submersible LED lights are standard on newer Easy Hauler models, which hold up significantly better to repeated submersion than incandescent setups

#### Matching the Trailer to Your Boat

**Small to mid-size aluminum fishing boats (14–17 feet)**

This is the most common Northern Ontario recreational boat, and a single-axle Easy Hauler in the appropriate weight class is the standard pairing. [CONFIRM-CASEY: Confirm specific single-axle series and GVWR range for this boat class.] Key considerations: bunk spacing matched to the hull, a spare tire carrier, and a bow stop that keeps the boat firmly on the trailer during highway travel.

**Fibreglass runabouts and bowriders (17–22 feet)**

Heavier hulls need a heavier trailer. Tandem axle trailers distribute the load across four wheels instead of two, which matters for stability on highway speeds and on uneven gravel roads. [CONFIRM-CASEY: Confirm tandem axle Easy Hauler availability and applicable GVWR range at Reyco.] If you are trailering a 20-foot fibreglass boat on the Trans-Canada, a tandem axle is worth the investment.

**Pontoon boats**

Pontoon trailering is a different geometry from a V-hull. The trailer needs to support the two pontoon tubes rather than a keel. #### A Note on Trailer Maintenance

Northern Ontario launches are not always clean-bottom concrete ramps. You are dragging a steel trailer through weeds, sand, and organic material on a regular basis. After every use, rinse the trailer — particularly the wheel bearings, brake assemblies (if equipped), and wiring connections. Grease the bearings at the start of each season. Check the lights every time. These are small habits that extend the life of a trailer by years.

When you buy from Reyco, we walk you through the trailer's maintenance schedule. It is not complicated, but it does need to happen.
