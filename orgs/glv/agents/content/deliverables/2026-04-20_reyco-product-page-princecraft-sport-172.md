### What the Princecraft Sport 172 Is

The Sport 172 is a 17-foot 2-inch aluminium fishing boat. It is built on a deep-V hull, carries six people, and is rated to a maximum of 115 horsepower. The beam measures 79 inches, which is wide enough to fish standing without feeling like you are balancing on a ladder, and narrow enough to trailer behind a half-ton truck without requiring a special permit.

The hull is all aluminium. Princecraft has been building aluminium boats in Canada since 1958, and the Sport line is the model that most Northern Ontario anglers know by name. The materials resist rust, tolerate the freeze-thaw cycle without delaminating, and clean up easily at the end of a season.

Inside, the cockpit gives you a livewell for keeping bait and catch alive, rod storage to keep your gear organized and protected, and raised casting decks fore and aft. The raised decks are not decoration. They give you a higher vantage point when you are scanning structure, and they put your feet above the bottom of the cockpit when you are standing and working a spot.

The fuel capacity is 45 litres. On a full-day run across Goulais Bay or up the river into the St. Marys rapids section, that is a reasonable range without feeling like you are stopping to fill up at every opportunity.

The Princecraft Sport 172 carries a limited lifetime warranty on the hull. On a boat that gets used hard on Northern Ontario water, that warranty has real value.

---

### Why Northern Ontario Anglers Choose the Sport 172

Northern Ontario is not a single type of water.

The St. Marys River at Sault Ste. Marie is a current environment. It has real flow, variable depth structure, and the kind of chop that builds up when wind and current oppose each other. Anglers targeting walleye, pike, and bass in the river need a hull that tracks well in current and recovers from chop without making every cast a balance exercise.

The Algoma district lakes north and east of the Sault, everything from the inland lakes off Highway 17 to the Spanish River system, tend to be shallower at the margins and can kick up quickly when afternoon wind moves through. These are not big-water environments, but they are not forgiving either.

Lake Superior's edges and Batchawana Bay are a different situation entirely. When the open water gets involved, you want freeboard and a hull that handles real wave action, not a flat-bottomed craft that gets pushed around.

The Sport 172's deep-V hull geometry addresses all three of these environments reasonably well. It is not a specialized tournament sled designed for one type of water. It is a capable, versatile aluminium fishing platform that a Northern Ontario angler can run confidently across the range of water they are likely to encounter.

The 17'2" length is intentional for this region. Long enough to carry gear, run at speed, and handle moderate chop, short enough to back down a typical Algoma boat launch without needing a spotter and a long push.

### What We Would Pair It With

If you are buying a Sport 172 as a new build, the pairing conversation comes down to the motor. The 115 hp max rating means you have a range of engine options, but our most common recommendation is the Mercury 115 FourStroke.

Here is the reasoning. The Sport 172 hull is balanced for the 115 hp class. You are not under-gunning it, and you are not loading it past what the transom is designed for. The Mercury 115 FourStroke at 374 lbs fits the weight balance of this hull well. You get real-world cruising speeds that put you on the water fast without burning unnecessary fuel, and the Mercury's 3-plus-3 year warranty pairs cleanly with the Princecraft hull warranty for a buying experience where both major components are covered.

We sell a significant number of Sport 172 packages with the Mercury 115 FourStroke, and the combination consistently produces satisfied customers. These are not numbers we invented for a brochure; they are the observations of a service department that sees these boats come back year after year.

The other conversation worth having is the Minnkota Terrova 80 as a bow-mount trolling motor add-on. For anglers doing serious walleye fishing on the St. Marys River or working weed edges on inland Algoma lakes, the Spot-Lock anchor feature holds the boat within five feet of a waypoint even in current. The Terrova 80 is not cheap, but for the angler who spends real time on the water, it changes the experience of fishing alone or with one other person.

### Who the Sport 172 Is Right For

The Sport 172 sells to a wide range of buyers at Reyco, and after years of seeing those buyers return for service and eventually for their next boat, we have a clear picture of who it works best for.

**The serious recreational angler.** Someone who fishes two or three times per week during the season, targets walleye on the St. Marys, pike on Algoma lakes, maybe runs toward Lake Superior on good days. This person wants a reliable, capable platform that handles real fishing, not a lifestyle boat with a rod holder bolted on as an afterthought.

**The tournament angler at the district level.** The Sport 172 is a common choice among anglers who fish local tournaments on the Sault area circuit. The casting decks, livewell, and hull characteristics are matched to what tournament fishing in this region demands. It is not a bass-circuit sled, but for Northern Ontario tournament fishing, it is a proper boat.

**Families where fishing is the primary use.** If the family activity is fishing, not tubing and wakeboarding, the Sport 172 is an appropriate family boat. Six-person capacity means two adults and several kids, a realistic crew for a day on the water. The wide beam keeps younger passengers from feeling like they are sitting on the edge of a bathtub.

**Buyers replacing an older aluminium fishing boat.** Many of our Sport 172 sales are to buyers who have been running 14-to-16-foot aluminium boats for years and are moving up. The step from 16 to 17'2" with a wider beam is significant enough to notice on the water, especially in chop.

### The Princecraft Difference

One of the more common questions we get from buyers who are choosing between boat brands is what makes Princecraft worth the price relative to imported aluminium boats sold at mass-market retailers.

There are three real answers.

The first is Canadian manufacturing. Princecraft has built boats in Princeville, Quebec, since 1958. The hull design and engineering for the Sport line is developed by people who understand Northern Ontario-style fishing environments, not people designing for flatwater lakes in the American southeast.

The second is warranty substance. Princecraft's limited lifetime hull warranty is not a marketing claim backed by fine print that makes it useless. It is the kind of warranty that we as a dealer can actually help you use if you have a legitimate claim. That only matters when something goes wrong, but if something does go wrong on a hull issue, you want to be at an authorized dealer rather than trying to navigate a warranty from a brand with no local presence.

The third is parts availability. As an authorized Princecraft dealer, we stock genuine parts and can source whatever we do not have on the shelf. A boat that sits on your trailer waiting two weeks for a part from an overseas supplier is not a boat you are fishing on during the best weeks of the Northern Ontario season.
