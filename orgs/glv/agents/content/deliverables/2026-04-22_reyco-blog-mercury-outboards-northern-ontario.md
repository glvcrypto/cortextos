### How to Choose the Right Mercury Outboard for Northern Ontario

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

Choosing an outboard motor is not a decision most boat owners make often. A well-maintained motor on the right hull should last 15 to 20 years. Get the match right the first time and that engine becomes a background detail — it starts, it runs, it does not give you trouble. Get it wrong and you spend the next decade compensating for the mismatch.

At Reyco Marine, we are an authorized Mercury Marine dealer. We have been fitting Mercury outboards to Northern Ontario boats long enough to have a clear picture of what works up here. This post covers the buying decision: what horsepower class makes sense for different Northern Ontario use cases, why shaft length matters more than most buyers realize, and what it means to purchase from an authorized dealer rather than sourcing a motor elsewhere.

---

#### Why Mercury in Northern Ontario

Mercury Marine is the most widely distributed outboard brand in Canada, which matters in Northern Ontario for a practical reason: service access. When your motor needs work — impeller replacement at the start of season three, a fuel system issue, a diagnostic scan after a fault light comes on — you want to be close to a dealer who knows the platform.

Mercury's FourStroke lineup has become the standard choice for Northern Ontario fishing and recreational boating for several reasons:

**No oil mixing.** Four-stroke outboards run on straight gasoline. There is no pre-mix, no separate oil tank, no calculation of ratios. For anglers fuelling at remote launches or marinas where two-stroke mix is not available, this matters.

**Cold-start reliability.** Mercury FourStroke motors use electronic fuel injection, which means consistent cold-start performance regardless of temperature. In Northern Ontario, where May launches can happen at near-freezing air temperatures, this is not a convenience feature — it is practical.

**Fuel efficiency at cruise.** Mercury's fuel management systems reduce consumption at steady-state cruise speeds, which is relevant to Northern Ontario anglers running distances to Spanish River country, Batchawana Bay, and Lake Superior coastal access points.

**Quiet operation.** Four-stroke motors run significantly quieter than two-stroke equivalents. For anglers making slow presentations on the St. Marys River or drifting structure on inland lakes, reduced motor noise is a real advantage.

---

#### Choosing the Right Horsepower

This is where most buyers start, and it is the right place. Every hull has a maximum horsepower rating stamped on the manufacturer's plate. That ceiling exists for structural and handling reasons. Never exceed it.

Within the rated ceiling, the right horsepower depends on what you are doing:

**Smaller fishing boats (14–16 feet, aluminum):** Boats in this class are typically rated to 50–75 hp. A Mercury FourStroke in the 40–60 hp range is the common match — enough power to get on plane quickly and run at comfortable speeds without over-powering the hull.

**Mid-size fishing and recreational boats (16–18 feet, aluminum):** The 90–115 hp range covers most boats in this class. The Mercury 115 FourStroke is the single most common motor we fit to aluminium fishing boats in the 16-to-18-foot range. The weight-to-power ratio works well for this hull type, and the fuel economy at cruise is strong.

**Larger fibreglass runabouts and bowriders (18–22 feet):** Hulls in this class are often rated to 150–225 hp. Mercury's mid-range FourStroke line covers these applications. If you are purchasing a larger fibreglass hull, the right motor discussion is part of the purchase process — bring the hull spec sheet.

**The common mistake:** Buying a motor rated just at the hull maximum because more power seems better. A motor at the top of a hull's rating will deliver maximum speed but often at the cost of fuel economy, handling balance, and long-term transom stress. Matching a motor to typical use, not peak performance, is usually the better outcome.

---

#### Shaft Length

This is the detail most buyers do not think about until installation day. It should come up earlier.

Outboard shaft length is measured from the mounting clamp to the cavitation plate. Standard residential boat transoms fall into two height categories, and the shaft length must match:

- **20-inch shaft:** For standard-height transoms, typically found on older aluminum fishing boats and some fibreglass hulls.
- **25-inch shaft:** For tall transoms, common on newer aluminum and fibreglass designs, particularly boats with higher freeboard.

Installing a motor with the wrong shaft length causes real problems. Too short and the cavitation plate sits above the waterline, allowing the motor to suck air and lose cooling water flow. Too long and the motor sits too deep, creating unnecessary drag and affecting trim.

If you are replacing an existing motor, measure the existing shaft length before you buy. If you are purchasing a new hull and motor together, we confirm the match at point of sale.

#### The Authorized Dealer Difference

Mercury outboard motors are available through private sales, online retailers, and grey-market channels. The purchase price through some of those channels will be lower than through an authorized dealer like Reyco.

Here is what changes when you buy outside the authorized network:

**Warranty.** Mercury's best warranty coverage — the 3-plus-3 FourStroke warranty (three years factory plus three years extended) — requires purchase from an authorized dealer and proper registration at point of sale. A motor purchased from a private seller or a non-authorized retailer does not carry this coverage into your hands, regardless of what the original buyer paid.

**Factory training.** Authorized Mercury dealers have factory-trained technicians. When a service bulletin comes out for your specific motor, our shop receives it. When a diagnostic issue requires Mercury-specific tooling, we have it. Independent shops may be capable, but they are working without the manufacturer's support structure.

**Parts supply.** Our dealer account gives us direct access to Mercury Precision Parts. For common service items — impellers, gear lube, filters — we stock them. For parts we do not carry on the shelf, dealer channel access is faster than consumer-side sourcing.

**Local relationship.** If something goes wrong in season two of your motor's life, you are not dealing with an online retailer. You are calling the same people who sold you the motor and handled the warranty registration.

#### Spring Service: Book Early

In the Sault Ste. Marie area, the spring rush hits the service department fast. Boats that were winterized in October come back in April and May, all at the same time.

If you are purchasing a Mercury outboard this season or want spring commissioning done before ice-off, call us before the end of March. Service slots fill up. A motor that comes in for commissioning in April is on water before opening weekend. A motor that calls in mid-May may not be.

Spring commissioning for an outboard covers impeller inspection and replacement if needed, gear lube change, fuel system inspection, battery and electrical checks, and a test run. It is the difference between a motor that starts on demand and one that becomes a conversation topic on the first warm day of the season.
