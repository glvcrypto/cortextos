### What the Toro Power Max HD 1028 Is

The Power Max HD 1028 is a 28-inch, two-stage snowblower powered by a 302 cc OHV engine. It is the heavy-duty tier of the Power Max line, which means it is built for the kind of demanding winter work conditions that would cause problems for a residential-grade machine.

The two-stage designation matters in Northern Ontario. A two-stage snowblower uses an auger to break up and collect snow, then a separate impeller to throw it. The separation of those two functions lets the machine handle heavy, wet, compacted snow that would either jam a single-stage blower or require multiple passes with a lower-powered two-stage.

The 28-inch clearing width means most residential driveways in Sault Ste. Marie can be cleared in a reasonable number of passes. A standard single-car driveway takes four to five passes. A double-car driveway at the end of a city block takes six to eight. Either way, the clearing time is significantly faster than shovelling or running a smaller single-stage machine.

### The Anti-Clogging System

The feature that distinguishes the Power Max HD 1028 from basic two-stage machines is Toro's Anti-Clogging System, known as ACS.

Here is the problem ACS solves. When wet, heavy snow enters a standard two-stage chute, it can pack against the chute walls as it is discharged. Once snow packs in the chute, the impeller loses its throw path and you stop clearing and start digging out the chute with a stick. In conditions where the snow is wet and heavy, this can happen multiple times per session.

Toro's ACS system uses a plastic serrated edge inside the chute housing that prevents the packed snow from adhering to the chute walls. The shape and material of the ACS component allow snow to release cleanly even when it is compacted and wet.

This is not a feature invented for a marketing brochure. We test the machines we sell, and the ACS system works in the conditions we get around the Sault: heavy, wet, lake-effect snow that comes in when temperatures are hovering around zero and everything is borderline slush.

From Casey, directly: "The ACS anti-clogging system genuinely works. I've tested it in the heaviest slush and it just keeps throwing. If you get a lot of wet snow off the lake, this is the one to get."

If you live on the south side of the Sault, anywhere from the waterfront up through the residential streets that face Lake Superior, or on any property in the city that gets hit by the lake-effect band, the ACS is not optional extra. It is the reason to buy the HD version over the standard Power Max.

### Quick Stick Chute Control

The Power Max HD 1028 uses Toro's Quick Stick chute control, which lets you adjust the chute direction with one hand while the machine is moving.

This is worth explaining because it changes how you clear a driveway.

Without one-hand chute control, redirecting where the snow goes requires stopping or slowing down, reaching to the chute handle, rotating it, and then continuing your pass. On a typical driveway clearing session with multiple passes, you redirect the chute several times to keep the discharge going to the right place, away from neighbours' properties, walkways, and areas you have already cleared.

The Quick Stick puts that adjustment on the handlebar. You change the chute direction with a lever while you keep moving. Over the course of a driveway clearing, the cumulative time saved and the reduction in process interruption is noticeable.

The chute also adjusts vertically, so you can drop the discharge angle when you need to throw snow into a pile close to the machine, or open it up to throw distance when you are clearing toward an open area.

### The 302 cc Engine

The power source for the Power Max HD 1028 is a 302 cc OHV engine. In practical terms, that engine size gives the machine the torque to auger through packed, crusted snow without bogging down.

One of the common failure modes for under-powered snowblowers in Northern Ontario conditions is the machine stalling when it hits the compacted snow layer that forms at the bottom of the driveway after a night of car traffic or a day of melting and refreezing. The crust layer at the base of accumulated snow in a Northern Ontario driveway can be several centimetres of dense, packed material that is closer in consistency to ice than fresh snow.

The 302 cc engine handles that crust layer without requiring you to make a preliminary shallow pass to break it up. You can run the machine at full clearing depth and let the auger do the work.

Electric start means you do not need to pull-start a cold engine on a January morning at minus twenty. Turn the key, wait a moment, and the machine runs. This is a standard feature at this price point, but worth confirming before you buy any snowblower in Northern Ontario.

### Drive System

The Power Max HD 1028 has six forward speeds and two reverse speeds. The drive system is self-propelled, meaning the machine moves itself across the driveway rather than requiring you to push it.

The practical reason this matters: a 28-inch, 302 cc snowblower is a heavy machine. Pushing that machine through a foot of wet snow across a 40-foot driveway would be significant physical work. The self-propelled drive transfers that effort to the engine and lets you guide rather than push.

The six forward speeds give you control over clearing pace. Slow speeds for heavy, compacted snow where the auger needs more dwell time to process material. Faster speeds for lighter, fluffy snow where the machine can move efficiently without jamming.

Two reverse speeds matter at the end of each pass and at the edge of the driveway. Backing the machine cleanly without fighting it reduces fatigue over the course of a full driveway clearing.

### Service at Reyco

Reyco Marine's service department handles small engine maintenance and repair, including snowblower service. We carry Toro belts, shear pins, auger paddles, and common wear parts on the shelf.

Shear pins are the small component most snowblower owners encounter first: if the auger hits a hidden object (a puck, a frozen garden hose, a rock buried under the snow), the shear pin breaks intentionally to protect the auger shaft. It is a safety feature, not a failure. We stock shear pins for Toro machines and can walk any buyer through the replacement process.

The standard service calendar for a snowblower in Northern Ontario looks like this: a pre-season tune-up before the first snowfall, and an end-of-season service and storage prep in April. Pre-season service includes fresh fuel, spark plug inspection, belt inspection, and a test run. End-of-season includes draining or stabilizing the fuel system, fresh oil if required, and confirming the machine is ready for storage.

We recommend booking fall service appointments in September or October, before the first snowfall. Once the first storm hits, the shop gets busy with emergency calls and wait times grow.
