### Troy-Bilt Outdoor Power Equipment for Northern Ontario Properties

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

A Northern Ontario property asks more of its outdoor equipment than most.

Lawn season starts late and ends abruptly, which means every cutting hour matters. Snow arrives in November and stays through March, sometimes April. The properties themselves tend to be larger than urban lots — an acre, two acres, a rural lot with a long gravel driveway. The equipment that handles that workload needs to be built for it.

At Reyco Marine, we carry Troy-Bilt outdoor power equipment. Troy-Bilt is a North American brand with a long history in residential and semi-professional power equipment — lawn tractors, riding mowers, snow blowers, string trimmers, and tillers. This post covers the core product categories and what matters when you are buying for Northern Ontario conditions.

---

#### Lawn Tractors and Riding Mowers

For properties over half an acre, a walk-behind mower is the wrong tool. It is slow and physically demanding on the larger lots common around the Sault and throughout the Algoma district. A lawn tractor or riding mower changes the equation.

Troy-Bilt's riding equipment includes lawn tractors and zero-turn mowers across several engine displacement and cutting deck size configurations. What matters for Northern Ontario use:

**Cutting deck width.** A wider deck covers more ground per pass and reduces your total cutting time. The tradeoff is manoeuvrability around obstacles. For large open lots with few trees, a wider deck makes sense. For properties with outbuildings, garden beds, and mature trees, a mid-size deck with a tighter turning radius is often more practical.

**Engine displacement and torque.** Northern Ontario lawns are not manicured fairways. Thick grass, uneven terrain, and occasional wet conditions require a machine with enough torque to push through without bogging down. Check engine displacement, not just horsepower ratings.

**Ground clearance and terrain handling.** If your property has soft spots, drainage channels, or elevation changes, look for a tractor with adequate ground clearance. Some of the lower-clearance zero-turn designs are better suited to flat terrain.

#### Snow Blowers

This is where Troy-Bilt's lineup earns its place in Northern Ontario. The Sault averages over 200 cm of snowfall per season, and a single-stage electric snow blower designed for a Toronto driveway is not the right machine for it.

Troy-Bilt builds both single-stage and two-stage snow blowers. The distinction matters:

**Single-stage snow blowers** use an auger that contacts the ground to scoop and throw snow in one motion. They work well on paved surfaces with lighter snowfall — 15 to 20 cm accumulations on a smooth driveway. They are compact and easier to store.

**Two-stage snow blowers** use an auger to collect snow and a separate high-speed impeller to throw it. They handle heavier accumulations, gravel surfaces (where a single-stage auger would throw gravel), and the packed, heavy snow that comes with a Northern Ontario season. For most rural and suburban properties in the Sault, a two-stage machine is the practical choice.

Key specs to look at:

- **Clearing width:** How wide a path the machine clears per pass. On a double-car driveway or longer rural lane, a wider clearing width saves time.
- **Intake height:** How deep a snowpack the machine can handle in a single pass. The Lake Superior snowbelt around the Sault can see significant single-event accumulations.
- **Electric start:** Worth specifying for Northern Ontario. Pull-starting a cold engine at -20°C is not the way you want to start a snow morning.
- **Heated hand grips:** Not a luxury when you are clearing snow at the coldest end of a Canadian winter.

Contact our service team for current availability on Troy-Bilt snow blower models at Reyco.

#### String Trimmers and Walk-Behind Equipment

For the detail work — trimming fence lines, cleaning up around outbuildings, cutting back along gravel edges — Troy-Bilt builds a gas and battery-powered string trimmer lineup alongside walk-behind push mowers.

For Northern Ontario property use, a gas-powered string trimmer generally handles heavier vegetation and extended run time better than battery alternatives, particularly on larger lots where a single charge may not be sufficient. If your trimming is light and close to a charging point, a battery trimmer is quieter and lower-maintenance.

Troy-Bilt also offers garden tillers — relevant for properties with kitchen gardens or those breaking new ground for beds. If seasonal tilling is part of your property routine, ask us about the tiller line when you come in.
