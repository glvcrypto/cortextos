### What the Mercury 115 FourStroke Is

The Mercury 115 FourStroke is a 115-horsepower, four-stroke outboard motor with electronic fuel injection. It is built on a 2.1-litre, four-cylinder powerhead and weighs 374 lbs.

Those numbers matter more than they might seem in a spec sheet. The four-stroke designation means this motor runs on straight gasoline with no oil mixing. That is relevant to Northern Ontario anglers who run early in the morning when temperatures are still low, who winter-store their motors for five to six months, and who want to fuel up at any marina pump without carrying a separate oil jug.

The electronic fuel injection means cold starts are consistent. You are not adjusting a choke and hoping. You turn the key and the motor starts. In the Sault Ste. Marie area, where May launch day can still see near-freezing morning temperatures, reliable cold-start behaviour is not a convenience feature. It is practical.

The shaft comes in two lengths: 20-inch and 25-inch. Shaft length selection depends on the transom height of the boat you are installing on. We will help you confirm the right length for your hull when you buy.

### Advanced Range Optimisation

Mercury's Advanced Range Optimisation (ARO) system is a fuel management technology built into the 115 FourStroke that adjusts fuel delivery based on load and throttle position to minimize consumption at cruising speeds.

This is relevant to Northern Ontario anglers who are running distances. Goulais Bay, the Spanish River system, the outer reaches of Batchawana Bay, these destinations involve run times that can stack up real fuel consumption over a season. A motor that can trim its fuel delivery at steady-state cruise extends your range and lowers the cost per hour of operation.

We do not have an official per-hour consumption figure to publish here, as it varies by hull, load, and speed. What we can say from customer reports and our own experience is that the Mercury 115 FourStroke runs notably more efficiently than older carbureted outboards of similar horsepower, and noticeably better than some competing EFI motors in the same class.

### Weight and Hull Matching

At 374 lbs, the Mercury 115 FourStroke sits in the right weight range for the aluminium fishing boats we sell in the 16-to-18-foot class. This is not an incidental number.

A motor that is too heavy for the hull shifts weight to the stern, affecting trim and putting stress on the transom over time. A motor that is too light for the hull leaves performance on the table and can make the bow rise unexpectedly at speed, affecting handling.

The 374-lb weight of the Mercury 115 FourStroke is balanced well against hulls like the Princecraft Sport 172, which is rated to 115 hp and has been engineered to handle motors in this weight class. This pairing is the most common combination we configure at Reyco, and the match is not coincidental.

If you are fitting a Mercury 115 FourStroke to an existing hull not purchased from us, bring the hull specs to the conversation and we can confirm the match is appropriate.

### Quiet Operation

Four-stroke outboards run quieter than two-stroke equivalents, and the Mercury 115 FourStroke is notably quiet at idle and at moderate cruise speeds. This matters to Northern Ontario anglers in ways that might not be obvious until you fish alongside a quieter motor.

On the St. Marys River, where walleye fishing often involves slow presentations in current near structure, motor noise that carries through the hull and into the water is a genuine concern among serious anglers. The Mercury 115 FourStroke's four-stroke architecture and vibration-managed powerhead keep the underwater noise signature low compared to carbureted two-strokes in the same horsepower class.

For family use, quieter operation means conversation on the boat does not require shouting over the motor. That is a quality-of-life improvement that shows up on every trip.

### Northern Ontario Conditions

The Mercury 115 FourStroke is built to operate across a temperature range that matches Northern Ontario's season. Start-up in near-freezing conditions, operation through summer heat, and proper winterization for the October-to-May storage window are all within the documented operating parameters of this motor.

The maintenance-free valve train is a practical advantage for Northern Ontario boat owners. Traditional valve train components require periodic adjustment. The Mercury 115 FourStroke's valve train is designed to run through its service life without requiring the kind of maintenance intervals that add cost and complexity to ownership.

The motor also benefits from Mercury's Adaptive Speed Control, which adjusts idle speed in response to engine temperature, eliminating the need for manual idle adjustments when switching between cold start and warm operating conditions. On a Northern Ontario morning launch, this means one less thing to think about before your first cast.

### Spring and Fall Service

Our service department handles spring commissioning and fall winterization for outboard motors. In the compressed Northern Ontario season, the difference between a motor that starts reliably on May long weekend and one that needs a week of troubleshooting is real.

Spring commissioning for an outboard typically includes impeller inspection and replacement if needed, gear lube change, fuel system inspection, battery and electrical checks, and a test run to confirm performance before you get to the water.

Fall winterization for the Mercury 115 FourStroke includes fogging the cylinders, draining the fuel system to prevent gumming, changing the gear lube, and confirming the motor is correctly stored for five to six months of inactivity.

We recommend booking spring service slots before the end of March. In Sault Ste. Marie, the spring rush hits fast and the shop fills up. A motor that goes in for commissioning in April comes out ready before ice-off. A motor that calls in May when everyone else does may not be ready for opening weekend.
