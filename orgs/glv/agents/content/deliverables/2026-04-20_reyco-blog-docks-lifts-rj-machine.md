### How to Choose the Right Dock and Lift for Your Northern Ontario Waterfront

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

No two Northern Ontario waterfronts are the same.

The St. Marys River at the Sault runs with current and fluctuates with the Great Lakes water cycle. Inland lakes in the Algoma district — Spanish River country, Lake Huron's North Shore, the lakes off Highway 17 — tend to be shallower near shore and freeze solid by December. Exposed Georgian Bay shorelines deal with wave action that sheltered river properties never see.

The right dock and lift for a property on the St. Marys is not necessarily the right choice for a camp on Batchawana Bay. This guide is for Northern Ontario property owners who want to buy once and install correctly, rather than making a costly mistake the first spring.

At Reyco Marine, we carry R&J Machine docks and lifts. R&J Machine is a Canadian manufacturer based in Ontario that has been building dock and lift systems since 1976. Their products are engineered for the freeze-thaw cycle, the variable water depths, and the seasonal install-and-remove rhythm that defines life on the water up here. This is not a product line designed for a cottage in Muskoka and carried here as an afterthought.

---

#### Start with Your Waterfront Type

Before you think about specific dock models or lift capacities, answer these four questions:

**1. How deep is the water at your install point?**
R&J Machine pipe dock systems are designed to install in water up to 15 feet deep at the end of the dock. If your property drops off quickly, or stays shallow for a long run, that changes which dock configuration makes sense. Bring a depth reading when you come in — even a rough one helps.

**2. Is your shoreline exposed or sheltered?**
Exposed shorelines — properties that face open water with a long fetch — see wave action that puts real stress on dock connections and lift legs. Sheltered coves and river properties are gentler. R&J Machine builds configurations for both, but the hardware selection is different.

**3. How much current does your water have?**
This is especially relevant for river properties and properties on flowages or channels. Current affects leg placement, anchoring, and in some cases the dock profile itself. St. Marys River properties are a common situation where this matters.

**4. What are you docking?**
A 14-foot aluminum fishing boat sits differently in a lift than a 20-foot bowrider. If you are running a PWC alongside a boat, that is a different setup than a single-slip dock with a lift. Know your watercraft before you buy.

---

#### Choosing Your R&J Machine Dock

R&J Machine offers several dock configurations. Their main types include Truss Pipe Docks (the most common choice for Northern Ontario seasonal use), Floating Truss docks for properties where water levels shift, and Lift-Up docks designed to come in and out of the water annually without heavy equipment. At Reyco, we stock the configurations that work best for Northern Ontario conditions — systems designed to go in and come out every year with a small crew.

Key considerations:

- **Pipe dock vs. floating dock:** R&J's Truss Pipe Docks anchor to the lake bed and work best where water depth is consistent and the bottom is firm enough to hold the legs. Their floating dock systems — including the WaveX and LUX Floating lines — adjust to water level changes, which is useful on the Great Lakes system where levels shift year to year.

- **Section length and configuration:** R&J docks are modular. A simple straight dock into deeper water. An L-shape or T-shape for multiple slips. Tell us your boat count and the space you are working with.

- **Decking material:** R&J offers three decking options: Western Red Cedar, PT Sienna pressure-treated lumber, and Pro PVC composite in multiple colours. For Northern Ontario winters, Pro PVC holds up better over years of freeze-thaw cycling than natural wood. #### Choosing Your Lift

If you are pulling a boat or PWC out of the water at the end of the season — or after every trip — you need a lift matched to the weight of what you are lifting.

**Capacity first:** R&J Machine cantilever lifts are available in 1,500 lb, 2,000 lb, 3,000 lb, 4,000 lb, 5,000 lb, and 6,000 lb ratings. The most common mistake is buying a lift rated just above your boat's dry weight. Factor in the weight of the boat, fuel, gear, and the engine. Lift capacity should clear your realistic loaded weight by a meaningful margin. [FLAG: Casey to confirm which lift capacity tiers Reyco currently stocks.]

**Cantilever vs. vertical lift:** R&J builds both. Cantilever lifts are the standard choice for most Northern Ontario installs — they work in shallower water and are easier to service. Vertical lifts are suited to installations where water levels fluctuate significantly, as the V-frame design handles a wider depth range. If you are on the Great Lakes system and have seen significant level changes in the past few years, ask us about vertical.

**PWC considerations:** R&J Machine makes dedicated personal watercraft lifts for Sea-Doos and similar craft. If you are lifting a PWC alongside or instead of a boat, confirm you are looking at a PWC-rated lift or cradle — the geometry is different from a boat lift and it matters.

---

#### Seasonal Install and Removal in Northern Ontario

This is the part that gets overlooked until the first time you have to do it.

Northern Ontario docks go in May and come out October, at the latest. On ice-prone lakes and river properties, leaving dock sections in the water past freeze-up is how you lose equipment. R&J Machine systems are designed for annual install and remove — with a small crew and a bit of practice, it should not take more than a few hours once you know the system.

When you purchase from Reyco, we walk you through the install process. If it is your first time setting up this dock system, come in before your planned install date and talk to us. We have seen the common mistakes and we can help you avoid them.

---

#### What to Bring When You Come In

To help us match you to the right R&J Machine dock and lift configuration, bring:

- A rough sketch or photo of your property showing the shoreline, any existing structures, and the install area
- A depth reading at the end of your planned dock run (even approximate)
- The make, model, and approximate weight of the boat or PWC you plan to dock
- Your seasonal install window — properties that ice earlier need to think about fall removal timing from the start
