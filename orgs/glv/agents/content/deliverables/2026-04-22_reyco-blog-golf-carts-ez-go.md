### Golf Carts for Cottage Country: What to Know Before You Buy in Northern Ontario

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

A golf cart is not just for the golf course anymore.

In Northern Ontario, the people buying them are cottage owners who want a quiet way to get around a large property without pulling out the truck, acreage owners who need a second vehicle that does not require a licence plate, and camp operators who want their guests off ATVs but still mobile. The market for utility golf carts and personal electric vehicles has grown steadily in this region, and the machines on offer today are a long way from the old gas-powered carts you would find at a resort in the 1990s.

At Reyco Marine, we carry EZ-GO golf carts and personal utility vehicles. This guide is for Northern Ontario buyers who want to understand what a modern golf cart can do before they come in.

#### Gas vs Electric: What Makes Sense in Northern Ontario

This is the first decision most buyers land on, and in Northern Ontario it has some specific considerations.

**Electric carts** are quieter, require less routine maintenance (no oil changes, spark plugs, or fuel stabiliser before winter storage), and are increasingly powered by lithium-ion batteries that hold a charge better in cold temperatures than older lead-acid setups. If your cottage has reliable shore power or a generator, an electric cart is worth a close look.

**Gas carts** offer unlimited range on any trip where you have fuel available, which matters if you are running long distances across a large property or cannot count on consistent charging. The trade-off is engine maintenance and the need to run the tank down or use fuel stabiliser before seasonal storage — something that matters when you are putting the cart away in October and pulling it out in May.

#### Cottage and Acreage Use in the Algoma District

A few Northern Ontario-specific considerations when choosing an EZ-GO:

**Terrain.** Most golf carts are designed for relatively level ground. If your property has significant elevation changes, ask about models with higher-torque drivetrains and appropriate tyre sizing. A standard cart that handles a groomed fairway will labour on a steep gravel driveway with a loaded cargo bed.

**Storage.** Golf carts are seasonal in Northern Ontario. That means winterising in the fall and first-of-season checks in the spring. Electric carts need battery maintenance through the off-season — particularly lead-acid units, which discharge faster in cold storage. Lithium units are more tolerant but still benefit from a maintenance charge mid-winter if stored in an unheated space.

**Licensing.** Golf carts are not street-legal on Ontario roads by default. If you plan to cross a public road or travel on a municipal street at any point, check your municipality's bylaws. The rules vary by region and are the buyer's responsibility to confirm before purchase.

**Hauling.** If light hauling is part of the plan — moving firewood, feed, supplies around the property — look at models with a rated rear cargo capacity and a factory or dealer-installed tow receiver. EZ-GO utility models are built for this kind of work.
