### Choosing the Right Hisun Side-by-Side for Northern Ontario: A Practical Guide

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

Northern Ontario asks a lot of a side-by-side.

The trails around Sault Ste. Marie are not groomed resort paths. They are cut through Crown land, climbing rocky ridgelines in the Algoma Highlands, running through soft spring mud, and pushing through hardpack that turns to ice by November. The Algoma ATV Club alone maintains hundreds of kilometres of trail that chew through lesser machines.

Add to that the farms and acreages north of the Sault — properties that need a reliable utility vehicle for moving firewood, feed, and equipment across terrain that a quad can't handle alone — and you have a clear picture of what a side-by-side needs to do around here.

At Reyco Marine, we carry Hisun side-by-sides because they handle this environment well and because the price-to-capability ratio is better than most brands at equivalent specs. This post is a practical guide to choosing the right Hisun model for your situation.

---

#### Trail Riding in the Algoma District

If your primary use is trail riding — Algoma ATV Club routes, Crown land access roads, connecting to the Trans Canada Trail network — you want a machine that is responsive, low enough to feel stable on side-slopes, and narrow enough to fit the tighter trail sections without dragging brush on both doors.

Hisun's sport-oriented models — including the Strike 250R and Stryker 750X — are built for this kind of riding. The Strike 250R is a compact youth-friendly machine suited to tighter trails; the Stryker 750X steps up for adults who want a sport-trail UTV with more displacement. Key things to confirm with us at the dealership before you buy:

- **Width:** Most Algoma trails are designed for machines under 60 inches. Confirm the width of any model you are considering, especially if you plan to run marked club trails.
- **Suspension travel:** More is better for the rocky Algoma terrain. Ask about front and rear travel specs on the model you are looking at.
- **Fuel capacity:** Crown land riding can put you 50+ kilometres from the truck. Know your range.

We can walk you through the current Hisun lineup at the store and match the specs to where you plan to ride.

---

#### Farm and Utility Use

If you are hauling more than you are chasing trails — moving wood, pulling a small drag, carrying fencing materials on a rural property north of the Sault — the calculation shifts to cargo capacity and towing.

Hisun's utility-focused models — including the Sector 550 EPS, the HS 500, and the HS 400 — offer larger cargo beds and higher tow ratings than the sport lineup. The Sector 550 EPS adds electronic power steering, which makes a real difference when you are manoeuvring on uneven ground with a loaded bed. What matters most for Northern Ontario utility use:

- **Cargo bed capacity:** Useful load in the bed. If you are moving 400 lbs of firewood at a time, confirm the rated capacity.
- **Tow rating:** Useful if you plan to pull an ATV trailer or small utility attachment behind.
- **Ground clearance:** Higher clearance handles the seasonal mud better. Spring breakup on Northern Ontario properties is not forgiving.

---

#### Winter Use

Side-by-sides see year-round use in Northern Ontario in a way that surprises people from southern Ontario. Snowploughs, tracks, and cab enclosures convert many Hisun models into functional winter work machines.

If winter use is part of your plan, ask us about:

- **Track kit compatibility** for the model you are considering — not all models accept the same kits
- **Cab enclosure options** — important for temperatures that regularly hit -20°C in Sault Ste. Marie winters
- **Battery performance** in cold weather — confirm cold-start performance rating on the engine
