## Option A — Wait for Domain, Use @reycomarine.com Bylines

- **Byline format:** "Reviewed by Casey Davieaux, Owner — casey@reycomarine.com"
- **Pro:** Cleanest long-term. Right email, right domain, maximum trust signal.
- **Con:** Delays product page completion. Can't launch with working contact links until Dealer Spike releases the domain. Blocks the 207-product content fill until transfer completes.

---

## Option B — Byline Without Email (Recommended)

- **Byline format:** "Reviewed by Casey Davieaux, Owner, Reyco Marine"
- **No email in the byline.** Link to the Contact page instead if an action is needed.
- **Pro:** No domain dependency. No info@ violation. Full authority signal from the name + title. Can go live on launch day. Email can be added later via a single find-and-replace when domain transfers.
- **Con:** No direct contact email in the review block — buyers who want to reach the reviewer click through to contact page.

**This is the recommended path.** It unblocks the product fill entirely, costs nothing to swap later, and doesn't create dead links on launch day.

---

## Option C — Temporary GLV Staff Byline, Swap Post-Transfer

- **Byline format:** "Reviewed by [GLV staff name], Reyco Marine Product Specialist — [name]@glvmarketing.ca"
- **Pro:** Has a live, working email address on launch day.
- **Con:** Puts a non-Reyco person's name on a Reyco product review. Looks odd if anyone looks closely. Requires a bulk swap post-transfer. More work, weaker signal.

Not recommended unless user needs a working email link on day one and Option A is not feasible in time.
