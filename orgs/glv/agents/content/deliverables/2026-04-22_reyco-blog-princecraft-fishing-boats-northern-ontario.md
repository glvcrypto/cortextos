### Princecraft Fishing Boats for Northern Ontario: What Makes Them the Right Choice

*By Casey Davieaux, Reyco Marine*

---

If you have spent any time talking to anglers in the Sault Ste. Marie area about aluminium fishing boats, Princecraft comes up consistently. It is not because of advertising. It is because people who bought one tend to still be running it ten years later, and the ones who have bought two tend to buy the same brand again.

At Reyco Marine, we are the only authorized Princecraft dealer in the Sault Ste. Marie area. We have been fitting Princecraft hulls to Northern Ontario anglers long enough to have an honest picture of why these boats hold up on the water up here — and what to look for when you are choosing the right model.

---

#### Why Aluminium for Northern Ontario

The case for aluminium starts with the environment.

Northern Ontario is not a single fishing environment. The St. Marys River runs with real current and variable structure. The inland lakes of the Algoma district can go from glassy to choppy in an afternoon when a westerly comes through. Out toward Batchawana Bay and the Lake Superior coast, you are dealing with open water that does not care what size your boat is.

Aluminium handles all of this better than fibreglass for the Northern Ontario market for a few specific reasons.

**Weight.** Aluminium boats are lighter than equivalent fibreglass hulls. Lighter means easier to trailer, easier to launch at single-lane ramps on Algoma back lakes, and quicker to get on plane with a matched motor.

**Durability.** Aluminium does not crack from freeze-thaw cycles the way fibreglass can. Northern Ontario boats sit on trailers through October, November, and freeze over in unheated outbuildings. The hull is going from cold to warm and back again, repeatedly, for five or six months of the year. Aluminium is built for that.

**Repairability.** A stone strike or a gravel launch contact on an aluminium hull is typically weldable. The equivalent damage on fibreglass often requires extensive layup work. In a region where service access matters, a hull that can be repaired locally by a competent fabricator is a practical advantage.

**Rust resistance.** Marine-grade aluminium alloy does not rust. It oxidizes on the surface and that oxidization forms a protective layer. Combined with routine maintenance, an aluminium boat in Northern Ontario can outlast the angler who bought it.

---

#### Princecraft's Advantage: Canadian Design

Princecraft has been building aluminium boats in Princeville, Quebec, since 1958. That is not a trivia fact — it is relevant context.

Boat design is informed by the water the designer is building for. Princecraft's engineering and hull geometry is developed by people whose reference frame is Canadian fishing lakes, Canadian river systems, and Canadian seasonal conditions. The hull depth, beam width, and weight distribution decisions on the Sport line reflect decades of feedback from anglers who fish water that looks like ours.

This is a real distinction from some imported aluminium brands that are engineered primarily for flat-water lake environments in the American southeast, then marketed into Northern Ontario without adjustment. The boats work in some conditions. They are less comfortable in others.

Princecraft also backs their hulls with a limited lifetime warranty. For a boat that is going to see a decade or more of hard Northern Ontario use, that warranty substance matters — and it is only available when you purchase from an authorized dealer who can actually help you exercise it if something goes wrong.

---

#### The Sport 172: The Northern Ontario Standard

The model we fit to Northern Ontario anglers more than any other in the Princecraft lineup is the Sport 172. It is a 17-foot 2-inch deep-V aluminium hull rated to 115 horsepower with a 79-inch beam and six-person capacity.

The reason it sells the way it does is that the specifications match the Northern Ontario use case almost exactly.

**Deep-V hull.** The deep-V geometry cuts through chop rather than slamming through it. On the St. Marys River when wind and current run against each other, or on a Batchawana Bay run when the lake is running a 2-foot swell, a flat or modified-V hull is working against the water. The Sport 172's deep-V tracks and rides in a way that flat-bottomed fishing boats do not.

**17'2" length.** Long enough to carry gear, run at comfortable cruise speeds, and handle moderate chop without feeling like you are on a cork. Short enough to back down a standard Algoma boat launch, fit on a typical half-ton trailer, and manoeuvre without a spotter.

**79" beam.** Wide enough to fish standing without feeling like you are balancing. For anglers making full casts from the casting deck, or working both sides of the boat while anchored on a walleye spot, the width matters. It also gives passengers appropriate lateral clearance — relevant when you have a 10-year-old as your first mate.

**Livewell and casting decks.** The Sport 172 comes with a livewell for keeping bait and catch alive, rod storage, and raised casting decks fore and aft. These are not afterthoughts. The elevated casting decks give you a better vantage point when scanning structure, and they get your feet above the cockpit floor when you are standing and working a spot for hours.

**45-litre fuel capacity.** On a full-day run across Goulais Bay or up into the Spanish River system, 45 litres is a realistic range without stopping constantly.

If you want a detailed look at the Sport 172's specifications and why we pair it with the Mercury 115 FourStroke, see the Sport 172 product page on our website.

#### The Motor Pairing: Why Mercury 115 FourStroke

The Sport 172 is rated to a maximum of 115 horsepower. In our experience, the right motor for this hull is the Mercury 115 FourStroke — not because it is the only option, but because the match is excellent.

The Sport 172 is balanced and optimized for the 115 hp class. Putting a smaller motor on it means leaving performance on the table. Putting a larger motor on it means exceeding the transom rating, which affects handling, warranty, and long-term structural wear.

The Mercury 115 FourStroke at 374 lbs fits the hull balance well. The fuel economy at cruise means you are getting realistic range out of that 45-litre tank. The Mercury's electronic fuel injection handles Northern Ontario cold starts reliably — including May launches where air temperatures can still be near freezing.

The other reason we recommend this pairing specifically is warranty coverage. The Mercury 3-plus-3 FourStroke warranty (three years factory, three years extended, through an authorized dealer purchase) aligns well with the Princecraft lifetime hull warranty. When you buy a Sport 172 with a Mercury 115 at Reyco, both major components are covered under manufacturer warranty programs that we can actually help you use.

For a full breakdown of the Mercury 115 FourStroke and what it does for Northern Ontario boaters, we cover that in our post on choosing a Mercury outboard for Northern Ontario.

#### Who Buys Princecraft in the Sault Ste. Marie Area

After years of watching who buys these boats and how they use them, we have a clear picture.

**The serious recreational angler.** Someone who fishes twice a week during the season, targets walleye on the St. Marys and pike on Algoma lakes, maybe runs toward Lake Superior on good weather days. This person wants a reliable platform built for actual fishing — not a lifestyle boat with a rod holder.

**Local tournament anglers.** The Sport 172 is a common choice among anglers fishing local circuits in the Sault area. The casting decks, livewell, and hull performance are appropriate for tournament fishing in Northern Ontario water conditions. It is not designed for the bass tournament circuit in Louisiana, but for what we fish around here, it works.

**Families where fishing is the point.** Six-person capacity means two adults and a realistic number of kids. The wide beam keeps younger passengers away from the edge. The cockpit layout gives everyone something to do. If the family activity is fishing, not tubing and wakeboarding, the Sport 172 makes sense as a family boat.

**Buyers stepping up from smaller aluminium boats.** Many Sport 172 purchases at Reyco are from anglers who have been running 14-to-16-foot boats for years. The move to 17'2" with a wider beam is noticeable on the water. The improvement in chop tolerance and stability tends to be the thing those buyers mention first.

#### Spring Service and Commissioning

In the Sault Ste. Marie area, the spring service window is short and it fills up.

The entire regional boating community is trying to get on the water at the same time. Boats that came in for winterization in October come back in April and May, all at once. Service slots for spring commissioning are limited by technician hours, not by willingness to take the work.

If you are purchasing a Princecraft this season — or if you already have one and want spring commissioning done before the opener — contact us before the end of March to get your slot confirmed. A boat that comes in for commissioning in April is on the water before opening weekend. A boat that calls in mid-May may not be.

Spring commissioning covers impeller inspection, gear lube change, fuel system inspection, battery and electrical checks, and a test run. It is the difference between a motor that starts on demand on the first warm Saturday of May and one that produces a conversation piece instead.
