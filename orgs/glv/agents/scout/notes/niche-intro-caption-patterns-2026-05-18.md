## Part 1: Pattern Research — 5-10 High-Performing Intro/Agency-Overview Posts

### Post Pattern Set A — Metric-Led Opener (Instagram / LinkedIn)
**Source:** insidea.com/blog/instagram/captions/marketing-agency/ — documented caption examples from marketing agency niche

| # | Caption opening | Hook type | Platform fit |
|---|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| A1 | "Proven, not promised. Our latest campaign delivered a 43% lift in conversions — here's how we got there." | Data-point + implied story | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| A2 | "When performance meets precision, your creative becomes a revenue generator." | Reframe (feature → outcome) | LinkedIn |
| A3 | "Want conversion rates that make your CFO smile? Start with a strategy you can measure, tweak, and scale." | Question → benefit close | Instagram |
| A4 | "Great marketing isn't loud — it's tuned. Relevance beats reach every time." | Contrarian + punchy | Instagram, Threads |
| A5 | "Visual grabs attention. Caption earns curiosity. Master both." | Parallel construction | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| A6 | "Write like a human. Think like a strategist. Optimize like a machine." | Three-part framework | LinkedIn, Threads |
| A7 | "We don't do cookie-cutter — and onboarding is where the custom strategy begins." | Differentiator framing | LinkedIn intro-3 |
| A8 | "Day one of strategy. Plenty of whiteboard squares to fill. Let's start shaping something memorable." | Behind-the-scenes + momentum | Instagram, Facebook |

**Pattern observed across A1–A8:**
- Metric or contrarian hook in the FIRST clause (before the dash or period)
- Second clause delivers the "so what" or the promise — never the services list
- No em-dashes in native form (replaced by " — " spaced dashes or short sentences)
- CTA is implied by story continuation, not commanded in the opener

## Part 3: Per-Platform Dominant Pattern + Intro Fit

### Instagram

**Dominant pattern (2026):**
Carousel intro posts in the SMB digital marketing agency niche lead with a metric or contrarian hook in the first 100 characters, deliver a 6-10 slide carousel where each slide has one job (problem → implication → solution → proof → CTA), and close with a comment-keyword CTA that triggers an auto-DM. The caption itself is short — 100–200 chars — because the carousel content carries the education. Saves and DM sends are the primary signals the algorithm rewards in 2026; likes are secondary. [Source: truefuturemedia.com/articles/instagram-reach-2026, buffer.com/resources/instagram-algorithms]

**Intro-1 (who is GLV) fit:** Contrarian or data-point hook → 2–3 sentence company introduction with local anchor ("We're the marketing team in Sault Ste. Marie that...") → comment-keyword CTA for a free resource.

**Intro-2 (what we do) fit:** Problem hook naming the pain ("Most SSM businesses are invisible on Google Maps") → 1-sentence service statement → result proof → comment-keyword CTA.

**Intro-3 (how we work) fit:** Process reveal in the carousel (3-step list, one step per slide) → caption as a one-liner teaser ("Here's how we go from audit to ranking in 6 weeks — swipe") → save CTA.

---

### Facebook

**Dominant pattern (2026):**
Facebook organic reach for business pages has declined; what still works is personal-feel content, local anchors, and video-forward posts. For intro/agency-overview posts specifically: shorter captions (40–150 chars), conversational tone, and geographic specificity ("We're based in Sault Ste. Marie and we work with local businesses who...") outperform generic brand copy. The "see more" truncation happens earlier on mobile — first sentence must stand alone. Reaction bait (asking a question that invites quick reactions) performs better than pure educational content on Facebook. [Source: postfa.st/blog/complete-guide-facebook-2026, docommunication.io/blog/trending-instagram-captions]

`unverified:` Comment-to-DM keyword automation on Facebook (via ManyChat) is technically functional and documented in tools, but organic reach on Facebook business pages for new accounts limits the comment volume enough that the CTA works best when paired with a small paid boost.

**Intro-1 (who is GLV) fit:** Warm local introduction — "We're GLV Marketing, a Sault Ste. Marie agency that helps local businesses get found on Google..." → community-feel question close ("What's your biggest challenge with getting found online?").

**Intro-2 (what we do) fit:** Short problem statement → service summary in 2–3 lines → call to tag a local business owner who needs this.

**Intro-3 (how we work) fit:** Behind-the-scenes feel ("Here's what working with us actually looks like...") → 3 bullet process → "DM us AUDIT to see where your business stands."

---

### Threads

**Dominant pattern (2026):**
Threads is text-first, algorithm rewards conversational hooks that invite reply. Short, punchy, opinionated. No link previews (links don't render well). Best agency content on Threads is contrarian takes and questions. No carousel format — threads are linear, so a multi-post thread functions as the equivalent. Intro posts work as a short statement of position ("Here's why most local businesses are invisible on Google — and it's not what you think") + a reply or thread to expand. [Source: outfy.com/blog/threads-marketing, sproutsocial.com/insights/threads-seo, embedsocial.com/blog/new-threads-features-2026]

**Key 2026 update confirmed:** Threads now supports search indexing; keyword-rich captions matter for discoverability on Threads, similar to Instagram. [Source: sproutsocial.com/insights/threads-seo]

`unverified:` Comment-to-DM keyword automation is NOT available on Threads natively as of May 2026. ManyChat Threads integration is unconfirmed. CTAs on Threads should route to Instagram or a direct link.

**Intro-1 (who is GLV) fit:** One punchy positioning statement ("We're the marketing agency SSM businesses call when they're invisible on Google") → question to invite reply ("What's your main struggle with online visibility right now?").

**Intro-2 (what we do) fit:** Contrarian take ("Local SEO doesn't have to be expensive. Most of what moves the needle is free — just ignored.") → brief service mention → route to Instagram for the carousel breakdown.

**Intro-3 (how we work) fit:** Process-as-thread (3-reply thread, one step per reply) → final reply with CTA to book or DM on Instagram.

---

### LinkedIn

**Dominant pattern (2026):**
Multi-image carousels have the highest average engagement rate of all LinkedIn content formats at 6.60%. [Source: socialpilot.co/blog/linkedin-carousel] For agency intro posts, the pattern is: hook in the first line (before the "see more" cut), a 6-10 slide carousel telling a clear story, and a question-based CTA in the caption close. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards dwell time (people swiping through all slides) and comment-replies over likes. "Comment X below" triggers are effective on LinkedIn but typically without auto-DM automation — the agency manually follows up. [Source: contentin.io/blog/linkedin-algorithm-2026, digitalapplied.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm-2026]

**B2B framing on LinkedIn:** The audience skews owner/manager. Position GLV as a trusted advisor, not a vendor. "We work with" language outperforms "We provide" language.

**Intro-1 (who is GLV) fit:** Personal-founder angle ("I started GLV because I watched SSM businesses lose leads they didn't know they were missing...") → team/company context → CTA to comment with their biggest marketing challenge.

**Intro-2 (what we do) fit:** Metric-led ("Our clients average X% more Google Maps calls within 60 days...") → 3 service pillars → question CTA.

**Intro-3 (how we work) fit:** Process carousel (6 slides: problem → our audit → what we find → what we fix → result → what's next for you) → caption asks "Which of these steps do you think most agencies skip?" → comment-invite CTA.

## Part 4: "Comment WORD" CTA — Converting Keywords + Lead Magnet Promises

### How the Mechanism Works

When an Instagram or Facebook post includes "Comment [KEYWORD] below" in the caption, ManyChat (or equivalent tool: Manychat, Inrō, LinkDM) detects comments containing that keyword and fires an automated DM sequence to the commenter. The DM delivers a lead magnet (PDF, link, checklist, Loom) and can optionally capture an email before delivery. [Source: manychat.com/blog/what-are-keywords-ig, sumgenius.ai/blog/comment-to-dm-automation-instagram-guide-2026, inro.social/blog/instagram-automation-guide-2025]

### Confirmed Converting Keywords in the Niche

| Keyword | Trigger context | Lead magnet delivered | Source |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|--------|
| **AUDIT** | "Comment AUDIT and I'll send you a free [Google Maps / social media] audit checklist" | Free audit template or personalized Loom | manychat.com keywords article, agencyanalytics.com lead-magnets |
| **GUIDE** | "Comment GUIDE for the full [topic] breakdown" | PDF guide or swipe file | sumgenius.ai, inro.social |
| **CHECKLIST** | "Comment CHECKLIST and I'll DM you the exact list we use with every new client" | Checklist PDF | inro.social automation guide 2025 |
| **FREE** | "Comment FREE to get the resource" | Any lead magnet | pragnakalp.com manychat guide |
| **MAP** | "Comment MAP for our local ranking guide" | Local SEO / Google Maps guide | manychat.com — city-name-as-keyword pattern adapted |
| **BOOK** | "Comment BOOK and I'll send you a link to schedule a free call" | Calendly or booking link | sumgenius.ai |
| **INFO** | "Comment INFO for details" | General info / pricing overview | sumgenius.ai |

**Most relevant for GLV's intro posts specifically:**

| Keyword | GLV use case | Lead magnet promise |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| **AUDIT** | All 3 intro types | "Free 15-min GBP audit — we'll tell you exactly why you're not showing up on Google Maps and what to fix first" |
| **MAP** | Intro-2 (what we do) | "Free Google Maps ranking guide for SSM businesses — the 5 things we check on every profile" |
| **CHECKLIST** | Intro-3 (how we work) | "The exact checklist we run on every new client's Google Business Profile — free" |

### Lead Magnet Promises That Convert (Confirmed in Agency Niche)

1. **Free audit** — highest-converting offer for service agencies. Must be specific ("free GBP audit" outperforms "free marketing audit"). [Source: agencyanalytics.com/blog/marketing-agency-lead-magnets, insites.com/platform/audits/local-seo]
2. **Checklist** — "simple to repurpose from existing blog content" and ranks as top lead magnet format for agencies. [Source: agencyanalytics.com]
3. **Template** — most popular format overall; "email, spreadsheet, social media" templates. [Source: agencyanalytics.com]
4. **Mini-guide / PDF** — works when hyper-specific. "Free SEO guide" generic = low conversion. "Free Google Maps ranking guide for [city] businesses" = high intent match. [Source: agencyanalytics.com, dintellects.com/10-high-converting-lead-magnet-examples]
5. **Free strategy call / consultation** — high-intent but lower volume; works best as a second-step CTA after the checklist/audit delivers value. [Source: agencyanalytics.com]

`unverified:` In comparable niches (real estate, med spa) auto-DM comment funnels convert at 64–85% from comment to resource claim when the keyword is directly tied to what the post content promises. [Source: sumgenius.ai — documented case studies in non-agency verticals; direct agency vertical equivalent not found in this search]

### Keywords to Avoid

- Generic single letters or abbreviations (too many false triggers from unrelated comments)
- "YES" — overused and triggers too broadly; leads to poor-quality list (manychat.com)
- `unverified:` All-caps long phrases ("SENDMETHEGUIDE") reduce comment friction resistance — simpler single words perform better

## Part 5: Recommended Caption Formula for GLV's 12 Intros

Based on patterns above, each of the 12 captions (4 platforms × 3 intros) should be built on this skeleton:

```
[HOOK — contrarian OR metric OR problem-name, ≤100 chars, no weasel words]

[1–2 sentences of body — either story snippet, service statement, or process reveal]

[CTA line — "Comment [KEYWORD] below and I'll DM you [specific promise]" 
 OR save/question close for LinkedIn/Threads]

[Hashtags: 3–5 niche-specific, appended or in first comment]
```

**Intro-1 (who is GLV) skeleton:**
> "[Contrarian take on local marketing]. We're GLV — [brief local anchor]. [One social proof or mission statement]. Comment [KEYWORD] and we'll send you [specific resource]."

**Intro-2 (what we do) skeleton:**
> "[Problem name + local specificity]. [Service statement that names the outcome, not the tactic]. [One metric or client-type reference]. Comment [KEYWORD] to get [specific deliverable]."

**Intro-3 (how we work) skeleton:**
> "Here's exactly what happens when a local SSM business starts working with us: [3-step process in 3 short lines or as carousel]. [Trust signal — no contract / no jargon / guaranteed]. Comment [KEYWORD] to book a free [15-min audit / strategy call]."
