## TL;DR — The 3-2-1 weekly cadence

Per Sprout Social, PostFast, Buffer, and QuickFrame 2026 guidance:

> **3 Reels + 2 feed posts + 1 promotional post per week** is the current sweet spot for FB Pages in organic growth mode.

Reels carry **3-5x the reach** of standard feed posts because Meta is aggressively pushing short-form video distribution to compete with TikTok. Quality beats frequency — posting 3-4 high-value posts/week beats daily filler.

Bank this as the **GLV FB Page baseline cadence** unless Aiden overrides.

## Content type 1 — Short-form Reels (3x/week)

**Why:** Reels are the single highest-leverage format for organic page growth in 2026 per QuickFrame's 2026 marketer guide. Meta has been pushing them aggressively post-launch and continues to in 2026.

**Format specs (T2 — Sprout Social + PostFast + QuickFrame):**
- **Length:** 15-60 seconds. Hard cap 90 seconds. Anything past 60s sees engagement drop-off.
- **Hook:** First 3 seconds must grab attention or the algorithm de-prioritizes.
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical (same as IG Reels — direct cross-post pathway).
- **Captions on screen:** Mandatory. ~85% of FB video is watched sound-off.
- **CTA:** Soft (single line at the end), not aggressive. "DM us 'audit' for a free 15-min Loom" type.

**5 GLV-specific Reel formulas:**

1. **"What I'd check on your Google Business Profile in 60 seconds"** — Aiden or Ben on camera, screen-share style, walk through 3 GBP fields.
2. **"Local SEO myth that won't die"** — counter-intuitive hook, debunk in 45s. (Example: "You don't need to post on GBP every day. Here's what actually moves the needle.")
3. **"How we audit a new client site in 3 minutes"** — speedrun-style screen capture of the GLV intake process.
4. **"This [local SSM business type] made one change and got X result"** — case study Reel, anonymized if needed.
5. **"BTS of a GLV strategy call"** — Aiden + Ben at the whiteboard / on Zoom planning a client move. Authenticity beats polish in 2026 per Postiz + SocialPilot.

**Cadence:** 3 Reels/week, ideally Mon / Wed / Fri.

## Content type 2 — Feed posts (2x/week)

**Why:** Feed posts build trust and authority where Reels build awareness. PostFast 2026: "Reels bring new people in through discovery, feed posts keep people around through trust."

**Format:** Single image OR carousel (FB supports 2-10 image carousels natively unlike Threads/Blotato pathway — different from [[threads-publishing-rules-2026-05-18]]) OR text-led post with one image. Long-form text posts (300-800 chars) outperform short bursts on FB Pages — opposite of Threads behaviour.

**5 GLV-specific feed-post formulas:**

1. **Case study micro-post** — 1 client, 1 metric, 1 image (before/after screenshot, GBP insights chart, local-pack ranking). 300-500 chars. CTA to read the full case study on glvmarketing.ca.
2. **Local business shoutout** — feature a SSM business GLV admires (NOT a client — pre-relationship value). Tag them. Builds local goodwill, often gets reciprocal share.
3. **Tip post with carousel** — 5-7 slide carousel teaching one concept (e.g. "5 things to fix on your homepage this weekend"). Carousels get ~1.4x engagement vs single images per Buffer 2026 data.
4. **Question post** — "What's the one marketing thing you keep meaning to fix but never do?" Drives comments. Per QuickFrame, "reply to every comment in the first 2 hours" measurably lifts reach.
5. **Long-form thought piece** — 600-800 char Aiden POV on a current local marketing topic. Lower-frequency (1x/month), higher trust per post.

**Cadence:** 2 feed posts/week, ideally Tue + Thu (interleaving Reel days).

## Content type 3 — Promotional post (1x/week)

**Why:** Without a promo cadence, the page builds audience but doesn't convert. Limit to 1/week so the page doesn't read as sales channel.

**Format:** Single image or short Reel (15-30s) with explicit offer. Always include verifiable proof point (testimonial, before/after metric, named client with consent).

**3 GLV-specific promo formulas:**

1. **Service spotlight** — one service (Local SEO / Web Build / Socials), one outcome, one CTA. ~250 chars. "We just helped [client] hit [metric] in [timeframe]. Want a free audit? DM 'audit'."
2. **Testimonial repost** — client quote as graphic, attribution, soft CTA. Per Cristanta Digital Marketing 2026 ideas: "real customers on video, connect back to their socials, lets people verify the review."
3. **Limited-window offer** — "Free 30-min strategy call for first 5 SSM businesses to DM this week." Scarcity + locality.

**Cadence:** 1 promo/week, ideally Sat (lower competition for feed real estate weekends).

## Content type 4 — Stories (daily background channel)

**Why:** Stories don't count toward the 3-2-1 weekly cadence — they're a separate top-of-mind channel. Per PostFast 2026 + ICUC, Stories keep brands "top-of-mind through repeat attention."

**Format:** Vertical 9:16, ~15s per slide, polls / questions / sliders for engagement. Auto-expires 24h.

**GLV daily Stories rhythm:**
- **Mon:** "What we're working on this week" — 2-3 slides BTS
- **Tue:** Repost the day's feed post as Story with sticker
- **Wed:** Repost the day's Reel
- **Thu:** Poll or question sticker
- **Fri:** Client win or shoutout
- **Sat:** Local-life slice (Aiden/Ben at a SSM event, dog walk, etc. — humanity)
- **Sun:** Recap Highlight added to permanent "This week" Highlight reel

**Cost:** ~5 min/day. **Reward:** keeps GLV in the daily home-feed Stories ribbon at the top of FB.

## Behind-the-scenes content (within the 20% engagement bucket)

Per SocialPilot 2026 + Postiz: BTS is **highly effective in 2026** specifically because audiences are tired of polished output and craving authenticity.

**GLV BTS angles that work:**
- Aiden + Ben at the whiteboard planning a client strategy
- Screen-record of the actual tools GLV uses (Ahrefs, GBP dashboard, Looker Studio) — narrate decisions
- "What it looks like to be a GLV client in week 1" — Loom-style walk-through
- Outtakes / bloopers from a Reel shoot
- Office / workspace tour
- "Why we built [a tool / process]" origin stories

**Format:** Short Reels (30-60s) or feed-post photo+long-caption. Rotate ~1/week within the engagement bucket.

## Local-event posting

SSM has a strong event calendar (Bon Soo Feb, Rotaryfest Jul, marine-season openers May, etc. per Tourism SSM page). FB Page should ride these for organic local reach.

**Pattern:**
- **Pre-event:** 1 post 7 days out with "we'll be there" or "5 local businesses we're excited to see at [event]"
- **During-event:** 2-3 Stories live from the event (or repost attendee Stories with permission)
- **Post-event:** 1 recap feed post with photo + tag local businesses who attended

**Don't:** Hijack the event hashtag with promo. Local audiences notice and downvote.

## Testimonials and case studies — the highest-converting Page content

Per HubSpot, Meta for Business case-studies portal, and Cristanta Digital Marketing 2026 ideas: client wins are the single most conversion-impactful FB Page format.

**Best-practice elements (T2):**
1. **Real client name + photo** (with consent, in writing).
2. **Specific metric** ("calls from GBP went from 4/mo to 31/mo in 90 days") not vague ("crushed it").
3. **Time period** ("90 days" not "fast").
4. **Format:** Video testimonial > photo + quote > text-only. Per HubSpot: "try to get real customers on video and connect back to their social media handles or company websites so people can verify the review."
5. **Cross-link:** Page post → website case-study page → DM CTA.

**Cadence target:** 1 testimonial / case-study post every 2 weeks. Don't burn supply — quality each one to feel earned.

**GLV-specific:** Reyco, Titan, Fusion case studies should each get a Page post in rotation once consented and live.

## What NOT to do (anti-patterns 2026)

Per Postiz, SocialPilot, ALM Corp (Meta Original Content Rules 2026):

1. **Don't repost unoriginal content as primary feed.** Meta's 2026 rules cap distribution and risk demonetization for pages that primarily repost. Reposts are fine as ~20% of mix, not 80%.
2. **Don't post daily filler.** 3-4 strong posts/week beats 7 mediocre. Buffer 2026 data backs this.
3. **Don't ignore comments.** Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours — directly measurable reach lift per QuickFrame.
4. **Don't run ad campaigns on a quiet organic page.** Per Metricool + business.com 2026: ads and organic must "speak with one voice" — a Page that posts nothing organically and only runs ads gets lower QualityScore + higher CPM.
5. **Don't use AI-tells.** Banked rule [[feedback-glv-social-content-rules]] applies to FB the same as IG/Threads: no em-dashes, no negation tricolons over-frequency, no "Excited to announce" openers.
6. **Don't post identical content across IG/FB/Threads.** Each platform has different format/hook conventions — see [[threads-publishing-rules-2026-05-18]] for the Threads delta. FB tolerates longer captions than Threads, longer videos than Reels, and carousels of more images than Threads (Blotato pathway).
